Valley City State University

Valley City, ND
101 College St SW, Valley City, ND 58072
701-845-7990

Valley City State University is a four-year public university located in Valley City, North Dakota. It is part of the North Dakota University System. The university offers bachelor's and master's degrees in a variety of disciplines.

Founded in 1890, Valley City State University is the third oldest institution in the North Dakota University System. The university began as a normal school, training teachers for the region's rural schools. The school became a four-year college in 1957 and began offering graduate programs in 1969.

Today, Valley City State University is a comprehensive university offering a wide range of academic programs. The university's most popular majors include education, business, and the sciences. Valley City State University is also home to a nationally recognized distance education program.

Valley City State University is located in the heart of the Red River Valley, a region of rich agricultural land. The university's campus is situated on a bluff overlooking the city of Valley City and the valley below. The university's buildings are spread out over a large area, providing students with plenty of space to study and relax.

Valley City State University is committed to providing a quality education at an affordable price. The university offers a variety of scholarships and financial aid programs to help students pay for their education. In addition, the university works closely with the community to provide job opportunities for students after graduation.
