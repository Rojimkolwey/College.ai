Frostburg State University is located in the Appalachian Mountains of Western Maryland, about 100 miles from Washington, D.C. and Baltimore. The University has an enrollment of about 5,000 students, representing 37 states and 42 countries. The student-faculty ratio is 16:1, and the average class size is 20. The University offers more than 50 undergraduate majors and 10 graduate programs.

Frostburg State University was founded in 1898 as a normal school to train teachers. The school became a four-year college in 1930 and began offering graduate programs in 1970. The University is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education.

Frostburg State University is committed to providing a quality education at an affordable price. The University offers a variety of scholarships and financial aid programs to help students meet the cost of tuition and other expenses.

Frostburg State University is located in a beautiful setting in the Appalachian Mountains. The campus includes more than 200 acres of woods and fields, a lake, and a stream. The University also has a recreation center, a performing arts center, and a planetarium.

Frostburg State University offers a wide range of academic programs. The University has strong programs in business, education, and the liberal arts. Frostburg State University also offers a unique program in Appalachian Studies. This program includes courses in Appalachian history, literature, music, and art.

Frostburg State University is an active member of the community. The University offers a variety of programs and services to the citizens of Frostburg and the surrounding area. The University also provides a variety of cultural and recreational opportunities for the community.
