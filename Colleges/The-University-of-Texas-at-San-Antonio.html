The University of Texas at San Antonio (UTSA) is a public research university in San Antonio, Texas. With over 32,000 students, it is the largest university in San Antonio and the eighth-largest in the state of Texas. It is a member of the University of Texas System.

The university was founded in 1969 as the San Antonio College of the University of Texas. It was renamed the University of Texas at San Antonio in 1971 and became a stand-alone institution in 1979 when it was separated from the UT System.

UTSA is a Hispanic-serving institution and is categorized as an R1 research university by the Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education. It is home to two major research institutes, the Cyber Security Hub and the Center for Infrastructure Assurance and Security.

The university offers over 140 undergraduate and graduate programs, including degrees in business, engineering, architecture, liberal arts, and sciences. It is also home to the UTSA Roadrunners athletics program, which competes in the NCAA Division I Conference USA.

Admission Requirements

The University of Texas at San Antonio (UTSA) is a public research university in San Antonio, Texas. It is one of the four campuses of the University of Texas System. UTSA offers bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees in over 140 fields of study.

UTSA is classified as a "Doctoral University: Higher Research Activity" and a "Community Engaged" university by the Carnegie Foundation, and it is one of only 115 research universities in the nation with that distinction. The university is also home to the National Security Agency and the Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency.

UTSA's admission requirements vary depending on the type of student you are. For freshmen, the requirements are:

- A high school diploma or equivalent

- A minimum 2.5 GPA on a 4.0 scale

- A minimum 18 on the ACT or 860 on the SAT

For transfer students, the requirements are:

- A minimum 2.5 GPA on a 4.0 scale

- A minimum of 24 transferable semester credit hours

For international students, the requirements are:

- A minimum 2.5 GPA on a 4.0 scale

- A minimum of 18 on the ACT or 860 on the SAT

- A minimum of 24 transferable semester credit hours

- Proof of English proficiency

For graduate students, the requirements vary by program. For more information, please visit the UTSA Graduate School website.
