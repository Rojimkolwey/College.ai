Rhode Island College

Providence, RI
600 Mount Pleasant Ave, Providence, RI 02908
401-456-8000

Founded in 1854, Rhode Island College is a comprehensive, public institution of higher education and a member of the five-campus Rhode Island state university system. The college's 180-acre campus is located in the Mount Pleasant neighborhood of Providence, Rhode Island. The college offers bachelor's degrees in over 50 majors and concentrations, as well as master's degrees in education, business, and social work.

In addition to its academic programs, Rhode Island College is home to a number of athletic teams, including men's and women's basketball, cross country, and track and field. The college is also home to a number of student organizations, including the Student Government Association, the Black Student Union, and the College Democrats.

Rhode Island College is accredited by the New England Association of Schools and Colleges.

Admission Requirements

In order to be admitted to Rhode Island College, students must submit an application, transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a nonrefundable application fee. Freshman applicants must also submit a high school counselor recommendation and an official high school transcript. Homeschooled applicants must submit a notarized affidavit of completion of high school and an official final transcript. GED applicants must submit official GED scores and transcripts.

International applicants must submit a TOEFL or IELTS score, transcripts, and a credential evaluation. They must also submit a nonrefundable application fee and an Affidavit of Support.

Transfer applicants must submit transcripts from all colleges and universities previously attended, a nonrefundable application fee, and a transfer credit evaluation form.

All applicants must have a minimum 2.0 GPA and must have completed a college preparatory curriculum including 4 years of English, 3 years of mathematics, 2 years of natural science, 2 years of social science, and 4 years of physical education.
