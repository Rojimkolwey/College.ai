Pacific Northwest University of Health Sciences

Yakima, WA
200 University Parkway, Yakima, WA 98901
509-452-5100

Pacific Northwest University of Health Sciences (PNWU) is a private, non-profit university located in Yakima, Washington. The university offers graduate-level programs in osteopathic medicine, physician assistant studies, physical therapy, occupational therapy, and pharmacology. PNWU is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities (NWCCU) and is a member of the Association of American Colleges and Universities (AAC&U).

PNWU was founded in 2001 with the mission to educate and train health care professionals to serve the people of the Pacific Northwest. The university opened its doors to students in 2004, and has since graduated over 1,500 health care professionals.

PNWU offers a unique educational experience, with a curriculum that emphasizes primary care and the integration of Western and Eastern medicine. The university also has a strong commitment to serving underserved and rural communities. PNWU's graduates are well-prepared to enter the workforce and make a positive impact in the communities they serve.

Admission Requirements

Pacific Northwest University of Health Sciences (PNWU) is a private, non-profit university located in Yakima, Washington. PNWU offers doctor of naturopathic medicine (ND) and doctor of acupuncture and Oriental medicine (DAOM) programs.

Admission to PNWU is competitive and based on a holistic review of the applicant's file. The Admissions Committee looks for evidence of the applicant's ability to succeed academically, as well as personal and professional qualities that will make the applicant a good fit for the PNWU community.

To be eligible for admission to PNWU, applicants must have a bachelor's degree from an accredited institution with a minimum GPA of 3.0. In addition, applicants must complete the following prerequisite courses:

-Two courses in general chemistry with labs

-Two courses in organic chemistry with labs

-Two courses in biology with labs

-One course in physics with a lab

-One course in English Composition

-One course in Statistics

Applicants must also submit official transcripts, letters of recommendation, a personal essay, and a resume.

PNWU uses a rolling admissions process, meaning that applications are reviewed and decisions are made on a rolling basis. The Admissions Committee will continue to review and accept applications until all seats in the incoming class are filled.
