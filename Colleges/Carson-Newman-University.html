Carson-Newman University is a private Christian liberal arts university in Jefferson City, Tennessee, USA. Carson-Newman is affiliated with the Tennessee Baptist Convention and is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools. The university was founded in 1851 as Carson College by Carson-Newman College and the Baptist minister J.J. Diggs. It was renamed Carson-Newman University in 1856. The university has an enrollment of approximately 2,700 students. The university offers bachelor's and master's degrees in a variety of disciplines.

Carson-Newman University is committed to providing a quality education that is grounded in the liberal arts tradition and shaped by the Judeo-Christian ethic of service to others. The university strives to develop students who are well-educated, spiritually alive, and prepared to lead and serve in a rapidly changing world. Carson-Newman University is committed to academic excellence, personal development, and global citizenship.

Admission Requirements

Carson-Newman University is a private Christian liberal arts university in Jefferson City, Tennessee, USA. Founded in 1851, it is the oldest university in Tennessee. The university has an enrollment of over 2,000 students.

Carson-Newman is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges to award baccalaureate and master's degrees. The university is also a member of the Council for Christian Colleges and Universities.

Carson-Newman offers over 50 undergraduate majors and 20 graduate programs. The university is organized into four colleges: the College of Arts and Sciences, the College of Business, the College of Education, and the College of Nursing.

Admission to Carson-Newman is selective. The university's admissions standards are in line with those of other private colleges and universities in the United States. In order to be considered for admission, applicants must submit an application, high school transcripts, and SAT or ACT scores.

Carson-Newman University is committed to providing a quality education to all who seek it. The university offers a variety of financial aid options to help make college affordable. For more information about admission requirements and financial aid, please visit the Carson-Newman website.
