Northwest University-College of Adult and Professional Studies

Kirkland, WA
5520 108th Ave NE, Kirkland, WA 98033
425-822-8266

Northwest University's College of Adult and Professional Studies (CAPS) is a unique, innovative and exciting place to learn. Our programs are designed specifically for working adults, and our faculty and staff are committed to providing an excellent educational experience for all students.

CAPS offers a variety of degree and certificate programs, as well as non-credit courses, all of which are designed to help students achieve their personal and professional goals. We offer flexible scheduling options, and our classes are taught by experienced professionals who are experts in their field.

We are proud to offer a supportive and friendly environment, and our staff is always available to help students with any questions or concerns they may have. We are dedicated to helping our students succeed, and we are confident that they will find our programs to be both challenging and rewarding.

Admission Requirements

Northwest University-College of Adult and Professional Studies is a regionally accredited institution that offers flexible and affordable programs for working adults. The admissions process is designed to be simple and straightforward, and the admissions team is committed to helping you every step of the way.

To be eligible for admission, you must have a high school diploma or equivalent. If you have not completed high school, you may still be eligible for admission if you have completed a GED or other equivalent program.

Once you have met the basic eligibility requirements, you will need to submit an application, transcripts, and any required supporting documents. The admissions team will review your application and determine if you meet the minimum admissions requirements.

If you are eligible for admission, you will be asked to complete an orientation and assessment process. This process is designed to help you determine if Northwest University-College of Adult and Professional Studies is the right fit for you.

Once you have completed the orientation and assessment process, you will be able to register for classes and begin working towards your degree.
