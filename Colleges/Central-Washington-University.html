Central Washington University (CWU) is a public university in Ellensburg, Washington. Founded in 1891, the university consists of five main academic divisions: the College of Arts and Humanities, the College of Business, the College of the Sciences, the School of Education, and the Graduate School. The university also operates a number of research centers and institutes, including the Center for Excellence in STEM Education, the Institute for Public Policy and Economic Analysis, and the Institute for Research on Poverty.

With over 12,000 students, CWU is the largest university in the region. The university offers a wide variety of undergraduate and graduate programs, as well as a number of professional and continuing education programs. CWU is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities.

CWU was founded in 1891 as the Washington State Normal School, a teacher-training institution. The school was renamed several times over the years, becoming Central Washington College of Education in 1937, Central Washington State College in 1961, and finally Central Washington University in 1977.

The university has grown significantly since its founding, expanding its academic programs and facilities. In recent years, CWU has added a number of new academic programs, including degrees in engineering, computer science, and nursing. The university has also built a number of new facilities, including the Samuelson Union Building, the Student Recreation Center, and the Surplus Property Warehouse.

CWU is committed to providing a quality education to its students. The university has a number of programs and services in place to help students succeed, including academic advising, tutoring, and career services. CWU also offers a number of scholarships and financial aid programs to help students pay for their education.

Admission Requirements

Central Washington University is a public research university in Ellensburg, Washington. The university offers bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees in a variety of disciplines.

Admission to Central Washington University is competitive. The university typically receives more than 20,000 applications each year and enrolls about 4,000 new students.

To be considered for admission, applicants must submit an application, transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a non-refundable application fee. International students must also submit proof of English proficiency.

Admission decisions are based on a holistic review of each applicant's academic record, test scores, personal statements, and other factors. The university strives to enroll a diverse and academically talented student body.

Central Washington University is committed to making higher education accessible and affordable. The university offers a variety of financial aid programs, including scholarships, grants, loans, and work-study. More than 80% of students receive some form of financial aid.

For more information about admission requirements and the application process, visit the Central Washington University website.
