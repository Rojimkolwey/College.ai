The University at Buffalo is a public research university with campuses in Buffalo and Amherst, New York, United States. It is the flagship institution of the State University of New York (SUNY) system, and offers more than 100 bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degree programs. The university is classified as an R1: Doctoral University (highest research activity) by the Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education.

Founded in 1846 as a private medical college, the University at Buffalo is a founding member of the Association of American Universities. The university has multiple campuses, including the flagship campus in Buffalo's University Heights neighborhood and the Amherst Campus in Amherst. The university also operates the SUNY Global Center in New York City, which serves as a resource for students and faculty pursuing international studies.

The University at Buffalo is a member of the NCAA Division I and competes as a member of the Mid-American Conference. The Bulls field 17 NCAA Division I level teams, including football, basketball, baseball, soccer, and volleyball. The university's athletic teams are nicknamed the Bulls.

The University at Buffalo was founded in 1846 as the University of Buffalo by a group of local businessmen and physicians. The university began with an initial class of just over 100 students and has grown steadily over the years to become one of the largest universities in the State University of New York system. The university is now home to over 29,000 students, making it the largest public university in New York State outside of the City University of New York system.

The University at Buffalo is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education. The university offers more than 100 undergraduate, graduate, and professional degree programs. The university's academic divisions include the College of Arts and Sciences, the School of Architecture and Planning, the School of Dental Medicine, the School of Education, the School of Engineering and Applied Sciences, the School of Law, the School of Management, the School of Medicine and Biomedical Sciences, the School of Nursing, the School of Pharmacy and Pharmaceutical Sciences, the School of Public Health and Health Professions, and the Graduate School of Arts and Sciences.

The University at Buffalo is a research-intensive university and is classified as an R1: Doctoral University by the Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education. The university is home to a number of research centers and institutes, including the Center for Computational Research, the New York State Center of Excellence in Materials Informatics, the Center for Unified Biometrics and Sensors, and the Institute for Lasers, Photonics, and Biophotonics.

The University at Buffalo is a member of the Association of American Universities, a group of 62 leading public and private research universities in the United States and Canada. The university is also a member of the Universities Research Association, a consortium of 89 of the leading research universities in the United States and Canada.

The University at Buffalo is home to the SUNY Global Center, a facility located in New York City that serves as a resource for students and faculty pursuing international studies. The Global Center offers a variety of programs and services, including an International Student and Scholar Services office, a language center, and a library.

The University at Buffalo is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education.

Admission Requirements

In order to be admitted to the University at Buffalo, students must meet the minimum admission requirements set forth by the university. These requirements include a high school diploma or equivalent, a minimum GPA of 2.5, and a minimum SAT score of 950 or ACT score of 21. In addition, students must also complete an application and submit all required supporting documents. Once all materials have been received, the university will review the application and make a decision based on the student's qualifications.
