New Saint Andrews College is a small, private, Christian college located in Moscow, Idaho. The school was founded in 1994 by Doug Wilson and his wife, Nancy. The college is accredited by the Transnational Association of Christian Colleges and Schools. New Saint Andrews offers a classical liberal arts education with a biblical worldview. The college has an enrollment of about 500 students, and the student-to-faculty ratio is 12-to-1. The average class size is 15 students. New Saint Andrews is committed to providing a high-quality, affordable education. The college offers need-based financial aid and scholarships.

Admission Requirements

New Saint Andrews College is a private, conservative, Christian college located in Moscow, Idaho. It was founded in 1994 by Douglas Wilson and is accredited by the Transnational Association of Christian Colleges and Schools. New Saint Andrews is committed to a classical liberal arts education from a biblical worldview.

The admissions process at New Saint Andrews is designed to identify students who are academically prepared for college-level work and who also demonstrate a commitment to the college's mission and values. The admissions requirements are as follows:

1. A completed application form with a non-refundable $50 application fee

2. An official high school transcript

3. SAT or ACT scores

4. A personal essay

5. A letter of recommendation from a teacher or counselor

6. An interview with an admissions counselor

Students who meet the above requirements will be considered for admission to New Saint Andrews. Admission is based on a holistic review of the student's application and is not solely based on test scores or GPA. The admissions committee looks for evidence of academic preparedness, personal character, and a commitment to the college's mission.
