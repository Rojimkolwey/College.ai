Edward Via College of Osteopathic Medicine

Blacksburg, VA
2265 Kraft Dr, Blacksburg, VA 24060
540-231-4000

Founded in 1974, Edward Via College of Osteopathic Medicine (VCOM) is a private, non-profit osteopathic medical school with its main campus in Blacksburg, Virginia and an additional campus in Spartanburg, South Carolina. VCOM offers the Doctor of Osteopathic Medicine (DO) degree and trains osteopathic physicians (DOs) to provide patient-centered care.

VCOM’s mission is to prepare globally-minded, community-focused physicians who are committed to lifelong learning and serving the underserved. The college is accredited by the Commission on Osteopathic College Accreditation (COCA) and offers a four-year DO degree program.

VCOM has a diverse student body with students coming from all over the United States and from various backgrounds. The college offers a supportive and collaborative environment with small class sizes and a low student-to-faculty ratio.

VCOM’s curriculum is designed to prepare students for a career in primary care and to provide them with the knowledge and skills necessary to be successful in any medical specialty. The curriculum includes didactic coursework, clinical rotations, and research opportunities.

VCOM graduates are highly sought-after by residency programs and are well-prepared to enter into any medical specialty. The college has a strong commitment to service and graduates often go on to work in underserved communities.

Admission Requirements

Admission to Edward Via College of Osteopathic Medicine (VCOM) is a competitive process that considers applicants’ academic record, MCAT scores, letters of recommendation, community service and extracurricular activities, and personal interviews. The Admissions Committee seeks to enroll a class of students who will be successful in the rigorous academic environment of VCOM and who will go on to become osteopathic physicians committed to serving the health care needs of rural and underserved communities.

VCOM does not have a minimum GPA or MCAT score requirement for admission. However, the Admissions Committee does consider these factors, along with the other elements of the application, when making admissions decisions.

VCOM does not offer conditional admission. All applicants must meet the requirements for admission prior to matriculation.

In order to be eligible for admission to VCOM, applicants must:

• Have earned a bachelor’s degree from a regionally accredited institution

• Have completed the required prerequisite coursework

• Have taken the MCAT within three years of the date of application

• Submit a complete and accurate application, including all required supporting documentation, by the deadline

• Interview with the Admissions Committee (by invitation only)

VCOM does not require that applicants have a specific major; however, all applicants must have completed the required prerequisite coursework prior to matriculation. The Admissions Committee looks for a strong academic record, including a minimum GPA of 3.0 in the sciences, in making admissions decisions.

VCOM requires that all applicants take the Medical College Admission Test (MCAT). The Admissions Committee will consider MCAT scores that are no more than three years old at the time of application.

All applicants must submit a complete and accurate application, including all required supporting documentation, by the deadline. Incomplete applications will not be considered.

All applicants who meet the minimum requirements for admission will be invited to interview with the Admissions Committee. Interviews are conducted on-campus and are by invitation only. The Admissions Committee uses the interview process to assess an applicant’s motivation for a career in osteopathic medicine, as well as to evaluate an applicant’s communication and interpersonal skills.
