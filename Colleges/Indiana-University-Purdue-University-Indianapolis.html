Indiana University-Purdue University-Indianapolis (IUPUI) is a public research university in Indianapolis, Indiana. A core campus of Indiana University that also hosts Purdue University programs, it is the result of a merger of the two universities in 1969. It is the state's largest university and the only public university in the city.

The campus is located just west of downtown Indianapolis, on the site of the former Indiana Central Canal. The school is a member of the Association of American Universities and the Big Ten Conference. It is also home to the Indiana University School of Medicine, the largest medical school in the United States.

IUPUI has an enrollment of more than 30,000 students, making it the second-largest university in Indiana after Indiana University Bloomington. The school offers more than 200 undergraduate, graduate, and professional degrees from IU and Purdue.

The university is divided into 20 academic schools and departments, which offer more than 350 degree programs. The most popular majors at IUPUI include business, health sciences, nursing, psychology, and social sciences.

IUPUI is known for its health sciences programs, which are some of the best in the country. The school is also home to a number of research centers, including the Indiana University Center for Health Services and Outcomes Research, the Indiana University Center for Aging Research, and the Regenstrief Institute.

The university is committed to diversity and inclusion, and its student body is reflective of that commitment. More than one-third of IUPUI students are from minority groups, and the school has a large international student population.

IUPUI is a dynamic and vibrant university that is constantly evolving to meet the needs of its students and the community. With a commitment to excellence in teaching, research, and service, IUPUI is poised to continue its tradition of being a leading urban public university.

Admission Requirements

Indiana University-Purdue University-Indianapolis (IUPUI) is a public research university located in Indianapolis, Indiana. Founded in 1969, IUPUI is the result of a merger between Indiana University and Purdue University. As a result, IUPUI is part of both Indiana University and Purdue University systems.

IUPUI is a large university, with an enrollment of over 30,000 students. The university is made up of 17 schools and colleges, offering over 200 undergraduate, graduate, and professional degrees.

The admissions process at IUPUI is selective. For undergraduate students, the university uses a holistic review process to evaluate applicants. The university considers a variety of factors in the admissions process, including academic achievement, test scores, letters of recommendation, and personal essays.

In order to be considered for admission to IUPUI, undergraduate students must submit an application, transcripts, test scores, and a non-refundable application fee. The university has a rolling admissions policy, meaning that applications are reviewed as they are received.

IUPUI is committed to enrolling a diverse student body and offers a variety of scholarships and financial aid programs to make college affordable for all students. For more information about admission requirements and the admissions process at IUPUI, please visit the university's website.
