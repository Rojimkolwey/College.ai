The College of Southern Nevada (CSN) is a public community college in the Las Vegas metropolitan area of the U.S. state of Nevada. It is the largest community college in the state of Nevada and the third largest community college in the United States.

CSN offers more than 200 degree and certificate programs. The college has four campuses in the Las Vegas area, including the North Las Vegas Campus, the Charleston Campus, the Henderson Campus, and the new CSN Downtown Campus.

CSN is accredited by the Commission on Institutions of Higher Education of the Northwest Association of Schools and Colleges. The college is also a member of the American Association of Community Colleges.

CSN was established in 1971 as Clark County Community College. The college changed its name to College of Southern Nevada in 1999.

In 2007, the college was named one of the top 10 community colleges in the nation by the Aspen Institute.

In 2014, the college was named one of the nation's 150 best community colleges by the Aspen Institute.

The college has an enrollment of more than 30,000 students.
