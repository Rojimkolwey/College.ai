Founded in 1925, Mount Saint Mary’s University is a private, Catholic, liberal arts university located in Los Angeles, California. The university is committed to academic excellence, personal development and leadership formation in the Catholic tradition. The beautiful 56-acre Chalon campus is situated atop Doheny Peak in the Hollywood Hills, providing stunning views of the city below. The university also has a 60-acre campus in Brentwood, which is home to the Doheny Eye Institute and the university’s graduate and professional programs.

Mount Saint Mary’s University offers more than 50 undergraduate and graduate programs, as well as a Doctor of Nursing Practice program. The most popular majors among undergraduates are business, psychology, biology and nursing. The university is also home to the Chalon School of Communication, the only stand-alone communication school in Los Angeles.

Mount Saint Mary’s University is accredited by the Western Association of Schools and Colleges and is a member of the Association of Catholic Colleges and Universities. The university’s nursing program is accredited by the Commission on Collegiate Nursing Education.

The university’s 5,000 students come from all over the world and represent a wide range of backgrounds and experiences. The student body is approximately 60 percent female and 40 percent male. The average class size is 20 students, and the student-to-faculty ratio is 11-to-1.

Admission Requirements

Mount Saint Mary’s University is a private, Catholic university in Los Angeles, California. The university was founded in 1925 as a women’s college and began admitting men in 1968. Today, Mount Saint Mary’s University enrolls nearly 3,000 students from diverse backgrounds.

The university is accredited by the Western Association of Schools and Colleges. Mount Saint Mary’s University offers bachelor’s degrees in more than 30 majors, as well as master’s degrees in business, education, nursing, and counseling.

To be considered for admission to Mount Saint Mary’s University, applicants must submit an application, high school transcripts, and SAT or ACT scores. The university also considers letters of recommendation, essays, and extracurricular activities in the admissions process.

Mount Saint Mary’s University has a rolling admissions policy, meaning that there is no set deadline for applications. However, the university recommends that applicants submit their materials as early as possible to ensure the best chance of admission.
