Boise State University is a public research university in Boise, Idaho. Founded in 1932 by the Episcopal Church, it became an independent junior college in 1934 and a four-year institution in 1965. Boise State offers more than 200 graduate programs, including master's degrees in business, public administration, education, engineering, nursing, and computer science, and doctorates in education and counseling psychology. The university's athletic teams, the Broncos, compete in Division I of the NCAA and are members of the Mountain West Conference. The university has an endowment of $220 million.

Admission Requirements

Boise State University is a public research university in Boise, Idaho. Founded in 1932 by the Episcopal Church, it became an independent junior college in 1934 and a four-year institution in 1965. Boise State offers more than 200 undergraduate and graduate degrees in over 175 fields of study.

The university has a highly selective admissions process, with an acceptance rate of just over 50%. In order to be considered for admission, applicants must submit an application, high school transcripts, and SAT or ACT scores. For students who are not able to submit standardized test scores, Boise State offers a number of alternative admission options, including the portfolio-based Boise State Achievement Scholarship and the opportunity to submit an essay in lieu of test scores.

Once admitted, students must complete a first-year experience course and maintain a 2.0 GPA in order to progress to sophomore year. Boise State also has a number of unique programs and opportunities for students, including the chance to study abroad, participate in research, and intern with local businesses and organizations.
