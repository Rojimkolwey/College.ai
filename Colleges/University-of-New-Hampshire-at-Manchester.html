The University of New Hampshire at Manchester is a public research university in Manchester, New Hampshire. It is one of the four campuses of the University of New Hampshire system. The university offers more than 50 undergraduate programs and 20 graduate programs, including a Doctor of Nursing Practice program. The university's main campus is located on a 300-acre (1.2 km2) site in the city's North End, and its downtown campus covers 15 acres (6.1 ha).

The university was founded in 1985 as the New Hampshire Technical Institute, a two-year technical school. In 1987, the school was renamed the New Hampshire Institute of Technology. In 2001, the institute was granted university status by the New Hampshire Legislature and became the University of New Hampshire at Manchester. The university has an enrollment of more than 4,000 students, most of whom are undergraduates.

The university is accredited by the New England Association of Schools and Colleges. The university's athletic teams compete in the NCAA Division III Northeast-10 Conference. The university's men's ice hockey team competes in the New England Hockey Conference.

Admission Requirements

The University of New Hampshire at Manchester is a public research university with an acceptance rate of 86%. Located in the state's largest city, UNH Manchester is part of the University of New Hampshire system and offers bachelor's, master's, and doctorate degrees. The university is situated on a picturesque campus along the Merrimack River.

To be admitted to UNH Manchester, students must submit an application, high school transcripts, and SAT or ACT scores. The university does not require essays or letters of recommendation. For students who are not native English speakers, the university requires proof of English proficiency. The university has a rolling admissions policy, meaning that applications are reviewed and decisions are made on a rolling basis.

UNH Manchester is a comprehensive university with a wide range of academic programs. The most popular majors at the university include business, psychology, nursing, and education. The university is also home to a number of research centers, including the Institute for the Study of Earth, Oceans, and Space and the New Hampshire Statewide STEM Education Center.

Students at UNH Manchester are active both on and off campus. The university offers a number of clubs and organizations, and students can also take advantage of the many cultural and recreational opportunities in the city of Manchester. The university is also home to a number of Division I sports teams, and students can participate in intramural sports as well.
