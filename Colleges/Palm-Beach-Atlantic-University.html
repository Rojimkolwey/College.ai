Palm Beach Atlantic University (PBA) is a private, accredited college located in West Palm Beach, Florida, United States. The university is affiliated with the Assemblies of God. PBA was founded in 1968 as a Christian college by Dr. Jess C. Moody, then the superintendent of the Florida District of the Assemblies of God. It was originally established as a two-year junior college, known as Palm Beach Atlantic College, and began offering classes in September 1969. In June 1986, the Florida Legislature authorized the college to offer its first four-year degree, and in December 1987, Palm Beach Atlantic was granted university status by the Florida Department of Education.

The university is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges to award bachelor's and master's degrees. PBA is also a member of the Council for Christian Colleges and Universities (CCCU). The university's mission is "to prepare students for lifelong learning and service to God and humanity in the marketplace."

PBA offers over 50 undergraduate majors and 10 graduate programs. The university is divided into five colleges: the College of Arts and Sciences, the Lloyd L. Gregory School of Pharmacy, the Rinker School of Business, the Warren L. Williamson, Jr. School of Nursing, and the School of Education. The university also has an Honors College and an Adult and Graduate Studies program.

PBA's main campus is located on 86 acres (35 hectares) of land in downtown West Palm Beach. The campus includes several buildings, including the Warren Library, the Greene Complex, the Rubenstein Arts Center, and the W. R. Grace Hall of Science. The university also has a satellite campus in Wellington, Florida, and a presence in downtown Orlando, Florida.

In 2019, U.S. News & World Report ranked PBA as the 121st best regional university in the South and the 7th best value school in the region. The university was also ranked as the 3rd most innovative school in the South and the 10th best college for veterans in the region.

Admission Requirements

Palm Beach Atlantic University (PBA) is a private, accredited, Christ-centered college located in West Palm Beach, Florida, USA. PBAU offers undergraduate and graduate degrees in a variety of fields, including business, nursing, education, communication, and the liberal arts.

PBAU is committed to providing a quality education that is affordable and accessible to all students. The admissions process is designed to be fair, transparent, and straightforward. All applicants are evaluated based on their academic merits, personal achievements, and potential to contribute to the PBA community.

PBAU has a rolling admissions policy, which means that applications are reviewed and decisions are made on a rolling basis. This means that there is no set deadline for applications, and students are encouraged to apply as early as possible.

To be eligible for admission to PBAU, applicants must have a high school diploma or equivalent. They must also submit official transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a personal essay.

PBAU is committed to enrolling a diverse student body, and offers a variety of scholarships and financial aid options to make college affordable for all students. For more information about admission requirements and the admissions process, please visit the PBAU website.
