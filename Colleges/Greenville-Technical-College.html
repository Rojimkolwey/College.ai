Greenville Technical College

Greenville, SC
506 S Pleasantburg Dr, Greenville, SC 29606
864-250-8000

Greenville Technical College, located in Greenville, South Carolina, is a two-year public institution of higher education that offers associate degrees, diplomas, and certificates in more than 100 areas of study. The college also provides adult education and continuing education courses, as well as workforce development programs.

Founded in 1962, Greenville Technical College is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges to award associate degrees. The college is comprised of six campuses located throughout Greenville County, as well as a corporate and continuing education center.

Greenville Technical College enrolls more than 23,000 students annually and employs more than 1,200 faculty and staff members. The college’s six campuses include the main campus in downtown Greenville, as well as the Northwest, Southeast, and South campuses. The college also has a Center for Manufacturing Innovation and a Center for Health Sciences.

Greenville Technical College offers a variety of associate degree and certificate programs, as well as adult education and continuing education courses. The college’s associate degree programs include business, health sciences, engineering technologies, and liberal arts. The college’s certificate programs include automotive technology, cosmetology, and welding.

Greenville Technical College’s workforce development programs provide training for a variety of careers, including healthcare, manufacturing, construction, and information technology. The college also offers a variety of online courses and programs.

Admission Requirements

Greenville Technical College (GTC) is a public institution of higher education located in Greenville, South Carolina. The college offers associate degrees, diplomas, and certificate programs in a variety of fields, as well as continuing education and workforce development opportunities.

GTC has an open admissions policy, meaning that any student who meets the minimum requirements for admission will be accepted into the college. The minimum requirements for admission are as follows:

- Must be a high school graduate or have earned a GED

- Must submit an application for admission

- Must submit official high school and/or college transcripts

- Must take a placement test (if required)

- Must meet any additional requirements for specific programs

Once students have met the minimum requirements for admission, they will be evaluated on a case-by-case basis to determine if they are ready for college-level coursework. Students who are not quite ready for college may be placed in a developmental education program to help them build the skills they need to be successful.

GTC is committed to helping all students succeed and reach their academic and career goals. The college provides a variety of services and resources to support students, including academic advising, tutoring, career services, and more.
