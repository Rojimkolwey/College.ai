Central Wyoming College

Riverton, WY
2660 Peck Avenue, Riverton, WY 82501
800-735-8418

Central Wyoming College (CWC) is a public two-year college in Riverton, Wyoming. It is the only community college in the state and one of the largest in the Rocky Mountain region. The college offers associate's degrees and certificate programs in a variety of fields, as well as a bachelor's degree in nursing. CWC also has a campus in Jackson, Wyoming, which offers a limited selection of courses.

CWC was founded in 1966 as a branch of the University of Wyoming. It became an independent institution in 1968 and began offering its own degrees in 1970. The college has grown steadily in the years since, adding new programs and facilities. Today, it serves over 4,000 students from across Wyoming and the surrounding states.

CWC is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and is a member of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools. It is also accredited by the Wyoming State Board of Nursing.

Admission Requirements

Central Wyoming College is an accredited two-year institution of higher education located in Riverton, Wyoming. The College offers Associate of Arts, Associate of Science, and Associate of Applied Science degrees, as well as one-year certificates in a variety of career and technical fields. Credentials awarded by the College are recognized by the Wyoming Community College Commission and the Higher Learning Commission.

Admission to Central Wyoming College is open to any student who has graduated from high school or who has successfully completed the General Educational Development (GED) test. Students who have not yet completed high school may be admitted on a provisional basis if they are at least 16 years of age and have demonstrated the ability to benefit from postsecondary education.

All new students are required to complete an application for admission and submit all required transcripts and test scores. International students must also submit proof of English proficiency. Once all required materials have been received, the College will review the student's file and determine whether or not they are eligible for admission.

Students who are eligible for admission will be notified of their acceptance and will be given instructions on how to register for classes. Registration for classes is done on a first-come, first-served basis, so it is important to register as soon as possible.

Once classes have begun, students may add or drop courses as necessary. However, students who drop below 12 credit hours may be subject to financial aid penalties. Therefore, it is important to speak with a financial aid advisor before making any changes to your schedule.
