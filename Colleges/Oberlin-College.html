Oberlin College is a private liberal arts college in Oberlin, Ohio. The college was founded in 1833 by John Jay Shipherd and Philo Stewart as the first coeducational college in the United States. The college is currently run by President Carmen Twillie Ambar.

The college is known for its academic excellence, its commitment to social justice, and its dedication to environmental sustainability. Oberlin College is consistently ranked as one of the top liberal arts colleges in the country.

The college offers a rigorous academic program, with a focus on the liberal arts and sciences. Oberlin College students have the opportunity to study a wide range of subjects, including the arts, humanities, social sciences, natural sciences, and more.

Oberlin College is committed to social justice, and its students are actively involved in a variety of social justice initiatives. The college has a long history of activism, and its students have played a significant role in the civil rights movement, the women's rights movement, and the LGBTQ rights movement.

Oberlin College is also dedicated to environmental sustainability. The college has been a leader in the green movement, and it has been ranked as one of the most sustainable colleges in the country.

Oberlin College is an excellent choice for students who are looking for an academically rigorous education with a focus on social justice and environmental sustainability.

Admission Requirements

Oberlin College is a highly selective liberal arts college located in Oberlin, Ohio. The college was founded in 1833 and is the oldest continuously operating coeducational institution in the United States. The college is known for its rigorous academic program, its commitment to social justice, and its musical tradition.

To be considered for admission to Oberlin College, students must submit a completed application, official high school transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and two letters of recommendation. The admissions committee will also consider the student's personal essay, extracurricular activities, and demonstrated interest in the college.

Oberlin College is committed to enrolling a diverse and talented student body. The college has a need-blind admissions policy, meaning that financial need will not be a factor in the admissions decision. Oberlin also meets the full demonstrated financial need of all admitted students.

Approximately 3,000 students apply to Oberlin College each year. Of those, approximately 2,000 are offered admission. The college has an acceptance rate of 67%.
