Hawaii Universities and Colleges


Hawaii is home to many colleges and universities that provide a wide range of educational opportunities for students. The state’s two main public universities are the University of Hawaii System and the Hawaii Community College System. The University of Hawaii System includes ten campuses located throughout the Hawaiian Islands, including the University of Hawaii at Manoa, the flagship campus located in Honolulu. The Hawaii Community College System consists of seven campuses located on five of the Hawaiian Islands, and offers associate degrees, certificates, and transfer programs.

In addition to the public universities, Hawaii also has several private colleges and universities. These include the University of Hawaii at Hilo, Chaminade University of Honolulu, Hawaii Pacific University, Brigham Young University-Hawaii, and Hawaii Tokai International College. These schools offer a variety of degrees and programs, including business, education, engineering, and healthcare.

Hawaii also offers many technical and vocational schools, including Hawaii Technology Academy, Maui Technical Education Center, and the Hawaii Career and Technical Education Center. These schools provide students with the skills and training necessary for a career in a variety of fields.

Hawaii is also home to numerous community colleges, including Honolulu Community College, Leeward Community College, Windward Community College, and Kauai Community College. These schools provide students with associate degrees, certificates, and transfer programs.

Hawaii also offers many specialized schools, such as the Kamehameha Schools and the Hawaiian Language Immersion Program. These schools provide students with the opportunity to learn Hawaiian culture and language.

Hawaii is an ideal place to pursue higher education, with a variety of options to choose from. Whether you are seeking a traditional college experience or a specialized education, there is something for everyone in Hawaii.
