Warner Pacific University

Portland, OR
2219 SE 68th Ave, Portland, OR 97215
503-517-1000

Founded in 1937, Warner Pacific is a Christ-centered, urban, liberal arts university located in Southeast Portland. As a community committed to preparing students for leadership and service, Warner Pacific University is an inclusive learning community that embraces students from a diversity of cultures and perspectives. The University offers more than 30 undergraduate majors and professional programs, as well as graduate degrees in business, education, and counseling.

Warner Pacific is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities. The University is also a member of the Council for Christian Colleges and Universities, the Association of Independent Colleges and Universities of Oregon, and the Oregon Association of Independent Colleges and Universities.

The University’s commitment to its students is evident in its low student-to-faculty ratio of 12:1 and average class size of just 16 students. Warner Pacific’s faculty members are experts in their field and are passionate about teaching and mentoring students. The University’s supportive environment and rigorous academic programs prepare students for success in their careers and in their personal lives.

Located in one of the most livable cities in the United States, Warner Pacific University is an ideal place to pursue your academic goals. The University’s campus is just minutes from downtown Portland, offering students easy access to the city’s many cultural and recreational attractions. Warner Pacific is also a short drive from the Oregon Coast, the Cascade Mountains, and the Willamette Valley, making it the perfect place to study, work, and play.

Admission Requirements

Warner Pacific University is a private, nonprofit university located in Portland, Oregon. The university is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities. Warner Pacific offers undergraduate and graduate degrees in a variety of disciplines, including business, education, nursing, and more.

Undergraduate admission to Warner Pacific is based on a holistic review of the applicant's academic record, test scores, extracurricular involvement, and personal essay. The university does not have a minimum GPA or test score requirement, but the average GPA of admitted students is 3.5 and the average SAT score is 1160.

Graduate admission to Warner Pacific is based on the applicant's academic record, professional experience, and letters of recommendation. The university does not have a minimum GPA requirement, but the average GPA of admitted students is 3.7.

Warner Pacific University is committed to providing a quality education to all students, regardless of their financial circumstances. The university offers a variety of scholarships and financial aid programs to help make college affordable. For more information on admission requirements and financial aid, please visit the Warner Pacific website.
