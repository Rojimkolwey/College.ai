William Paterson University of New Jersey is a comprehensive public university located in Wayne, New Jersey, United States. Founded in 1855, William Paterson is the second-oldest of the nine state colleges and universities in New Jersey. It is also the largest public university in the state, with an enrollment of over 11,000 students. The university offers bachelor's and master's degrees in a wide variety of disciplines, including business, education, the humanities, the sciences, and the social sciences.

The university is situated on a 370-acre campus in the suburban town of Wayne, just 20 miles from New York City. The campus features a variety of buildings and facilities, including a performing arts center, a planetarium, and a library with over one million volumes. The university also has a strong commitment to community engagement, with a variety of programs and initiatives designed to promote civic engagement and service-learning.

William Paterson University is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education. The university's athletic teams compete in the NCAA Division III and are members of the New Jersey Athletic Conference.
