Willamette University is a private university in Salem, Oregon. Founded in 1842, it is the oldest university in the state and the first university established west of the Rocky Mountains. The university is a member of the National Association of Independent Colleges and Universities and is accredited by the Northwest Association of Schools and Colleges. The university's motto is "Not unto us, O Lord, not unto us, but unto thy name give glory."

Willamette has an undergraduate student body of approximately 2,000 students. The university offers bachelor's degrees in over 30 majors and is organized into four colleges: the College of Liberal Arts, the Atkinson Graduate School of Management, the College of Education, and the College of Law. The university also has a School of Music and a School of the Arts.

Willamette University was founded in 1842 by the Methodist Episcopal Church. The university's original mission was to "provide an education for the young men of the West in the liberal arts and sciences." The university was named after the Willamette River, which runs through Salem. The first class of students graduated in 1846.

In 1853, the university became the first in the West to grant a woman a Bachelor of Arts degree. In 1867, the university began admitting women on an equal basis with men.

Willamette University is located in Salem, Oregon, a city of approximately 170,000 people. Salem is the state capital and is located in the Willamette Valley, which is known for its fertile farmland and its mild climate.

Willamette University is a comprehensive university with a strong commitment to the liberal arts. The university offers a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs.

The university's faculty is committed to teaching and research. Willamette University is home to the Oregon State Archives and the Oregon Historical Society. The university is also a member of the Association of American Colleges and Universities.

Admission Requirements

Willamette University is a private, independent, liberal arts institution located in Salem, Oregon. The university was founded in 1842 and is the oldest university in the state of Oregon. The university is accredited by the Northwest Association of Schools and Colleges.

Willamette University has an undergraduate enrollment of approximately 2,000 students. The university offers bachelor's degrees in over 30 majors and minors. The most popular majors at Willamette University include business, economics, English, history, and psychology.

Willamette University has a competitive admissions process. The university does not have a minimum GPA or standardized test score requirement for admission. However, the university does consider academic achievement, leadership potential, and personal character when making admissions decisions.

To be considered for admission to Willamette University, students must submit an application, official high school transcripts, and SAT or ACT scores. Students are also required to submit an essay and two letters of recommendation. The university has a rolling admissions policy and applications are considered on a rolling basis.
