University of Illinois Chicago

Chicago, IL
601 S Morgan, Chicago, IL 60607
312-996-7000

Founded in 1867, the University of Illinois at Chicago is the largest university in the Chicago area, with over 33,000 students enrolled in 15 colleges. The university is home to the nation's first medical school, and its hospital is the second-largest in the city. UIC also has the largest public library in the Chicago area. The university's campus is located in the heart of the city, near some of the city's most popular attractions.

Admission Requirements

The University of Illinois at Chicago is a public research university in Chicago, Illinois. It is the largest university in the Chicago area, with over 30,000 students enrolled in 15 colleges. The university is a member of the Association of American Universities.

The university offers over 200 undergraduate and graduate programs, including business, engineering, law, medicine, and dentistry. The University of Illinois at Chicago is a highly selective university, with an acceptance rate of only 42%.

To be eligible for admission to the University of Illinois at Chicago, students must meet the following requirements:

- Submit an application through the Common Application or the Coalition Application

- Submit official high school transcripts

- Submit SAT or ACT scores

- Submit two letters of recommendation

- Submit a personal statement

International students must also submit the following:

- Submit official high school transcripts

- Submit SAT or ACT scores

- Submit two letters of recommendation

- Submit a personal statement

- Submit proof of English proficiency

Students who do not meet the above requirements may still be eligible for admission through the university's holistic review process.
