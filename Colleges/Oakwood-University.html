Oakwood University

Huntsville, AL
7000 Adventist Blvd NW, Huntsville, AL 35896
256-726-7000

Oakwood University is a historically black college located in Huntsville, Alabama. The school is affiliated with the Seventh-day Adventist Church and offers a Christ-centered education. The university was founded in 1896 as Oakwood Industrial Academy and began offering college-level courses in 1917. Today, Oakwood University is a comprehensive institution offering a wide range of academic programs. The school is known for its strong science and engineering programs, as well as its music and nursing programs. Students at Oakwood University benefit from a supportive and close-knit community. The university is committed to providing a quality education at an affordable price, and offers a variety of financial aid and scholarships to help students meet their educational goals.

Admission Requirements

Oakwood University is a private, historically black university located in Huntsville, Alabama. The university is affiliated with the Seventh-day Adventist Church. Founded in 1896 as Oakwood Industrial Academy, it was the first Adventist school to offer higher education for African Americans.

The university offers associate, bachelor, and master's degrees in a variety of disciplines. Admission requirements vary depending on the level of study. For associate and bachelor's degrees, applicants must submit high school transcripts and SAT or ACT scores. For master's degrees, applicants must submit transcripts from all previous colleges and universities attended, as well as GRE or GMAT scores.

Oakwood University is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges.
