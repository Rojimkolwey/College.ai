Freed-Hardeman University

Henderson, TN
158 E Main St, Henderson, TN 38340-2399
800-348-3481

Freed-Hardeman University is a private university in Henderson, Tennessee. The university is affiliated with the churches of Christ. The university was founded in 1869 as a school for preachers and teachers. The school was named after two of its founders, David Lipscomb and James A. Harding. The school became a four-year college in 1949 and a university in 1991. The university offers bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees. The university has an enrollment of about 2,000 students.

Admission Requirements

Freed-Hardeman University is a private Christian university in Henderson, Tennessee. The university is affiliated with the churches of Christ.

Admission to Freed-Hardeman University is based on a student's academic record, ACT or SAT scores, and a personal essay. The university does not require a minimum GPA for admission, but the average GPA of incoming students is 3.5.

Freed-Hardeman University offers a variety of scholarships and financial aid programs to help students pay for their education. Scholarships are available for students who demonstrate academic excellence, leadership, and service.

Freed-Hardeman University is committed to providing a quality education at an affordable price. The university offers a variety of payment options, including a payment plan, to help make college more affordable for students and their families.
