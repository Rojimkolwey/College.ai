The University of Virginia-Main Campus is a public research university located in Charlottesville, Virginia. The university was founded in 1819 by Thomas Jefferson and is one of the original eight Public Ivy schools. The university is known for its historical foundations, academic excellence, and commitment to public service. The university is home to a number of prestigious schools and programs, including the School of Law, the Darden School of Business, the School of Engineering and Applied Science, and the School of Medicine. The university also houses a number of research centers and institutes, including the Center for Advanced Study in the Visual Arts and the Institute for Advanced Technology in the Humanities. The university enrolls over 23,000 students from all 50 states and over 100 countries. The university is ranked as one of the top public universities in the United States and is consistently ranked among the top universities in the world.

Admission Requirements

The University of Virginia-Main Campus is a public research university located in Charlottesville, Virginia. The university was founded in 1819 by Thomas Jefferson and is one of the eight original Public Ivy schools. The university is ranked as the 35th best university in the United States and the 5th best public university by U.S. News & World Report.

The university has an undergraduate enrollment of 16,013 and a graduate enrollment of 5,964. The student-to-faculty ratio is 11:1 and the average class size is 20. The university offers 87 bachelor's degrees, 99 master's degrees, and 58 doctoral degrees.

The admission requirements for undergraduate students vary depending on their academic background and whether they are applying as a first-time student or as a transfer student. First-time students must submit an application, high school transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a non-refundable application fee. Transfer students must submit an application, college transcripts, and a non-refundable application fee. The university also requires all applicants to submit a personal essay and two letters of recommendation.

The University of Virginia-Main Campus has a need-blind admissions policy for all first-time, domestic students. The university meets 100% of the demonstrated financial need of all admitted students. For students who are not eligible for need-based financial aid, the university offers a variety of merit-based scholarships.
