Midwest College of Oriental Medicine-Evanston

Evanston, IL
1601 N Sherman Ave, Evanston, IL 60201
800-593-2320

Midwest College of Oriental Medicine-Evanston (MCOM-E) is a nationally accredited, professional school of acupuncture and Oriental medicine located in Evanston, Illinois, a suburb of Chicago. MCOM-E was founded in 1984 and offers a four-year, full-time Master of Science in Oriental Medicine (MSOM) degree program, as well as a two-year, full-time Master of Science in Acupuncture (MSAc) degree program. The MSOM program at MCOM-E is one of the longest-running programs in the United States, and the MSAc program is one of the few programs in the country that is accredited by the Accreditation Commission for Acupuncture and Oriental Medicine (ACAOM).

The MSOM program at MCOM-E is a comprehensive program that includes coursework in Oriental medicine theory, acupuncture, Chinese herbs, Western medicine, and clinical training. The MSAc program at MCOM-E is a more focused program that includes coursework in acupuncture theory and technique, Chinese herbs, and clinical training. Both programs at MCOM-E include a one-year, full-time clinical internship.

Graduates of the MSOM program at MCOM-E are eligible to sit for the national board exams administered by the National Certification Commission for Acupuncture and Oriental Medicine (NCCAOM). Graduates of the MSAc program at MCOM-E are eligible to sit for the national board exams administered by the ACAOM. Upon passing the national board exams, graduates are then eligible to apply for state licensure.

The faculty at MCOM-E consists of experienced practitioners and scholars who are committed to providing students with a high-quality education in Oriental medicine. The clinical training program at MCOM-E is one of the largest and most comprehensive in the country, and the school has a strong commitment to community service. MCOM-E is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and is a member of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools.

Admission Requirements

The Midwest College of Oriental Medicine-Evanston (MCOM-E) is a nationally accredited institution offering a four-year Master of Science in Traditional Oriental Medicine (MSTOM) degree. The MSTOM degree program is a full-time, on-campus program that integrates classroom didactic instruction with clinical training.

MCOM-E is committed to providing an excellent educational experience that prepares our graduates to become licensed acupuncturists and practitioners of Oriental medicine. Our admissions requirements are designed to identify those applicants who have the potential to succeed in our rigorous academic program and who will contribute to the diversity of our student body.

We welcome applications from all qualified individuals. To be considered for admission, applicants must submit the following:

• A completed application form

• A non-refundable application fee of $85

• Official transcripts from all colleges and universities attended

• A minimum of 60 semester hours (90 quarter hours) of undergraduate coursework from an accredited institution

• A cumulative GPA of 2.5 or higher on a 4.0 scale

• A minimum grade of "C" in all prerequisite coursework

• Two letters of recommendation

• A personal essay

• A resume or CV

• TOEFL scores (for international applicants only)

MCOM-E does not require the GRE for admission into the MSTOM program.

Applicants who meet the minimum admissions requirements will be invited to participate in an interview with the Admissions Committee. The interview is an opportunity for the applicant to learn more about the program and for the Admissions Committee to assess the applicant's motivation, maturity, and ability to succeed in the program.

Upon review of all application materials, the Admissions Committee will make a decision to offer admission, deny admission, or place the applicant on the waitlist. Applicants who are offered admission must submit a non-refundable deposit of $500 to secure their place in the incoming class.
