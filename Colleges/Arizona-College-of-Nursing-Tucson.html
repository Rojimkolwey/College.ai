Arizona College of Nursing-Tucson (ACN-Tucson) is a private, for-profit nursing school located in Tucson, Arizona. It is owned and operated by Education Management Corporation (EDMC), a publicly traded company. ACN-Tucson is one of four nursing schools in the state of Arizona.

ACN-Tucson was founded in 2002. The school is accredited by the Commission on Collegiate Nursing Education (CCNE). It offers two nursing programs: an Associate of Science in Nursing (ASN) and a Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BSN). The ASN program is a two-year program that leads to eligibility to take the NCLEX-RN exam to become a registered nurse. The BSN program is a four-year program that leads to eligibility to take the NCLEX-RN exam and the NCLEX-PN exam to become a registered nurse and a licensed practical nurse, respectively.

ACN-Tucson has an enrollment of approximately 300 students. The student population is approximately 60% female and 40% male. The average age of students is 28. The student-to-faculty ratio is 15-to-1.

The tuition for the ASN program is $17,500 per year. The tuition for the BSN program is $21,000 per year. Financial aid is available for those who qualify.

ACN-Tucson has a job placement rate of approximately 80%. The average starting salary for ACN-Tucson graduates is $46,000 per year.

Admission Requirements

Arizona College of Nursing-Tucson is a private nursing school located in Tucson, Arizona. The college offers a Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BSN) program. The program is accredited by the Commission on Collegiate Nursing Education (CCNE).

Admission to the BSN program at Arizona College of Nursing-Tucson is competitive. The college uses a holistic review process when considering applicants for admission. The college looks at factors such as academic achievement, letters of recommendation, and personal essays when making admissions decisions.

Arizona College of Nursing-Tucson has a rolling admissions policy. This means that applications are reviewed and decisions are made on a rolling basis. The sooner an applicant applies, the sooner a decision will be made.

Applicants to the BSN program must have a high school diploma or equivalent. They must also have completed prerequisite coursework in English, math, and science. Arizona College of Nursing-Tucson does not require applicants to take the Nursing Entrance Test (NET).

Once admitted to the program, students must complete a total of 120 credit hours to earn their BSN. The program can be completed in four years of full-time study.
