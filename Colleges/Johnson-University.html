Johnson University is a private, non-profit, Christian university located in Knoxville, Tennessee, United States. It was founded in 1893 by Ashley S. Johnson and is affiliated with the Tennessee Baptist Convention. The university offers associate, bachelor's, and master's degrees in a variety of disciplines.

Johnson University was founded in 1893 by Ashley S. Johnson, a minister in the Tennessee Baptist Convention. The university was originally located in Johnson City, Tennessee, but moved to Knoxville in 1911. The university offers a variety of associate, bachelor's, and master's degrees in disciplines such as business, education, nursing, and ministry.

The university is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges. Johnson University is a member of the Council for Christian Colleges and Universities. The university's athletic teams compete in the National Association of Intercollegiate Athletics.

Johnson University's campus is located on 150 acres (61 ha) in Knoxville, Tennessee. The campus includes several academic and administrative buildings, a library, a student center, a gymnasium, and athletic fields. The university also owns and operates a retreat center in Gatlinburg, Tennessee.

Johnson University has an enrollment of approximately 1,500 students, most of whom are undergraduates. The student body is composed of students from across the United States and more than 40 countries. The university employs more than 200 faculty and staff members.

Johnson University is committed to providing a Christ-centered education that equips students for leadership and service in the Church and the world. The university offers a variety of programs and activities that foster spiritual growth and personal development. Johnson University is a place where students can learn, grow, and prepare for a lifetime of service to God and others.
