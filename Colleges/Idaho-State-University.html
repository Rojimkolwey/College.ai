Idaho State University is a public research university in Pocatello, Idaho. Founded in 1901 as the Academy of Idaho, ISU offers more than 280 programs of study, including master's, doctoral, and professional degrees. ISU is the state's designated lead institution in health professions and medical research. It is also the largest university in Idaho, with an average enrollment of over 13,000 students. ISU's athletic teams compete in Division I of the NCAA and are known as the Bengals.

Admission Requirements

Idaho State University is a public research university in Pocatello, Idaho. Founded in 1901, it is the state's largest university.

ISU offers over 280 programs of study, including bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees. Admission to the university is competitive, with an acceptance rate of 79% for undergraduate students in fall 2019.

ISU has a rolling admissions policy, meaning that applications are reviewed and decisions are made on a rolling basis throughout the year. However, applicants are encouraged to submit their materials as early as possible to improve their chances of being admitted.

To be considered for admission, applicants must submit an application form, transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a non-refundable application fee. International students must also submit proof of English proficiency.

Once all materials have been received, the admissions committee will review the application and make a decision. Decisions are typically rendered within two weeks of the application being completed.

If an applicant is admitted, they will be required to submit a non-refundable deposit to secure their spot in the incoming class. This deposit will be applied towards the student's tuition and fees.
