Founded in 1848, the University of Wisconsin-Madison is a world-renowned public research university and the flagship institution of the University of Wisconsin System. With more than 42,000 students, UW-Madison is the largest university in Wisconsin and one of the nation’s leading research universities. The university is organized into 20 schools and colleges, which offer more than 150 undergraduate majors, along with nearly 200 graduate programs.

UW-Madison is consistently ranked among the world’s top universities. In 2019, UW-Madison was ranked as the #5 university in the United States and #15 in the world by the Times Higher Education World University Rankings. The university is also ranked #9 in the world for employability by the same publication. UW-Madison is a member of the Association of American Universities, a select group of 62 leading public and private research universities in the United States.

UW-Madison is located in Madison, Wisconsin, a vibrant, cosmopolitan city with a population of more than 250,000. The city is home to a diverse array of cultural and recreational attractions, and is consistently ranked as one of the best places to live in the United States.

UW-Madison is a comprehensive research university with a long tradition of excellence in teaching, research, and public service. The university’s research enterprise is among the largest in the world, with an annual research budget of more than $1 billion. UW-Madison is home to world-renowned faculty and researchers, who are leaders in their fields and are committed to teaching and mentoring the next generation of scholars.

The university’s beautiful 936-acre campus is located on an isthmus between two lakes, offering stunning views and endless opportunities for recreation. The campus is home to more than 500 buildings, including iconic landmarks like the Wisconsin State Capitol, Camp Randall Stadium, and Bascom Hill.

UW-Madison is a dynamic and vibrant community, and we are proud to be one of the world’s leading research universities.

Admission Requirements

The University of Wisconsin-Madison is a public research university located in Madison, Wisconsin. The university is a member of the Association of American Universities and is categorized as an R1 Doctoral Research University in the Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education. Founded in 1848, UW-Madison is the flagship institution of the University of Wisconsin System and is the oldest and largest public university in Wisconsin. The university offers more than 200 undergraduate majors and over 2,000 graduate programs.

UW-Madison is a highly selective university, with an acceptance rate of 49.7% for the class of 2024. The university uses a holistic review process when considering applications, taking into account academic performance, test scores, extracurricular activities, essays, and letters of recommendation. The university does not have a minimum GPA or test score requirement, but the average GPA of admitted students is 3.8 and the average ACT score is 32.

UW-Madison is ranked as the 21st best university in the world by Times Higher Education and as the 28th best university in the United States by U.S. News & World Report. The university is also ranked as the 5th best public university in the United States by U.S. News & World Report.
