Washburn University

Topeka, KS
1700 SW College Avenue, Topeka, KS 66621-0001
785-670-1010

Washburn University is a public university in Topeka, Kansas. The university offers undergraduate and graduate programs. Washburn has about 6,700 students. The university was founded in 1865 as Lincoln College. It was renamed Washburn College in 1868 after Ichabod Washburn donated $25,000 to the school. Washburn University is governed by the Kansas Board of Regents. The university is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission.

Washburn University is a public university located in Topeka, Kansas. The university offers undergraduate and graduate programs. Washburn has about 6,700 students. The university is governed by the Kansas Board of Regents. The university is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission.

Washburn University's main campus is located in downtown Topeka. The campus covers about 80 acres. The university also has a campus in Wichita, Kansas. Washburn University is home to the School of Law, the School of Business, the College of Arts and Sciences, and the School of Nursing.

The Washburn School of Law was founded in 1903. The school is accredited by the American Bar Association. The Washburn School of Business is accredited by the Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business. The Washburn College of Arts and Sciences offers degrees in over 40 majors. The School of Nursing is accredited by the Commission on Collegiate Nursing Education.

Washburn University offers a variety of student organizations, including fraternities and sororities, academic clubs and organizations, and sports clubs. The university also has a student newspaper, the Washburn Review.

Washburn University is a member of the NCAA Division II. The university's athletic teams are known as the Ichabods. The university's mascot is the buffalo. Washburn University's colors are green and gold.
