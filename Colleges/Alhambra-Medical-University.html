Alhambra Medical University

Alhambra, CA
2215 W. Mission Road, Alhambra, CA 91803
626-289-7719

Alhambra Medical University (AMU) is a private, for-profit university located in Miramar, Florida. It was founded in 2003 and is accredited by the Commission on Accreditation of Healthcare Management Education (CAHMME). The university offers bachelor's and master's degrees in healthcare management and administration.

AMU's mission is to provide quality healthcare education that is affordable and accessible to all. The university's goal is to prepare students for successful careers in the healthcare industry. AMU offers a unique curriculum that combines classroom instruction with hands-on experience. Students have the opportunity to learn from experienced healthcare professionals and to gain practical experience through internships and externships.

AMU's faculty is comprised of experienced healthcare professionals who are dedicated to teaching and preparing students for successful careers in the healthcare industry. The university's small class sizes allow for personal attention and interaction between students and faculty.

AMU is committed to providing its students with the knowledge and skills necessary to succeed in the healthcare industry. The university offers a variety of resources to help students succeed, including career counseling, academic advising, and tutoring. AMU also offers a variety of extracurricular activities, including clubs and organizations, that provide students with opportunities to network and build relationships with other healthcare professionals.

Admission Requirements

Admission requirements at Alhambra Medical University are as follows:

- A minimum GPA of 2.5 is required for admission consideration.

- Admission test scores (SAT/ACT) are required for all applicants.

- Official transcripts from all colleges and universities attended must be submitted.

- A non-refundable application fee of $50 is required.

- An interview with the Admissions Committee is required for all applicants.

- Applicants must submit a completed application form, including all required supporting documents, to the Office of Admissions.
