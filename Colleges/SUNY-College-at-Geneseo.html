SUNY College at Geneseo

Geneseo, NY
1 College Circle, Geneseo, NY 14454-1465
585-245-5000

SUNY College at Geneseo is a public college in the State University of New York system. It was founded in 1871 as the Geneseo Literary and Scientific Institution. The college is located in the village of Geneseo, in the Finger Lakes region of New York. The college offers bachelor's degrees in over 50 majors and minors. The most popular majors at the college are Education, Biology, Psychology, Communication Studies, and English. The college has an extensive study abroad program, with over 200 programs in 60 countries. The college also offers a Master's degree in Education. SUNY Geneseo is ranked as one of the top public colleges in the North by U.S. News & World Report.

Admission Requirements

The State University of New York at Geneseo is a highly selective liberal arts college located in the Finger Lakes region of upstate New York. The college is consistently ranked among the top public liberal arts colleges in the country. In order to be considered for admission to Geneseo, students must submit an application through the Common Application or SUNY Application.

In addition to the application, students must submit high school transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and two letters of recommendation. An essay is also required. The college uses a holistic admissions process and takes into account the student's entire academic record, personal achievements, and extracurricular involvement.

Geneseo has a strong commitment to academic excellence and offers a rigorous curriculum that prepares students for success in a wide range of careers. The college also offers a variety of extracurricular activities and opportunities for students to get involved on campus. Geneseo is an excellent choice for students who are looking for a challenging academic experience and a supportive community.
