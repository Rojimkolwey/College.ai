3000 NE Fourth St, Renton, WA 98056-4123
425-235-2352

Renton Technical College (RTC) is a public technical college in Renton, Washington. The college offers associate degrees, professional certificates, and apprenticeships in a variety of technical and trade fields. RTC is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities.

RTC was founded in 1937 as a vocational school to serve the needs of the local community and economy. The college has grown and changed over the years, but its commitment to providing quality technical and trade education has remained the same. Today, RTC offers over 50 different programs, preparing students for careers in a variety of fields.

RTC is an affordable option for students looking to get a quality education without incurring a lot of debt. The college also offers a number of financial aid and scholarship opportunities to help students cover the cost of tuition and other expenses.

RTC is located in the heart of the Seattle metropolitan area, providing students with access to a variety of cultural and recreational activities. The college also has a strong partnership with the local business community, providing students with opportunities to gain real-world experience through internships and other programs.

Renton Technical College (RTC) is a public two-year technical college in Renton, Washington. RTC is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities. RTC is the only technical college in the state of Washington accredited by the commission.

RTC offers over 50 programs leading to associate degrees, certificates, and apprenticeships. Admission to RTC is open to all high school graduates and GED holders. Some programs may have additional admission requirements.

RTC has an open-entry, open-exit policy, which means that students can begin their studies at any time and complete their programs at their own pace. RTC also offers evening and weekend classes to accommodate students' work schedules.
