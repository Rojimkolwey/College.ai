Lewis-Clark State College is a public institution of higher education located in Lewiston, Idaho, in the United States. Founded in 1893, it has an open-admission policy and offers associate's and bachelor's degrees, as well as professional certificates. The college has an annual enrollment of approximately 4,200 students.

The majority of students at Lewis-Clark State College are Idaho residents. Out-of-state and international students make up a small percentage of the student body. Admission to the college is open to all students who have a high school diploma or equivalent. However, preference is given to students who have completed college-preparatory coursework, such as English, math, and science.

Lewis-Clark State College does not have a minimum GPA or test score requirement for admission. However, students who have a GPA of 2.5 or higher and who have completed college-preparatory coursework are more likely to be admitted. The college also offers a number of need-based and merit-based scholarships.
