Johnson C Smith University is a private, historically black university in Charlotte, North Carolina. It is affiliated with the Presbyterian Church (USA). The university was founded in 1867 by the Rev. Dr. J.C.S. Blackburn, D.D., and the Rev. Dr. W.L. Miller, D.D. as Biddle Memorial Institute. It was renamed in 1869 for Major General Oliver Otis Howard, the first Commissioner of the Freedman's Bureau. The campus moved several times in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, finally settling on its current location in 1952. The university offers undergraduate and graduate degrees in a variety of disciplines.

Johnson C Smith University is a private, historically black university located in Charlotte, North Carolina, United States. The university is affiliated with the Presbyterian Church. It was founded in 1867 by the Rev. Dr. Martin R. Delany and the Rev. Dr. James O. Saunders, with the support of the Presbyterian Synod of North Carolina. The campus is located in the Biddleville community, just west of downtown Charlotte. The university has an enrollment of about 1,300 students. The university offers bachelor's degrees in 36 majors, 10 master's degrees, and one doctoral degree program. The university is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools.

Admission to Johnson C Smith University is selective. The university typically receives about 3,500 applications each year and accepts about 60% of those applicants. The average SAT score for incoming freshmen is 950. The average ACT score is 20. The university does not require the SAT or ACT for admission. However, applicants are encouraged to submit test scores as they may be used for placement purposes. The university does not have a minimum GPA requirement for admission, but the average GPA for incoming freshmen is 3.0.
