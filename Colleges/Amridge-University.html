Amridge University is a private, coeducational university located in Montgomery, Alabama, United States. The university is accredited by the Commission on Colleges of the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools to award associate, baccalaureate, and master's degrees. Amridge University was founded in 1967 as Alabama Christian College, a two-year institution, by the Alabama Christian Education Association. In 1974, the college became a four-year institution and was renamed Amridge University in 2004. The university's mission is "to prepare students for responsible Christian living and effective leadership in a global society through excellence in teaching, personal attention, and spiritual formation."

Amridge University offers associate, baccalaureate, and master's degrees in a variety of disciplines including business, education, counseling, ministry, and the liberal arts. The university has an enrollment of approximately 1,200 students. The student-to-faculty ratio is 15-to-1. The average class size is 20. The university offers a variety of student services and activities including career services, tutoring, and an on-campus bookstore.

Amridge University is accredited by the Commission on Colleges of the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools to award associate, baccalaureate, and master's degrees. The university is also accredited by the following organizations: the Association for Biblical Higher Education, the Council for Accreditation of Counseling and Related Educational Programs, the National Association of Schools of Music, and the Teacher Education Accreditation Council.

Admission to Amridge University is open to all qualified students regardless of race, color, creed, or national origin. The school does not discriminate on the basis of sex, age, or handicap in the administration of its educational policies, admissions policies, or scholarship and loan programs.

To be eligible for admission to Amridge University, students must have a high school diploma or equivalent. Students must also submit official transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a non-refundable application fee. Additional requirements may apply for certain programs.

Once all required materials have been received, the Admissions Committee will review the application and make a decision. Admission decisions are based on a number of factors, including academic record, test scores, and personal characteristics.

Amridge University is committed to providing a quality education at an affordable price. The school offers a variety of scholarships and financial aid programs to help make college more affordable. For more information, please visit the Amridge University website.
