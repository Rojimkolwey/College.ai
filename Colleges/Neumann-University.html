Neumann University

Aston, PA
One Neumann Drive, Aston, PA 19014-1298
610-459-0905

Neumann University is a private, coeducational Catholic university in Aston, Pennsylvania. The university is located on 108 acres (44 ha) of land. Neumann is affiliated with the Sisters of St. Francis of Philadelphia, who founded the school in 1965. The university offers undergraduate and graduate degrees, as well as certificate and diploma programs.

Neumann University was founded in 1965 by the Sisters of St. Francis of Philadelphia. The university is located on 108 acres (44 ha) of land in Aston, Pennsylvania. The school was originally established as a two-year college for women, but began offering four-year degrees in 1974. Neumann became a coeducational institution in 1985.

The university offers a variety of undergraduate and graduate degrees, as well as certificate and diploma programs. Neumann's academic programs are divided into five schools: the School of Arts and Sciences, the School of Business and Economics, the School of Education, the School of Nursing and Health Professions, and the School of Social and Behavioral Sciences.

Neumann University is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education. The university's nursing program is accredited by the Commission on Collegiate Nursing Education.

Neumann University enrolls approximately 2,800 students, the majority of whom are undergraduates. The student body is approximately evenly split between men and women. The university has a faculty-to-student ratio of 1:14.

Approximately 85% of Neumann University students are Catholic. The university offers a variety of campus ministry programs and services, including retreats, service opportunities, and faith-based clubs and organizations.

Neumann University's athletics teams compete in the NCAA Division III. The university is a member of the Colonial States Athletic Conference. The university's mascot is the Knights.

Neumann University's main campus is located in Aston, Pennsylvania, about 15 miles (24 km) southwest of Philadelphia. The university also has a campus in Rome, Italy.

Admission Requirements

Neumann University is a private, co-educational, Catholic university located in Aston, Pennsylvania. The university offers bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees in a variety of disciplines.

Undergraduate admission to Neumann University is selective. The university looks for students who have demonstrated academic excellence, leadership potential, and a commitment to service.

Neumann University requires all applicants to submit an application, high school transcripts, and SAT or ACT scores. The university also requires a personal essay and a letter of recommendation.

Neumann University offers a variety of scholarships and financial aid programs to help make college affordable. The university also offers a deferred payment plan and a monthly payment plan to help students finance their education.
