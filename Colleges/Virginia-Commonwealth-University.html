Virginia Commonwealth University

Richmond, VA
910 W Franklin St, Richmond, VA 23284-2512
804-828-0100

Founded in 1838, Virginia Commonwealth University is a public research university located in Richmond, Virginia. The university comprises eight schools and one college, offering more than 200 degree programs to its 32,000 students. The university is also home to several research institutes and centers, including the VCU Massey Cancer Center, the VCU Institute for Drug and Alcohol Studies, and the VCU Center for the Study of Biological Complexity.

VCU was originally established as the medical department of Hampden-Sydney College, before becoming a standalone institution in 1854. The university underwent a period of significant growth in the early 20th century, adding a number of new schools and programs. In 1968, the university merged with Richmond Professional Institute, greatly expanding its size and scope. In the years since, VCU has continued to grow, adding new academic programs and facilities.

VCU is classified as a research university with high research activity by the Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education. The university is also ranked as one of the top 100 public universities in the United States by several measures. VCU is home to a number of notable alumni, including several politicians, businesspeople, and artists.

Admission Requirements

In order to be admitted to Virginia Commonwealth University, applicants must submit an application, high school transcripts, and SAT or ACT scores. The admissions committee will review these materials and make a decision based on the student's academic record and potential. The admissions process is competitive, and students are encouraged to submit their materials early.
