Monmouth University is a private university located in West Long Branch, New Jersey, United States. Founded in 1933 as Monmouth Junior College, it became Monmouth College in 1956 and Monmouth University in 1995 after receiving its charter. The university consists of six schools: the School of Business, the Leon Hess Business School, the School of Education, the Marjorie K. Unterberg School of Nursing and Health Studies, the Wayne D. McMurray School of Humanities and Social Sciences, and the School of Science. The university offers over 50 undergraduate and 30 graduate programs and degrees.

Monmouth University was founded in 1933 as Monmouth Junior College by Dr. William Allan Neilson, then president of Monmouth College in Illinois. The campus moved to its current location in West Long Branch in 1956, and became a four-year institution in 1957 as Monmouth College. The college was granted university status in 1995 and changed its name to Monmouth University. The university is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education.

The university consists of six schools: the School of Business, the Leon Hess Business School, the School of Education, the Marjorie K. Unterberg School of Nursing and Health Studies, the Wayne D. McMurray School of Humanities and Social Sciences, and the School of Science. The School of Business offers degrees in accounting, finance, management, and marketing. The Leon Hess Business School offers degrees in business administration and economics. The School of Education offers degrees in elementary and secondary education, special education, and educational leadership. The Marjorie K. Unterberg School of Nursing and Health Studies offers degrees in nursing, health studies, and public health. The Wayne D. McMurray School of Humanities and Social Sciences offers degrees in English, history, modern languages, communication, and the arts. The School of Science offers degrees in biology, chemistry, computer science, mathematics, and physics.

Monmouth University offers over 50 undergraduate and 30 graduate programs and degrees. Undergraduate programs include Bachelor of Arts, Bachelor of Science, and Bachelor of Science in Nursing degrees. Graduate programs include Master of Arts, Master of Business Administration, Master of Education, Master of Science, and Doctor of Nursing Practice degrees. The university also offers several online programs.

Monmouth University is located on a picturesque 176-acre campus in West Long Branch, New Jersey, just minutes from the Jersey shore. The campus features six residence halls, two dining halls, a recreation center, a library, and several academic buildings. The university also has a satellite campus in Wall, New Jersey, and a study abroad program in London, England.

Admission Requirements

Monmouth University is a private university located in West Long Branch, New Jersey. The university was founded in 1933 as Monmouth Junior College. In 1956, the school became Monmouth College and in 1995, it achieved university status. The university is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education.

The university enrolls approximately 6,000 undergraduate and 1,000 graduate students. The student-faculty ratio is 14:1 and the average class size is 20. The university offers over 50 undergraduate majors and 30 graduate programs.

Admission to Monmouth University is competitive. For fall 2019, the university received 11,872 applications and accepted 6,819 (57.4%). The middle 50% of admitted students had SAT scores between 1160 and 1290 and ACT scores between 25 and 29. The average GPA of admitted students was 3.52.

Monmouth University uses a holistic admissions process. The admissions committee considers all aspects of an applicant's file, including academic performance, standardized test scores, essays, letters of recommendation, and extracurricular activities. The university does not have a minimum GPA or test score requirement, but strong academic performance is necessary for admission.

Monmouth University is committed to enrolling a diverse student body and offers a variety of need-based and merit-based scholarships and financial aid. In 2018-2019, over 90% of students received some form of financial aid.
