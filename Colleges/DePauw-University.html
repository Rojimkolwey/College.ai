DePauw University

Greencastle, IN
313 S Locust St, Greencastle, IN 46135-1772
765-658-4800

DePauw University is a private university in Greencastle, Indiana. The school was founded in 1837 as Indiana Asbury University. The school is the oldest private university in Indiana and the 10th oldest private university in the United States. The school has an enrollment of about 2,400 students. The school's campus is located in the town of Greencastle, about 45 miles west of Indianapolis. The school is a member of the NCAA Division III and the North Coast Athletic Conference. The school's mascot is the Tiger. The school's colors are black and gold.

The school offers a variety of undergraduate and graduate programs. The school's most popular majors include business, communication, education, and psychology. The school is also home to a number of research centers and institutes, including the Center for the Study of the Civil War Era, the Center for the Study of the Environment, and the Center for the Study of Global Change.

DePauw University is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. The school's athletic teams compete in the NCAA Division III. The school's men's basketball team has won two national championships, in 1959 and 2016. The school's women's basketball team has also won two national championships, in 2005 and 2007.

Admission Requirements

DePauw University is a highly selective, national liberal arts university with an enrollment of 2,400 students. Located in Greencastle, Indiana, DePauw is a member of the highly regarded Great Lakes Colleges Association and the North Coast Athletic Conference.

The university seeks students who will thrive in a rigorous academic environment and who will contribute to the vibrant community that is DePauw. We look for students who have demonstrated academic excellence, leadership, and service, and who will add to the diversity of our campus.

DePauw University is committed to a policy of equal opportunity and nondiscrimination on the basis of race, color, national origin, religion, sex, age, disability, veteran status, or sexual orientation in its educational programs and activities.

To be considered for admission, applicants must submit the following:

-A completed Common Application or Coalition Application

-A non-refundable $50 application fee or fee waiver

-Official high school transcripts

-Official SAT or ACT scores

-Two letters of recommendation from a teacher or counselor

DePauw University also offers a number of merit-based scholarships, which are awarded based on a student's academic achievement, leadership, and service. For more information on scholarships, please visit the financial aid website.
