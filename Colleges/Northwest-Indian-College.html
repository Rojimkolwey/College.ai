Northwest Indian College (NWIC) is a tribal college located on the Lummi reservation in Bellingham, Washington, United States. It is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities. NWIC offers Associate of Arts, Associate of Science, and Bachelor of Science degrees, as well as certificate programs. The college also provides community education and continuing education courses.

NWIC was founded in 1974 as the Lummi Community College. It was the first tribal college in the Pacific Northwest and one of the first in the nation. The college was renamed Northwest Indian College in 1984.

NWIC’s mission is to empower Indigenous people and communities through higher education that is culturally responsive and academically excellent. The college is committed to supporting the self-determination of Indigenous peoples and promoting social and economic justice.

Admission Requirements

Northwest Indian College (NWIC) is a public two-year tribal college located in Bellingham, Washington, United States. Founded in 1974, NWIC is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities. It is the only accredited tribal college in the state of Washington.

NWIC is open to any person who wishes to attend, regardless of race, creed, or nationality. However, the college gives preference to members of federally recognized tribes in the admissions process.

To be eligible for admission to NWIC, applicants must have a high school diploma or equivalent. GED scores are also accepted. Applicants must submit official transcripts from all previous colleges and universities attended.

NWIC does not have an application fee. However, there is a $25 registration fee for all new students.

Once admitted, students are required to complete a new student orientation prior to the start of classes. Orientation is held the week before classes start and is mandatory for all new students.
