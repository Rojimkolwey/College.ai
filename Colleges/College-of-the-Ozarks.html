College of the Ozarks

Point Lookout, MO
100 Opportunity Avenue, Point Lookout, MO 65726-0017
417-334-6411

The College of the Ozarks is a unique and special place. It is a Christian college, founded in 1906, located in the beautiful Ozarks of southwest Missouri. The college is committed to providing a quality education at an affordable price. The college has a unique work program, which allows students to work 15 hours per week in various jobs on campus, in exchange for their tuition. This work program not only helps to keep the cost of tuition down, but it also helps students to learn the value of work and to develop a strong work ethic. The college also has a strong commitment to service, and students are required to complete 40 hours of community service per semester. The college is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and is a member of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools.

Admission Requirements

Admission to College of the Ozarks is based on a student's high school GPA, class rank, and standardized test scores. The college does not consider race, religion, or financial need when making admissions decisions. In order to be eligible for admission, students must have a 2.5 GPA or higher and a class rank in the top half of their graduating class. They must also submit SAT or ACT scores that meet or exceed the college's minimum requirements. College of the Ozarks has a rolling admissions policy, meaning that applications are reviewed and decisions are made on a rolling basis throughout the year. This means that there is no set deadline for applications, and students are encouraged to apply as early as possible.
