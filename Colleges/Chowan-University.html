Chowan University is a private university in North Carolina. It was founded in 1848 by the Baptist Church. The university's mission is to provide a quality education to its students and to prepare them for leadership in their chosen field. The university offers a variety of programs and degrees, including business, education, nursing, and the liberal arts. Chowan is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools. Admission Requirements

Chowan University is a private, coeducational university located in Murfreesboro, North Carolina, United States. The school was founded in 1848 by Baptist churches in Chowan County, North Carolina. Chowan University is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges to award baccalaureate and master's degrees. The university's five academic divisions include: Business and Professional Studies, Education, Humanities and Social Sciences, Natural Sciences and Mathematics, and Nursing and Health Sciences.

Admission to Chowan University is selective, and the university typically admits students who have a strong academic record and a demonstrated commitment to their chosen field of study. In order to be considered for admission, students must submit an application, transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a letter of recommendation. International students must also submit proof of English proficiency. Once all materials have been received, the admissions committee will review the application and make a decision.

Chowan University is committed to providing a quality education to all of its students. The university offers a variety of scholarships and financial aid options to help make college more affordable. For more information about admission requirements and the application process, interested students are encouraged to contact the admissions office.
