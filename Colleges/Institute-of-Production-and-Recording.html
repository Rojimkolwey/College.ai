Institute of Production and Recording

Minneapolis, MN
300 North 1st Ave, Suite 100, Minneapolis, MN 55401
612-351-0631

The Institute of Production and Recording (IPR) is a for-profit college located in Minneapolis, Minnesota. IPR offers Associate's and Bachelor's degrees in Audio Production and Recording Arts. IPR is accredited by the National Association of Schools of Art and Design (NASAD). IPR's campus is located in the Warehouse District of Minneapolis. IPR has a student-to-faculty ratio of 8:1.

IPR was founded in 1974 by Bob Kosinski and Dave Young. IPR's mission is to provide students with the knowledge and skills necessary for a successful career in the audio production and recording industry. IPR offers a variety of degree programs that prepare students for careers in audio engineering, live sound, music production, and more.

IPR's faculty is made up of industry professionals who are passionate about teaching and helping students reach their potential. IPR's faculty members have a wealth of experience in the audio industry and are committed to providing students with the best possible education.

IPR's campus is located in the Warehouse District of Minneapolis, which is home to a number of music venues, recording studios, and music businesses. IPR's campus is within walking distance of many of these businesses, which provides students with opportunities to gain real-world experience while they are still in school.

IPR is accredited by the National Association of Schools of Art and Design (NASAD). IPR's audio production and recording programs are both accredited by the National Association of Schools of Music (NASM).

IPR has a student-to-faculty ratio of 8:1. IPR's small class sizes allow for more individualized attention and instruction.

IPR's graduates are prepared for careers in audio engineering, live sound, music production, and more. IPR's graduates have gone on to work for a variety of companies, including Sony Music, Universal Music Group, and Walt Disney Records.

Admission Requirements

Admission requirements at the Institute of Production and Recording vary depending on the program you wish to pursue. The Institute offers both undergraduate and graduate programs, and each has different requirements.

For the undergraduate programs, you will need to have a high school diploma or equivalent. You will also need to submit your transcripts, along with a letter of recommendation and a personal essay. The Institute does not require standardized test scores for admission.

For the graduate programs, you will need to have a bachelor's degree from an accredited institution. You will also need to submit your transcripts, along with a letter of recommendation and a personal essay. The Institute does not require standardized test scores for admission.
