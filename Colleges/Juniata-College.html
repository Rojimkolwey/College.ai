Huntingdon, PA
1700 Moore St, Huntingdon, PA 16652-2196
814-641-3000

Juniata College is a private liberal arts college in Huntingdon, Pennsylvania. The school was founded in 1876 by a group of local businessmen and it is affiliated with the Church of the Brethren. The college's campus is located on a 200-acre farm. The school offers a variety of undergraduate and graduate programs. The college is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education.

Admission Requirements

Juniata College is a private liberal arts college in Huntingdon, Pennsylvania. The school was founded in 1876 and is affiliated with the Church of the Brethren. Juniata has an enrollment of about 1,600 students. The school offers bachelor's degrees in over 50 majors and also features a master's degree program in business administration.

Juniata College has a rolling admissions policy, meaning that applications are accepted on a rolling basis throughout the year. However, the college does have a priority deadline of February 1 for students who wish to be considered for merit-based scholarships. For students who apply after the priority deadline, scholarship awards will be based on availability.

To be considered for admission to Juniata College, students must submit an application, high school transcripts, and SAT or ACT scores. The admissions committee will also consider letters of recommendation and an essay or personal statement. Admission to Juniata is competitive, with an acceptance rate of about 60%.
