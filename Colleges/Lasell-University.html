Lasell University is a private university located in Newton, Massachusetts. The university was founded in 1851 as the Lasell Female Seminary and is now a coeducational institution. The university offers bachelor's and master's degrees in a variety of fields.

Lasell University is committed to providing a liberal arts education that is both rigorous and relevant. The university offers a variety of majors and minors, as well as the opportunity to pursue a double major or minor. The university also offers a variety of study abroad programs.

Lasell University is home to a number of student organizations, including the Student Government Association, the Student Activities Board, and the Interfraternity Council. The university also offers a variety of intramural sports.

Lasell University is located in Newton, Massachusetts, a suburb of Boston. The university is accessible by the MBTA green line.

Admission Requirements

Lasell University is a small, private school located in Newton, Massachusetts. The school has an open admissions policy, meaning that any student who meets the basic requirements for admission will be accepted.

The requirements for admission to Lasell University are as follows:

1. A completed application form.

2. A non-refundable application fee of $50.

3. Official high school transcripts or GED scores.

4. SAT or ACT scores (optional).

5. Two letters of recommendation (optional).

6. A personal essay (optional).

7. An interview (optional).

8. Proof of immunization against measles, mumps, and rubella.

9. A criminal background check (if applicable).

Once all of the required materials have been submitted, the admissions committee will review the file and make a decision. If you are admitted to Lasell University, you will be required to submit a non-refundable deposit of $250 to secure your spot in the incoming class.
