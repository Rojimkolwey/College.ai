Claremont Graduate University

Claremont, CA
150 E Tenth St, Claremont, CA 91711-6160

Claremont Graduate University (CGU) is a private, all-graduate research university located in Claremont, California, a city 35 miles (56 km) east of downtown Los Angeles. Founded in 1925, CGU is a member of the Claremont Colleges which includes five undergraduate (Pomona College, Claremont McKenna College, Harvey Mudd College, Scripps College, and Pitzer College) and two graduate (CGU and Keck Graduate Institute of Applied Life Sciences) institutions of higher education.

The university offers master's and doctoral degrees in 24 departments and programs, and emphasizes interdisciplinary study. Ranked as the 16th-best graduate school in the nation by U.S. News & World Report in 2020, Claremont Graduate University is tied for the 60th-best private university in the nation. It is tied for the 80th-best university in the nation for producing Fulbright Scholars.

CGU was founded in 1925 as the Claremont Graduate School. The founding president was James A. Blaisdell, who served from 1925 until his death in 1940. The school awarded its first doctorate in 1930.

In the 1940s, the Claremont Graduate School joined with the other Claremont Colleges (Pomona College, Claremont McKenna College, Harvey Mudd College, Scripps College, and Pitzer College) to form the Claremont Colleges consortium.

In the 1950s, the school began awarding master's degrees, and in the 1960s, the school began offering doctorates in several fields. In 1970, the school was renamed Claremont Graduate University.

Today, Claremont Graduate University is a private, all-graduate research university with 24 departments and programs. The university offers master's and doctoral degrees, and emphasizes interdisciplinary study.

The university is ranked as the 16th-best graduate school in the nation by U.S. News & World Report in 2020, and is tied for the 60th-best private university in the nation. It is tied for the 80th-best university in the nation for producing Fulbright Scholars.

Admission Requirements

CGU is known for its programs in the humanities, social sciences, and natural sciences.

Admission to Claremont Graduate University is highly competitive. The university typically receives over 4,000 applications for its 400 available spots. Claremont Graduate University has a holistic admissions process and considers all aspects of an applicant's file, including transcripts, test scores, letters of recommendation, and essays.

The average GPA for admitted students is 3.7, and the average GRE score is 315. However, CGU does not have strict cutoffs for GPA or GRE scores and instead looks at the entire application to get a sense of the applicant's academic ability and potential.

CGU does not require letters of recommendation, but they are encouraged. Letters of recommendation should come from someone who can speak to the applicant's academic ability and potential, such as a professor or employer.

The essays are an important part of the application and give the admissions committee a chance to get to know the applicant on a personal level. There are three essay prompts, and applicants are encouraged to choose the prompt that best allows them to share their story.

Claremont Graduate University is committed to diversity and welcomes applications from all qualified applicants. The university offers a variety of need-based and merit-based scholarships to help make graduate education accessible to all.
