Kansas State University

Manhattan, KS
919 Mid-Campus Drive, Anderson Hall, Manhattan, KS 66506
785-532-6011

Kansas State University (K-State) is a public research university with its main campus in Manhattan, Kansas, United States. It is the state's land-grant university and a member of the Kansas Board of Regents. Kansas State University was founded in 1863 as a land-grant college under the Morrill Act. The university is classified as one of 115 research universities with very high research activity by the Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education.

As of fall 2017, the university had 2,352 faculty members—1,172 tenure-track faculty members and 1,180 non-tenure track faculty members—and a student-to-faculty ratio of 18:1. The university's academic offerings are administered through ten colleges, including the College of Agriculture, the College of Architecture, Planning & Design, the College of Arts & Sciences, the College of Business Administration, the College of Education, the College of Engineering, the College of Human Ecology, the College of Veterinary Medicine, and the Graduate School.

K-State is a member of the Association of American Universities (AAU), the Big 12 Conference, and the Universities Research Association (URA). It is the second-oldest public university in the state of Kansas and one of the nation's first land-grant colleges. The university offered its first degree in 1865. The school's athletic teams, the Wildcats, compete in the NCAA Division I men's basketball, baseball, and football. The school's men's cross country team has won 15 conference championships. K-State's track and field program has produced two Olympians and 59 conference champions.

Admission Requirements

Kansas State University is a public research university with an acceptance rate of 86%. Located in Manhattan, Kansas, Kansas State University was founded in 1863 and is the oldest public university in the state of Kansas. The university is divided into eight colleges, including the College of Agriculture and Natural Resources, the College of Architecture, Planning & Design, the College of Arts & Sciences, the College of Business Administration, the College of Education, the College of Engineering, the College of Human Ecology, and the College of Veterinary Medicine.

To be admitted to Kansas State University, students must submit an application, high school transcripts, and SAT or ACT scores. The university does not require essays or letters of recommendation. For students who are interested in applying to the Honors College, there is an additional application.

Kansas State University is a highly ranked university, and its eight colleges offer a wide variety of majors and programs. The university is committed to providing a quality education at an affordable price, and its admissions process is designed to be accessible and straightforward.
