DeVry University-Arizona

DeVry University-Arizona is a private university located in Phoenix, Arizona. It is a part of the DeVry Education Group, which also includes Keller Graduate School of Management, and Carrington College. DeVry University-Arizona offers associate, bachelor's, and master's degrees in business, technology, and healthcare.

The university was founded in 1931 as the DeForest Training School, a two-year trade school. In 1953, the school was renamed DeVry Technical Institute and began offering four-year programs in electronics and business. In 1976, DeVry was granted university status and became DeVry Institute of Technology. In 2002, the school changed its name to DeVry University.

DeVry University-Arizona is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. The business, technology, and healthcare programs are accredited by various professional organizations.

The university has an enrollment of over 1,700 students, most of whom are pursuing degrees in business, technology, or healthcare. The average class size is 20 students. The student-to-faculty ratio is 15:1.

DeVry University-Arizona offers a variety of student services, including academic advising, career services, and tutoring. The university also has an active student government and a variety of clubs and organizations.

DeVry University-Arizona is located in Phoenix, Arizona, a city with a population of over 1.5 million people. The city is home to a number of Fortune 500 companies, as well as numerous cultural and recreational attractions.
