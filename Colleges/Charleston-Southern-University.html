Charleston Southern University is a private, four-year liberal arts college located in North Charleston, South Carolina, United States. The university is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools. Founded in 1964, the school was originally established as Charleston Southern College, a two-year college. In 1992, the college gained university status and changed its name to Charleston Southern University. The school's current president is Dr. Jairy C. Hunter, Jr.

The university offers over 50 undergraduate majors and programs, as well as graduate degrees in business administration, education, and nursing. Charleston Southern University is affiliated with the South Carolina Baptist Convention and is considered a "Bible college" by some sources.

Admission to Charleston Southern University is competitive. For fall 2021, the university received 11,872 applications and had an acceptance rate of 54%. To be considered for admission, applicants must submit an application, high school transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a non-refundable application fee. International students must also submit proof of English proficiency.

The university has a rolling admissions policy, meaning that there is no set deadline for applications. However, students are encouraged to apply early as the university does tend to fill up quickly. Once all required materials have been received, the admissions committee will review the application and make a decision. Decisions are typically rendered within two weeks of the application being completed.

Charleston Southern University is committed to providing a quality education at an affordable price. For fall 2021, tuition and fees for full-time, undergraduate students is $29,950. Financial aid is available for those who qualify.
