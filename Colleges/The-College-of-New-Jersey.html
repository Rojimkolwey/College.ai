The College of New Jersey

Ewing, NJ
2000 Pennington Road, Ewing, NJ 08628-7718
609-771-1855

The College of New Jersey (TCNJ) is a public university in Ewing Township, New Jersey. It is one of the oldest schools in the state, having been founded in 1746. The college is noted for its programs in business, education, engineering, and nursing, as well as its strong liberal arts curriculum. TCNJ is a member of the prestigious Ivy League, and its students come from all over the world. The college offers a wide range of extracurricular activities, including a highly competitive Division I athletics program. TCNJ is also home to a number of research centers and institutes, which provide students with opportunities to get involved in cutting-edge research.

Admission Requirements

Admission to The College of New Jersey is highly competitive. In order to be considered for admission, students must have a strong academic record, as well as extracurricular involvement and community service. The College of New Jersey does not consider standardized test scores in the admissions process.

In order to be eligible for admission, students must have completed a minimum of 24 college credits. The College of New Jersey does not have a preference for any particular type of high school diploma, but students must have a minimum GPA of 3.0. In addition to a strong academic record, students must also demonstrate extracurricular involvement and community service. The College of New Jersey does not consider standardized test scores in the admissions process.
