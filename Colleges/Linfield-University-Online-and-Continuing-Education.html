Linfield University-Online and Continuing Education

McMinnville, OR
900 SE Baker Street, McMinnville, OR 97128-6894
503-883-2447

Linfield University is a private institution that was founded in 1858. The school has a total undergraduate enrollment of 1,212, its setting is suburban, and the campus size is 105 acres. It utilizes a semester-based academic calendar. Linfield University's ranking in the 2020 edition of Best Colleges is National Liberal Arts Colleges, #127. Its tuition and fees are $41,950 (2019-20).

Linfield University-Online and Continuing Education is an accredited online university that offers a variety of undergraduate and graduate programs. Students can choose from over 60 majors and minors, as well as several pre-professional programs. The university also offers a variety of online and evening courses for adult learners.

Linfield University-Online and Continuing Education is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities. The university is also a member of the Oregon Consortium for Nursing Education and the Oregon State Board of Nursing.

Linfield University-Online and Continuing Education has an open admissions policy and does not require applicants to submit SAT or ACT scores. The university has rolling admissions and accepts applications on a rolling basis.

Admission Requirements

Linfield University-Online and Continuing Education offers a variety of online and evening degree programs that fit the schedules of busy adults. Whether you are looking to finish your degree, earn a promotion, or change careers, Linfield University can help you achieve your goals.

Linfield University is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities. Our online and evening programs are designed to fit the schedules of busy adults. We offer a variety of degree programs that can be completed entirely online or through a combination of online and evening courses.

To be eligible for admission to Linfield University, you must have a high school diploma or equivalent. If you have not completed high school, you may be eligible for admission if you pass the General Educational Development (GED) test or the High School Equivalency Test (HiSET).

If you have been out of high school for more than five years, you may be eligible for admission through our Prior Learning Assessment program. This program allows you to earn college credit for learning that has been acquired through work, military training, or other life experiences.

Once you have been admitted to Linfield University, you will work with an advisor to develop a plan of study that meets your individual needs and goals. Our advisors are here to help you every step of the way, from choosing the right courses to enrolling in the right degree program.

We offer a variety of financial aid options to help you pay for your education, including scholarships, grants, and loans. We also offer a flexible payment plan that allows you to spread your tuition payments out over the course of your program.

Linfield University is committed to making higher education accessible and affordable for all students. We offer a variety of programs and services to help you succeed in your academic and professional goals.
