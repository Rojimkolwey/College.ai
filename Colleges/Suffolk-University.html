Suffolk University

Founded in 1906, Suffolk University is a private, coeducational university located in the heart of downtown Boston. With more than 8,000 students enrolled in over 200 programs, Suffolk is the largest independent university in New England.

Suffolk offers an innovative and rigorous curriculum, with over 90 undergraduate programs and more than 60 graduate programs. The University is divided into six schools and colleges, each with a unique focus and area of study: Sawyer Business School, College of Arts & Sciences, Law School, School of Nursing, New England School of Communications, and the School of Health Sciences.

Suffolk is committed to providing a world-class education to its students, preparing them for successful careers and lives of purpose. The University offers a variety of unique opportunities for students to get involved on campus, including over 100 student organizations, Division I athletics, and a thriving performing arts scene.

Suffolk is located in the heart of Boston, one of the most vibrant and exciting cities in the world. With its rich history, diverse population, and abundance of cultural and recreational activities, Boston is the perfect place to call home.

Suffolk University is an institution of higher learning that is dedicated to providing its students with a well-rounded education. The University offers a variety of programs and majors to choose from, ensuring that there is something for everyone. Whether you are interested in business, law, communications, or the sciences, Suffolk has a program that will fit your needs.

Suffolk is located in the heart of downtown Boston, providing its students with easy access to all that the city has to offer. From the Freedom Trail to Fenway Park, there is something for everyone to enjoy. With its convenient location and commitment to excellence, Suffolk is the perfect choice for your college education.

Admission Requirements

Suffolk University is a private research university in Boston, Massachusetts. Founded in 1906 by lawyer Gleason Archer, Jr. as an extension of Suffolk Law School, the university today has an undergraduate student body of over 7,000 and a graduate student body of over 2,800.

Suffolk University offers over 200 programs across a wide range of disciplines, with the majority of undergraduate students enrolled in the Sawyer Business School, the School of Health Sciences, and the College of Arts & Sciences. The university also has a School of Law and a School of Nursing.

To be eligible for admission to Suffolk University, applicants must have a high school diploma or equivalent. The university uses a holistic review process when considering applicants, taking into account factors such as academic performance, letters of recommendation, and personal essays.

Suffolk University is committed to providing access to a quality education, and as such, the university offers a number of need-based and merit-based scholarships and financial aid programs. For students who are unable to pay the full cost of tuition, the university offers a number of payment plan options.
