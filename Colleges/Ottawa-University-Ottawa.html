1001 South Cedar, Ottawa, KS 66067-3399

Founded in 1865, Ottawa University is a private, non-profit institution of higher learning affiliated with the Baptist Church. The university's main campus is located in Ottawa, Kansas, United States, with additional campuses in Surprise, Arizona and Online. It is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. The university offers undergraduate and graduate degrees in a variety of disciplines, including business, education, nursing, and the liberal arts.

Ottawa University was founded in 1865 by Baptist missionaries, and was originally known as Ottawa Baptist College. The college was established in order to provide higher education to the residents of Franklin County, Kansas. The first class of students consisted of just six students, and the first graduation ceremony was held in 1868. In 1869, the college was renamed Ottawa University, and began offering a wider range of courses. In 1874, the university moved to its current location in Ottawa, Kansas.

The university has undergone several changes over the years, but its commitment to providing a quality education remains the same. Today, Ottawa University is a thriving institution of higher learning, with over 3,000 students enrolled in its various programs. The university offers a wide range of undergraduate and graduate degrees, in disciplines such as business, education, nursing, and the liberal arts. Ottawa University is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission, and is a member of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools.

Admission Requirements

Admission requirements vary depending on the program to which you are applying. However, all applicants must meet the general admission requirements, which include holding a minimum grade point average (GPA) of 2.5 on a 4.0 scale, providing official transcripts from all colleges and/or universities attended, and submitting a completed application form.

Some programs may have additional requirements, such as letters of recommendation, a personal essay, or an interview. Please consult the specific program requirements for more information.

International students whose first language is not English must also submit proof of English proficiency, such as a TOEFL score. The minimum score required varies by program.

Ottawa University-Ottawa is committed to providing an accessible and inclusive learning environment for all students. If you have any questions or concerns about the admission process, please contact the Office of Admissions.
