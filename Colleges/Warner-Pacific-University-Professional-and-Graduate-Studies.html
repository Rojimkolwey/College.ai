Warner Pacific University Professional and Graduate Studies

Portland, OR
2600 SE 98th Ave Ste 200, Portland, OR 97266
503-517-1550

Warner Pacific University Professional and Graduate Studies is a private, Christian university located in Portland, Oregon. The school offers a variety of undergraduate and graduate programs, as well as professional development opportunities.

Undergraduate programs include business, education, nursing, and liberal arts. The business program offers concentrations in accounting, finance, and marketing. The education program offers licensure in elementary, secondary, and special education. The nursing program offers a Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BSN) and a Registered Nurse to Bachelor of Science in Nursing (RN-BSN) completion program. The liberal arts program offers a Bachelor of Arts (BA) in English, History, Psychology, and Sociology.

The graduate programs offered include business, education, nursing, and counseling. The business program offers a Master of Business Administration (MBA) and concentrations in accounting, finance, and marketing. The education program offers a Master of Arts in Education (MAEd) with concentrations in elementary, secondary, and special education. The nursing program offers a Master of Science in Nursing (MSN) and a Doctor of Nursing Practice (DNP). The counseling program offers a Master of Counseling (MC) with concentrations in Clinical Mental Health Counseling and School Counseling.

In addition to degree programs, Warner Pacific University Professional and Graduate Studies offers a variety of professional development opportunities. These include certificate programs, workshops, and conferences. The certificate programs are designed for working professionals who want to improve their skills in a specific area. The workshops and conferences are designed for educators and counselors who want to stay up-to-date on the latest research and best practices.

Admission Requirements

Warner Pacific University Professional and Graduate Studies (WPUP) offers a variety of programs for students seeking to further their education and career. The programs at WPUP are designed to meet the needs of working adults and provide them with the knowledge and skills necessary to succeed in today's workforce.

WPUP offers both undergraduate and graduate programs. Undergraduate programs include business, education, and nursing. Graduate programs include business administration, counseling, and education.

Admission to WPUP is based on a student's academic record, test scores, and letters of recommendation. Students must also submit an essay and a resume. Admission to WPUP is competitive and students are encouraged to apply early.
