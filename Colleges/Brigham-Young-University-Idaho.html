Brigham Young University-Idaho (BYU-Idaho) is a private university in Rexburg, Idaho. Founded in 1888, BYU-Idaho is owned and operated by The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS Church). It is a four-year institution with over 34,000 students. The university offers associate's, bachelor's, and master's degrees.

BYU-Idaho's mission is to develop disciples of Jesus Christ who are leaders in their homes, the Church, and their communities. The university strives to create an environment that fosters learning and personal growth, where students can develop their talents and serve others.

BYU-Idaho is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities. The university is organized into seven colleges: Agricultural and Life Sciences, Business and Communications, Education, Engineering and Technology, Family, Home, and Social Sciences, Humanities, and Physical and Mathematical Sciences.

The university offers a variety of programs and activities to help students succeed academically, spiritually, and socially. BYU-Idaho provides a rigorous academic environment, with small class sizes and a focus on teaching. The university also offers a variety of extracurricular activities, including clubs and organizations, intramural sports, and service opportunities.

BYU-Idaho is committed to providing an affordable education. The university offers a variety of scholarships and financial aid options to help students pay for their education. BYU-Idaho also offers flexible scheduling options, including online and evening classes, to help students balance their education with other responsibilities.

Admission Requirements

Brigham Young University-Idaho is a private university located in Rexburg, Idaho. The university is owned and operated by The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS Church). BYU-Idaho is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities.

Admission to BYU-Idaho is based on the applicant's academic record, ACT or SAT scores, and a personal essay. The university does not consider an applicant's race, color, national origin, sex, or disability in the admission process.

BYU-Idaho requires all undergraduate students to be members of the LDS Church and to adhere to the university's Honor Code, which includes standards of behavior and dress. The university also requires students to complete an LDS Church-sponsored mission before they enroll.
