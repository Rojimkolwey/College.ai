About the District of Columbia

The District of Columbia is well known as the capital of the United States. The city is home to many of the country's most famous landmarks and institutions, including the White House, the Capitol, the Supreme Court, and the Library of Congress. The city is also home to a number of museums and galleries, as well as a number of professional and collegiate sports teams.

The District of Columbia is located on the east coast of the United States, between the states of Maryland and Virginia. The city is situated on the Potomac River, which forms the border between the two states. The District of Columbia is bordered by the Anacostia River to the east and the Potomac River to the west.

The District of Columbia has a population of over 600,000 people. The city is divided into four quadrants: Northwest, Northeast, Southeast, and Southwest. The city is also home to a number of neighborhoods, each with its own unique character.

The District of Columbia is served by two major airports: Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport and Washington Dulles International Airport. The city is also served by a number of public transportation options, including the Metro system, buses, and taxis.
