Transylvania University

Lexington, KY
300 N Broadway, Lexington, KY 40508-1797
859-233-8300

Transylvania University is a private university in Lexington, Kentucky. It was founded in 1780, making it the first university in Kentucky and among the oldest in the United States. It is the 28th-oldest continuously operating university in the country. The university's name comes from its location in the historic region of Transylvania.

The university began as a log cabin college in a remote wilderness area. It moved to Lexington in 1789, where it was chartered as Transylvania Seminary. In 1818, the Kentucky General Assembly chartered it as Transylvania University. Its main campus covers more than 100 acres (40 ha) in downtown Lexington. The university has about 1,000 students in 30 undergraduate and 20 graduate programs.

The university's medical program, now the School of Medicine, began in 1837. It was the first in the United States to award the Doctor of Medicine degree west of the Allegheny Mountains. The School of Nursing, established in 1889, is the oldest in Kentucky. The university also operates a law school, which was established in 1799 and is the oldest west of the Alleghenies.

In 1780, the Virginia General Assembly chartered Transylvania Seminary, located in what was then Kentucky County, Virginia, in the Virginia District of the Colony and Dominion of Virginia. The seminary was Kentucky's first institute of higher learning. It was founded by the Transylvania Company, a group of Virginians who had recently settled in Kentucky. The company's shareholders included Lexington's leading citizens, such as Henry Clay and James McAfee.

The seminary's first president was the Reverend David Rice, a Presbyterian minister from Virginia. He taught the school's first class of seven students in a one-room log cabin near present-day Lexington. The log cabin is now a historical site on the Transylvania University campus.

In 1789, the seminary moved to Lexington, the new capital of Kentucky. The Kentucky General Assembly chartered the school as Transylvania University in 1780. The legislature also decreed that the university should never be located outside of Lexington.

In 1818, the Kentucky General Assembly chartered Transylvania University as a degree-granting institution. The university began admitting women in 1880.

Transylvania University has a rich history and tradition of educational excellence. It is a dynamic and innovative institution of higher learning, offering a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs. The university is committed to providing a liberal arts education that is grounded in the traditions of the past and prepares students for the challenges of the future.

Admission Requirements

In order to be admitted to Transylvania University, students must have a high school diploma or equivalent. They must also submit an application, transcripts, and SAT or ACT scores.

Students who have a GPA of 3.0 or higher and an SAT score of 1200 or higher are eligible for automatic admission. Those who do not meet these requirements may still be considered for admission if they have strong grades and test scores and submit a well-written essay.

Transylvania University is committed to admitting a diverse group of students, and considers a variety of factors when making admissions decisions. These factors include academic achievement, leadership potential, extracurricular involvement, and personal character.

The admissions process at Transylvania University is designed to be personal and comprehensive, and the admissions staff is available to help students and families through every step of the process.
