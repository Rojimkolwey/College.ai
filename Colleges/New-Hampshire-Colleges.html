New Hampshire Universities and Colleges


New Hampshire is home to a wide variety of colleges and universities, ranging from small liberal arts colleges to large research universities. The University of New Hampshire (UNH) is the state's flagship university, offering a wide range of undergraduate and graduate degree programs. Located in Durham, UNH is ranked among the top public universities in the country, and is home to a large number of highly-regarded programs, including an Honors College, the Whittemore School of Business and Economics, and a School of Marine Science and Ocean Engineering.

Other public universities in the state include Plymouth State University, Keene State College, Granite State College, and the Community College System of New Hampshire. These institutions provide students with access to a variety of degree programs, including those in the liberal arts, business, engineering, and the sciences.

The state is also home to a number of private colleges and universities, including Dartmouth College, Saint Anselm College, Franklin Pierce University, and the New England College. These institutions offer a range of degree programs in the liberal arts, business, and the sciences.

New Hampshire is also home to a number of specialty schools, including the New Hampshire Institute of Art, the New Hampshire Technical Institute, the New Hampshire School of Law, and the New England College of Optometry. These institutions provide students with the opportunity to pursue specialized degree programs in their chosen field.

No matter what type of college or university you are looking for, New Hampshire has something to offer. From small liberal arts colleges to large research universities, the state has a wealth of options for students. With its excellent programs, diverse student body, and high-quality educational opportunities, New Hampshire is a great place to pursue higher education.
