Creighton University is a private research university in Omaha, Nebraska. Founded in 1878, the school is one of 28 member institutions of the Association of Jesuit Colleges and Universities. The university is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and has a student body of over 8,000 students. The school offers programs at the undergraduate, graduate, and professional levels, as well as online courses and degrees. The university's campus covers over 150 acres and includes several buildings of architectural significance. The school's athletic teams, known as the Bluejays, compete in Division I of the NCAA and are members of the Big East Conference.

Creighton University was founded in 1878 by the Society of Jesus. The school was originally located in downtown Omaha, but moved to its current location in the city's Dundee neighborhood in 1887. The university has grown steadily in the intervening years, adding new programs and buildings to its campus. The school now offers over 100 undergraduate and graduate programs, as well as professional degrees in law and medicine. The university's campus covers 150 acres and includes several buildings of architectural significance, such as the Henningson Memorial Center and the Harper Center. The school's athletic teams, known as the Bluejays, compete in Division I of the NCAA and are members of the Big East Conference. The Bluejays have won numerous conference championships and made several appearances in the NCAA tournament.

Admission Requirements

Creighton University is a private university located in Omaha, Nebraska. The university was founded in 1878 and is affiliated with the Jesuit order of the Catholic Church. The university comprises eight schools and colleges, including the College of Arts and Sciences, the School of Business, the School of Education, the School of Law, the School of Medicine, the School of Nursing, and the Graduate School. The university enrolls approximately 4,000 undergraduate and 2,000 graduate students.

The admissions process at Creighton University is selective. The university evaluates applicants on their academic record, standardized test scores, extracurricular activities, and personal essays. The university also considers letters of recommendation and demonstrated interest in the university when making admissions decisions.

Creighton University has a holistic admissions process and considers each applicant as an individual. The university looks for students who have the potential to succeed academically and contribute to the university community.
