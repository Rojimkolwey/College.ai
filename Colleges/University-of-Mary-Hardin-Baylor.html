Founded in 1845, the University of Mary Hardin-Baylor is a private Christian university located in Belton, Texas. UMHB is affiliated with the Baptist General Convention of Texas and accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges. The university offers more than 80 undergraduate and graduate programs through four academic colleges: the College of Business, the College of Christian Studies, the College of Education, and the College of Nursing and Health Sciences. UMHB is committed to preparing students for leadership and service through a Christ-centered education. The university's core values include faith, learning, integrity, and service.

UMHB was founded as an all-male institution and began admitting women in 1951. Today, the university enrolls more than 3,000 students from across the United States and around the world. The campus is home to a variety of student organizations, including academic, service, social, and athletic clubs. UMHB also offers a number of on-campus housing options, including residence halls, apartments, and townhouses.

The University of Mary Hardin-Baylor is dedicated to providing a quality education that is affordable and accessible. The university offers a variety of financial aid options, including scholarships, grants, loans, and work-study programs. UMHB also participates in the Texas Tuition Equalization Grant program, which provides need-based financial assistance to eligible Texas residents.

UMHB is committed to preparing students for success in their chosen field of study. The university offers a number of career-related services, including resume and cover letter assistance, job search resources, and internships. The university's career services staff also provides guidance on graduate school and professional school options.

The University of Mary Hardin-Baylor is located in Belton, Texas, a city of approximately 20,000 people located in the heart of Central Texas. Belton is situated within the Killeen-Temple-Fort Hood metropolitan area, which has a population of more than 450,000. The city is home to a number of businesses and industries, including healthcare, education, manufacturing, and retail.

UMHB is accessible by a number of major highways, including Interstate 35, Highway 190, and Highway 84. The university is also served by the Killeen-Fort Hood Regional Airport, which is located just minutes from campus.

Admission Requirements

Admission to the University of Mary Hardin-Baylor is based on the student's high school academic record, ACT or SAT scores, and class rank (if available). The University admits students of any race, color, national and ethnic origin to all the rights, privileges, programs, and activities generally accorded or made available to students at the school.
