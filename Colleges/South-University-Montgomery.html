South University-Montgomery

Montgomery, AL
5355 Vaughn Rd, Montgomery, AL 36116
334-395-8800

South University-Montgomery is a private, for-profit university in Montgomery, Alabama. The university is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges to award associate's, bachelor's, and master's degrees.

The university was founded in 1899 as the Southern Business College, and later became the Southern College of Business. In 1974, the college was renamed South University. The university's first Montgomery campus opened in 1984.

South University-Montgomery offers associate's, bachelor's, and master's degrees in business, nursing, and health sciences. The university also offers a Doctor of Pharmacy program.

The university's nursing program is accredited by the Accreditation Commission for Education in Nursing. The university's health sciences programs are accredited by the Commission on Accreditation of Allied Health Education Programs.

The university is a member of the Association of Independent Colleges and Universities of Alabama.

South University-Montgomery has an enrollment of approximately 1,200 students. The university's student-to-faculty ratio is 15-to-1.

The university's campus covers 30 acres (12 ha) of land. The university's library has a collection of over 40,000 volumes.

The university's athletics teams compete in the National Association of Intercollegiate Athletics. The university's sports teams are nicknamed the Jaguars.

Admission Requirements

In order to be admitted to South University-Montgomery, applicants must submit a completed application form, high school transcripts, and SAT or ACT scores. The admissions committee will review all materials and make a decision based on the student's academic record, test scores, and personal qualifications.
