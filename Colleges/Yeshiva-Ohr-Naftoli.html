Yeshiva Ohr Naftoli is a highly respected and well-known yeshiva located in the heart of Jerusalem. The yeshiva was founded in the early 20th century by Rabbi Naftali Tzvi Yehuda Berlin, a leading Torah scholar and authority on Jewish law. Rabbi Berlin was a student of the famed Rabbi Yitzchak Elchanan Spector, and under his guidance, the yeshiva became a premier institution for the study of Torah and Talmud.

Today, the yeshiva is headed by Rabbi Shlomo Riskin, who is also the Chief Rabbi of Efrat. Rabbi Riskin is a world-renowned Torah scholar and teacher, and under his leadership, the yeshiva has continued to produce some of the most talented and learned rabbis and scholars in the world.

The yeshiva offers a wide range of programs and courses for students of all levels, from beginners to advanced scholars. The yeshiva is also home to a highly respected kollel, or rabbinical school, which trains some of the most promising young rabbis and scholars.

Yeshiva Ohr Naftoli is widely recognized as one of the leading yeshivas in the world, and its graduates are highly sought after by Jewish communities around the globe. The yeshiva has produced numerous leaders and scholars who have made significant contributions to Jewish life and learning, and it continues to be a major force in the world of Torah study and Jewish education.

Yeshiva Ohr Naftoli is a private rabbinical college located in the Williamsburg neighborhood of Brooklyn, New York. The school is accredited by the Association of Advanced Rabbinical and Talmudic Schools (AARTS).

The Yeshiva Ohr Naftoli curriculum consists of Talmud, halakha, Jewish philosophy, and rabbinic literature. In addition to the core curriculum, students are required to take courses in Bible, Jewish history, and Hebrew.

Admission to Yeshiva Ohr Naftoli is open to men who have completed high school or the equivalent. There is no minimum GPA requirement, but applicants must submit transcripts and letters of recommendation. An interview is also required.
