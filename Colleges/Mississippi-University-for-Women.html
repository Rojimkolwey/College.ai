Mississippi University for Women

Columbus, MS
1100 College Street, Columbus, MS 39701
662-329-4750

Mississippi University for Women (MUW) is a public university in Columbus, Mississippi. It is the only public women's college in the United States. The school was founded in 1884 as the Industrial Institute and College for the Education of White Girls. It became a state-supported college for women in 1974 and began admitting men in 1982.

MUW offers bachelor's and master's degrees in a variety of disciplines. The school is known for its nursing program, which is the largest in the state. Other popular majors include education, business, and the humanities.

The school's campus is located in the historic district of downtown Columbus. The school's buildings are a mix of old and new, with the most notable being the Muw Administration Building, which is on the National Register of Historic Places.

MUW has a strong commitment to service learning and offers a variety of opportunities for students to get involved in the community. The school is also home to a number of clubs and organizations, including the Student Government Association, which represents the student body.

MUW is a diverse and welcoming community. The school's student body is made up of students from all over the world. The school is committed to providing an inclusive environment for all students.
