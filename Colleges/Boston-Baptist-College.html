Boston Baptist College is a small, private, Christian college located in Boston, Massachusetts. The college was founded in 1873 by a group of Baptist ministers and laymen, and was originally known as the New England Baptist Training School. The school's mission was to train men for the Baptist ministry. In its early years, the school struggled financially, and was often forced to close its doors for short periods of time. However, the school persevered, and in time, became one of the leading Baptist colleges in the country. Today, Boston Baptist College is an accredited institution, offering a variety of undergraduate and graduate programs. The college is committed to providing a Christ-centered education, and to preparing students for leadership and service in the church and the world.

Boston Baptist College is a private, Christian college located in Boston, Massachusetts. The college was founded in 1873 and is accredited by the Association of Biblical Higher Education and the New England Association of Schools and Colleges. Boston Baptist College offers associate, bachelor, and master's degrees in a variety of disciplines.

The admissions process at Boston Baptist College is designed to help students discern their call to ministry and to prepare them for leadership in the church. The college seeks to enroll students who are committed to Christ and His Church, and who have a desire to learn and grow in their faith.

Prospective students must submit an application, transcripts, and a personal essay. The admissions committee will review these materials and make a decision based on the student's academic record, personal testimony, and potential for ministry.
