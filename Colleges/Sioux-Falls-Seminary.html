Sioux Falls Seminary

Sioux Falls, SD
2100 S Summit Ave, Sioux Falls, SD 57105
605-336-6588

Sioux Falls Seminary is a private, evangelical seminary in Sioux Falls, South Dakota. It is accredited by the Association of Theological Schools in the United States and Canada and the Higher Learning Commission. The seminary offers master's degrees in divinity, counseling, and ministry, as well as a Doctor of Ministry degree.

Founded in 1869, Sioux Falls Seminary is the oldest seminary in the Dakotas. It was originally founded as a training ground for missionaries to the Dakota Territory, and its first president was the Rev. George H. Pond, a missionary to the Sioux. The seminary has been accredited by the ATS since 1925.

The seminary's campus is located in the historic district of downtown Sioux Falls. The campus includes several buildings, including the main academic building, which houses the library, classrooms, and offices; the President's House; and the Student Center.

The seminary's faculty includes scholars in the fields of Old and New Testament studies, church history, systematic theology, counseling, and ministry. The seminary offers a variety of degree programs, including the Master of Divinity, Master of Arts in Counseling, Master of Arts in Ministry, and Doctor of Ministry.

The seminary's student body is diverse, with students coming from a variety of backgrounds and denominations. The seminary offers a supportive community for students, with a variety of student organizations and activities.

Sioux Falls Seminary is committed to preparing students for ministry in the church and the world. The seminary offers a rigorous academic program, equipping students with the knowledge and skills they need to serve in a variety of ministry contexts. The seminary also offers opportunities for students to gain practical experience through internships and other field education opportunities.

Admission Requirements

In order to be admitted to Sioux Falls Seminary, applicants must have a bachelor's degree from an accredited institution. They must also submit official transcripts, a personal essay, letters of recommendation, and a resume. In some cases, applicants may also be required to submit GRE or GMAT scores. Once all materials have been received, the Admissions Committee will review the file and make a decision.
