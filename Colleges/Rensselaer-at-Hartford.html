Rensselaer at Hartford

Hartford, CT
275 Windsor St, Hartford, CT 06120
860-548-2400

Rensselaer at Hartford, also known as RPI, is a private university located in Hartford, Connecticut. It was founded in 1824 and is one of the oldest universities in the United States. The university is known for its engineering and science programs, and its alumni include several astronauts, Nobel laureates, and CEOs of major corporations. RPI also has a strong athletic program, with its men's hockey team winning the NCAA Division I championship in 1985. The university enrolls approximately 5,000 students and has a faculty of over 400.

Admission Requirements

Rensselaer at Hartford is a highly selective engineering school that requires all applicants to have a strong academic record. The admissions process is competitive, and students are encouraged to apply early. All applicants must submit an application, transcripts, and test scores. The admissions committee will also consider letters of recommendation, essays, and extracurricular activities. Rensselaer at Hartford is committed to enrolling a diverse student body and offers a variety of scholarships and financial aid programs.
