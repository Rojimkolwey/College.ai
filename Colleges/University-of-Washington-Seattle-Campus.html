Admission Requirements

Admission to the University of Washington is competitive. The university uses a holistic review process, taking into account an applicant's academic record, test scores, extracurricular activities, and personal statements. The university also considers an applicant's demonstrated interest in the university, as well as their ability to contribute to the diversity of the university community.

The university has a minimum GPA requirement of 3.0 for admission. However, the average GPA of admitted students is much higher, at 3.75. The university also requires all applicants to submit SAT or ACT scores. The average SAT score of admitted students is 1290, and the average ACT score is 29.

The University of Washington is committed to admitting a diverse student body, and the university offers a number of programs and initiatives to support underrepresented students. The university offers a fee waiver for applicants who demonstrate financial need, and also offers a number of scholarships and grants to support students from underrepresented groups.
