Colorado Universities and Colleges


Colorado is home to many higher education institutions and universities, offering an array of educational opportunities for students. The Colorado Community College System (CCCS) is made up of 13 community colleges, offering associate degrees and certificate programs in a variety of areas. Colorado State University is the state's flagship institution, offering bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees in a variety of disciplines. The University of Colorado System is composed of four campuses: Boulder, Colorado Springs, Denver, and Anschutz Medical Campus. These schools offer a wide range of degree programs, from undergraduate to doctoral and professional programs. Colorado is also home to many private institutions, such as the University of Denver, Regis University, and Colorado Christian University. These schools offer a variety of degree programs, ranging from liberal arts to business and law. Colorado also has five tribal colleges, providing associate and bachelor's degrees to Native American students. In addition to traditional four-year institutions, Colorado is home to many technical colleges and career schools, offering certificate and associate degree programs in areas such as automotive technology, culinary arts, and cosmetology. Colorado also has many continuing education programs, both online and in-person, for those looking to advance their career. With so many options available, Colorado is an ideal state for those looking to pursue higher education.
