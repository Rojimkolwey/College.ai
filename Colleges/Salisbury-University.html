Salisbury University is a public university located in Salisbury, Maryland, United States. Founded in 1925, Salisbury is one of the oldest public universities in Maryland. The university is a member of the University System of Maryland.

The university offers bachelor's and master's degrees in a variety of disciplines. Salisbury is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education. The university's main campus is located on a 940-acre (3.8 km2) campus in the city of Salisbury.

The university has an enrollment of over 8,000 students. The student body is diverse, with students coming from all over the United States and more than 40 countries.

Salisbury offers a variety of undergraduate and graduate programs. The university is home to the Frank R. Perdue School of Business, the Henry A. School of Education, the Samuel W. and Marilyn C. Seidel School of Nursing and the Charles R. and Martha N. Fulton School of Liberal Arts.

The university is also home to a number of research institutes and centers, including the Center for Education Policy Analysis, the Institute for Conflict Analysis and Resolution, and the Institute for Public Affairs and Civic Engagement.

Salisbury University is committed to providing a quality education at an affordable price. The university offers a variety of financial aid options, including grants, loans, and scholarships.

Salisbury is a great place to live, work, and learn. The university offers a variety of on-campus housing options, including apartments, townhouses, and suites. The university also offers a variety of dining options, including a food court, a dining hall, and a number of on-campus restaurants.

The university is located in the heart of Salisbury, Maryland, a beautiful historic city on the Eastern Shore of Maryland. Salisbury is a short drive from Baltimore, Washington, D.C., and Philadelphia.
