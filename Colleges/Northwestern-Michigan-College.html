Admission Requirements

Northwestern Michigan College (NMC) is a public community college in Traverse City, Michigan. NMC offers Associate's degrees and professional certificates, and works with four-year colleges and universities to offer Bachelor's and advanced degrees.

NMC is an open-admission college, meaning that any student who meets the minimum requirements for admission will be accepted. However, some programs at NMC may have additional requirements, and preference may be given to students who live in the college's district.

The minimum requirements for admission to NMC are as follows:

* A high school diploma or equivalent

* A minimum 2.0 GPA on a 4.0 scale

* Satisfactory scores on the ACT or SAT (if applicable)

Students who do not meet the minimum GPA requirement may still be eligible for admission if they have completed at least 12 credits of college-level coursework with a 2.0 GPA or higher.

Students who have been out of high school for more than five years may be eligible for admission without a high school diploma or equivalent if they can demonstrate the ability to benefit from college-level coursework.

NMC also offers a number of programs that allow students to earn college credit while still in high school. These programs include dual enrollment, early college, and Advanced Placement.
