Mount St. Mary's University

Emmitsburg, MD
16300 Old Emmitsburg Road, Emmitsburg, MD 21727-7799
301-447-6122

Founded in 1808, Mount St. Mary's University is the second-oldest Catholic university in the United States. The university is located in Emmitsburg, Maryland, about 50 miles (80 km) north of the United States Capitol in Washington, D.C. and about an hour northwest of Baltimore. The university has more than 2,000 undergraduate and graduate students from 36 states and 43 countries.

The school was founded by the Society of St. Sulpice, a Catholic seminary priest order, and was originally located in Baltimore. The seminary moved to Emmitsburg in 1810 to be closer to a large population of Catholic immigrants from Ireland and Germany who were moving to the area.

The Mount was ranked the #1 Regional College in the North by U.S. News & World Report. The Mount also offers over 55 undergraduate and graduate degree programs.

The campus of Mount St. Mary's University is located on a picturesque, 185-acre (0.75 km2) mountaintop campus in Emmitsburg. The campus is home to several historical buildings, including the Mother Seton House (the birthplace of Saint Elizabeth Ann Seton) and the Basilica of the Immaculate Conception, the largest church in the United States.

The university is governed by a Board of Trustees, which includes 18 laypeople, 2 priests, 2 religious brothers, and the university president. The Mount is a member of the Association of Catholic Colleges and Universities (ACCU) and the Council of Independent Colleges (CIC).

The university offers a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs, including business, education, engineering, nursing, and the liberal arts. The Mount also offers a unique 5-year Bachelor of Science in Nursing/Master of Science in Nursing program, which allows students to complete both degrees in just five years.

The Mount has a strong commitment to service and leadership, and offers a number of service-learning and leadership programs, including the Mount Leadership Scholars program and the Mount Global Fellows program.

The Mount athletics teams compete in the NCAA Division III and are members of the Landmark Conference. The Mount has 19 varsity teams, including baseball, basketball, cross country, golf, lacrosse, soccer, softball, tennis, track and field, and volleyball.

The university is also home to a number of student organizations, including the Student Government Association, the Residence Hall Association, and the Inter-Fraternity Council.

Mount St. Mary's University is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education.

Admission Requirements

Mount St. Mary's University is a Catholic university in Los Angeles, California. It is the oldest Catholic university in the state of California. The university is open to students of all faiths. The university has an undergraduate enrollment of 1,665 students and a graduate enrollment of 2,664 students. The university offers bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees.

Admission to Mount St. Mary's University is based on a holistic review of the applicant's academic record, test scores, personal essay, and letters of recommendation. The university does not have a minimum GPA or test score requirement for admission. The university does not consider race, ethnicity, or national origin in the admission process.

Mount St. Mary's University is accredited by the Western Association of Schools and Colleges. The university is also a member of the Association of Catholic Colleges and Universities.
