11139 Anderson Street, Loma Linda, CA 92350

Loma Linda University (LLU) is a Seventh-day Adventist coeducational health sciences university located in Loma Linda, California, United States. The University comprises eight schools and the Faculty of Graduate Studies. Founded in 1905, Loma Linda University is a health sciences university with programs in medicine, dentistry, pharmacy, public health, nursing, allied health, and biomedical sciences. The main campus is located in the San Bernardino County city of Loma Linda, about 35 miles (56 km) east of downtown Los Angeles and about 80 miles (130 km) west of Palm Springs.

Loma Linda University is accredited by the Western Association of Schools and Colleges and is a member of the Association of Universities and Colleges of Canada. The University is also accredited by the Adventist Accrediting Association. Loma Linda University is organized into eight schools: the School of Allied Health Professions, the School of Behavioral Health, the School of Biomedical Sciences, the School of Dentistry, the School of Medicine, the School of Nursing, the School of Pharmacy, and the School of Public Health.

The University offers more than 140 degree programs in over 100 areas of study. Degree programs are offered at the bachelor's, master's, doctoral, and professional levels. Loma Linda University is home to the world-renowned Loma Linda University Medical Center, which includes Loma Linda University Children's Hospital, Loma Linda University Heart and Surgical Hospital, Loma Linda University Behavioral Medicine Center, and Loma Linda University Cancer Center. The University also operates the Loma Linda University School of Medicine, the Loma Linda University School of Dentistry, and the Loma Linda University School of Public Health.

Loma Linda University is committed to the education of health care professionals and the advancement of medical knowledge. The University's faculty and staff are dedicated to providing students with a quality education and a caring environment. The University is committed to academic excellence and the highest standards of professional and ethical conduct. Loma Linda University is an equal opportunity employer and does not discriminate on the basis of race, color, national origin, gender, age, or disability.

Loma Linda University is a private, nonprofit, coeducational health sciences university located in Loma Linda, California, United States. The university is affiliated with the Seventh-day Adventist Church. It offers programs in medicine, dentistry, pharmacy, public health, nursing, allied health, and graduate studies. Loma Linda University School of Medicine is one of only four medical schools in the world that practice and teach the Seventh-day Adventist health message of "Wholeness".

Loma Linda University is accredited by the Western Association of Schools and Colleges (WASC) and is a member of the Association of Universities and Colleges of Canada (AUCC). The School of Medicine is accredited by the Liaison Committee on Medical Education (LCME). The School of Dentistry is accredited by the American Dental Association (ADA), and the School of Pharmacy is accredited by the Accreditation Council for Pharmacy Education (ACPE).

Loma Linda University has an enrollment of over 4,000 students from more than 70 countries. The student body includes Adventists, Muslims, Buddhists, Christians, and people of other faiths.

Admission to Loma Linda University is competitive. For the School of Medicine, the average GPA of incoming students is 3.8 and the average MCAT score is 511. For the School of Dentistry, the average GPA of incoming students is 3.5 and the average DAT score is 19. For the School of Pharmacy, the average GPA of incoming students is 3.4 and the average PCAT score is 520.

Loma Linda University does not have a minimum GPA or test score requirement for admission. However, all applicants must complete a minimum of 90 semester hours (or 135 quarter hours) of undergraduate coursework before applying. In addition, all applicants must complete the following courses:

- 1 year of Biology with labs

- 1 year of Chemistry with labs

- 1 year of Physics with labs

- 1 year of English

- 1 semester of Biochemistry

- 1 semester of Genetics

- 1 semester of Psychology

- 1 semester of Sociology

- 1 semester of Statistics

- 1 semester of Calculus

- 1 semester of Cell Biology

- 1 semester of Immunology

- 1 semester of Microbiology
