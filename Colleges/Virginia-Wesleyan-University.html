Virginia Wesleyan University

Virginia Beach, VA
5817 Wesleyan Drive, Virginia Beach, VA 23455
757-455-3200

Founded in 1961, Virginia Wesleyan University is a private, liberal arts university located in Virginia Beach, Virginia. The university has an enrollment of approximately 1,500 students and offers over 30 majors and minors. The university is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges.

Virginia Wesleyan University was founded as a two-year college by the United Methodist Church. The college was originally located in Norfolk, Virginia and was named Norfolk Wesleyan College. In 1968, the college relocated to Virginia Beach and became a four-year institution. In 1972, the college was renamed Virginia Wesleyan College. In 1999, the college attained university status and was renamed Virginia Wesleyan University.

The university is situated on a 150-acre campus in Virginia Beach. The campus features several academic and administrative buildings, including the Batten Arts and Letters Building, the Bird Hall Science Building, the Eure Commons Student Center, the Hofheimer Library, the Larchmont Residence Halls, and the Virginia Beach Field House.

The university offers a variety of undergraduate and graduate degrees. Undergraduate degrees are offered in the fields of Arts and Letters, Business, Education, Health Sciences, and Social Sciences. Graduate degrees are offered in the fields of Education, Health Sciences, and Business.

The university is home to several athletic teams, which compete in the NCAA Division III. The university’s athletic teams are nicknamed the Marlins. The university’s sports teams include baseball, basketball, cross country, golf, lacrosse, soccer, softball, tennis, and volleyball.

Virginia Wesleyan University is committed to providing a quality education to its students. The university’s faculty members are experts in their respective fields and are dedicated to teaching and mentoring students. The university offers a variety of student services, including academic advising, career counseling, and tutoring. The university also has a robust financial aid program to help students finance their education.

Admission Requirements

Virginia Wesleyan University is a private liberal arts university in Virginia Beach, Virginia. The university offers bachelor's degrees in over 30 majors, as well as master's degrees in business administration, education, and nursing.

Admission to Virginia Wesleyan is selective, and the university typically admits students who have a strong academic record and a demonstrated commitment to service and leadership. The university also looks for students who will contribute to the diversity of the student body.

To be considered for admission, students must submit an application, transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a personal essay. The university also requires two letters of recommendation, one of which must be from a teacher.

Virginia Wesleyan University is committed to providing a quality education to all students, and the university offers a number of financial aid and scholarship programs to help make college affordable. For more information about admission requirements and financial aid, interested students should contact the Virginia Wesleyan University Office of Admissions.
