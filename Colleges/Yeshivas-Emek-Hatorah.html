Yeshivas Emek Hatorah is a world-renowned Torah institution located in the heart of Jerusalem. Founded over 50 years ago, the yeshiva has been a leading light in the Torah world, attracting students from all over the globe who come to study under the guidance of its world-renowned rabbinic faculty.

The yeshiva's curriculum is based on the study of the Talmud and other core Jewish texts, and its students are renowned for their deep knowledge and understanding of Jewish law and tradition. The yeshiva also places a strong emphasis on character development and spiritual growth, and its graduates are known for their dedication to Jewish values and their commitment to leading meaningful and productive lives.

Yeshivas Emek Hatorah has produced some of the most respected rabbis and scholars in the world, and its graduates can be found in leadership positions in Jewish communities across the globe. The yeshiva is a true treasure of the Jewish people, and its impact on the world of Torah is immeasurable.

Admission Requirements

Yeshivas Emek Hatorah is a private Orthodox Jewish high school located in Queens, New York. The school is open to students of all Jewish backgrounds, and the admissions process is conducted primarily through a lottery system.

Yeshivas Emek Hatorah seeks to provide a well-rounded Jewish and secular education to its students. The school offers a rigorous academic program, including classes in Jewish studies, Hebrew, and English. In addition to academics, the school emphasizes extracurricular activities and leadership development.

To be eligible for admission to Yeshivas Emek Hatorah, students must be residents of New York City and must have completed eighth grade at a Jewish day school. Students must also take a placement test and interview with the school.
