Macalester College is a private, coeducational liberal arts college in Saint Paul, Minnesota, United States. Founded in 1874, Macalester is exclusively an undergraduate four-year institution and enrolled 2,174 students in the fall of 2013 from 50 U.S. states and 90 countries. It is one of the oldest and most selective colleges in the Midwest and is known for its highly competitive academic environment.

Admission Requirements

Macalester College is a private liberal arts college in Saint Paul, Minnesota. Founded in 1874, Macalester is exclusively an undergraduate institution and enrolls 2,138 students. The college offers Bachelor of Arts degrees in more than 50 majors and concentrations.

Admission to Macalester is highly selective. For the class of 2024, Macalester received 8,606 applications and accepted 1,496 (17.4%). The middle 50% of admitted students scored between 1240 and 1410 on the SAT, and between 28 and 32 on the ACT. The college has a strong commitment to academic excellence and diversity, and looks for students who will contribute to the intellectual and social life of the college.

Macalester has a need-blind admissions policy, which means that financial need is not a factor in the admissions decision. The college meets 100% of demonstrated financial need for all admitted students.

In order to be considered for admission, students must submit an application, transcripts, test scores, and a personal essay. Macalester also offers an optional interview, which can be a valuable way to demonstrate interest in the college and to learn more about the admissions process.
