Lehigh University

Bethlehem, PA
27 Memorial Drive West, Bethlehem, PA 18015
610-758-3000

Lehigh University is a private research university in Bethlehem, Pennsylvania. The university was founded in 1865 by Asa Packer. Lehigh is one of the nation's premier research universities, with highly ranked programs in engineering, business, and the sciences. The university is also home to a world-renowned music program.

Lehigh offers a comprehensive education that prepares students for success in a wide range of fields. The university's rigorous academic programs challenge students to think critically and develop the skills they need to succeed in their chosen careers. Lehigh's faculty are leaders in their fields, and the university provides students with ample opportunities to engage in research and creative work.

Lehigh is committed to providing an inclusive and welcoming environment for all members of the university community. The university offers a wide range of resources and support services for students, faculty, and staff. Lehigh is also home to a thriving community of students from all backgrounds.

Lehigh University is a dynamic and vibrant community that offers something for everyone. The university is dedicated to providing an excellent education and preparing students for a successful future.

Admission Requirements

Lehigh University is a private research university in Bethlehem, Pennsylvania. It was founded in 1865 by Asa Packer. The university has four colleges: the P.C. Rossin College of Engineering and Applied Science, the College of Arts and Sciences, the College of Business and Economics, and the College of Education. The university offers bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees in a variety of disciplines.

To be admitted to Lehigh University, students must submit an application, transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a non-refundable application fee. The admissions committee will review the applicant's academic record, test scores, and personal statement to make a decision. Lehigh University is committed to admitting students who will be successful in their academic and professional careers.
