Madonna University is a private Catholic university in Livonia, Michigan, United States. The school was founded in 1937 by the Sisters of the Holy Cross as a two-year women's college. It became a four-year university in 1959 and began offering graduate degrees in 1974. The school is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. The university's sports teams are known as the Crusaders and compete in the National Association of Intercollegiate Athletics as a member of the Wolverine-Hoosier Athletic Conference.

Madonna University is a private Catholic university in Livonia, Michigan. The university was founded in 1937 by the Felician Sisters. The university has an undergraduate enrollment of 2,841 and a graduate enrollment of 1,145. The university offers over 70 undergraduate majors and 20 graduate programs. The university is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission.

Admission to Madonna University is selective. The university has an acceptance rate of 62%. The average GPA of incoming freshmen is 3.4. The average SAT score is 1060. The average ACT score is 21. The university does not require the SAT or ACT for admission. However, the university does require high school transcripts and a minimum GPA of 2.5 for admission. The university also requires letters of recommendation, a personal essay, and an interview for admission.
