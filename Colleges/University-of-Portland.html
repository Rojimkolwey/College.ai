The University of Portland is a private university located in Portland, Oregon. Founded in 1901, UP has an undergraduate enrollment of 4,144 students and a graduate enrollment of 1,327. The university is affiliated with the Congregation of Holy Cross, and its campus is located on a bluff overlooking the Willamette River. UP is ranked #3 in the West for Regional Universities by U.S. News & World Report and is one of 28 member institutions of the Association of Jesuit Colleges and Universities. The university offers bachelor's degrees in 50 majors, as well as master's, doctoral, and professional degrees.

The University of Portland is committed to providing an excellent education rooted in the liberal arts and Catholic faith. The university offers a rigorous academic experience that prepares students for success in their chosen careers. UP's beautiful campus, located on a bluff overlooking the Willamette River, is home to a diverse community of students, faculty, and staff. The university is dedicated to creating an inclusive environment that values all members of the community.

UP's 50 majors allow students to pursue their passions and prepare for their future careers. The university offers a variety of programs to support students' academic and personal success. The Center for Career Development helps students explore their career options and connect with employers. The Office of Student Financial Aid provides assistance to students who need help paying for their education. UP's Counseling Center offers support to students who are struggling with personal or academic issues.

The University of Portland is committed to serving the community. The university offers a variety of service-learning opportunities that allow students to apply their knowledge to real-world problems. UP's Community Engagement Center connects students with local organizations that need volunteers. The center also offers programs that help students learn about social justice issues.

The University of Portland is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities. The university's nursing program is accredited by the Commission on Collegiate Nursing Education.

Admission Requirements

The University of Portland is a private university located in Portland, Oregon. Founded in 1901, UP has an undergraduate enrollment of 4,144 students. The university is affiliated with the Congregation of Holy Cross, and its motto is "Educating Hearts and Minds for a Better World."

UP's campus is located on a bluff overlooking the Willamette River. The university is made up of five schools: the School of Education, the School of Nursing, the Shiley School of Engineering, the School of Business Administration, and the College of Arts and Sciences.

The admissions process at UP is competitive. For fall 2019, the university received 11,874 applications and accepted 4,874 (41%). Of those accepted, 1,606 (33%) chose to enroll. The average GPA of incoming freshmen was 3.72.

To be considered for admission, applicants must submit an application, high school transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a non-refundable application fee. UP does not require letters of recommendation or an essay.

The university offers a number of scholarships and financial aid programs to help students pay for their education. In 2018-19, 97% of UP students received some form of financial aid.

UP is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities. The university is also a member of the Association of Jesuit Colleges and Universities and the National Association of Independent Colleges and Universities.
