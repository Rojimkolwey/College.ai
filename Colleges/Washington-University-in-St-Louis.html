Washington University in St Louis

Saint Louis, MO
One Brookings Drive, Saint Louis, MO 63130-4899
314-935-5000

Washington University in St. Louis is a private research university located in St. Louis, Missouri. The university is made up of seven schools and colleges, including the Sam Fox School of Design & Visual Arts, the Olin Business School, the School of Engineering & Applied Science, and the Brown School of Social Work. Washington University is a member of the Association of American Universities and is ranked 14th in the nation by U.S. News & World Report. The university was founded in 1853 by Wayman Crow and George Washington Cable and is named after George Washington. The university has an endowment of $7.8 billion and employs over 4,000 faculty members. Washington University is home to a number of research centers and institutes, including the Center for the Study of Race, Ethnicity, and Gender, the Center for the Study of Religion, and the Institute for Public Health.

Admission Requirements

In order to be admitted to Washington University in St. Louis, you will need to submit an application, high school transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a personal essay. The admissions committee will also consider your extracurricular activities, letters of recommendation, and demonstrated interest in the university. There is no minimum GPA or test score requirement for admission, but the average GPA of admitted students is 3.7 and the average SAT score is 1370. If you are applying for early decision, you will need to submit your application by November 1. For regular decision, the deadline is January 1.
