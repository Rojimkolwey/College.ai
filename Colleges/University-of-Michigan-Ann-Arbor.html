The University of Michigan-Ann Arbor is one of the top public research universities in the United States. Located in Ann Arbor, Michigan, the university has a large and diverse student body, with students from all 50 states and more than 100 countries. The university is home to 19 schools and colleges, and offers more than 200 undergraduate and graduate programs. The university is also a leading research institution, with more than $1.5 billion in research funding annually. The university's research is conducted in a variety of areas, including the life sciences, engineering, and the social sciences. The University of Michigan-Ann Arbor is also home to a number of cultural and artistic organizations, including the Michigan Opera Theater, the Michigan Theater, and the University Musical Society.

Admission Requirements

The University of Michigan-Ann Arbor is a public research university located in Ann Arbor, Michigan. The university is Michigan's oldest, largest, and most selective university, and was founded in 1817. The university is a member of the Association of American Universities.

The University of Michigan-Ann Arbor has an acceptance rate of 27.8%. The university is ranked #3 in National Universities and #29 in Best Value Schools. The university has an average SAT score of 1390 and an average ACT score of 32. The middle 50% of SAT scores for admitted students range from 1330-1460, and the middle 50% of ACT scores range from 29-34.

To be eligible for admission to the University of Michigan-Ann Arbor, students must complete a secondary school diploma or equivalent, and must have taken the SAT or ACT. The university does not have a preference for either test, and will consider the highest score from either test. The university also requires two SAT Subject Tests, one in Math and one in either Science or a foreign language.

The University of Michigan-Ann Arbor is need-blind for domestic students and offers a holistic admissions process. The university considers academic achievement, extracurricular involvement, leadership, service, and personal characteristics when making admissions decisions. The university also offers a variety of merit-based scholarships and need-based financial aid.
