Minnesota Universities and Colleges


Minnesota is home to a wide variety of colleges and universities, ranging from small private schools to large public institutions. The University of Minnesota is the state’s largest and most renowned university, with five campuses across the state and a wide variety of degree programs. It is a member of the Big Ten Conference and has a strong reputation in a range of disciplines, including engineering, medicine, and law.

Minnesota State University is another large public university system, with seven campuses across the state. It offers undergraduate and graduate degrees in a range of fields, including business, education, and the sciences.

St. Olaf College is a small, private liberal arts college located in Northfield, Minnesota. It has a strong reputation for its rigorous academics and is known for its strong emphasis on the liberal arts.

Macalester College is a small, private liberal arts college located in St. Paul, Minnesota. It has a strong reputation for its rigorous academics and its commitment to social justice.

The University of St. Thomas is a private, Catholic university located in St. Paul. It offers undergraduate, graduate, and professional degrees in a range of fields, including business, education, and the sciences.

The University of Minnesota-Duluth is a public university located in Duluth. It offers a range of undergraduate and graduate degree programs in a variety of fields, including business, engineering, and the sciences.

Minnesota also has a large number of community colleges, which offer associate’s degrees and certificates in a range of fields. These include Anoka-Ramsey Community College, North Hennepin Community College, and Minneapolis Community and Technical College.

Minnesota is also home to a number of specialized schools, such as the Minnesota School of Business, the Minnesota School of Cosmetology, and the Minnesota School of Professional Psychology.

Overall, Minnesota has a wide variety of colleges and universities, ranging from large public institutions to small private schools. Whatever your educational goals may be, you can find a college or university in Minnesota that will meet your needs.
