Virginia Universities and Colleges


Located in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States, Virginia is home to many prestigious colleges and universities, ranging from small liberal arts schools to large public research institutions. Virginia’s public colleges and universities are part of the Virginia Community College System, and include the University of Virginia, Virginia Tech, Old Dominion University, Virginia Commonwealth University, and George Mason University. Virginia also boasts a number of private universities, such as the College of William and Mary, Washington and Lee University, and the University of Richmond.

The University of Virginia is the oldest university in the state, founded by Thomas Jefferson in 1819. It is ranked among the top public universities in the nation and is home to the prestigious Darden School of Business. Virginia Tech is a large public research university and one of the largest universities in the state. It offers a wide range of degree programs and is known for its engineering and science programs. Old Dominion University is a public research university located in Norfolk, Virginia. It is home to the Batten College of Engineering and Technology, and offers a variety of degree programs.

Virginia Commonwealth University is a public research university located in Richmond. It is home to the School of Business and the School of Medicine, and offers a variety of degree programs. George Mason University is a public research university located in Fairfax, Virginia. It is home to the School of Business and the School of Law, and offers a variety of degree programs.

The College of William and Mary is the second oldest college in the United States, founded in 1693. It is a small liberal arts college with a strong academic reputation. Washington and Lee University is a private university located in Lexington, Virginia. It is home to the School of Law and the Williams School of Commerce, and offers a variety of degree programs. The University of Richmond is a private university located in Richmond, Virginia. It is home to the Robins School of Business, and offers a variety of degree programs.

Virginia’s colleges and universities offer a wide range of degree programs and are highly ranked in the nation. Whether you’re looking for a large public research university or a small liberal arts college, Virginia has something for everyone.
