The University of Montana is a public research university in Missoula, Montana. Founded in 1893, the university is the state's flagship institution and offers bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees in a wide range of disciplines. The university is a member of the Montana University System and is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities. The University of Montana is home to over 11,000 students from across the United States and more than 80 countries. The university offers a variety of academic programs, including liberal arts, business, education, law, and sciences. The University of Montana is also home to a number of research institutes and centers, including the Montana Bureau of Mines and Geology, the Montana Cooperative Wildlife Research Unit, and the Montana Space Grant Consortium. The University of Montana is a land-grant, sea-grant, and space-grant institution and is classified as an R1 Doctoral University by the Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education.
