Colorado College

Colorado Springs, CO
14. E Cache La Poudre St., Colorado Springs, CO 80903
719-389-6000

Colorado College is a small, private liberal arts college in Colorado Springs, Colorado. The college was founded in 1874 by Thomas Nelson Haskell. It is one of the few colleges in the United States that follows a block plan, in which students take one class at a time for three-and-a-half weeks. This allows students to immerse themselves in their coursework and explore other interests outside of the classroom.

Colorado College is ranked as one of the top liberal arts colleges in the country. It is known for its rigorous academic program, diverse student body, and beautiful campus. The college has a strong commitment to sustainability and is a leader in environmental stewardship.

Colorado College offers a wide range of undergraduate programs, including arts and sciences, business, engineering, and health sciences. The college also has a highly respected graduate program in education. Students can choose to study abroad or participate in service-learning opportunities. Colorado College is committed to preparing students for success in their careers and in their lives.

Admission Requirements

Colorado College is a highly selective liberal arts college located in Colorado Springs, Colorado. The college offers a unique curriculum that allows students to take one course at a time in an intensive, block-style format. As a result, Colorado College is able to provide a rigorous and challenging academic experience that is also highly flexible and individualized.

In order to be considered for admission to Colorado College, students must submit an application through the Common Application or Coalition Application. Along with the application, students must submit high school transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and two letters of recommendation. Colorado College also requires all applicants to submit a personal essay.

The admissions committee at Colorado College takes a holistic approach to reviewing applications, and considers a variety of factors when making admissions decisions. These factors include academic achievement, test scores, letters of recommendation, the personal essay, and extracurricular involvement.

Colorado College is committed to enrolling a diverse and talented student body, and offers a number of need-based and merit-based scholarships and financial aid programs. For students who are interested in attending Colorado College, the admissions process is highly competitive. However, the admissions committee looks beyond test scores and grades to identify students who will thrive in the unique academic environment at Colorado College.
