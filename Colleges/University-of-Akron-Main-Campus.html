The University of Akron Main Campus, located in Akron, Ohio, is a large public research university with a diverse array of programs and majors. The school was founded in 1870 as a polytechnic institute and has since grown into a comprehensive university with 11 colleges and schools. The university is known for its strong engineering and business programs, as well as its nationally ranked rubber and polymer science programs. Admission Requirements

The University of Akron Main Campus is a public research university located in Akron, Ohio, United States. The university is part of the University System of Ohio. As a comprehensive research-intensive university, it offers bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees in over 200 fields of study.

The university is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. The Main Campus is also home to a branch of the University of Akron School of Law and the Akron-based Ohio Center for the Book.

The University of Akron has an open admissions policy, meaning that any student who has graduated from high school or earned a GED is eligible to apply for admission. The university does not require applicants to submit SAT or ACT scores, but does recommend that students submit their scores if they have them.

Once an applicant has been admitted to the university, they will need to complete a course registration form and pay a registration fee in order to enroll in classes. The university offers a variety of financial aid options to help students cover the cost of tuition, room and board, and other expenses.
