Saint Louis University is a private research university located in St. Louis, Missouri. Founded in 1818 by the Most Reverend Louis William Valentine Dubourg, Bishop of Louisiana and the Floridas, it is the oldest university west of the Mississippi River and the second-oldest Jesuit university in the United States. It is one of 28 member institutions of the Association of Jesuit Colleges and Universities. The university is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission.

Saint Louis University's undergraduate programs are divided into four colleges: the College of Arts and Sciences, the Richard A. Chaifetz School of Business, the College of Nursing and Health Sciences, and the Parks College of Engineering, Aviation and Technology. The School of Law is the university's only graduate school.

Admission to Saint Louis University is selective, with an acceptance rate of 70%. The average SAT score for incoming freshmen is 1210-1340. The average ACT score is 27-32. The middle 50% of incoming freshmen have a GPA of 3.5-3.9.

To be considered for admission, applicants must submit an application, high school transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a non-refundable application fee. International students must also submit proof of English proficiency. Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis, and decisions are typically rendered within two weeks of receipt of all required materials.
