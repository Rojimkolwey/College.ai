Oregon Institute of Technology (Oregon Tech) is a public university in Klamath Falls, Oregon. The university offers bachelor's and master's degrees in a variety of STEM disciplines, as well as professional degrees in health technologies and engineering management.

Oregon Tech was founded in 1947 as the Oregon Vocational School to provide training in the emerging field of electronics. In 1964, the school was renamed Oregon Institute of Technology and began offering bachelor's degrees. The university has grown steadily in the ensuing years, adding programs and facilities to meet the needs of its students and the changing workforce.

Today, Oregon Tech is a comprehensive university with an enrollment of over 4,000 students. The university offers over 50 degree programs, including engineering, health technologies, business, and computer science. Oregon Tech is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities, and its engineering programs are accredited by the Accreditation Board for Engineering and Technology (ABET).

Oregon Tech is committed to providing an excellent education to its students. The university offers small class sizes, hands-on learning opportunities, and a supportive community. Oregon Tech faculty are experienced professionals who are dedicated to teaching and research. The university provides ample opportunities for students to get involved in extracurricular activities, including clubs, sports, and student government.

Oregon Tech is located in the beautiful city of Klamath Falls, Oregon. The city is situated on the shores of Upper Klamath Lake and surrounded by mountains. Klamath Falls is a vibrant community with a variety of shops, restaurants, and cultural activities.

Admission Requirements

Oregon Institute of Technology (Oregon Tech) is a public university in Oregon with its main campus in Klamath Falls and additional campuses in Wilsonville and Salem. Oregon Tech is the only polytechnic university in the Pacific Northwest.

Oregon Tech offers bachelor's and master's degrees in engineering, health technologies, management, communication, and applied sciences. The university has an enrollment of about 4,200 students.

Oregon Tech is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities. The university's engineering programs are accredited by the Accreditation Board for Engineering and Technology (ABET).

To be admitted to Oregon Tech, applicants must submit an application, transcripts, test scores, and a non-refundable application fee. Applicants must also meet the university's English proficiency requirements.

Oregon Tech uses a holistic review process when considering applicants for admission. The university looks at each applicant's academic record, test scores, essays, letters of recommendation, and extracurricular activities when making admission decisions.

Oregon Tech has a rolling admission policy, which means that applications are reviewed and decisions are made on a rolling basis. The university recommends that applicants submit their materials as early as possible to ensure the best chance of admission.
