The University of Missouri-Columbia (Mizzou), is a public research university located in Columbia, Missouri, United States. Founded in 1839, it is the flagship campus of the University of Missouri System, and the largest university in Missouri. It is one of the 34 public universities that are members of the Association of American Universities. There are more than 300,000 MU alumni living worldwide.

Mizzou is a member of the Southeastern Conference and is the only public university in Missouri with a member of the Association of American Universities. The university also has the largest number of students enrolled in online courses among public universities in the United States.

The University of Missouri was created by an act of the Missouri General Assembly in 1839. The university was first located in St. Louis, but moved to Columbia in 1843. The university now encompasses 1,262 acres (5.1 km2) in Columbia, including the main campus, the University of Missouri Research Reactor Center, andMU Health Care.

The University of Missouri is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. Mizzou has 19 schools and colleges, including the Sinclair School of Nursing, the Missouri School of Journalism, the College of Education, the Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communication, and the School of Medicine.

The University of Missouri has a strong research program. In FY 2016, MU's research expenditures totaled $251 million, ranking it 64th among public universities in the United States. The university is home to the University of Missouri Health Care system, which includes five hospitals and a children's hospital.

Mizzou athletics compete in the NCAA's Division I and are members of the Southeastern Conference. The university fields teams in 10 men's and 11 women's sports. The men's basketball team has won the most conference championships with 13, and the women's track and field team has won the most national championships with 17.

The University of Missouri is committed to a diverse and inclusive campus community. The university offers a variety of programs and resources to support students, faculty, and staff from diverse backgrounds. Mizzou also has a strong international presence, with more than 4,000 international students from more than 100 countries enrolled at the university.

The University of Missouri-Columbia is a public research university located in Columbia, Missouri. Founded in 1839, it is the flagship campus of the University of Missouri System and the largest university in the state. It is one of the 34 public universities that are members of the Association of American Universities.

The university offers more than 300 degree programs in 21 academic colleges and schools. The university's research expenditures totaled $261 million in FY 2018. The campus comprises 1,262 acres (510 ha), including the main academic campus, a South Campus, and a Downtown Campus.

The university employs more than 10,000 faculty and staff, and has an enrollment of over 30,000 students, making it the fourth-largest university in the state. The university is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission.

In order to be admitted into the University of Missouri-Columbia, applicants must submit an application, transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a non-refundable application fee. The university uses a holistic review process when considering applicants for admission. The admissions committee looks at a variety of factors when making admissions decisions, including academic achievement, leadership experience, extracurricular involvement, and community service.

The University of Missouri-Columbia is committed to enrolling a diverse student body and offers a variety of scholarships and financial aid programs to make college affordable for all students. For more information about admission requirements and the application process, please visit the university's website.
