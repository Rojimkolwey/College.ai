Bridgewater College

Bridgewater, VA
402 E College St, Bridgewater, VA 22812-1599
540-828-8000

Founded in 1880, Bridgewater College is a private, four-year liberal arts college located in the Central Shenandoah Valley of Virginia. The college has approximately 1,850 undergraduate students and offers more than 50 majors, minors and special programs. The college is affiliated with the Church of the Brethren.

Bridgewater College was founded by John D. Heatwole, a local farmer, and D.J. Miller, a local preacher. The two men wanted to provide higher education opportunities for the young people of the Shenandoah Valley. The college opened its doors in September 1880 with 12 students.

Today, Bridgewater College is a thriving community of scholars and learners. The college is committed to providing an excellent liberal arts education. The faculty is dedicated to teaching and the students are engaged in their learning. The college offers a variety of academic programs and extracurricular activities.

The college is located in the town of Bridgewater, Virginia. The town is situated in the Shenandoah Valley, between the Blue Ridge and Allegheny Mountains. The college campus is just a short walk from downtown Bridgewater.

Bridgewater College is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges. The college is also a member of the Association of American Colleges and Universities, the Council of Independent Colleges, and the Virginia Association of Independent Colleges.

Admission Requirements

Bridgewater College is a small, private, liberal arts college located in the Central Shenandoah Valley of Virginia. The college was founded in 1880 by Swedish immigrants and is affiliated with the United Methodist Church. The college offers bachelor's degrees in over 40 majors and minors, as well as a number of graduate programs.

Admission to Bridgewater College is selective and competitive. The college uses a holistic admissions process, taking into account a student's academic record, test scores, extracurricular activities, and personal essay. The college also considers a student's "fit" with the Bridgewater community when making admissions decisions.

Bridgewater College has a strong commitment to academic excellence and personal growth. The college provides a supportive and challenging environment that helps students develop the skills and knowledge they need to succeed in their chosen field.
