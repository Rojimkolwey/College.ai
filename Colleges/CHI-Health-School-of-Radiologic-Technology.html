CHI Health School of Radiologic Technology

Omaha, NE
6901 North 72nd St, Omaha, NE 68122
402-572-3654

CHI Health School of Radiologic Technology is a two-year program that trains students to become radiologic technologists. The program is accredited by the Joint Review Committee on Education in Radiologic Technology and is affiliated with CHI Health, a regional healthcare system in Nebraska and Iowa. The program curriculum includes coursework in anatomy, physiology, medical terminology, imaging physics, and radiographic procedures. clinical education is provided at CHI Health hospitals and clinics, and students must complete a minimum of 1,040 hours of clinical training. Upon completion of the program, graduates are eligible to take the American Registry of Radiologic Technologists (ARRT) examination to become certified and registered radiologic technologists.

Admission Requirements

The CHI Health School of Radiologic Technology requires that applicants have a high school diploma or equivalent, and must have completed high school level courses in English, algebra, and biology. In addition, applicants must take and pass a health occupations aptitude examination, as well as a criminal background check. Once accepted into the program, students must complete an intensive, full-time, 24-month course of study, which includes classroom, laboratory, and clinical instruction. Upon successful completion of the program, graduates are eligible to take the national certification examination to become a Registered Radiologic Technologist.
