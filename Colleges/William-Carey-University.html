William Carey University is a private, Christian university located in Hattiesburg, Mississippi, United States. The university is affiliated with the Mississippi Baptist Convention and offers bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees in a variety of disciplines. Founded in 1892 as Mississippi Woman's College, the school became William Carey College in 1934 and William Carey University in 2006. The university is named after William Carey, an English Baptist missionary and pioneer of the modern missionary movement.

The university enrolls approximately 3,500 students from over 40 states and 30 countries. The campus covers over 100 acres and includes several buildings, including a library, a chapel, and a recreation center. The school's colors are green and gold, and its mascot is the Crusader.

The university is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges and the Mississippi Department of Education. It is also a member of the Mississippi Association of Colleges and Universities and the Council for Christian Colleges and Universities.

William Carey University offers a variety of undergraduate and graduate programs. Undergraduate programs include business, education, liberal arts, nursing, and science. Graduate programs include business, counseling, education, nursing, and science. The university also offers a number of online programs.

The university is committed to providing a quality education at an affordable price. In addition to offering scholarships and financial aid, the university has a number of payment plans available to make tuition more affordable.

William Carey University is dedicated to preparing students for success in their careers and in their personal lives. The university offers a number of services and resources to help students succeed, including career counseling, tutoring, and mentoring. The university also has a strong commitment to service and offers a number of service-learning opportunities.
