United Theological Seminary

Dayton, OH
4501 Denlinger Rd., Dayton, OH 45426-2308
937-529-2201

United Theological Seminary is a private, ecumenical graduate school of theology located in Dayton, Ohio. It is affiliated with the United Methodist Church. The seminary was founded in 1871 and is one of 13 United Methodist seminaries in the United States. The seminary offers masters and doctoral degrees in a variety of theological disciplines. The seminary's library is one of the largest theological libraries in the country. The seminary is also home to the Center for Pastoral Leadership, which offers a variety of resources and programs for clergy and lay leaders.

Admission Requirements

United Theological Seminary is a graduate school that offers masters and doctoral degrees in theology and ministry. The seminary is located in Dayton, Ohio, and is affiliated with the United Methodist Church.

Admission to United Theological Seminary is open to any student who holds a bachelor's degree from an accredited institution. There are no specific course requirements for admission, but all applicants must submit transcripts, a personal essay, and three letters of recommendation.

The seminary offers a variety of degree programs, including Master of Divinity, Master of Arts in Theological Studies, and Doctor of Ministry. Coursework for all programs is grounded in the liberal arts and emphasizes the study of Scripture, theology, and ministry.

United Theological Seminary is committed to preparing students for ministry in the church and the world. The seminary offers a variety of resources and opportunities for students to develop their skills and knowledge, including internships, practicums, and field education.
