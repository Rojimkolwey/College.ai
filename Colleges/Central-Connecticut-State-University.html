Central Connecticut State University is located in New Britain, Connecticut and is a public university that was founded in 1849. The university is part of the Connecticut State University System and has an enrollment of over 12,000 students. The university offers associate's, bachelor's, and master's degrees in a variety of disciplines.

Central Connecticut State University is accredited by the New England Association of Schools and Colleges. The university's athletic teams compete in the NCAA Division I and are members of the Northeast Conference. The university's mascot is the Blue Devil and its colors are blue and white.

Central Connecticut State University is committed to providing a high-quality education to its students. The university offers a variety of programs and services to help students succeed. The university has a strong focus on teaching and research and is home to a number of research centers and institutes. The university is also committed to serving the community and offers a number of outreach programs.

Admission Requirements

Central Connecticut State University (CCSU) is a public university in New Britain, Connecticut. Founded in 1849 as the State Normal School, it is the oldest public university in Connecticut. CCSU offers bachelor's and master's degrees in more than 100 majors and concentrations, and doctorates in educational leadership, physical therapy, and nursing.

To be eligible for admission to CCSU, applicants must have a high school diploma or equivalent. Applicants must also submit official transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a non-refundable application fee. International students must also submit proof of English proficiency.

Once all required materials have been received, the Office of Admissions will review the application and make a decision. Applicants who are offered admission will be required to submit a non-refundable deposit to secure their spot in the incoming class.
