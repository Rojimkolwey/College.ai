Northeast Ohio Medical University (NEOMED) is a public medical school in Rootstown, Ohio. NEOMED is part of the University System of Ohio and offers the Doctor of Medicine (MD) and Master of Science in Pharmacy (MSP) degrees. NEOMED also has a partnership with Kent State University which offers the Doctor of Pharmacy (PharmD) degree.

NEOMED was established in 1973 and is one of eight public medical schools in Ohio. The school has a mission to "improve the health of the people of Ohio and the nation by educating exemplary physicians and pharmacists, by providing cutting-edge research and by serving the community through the provision of patient care." NEOMED is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and the Liaison Committee on Medical Education.

The school offers a unique curriculum that includes a year-long Introduction to Clinical Medicine course which all students must complete before beginning their clinical rotations. NEOMED also has a rural track program which provides students with the opportunity to complete their clinical rotations in rural communities.

NEOMED is home to the Center for Excellence in Medical Education which provides support and resources for faculty and students. The Center for Excellence in Medical Education also offers a variety of educational programs and workshops.

The school has a strong commitment to research and offers a variety of research opportunities for students. NEOMED is home to the Center for Clinical and Translational Science which is a partnership between NEOMED, the Cleveland Clinic, and Summa Health System. The Center for Clinical and Translational Science supports clinical and translational research projects.

NEOMED is also home to the Ohio Center for Health Data and Analytics. The Ohio Center for Health Data and Analytics is a partnership between NEOMED and the Ohio Department of Health. The Ohio Center for Health Data and Analytics provides data and analytics support for NEOMED and the Ohio Department of Health.

NEOMED is located on a 350-acre campus in Rootstown, Ohio. The school is situated in a rural area and is adjacent to the Northeast Ohio Medical University Hospital.

Northeast Ohio Medical University (NEOMED) is a community-based, public medical school in Rootstown, Ohio. NEOMED is one of six public colleges of medicine in Ohio and is affiliated with several universities, including the University of Akron, Kent State University, Youngstown State University and Northeast Ohio Consortium for Higher Education. NEOMED is also a part of the Ohio State University System.

The medical school offers a Doctor of Medicine (MD) degree, as well as joint degrees with a Master of Business Administration (MBA), a Master of Public Health (MPH) and a PhD in medical science. NEOMED also offers residency and fellowship programs.

Admission to NEOMED is highly competitive. For the class of 2025, the medical school received 8,062 applications and offered interviews to 1,092 applicants. Of those interviewed, 496 students were offered admission. The admitted students had an average GPA of 3.8 and an average MCAT score of 517.

To be eligible for admission to NEOMED, applicants must have a bachelor's degree from an accredited institution, a minimum GPA of 3.2 and a minimum MCAT score of 500. In addition, applicants must complete prerequisite coursework in biology, chemistry, physics, math and English.

NEOMED uses a holistic review process when considering applicants for admission. The medical school looks at factors such as academic achievement, MCAT scores, letters of recommendation, essays, work and volunteer experiences, and extracurricular activities. NEOMED also offers a conditional admission program for students who do not meet the minimum GPA or MCAT requirements but show promise for success in medical school.

Once admitted, students at NEOMED can expect a rigorous academic experience. The medical school curriculum is designed to prepare students for a career in medicine. Students will take courses in the basic sciences, such as anatomy, physiology and biochemistry, as well as courses in the clinical sciences, such as medicine, surgery and pediatrics. In addition, students will participate in clerkships and clinical rotations, which will provide them with hands-on experience in patient care.

After completing the four-year MD program, graduates of NEOMED will be eligible to take the United States Medical Licensing Examination (USMLE) and begin their careers as physicians.
