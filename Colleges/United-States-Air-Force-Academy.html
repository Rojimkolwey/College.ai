United States Air Force Academy

USAF Academy, CO
2304 Cadet Drive, Suite 3800, USAF Academy, CO 80840-5002
719-333-1818

The United States Air Force Academy (USAF Academy, or simply the Academy), is a military academy for officer candidates for the United States Air Force. Its campus is located in the western United States in Colorado, immediately north of Colorado Springs in El Paso County. The Academy's stated mission is "to educate, train, and inspire men and women to become leaders of character, motivated to lead the United States Air Force in service to our nation." It is the youngest of the five U.S. service academies, having been founded in 1954, and is also the smallest of the five with a student body of about 4,000.

The Academy is a degree-granting institution with an emphasis on engineering, mathematics, and the sciences. The majority of cadets are drawn from high schools in the western United States, although a significant number are from the eastern United States, Puerto Rico, Guam, the Virgin Islands, the Marianas, the Marshall Islands, and the Federated States of Micronesia. The Academy graduates about 1,000 cadets each year. Cadets are required to adhere to the Cadet Honor Code, which states "We will not lie, steal, or cheat, nor tolerate among us anyone who does." The Academy's motto is "Integrity First, Service Before Self, and Excellence in All We Do."

The Academy is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and currently offers bachelor's degrees in a variety of fields. The majority of graduates go on to serve as active-duty officers in the United States Air Force, although a small number of graduates have chosen to pursue careers in the private sector or public service.

Admission Requirements

All applicants must meet the age, citizenship, medical, physical, and academic requirements to be eligible for admission to the United States Air Force Academy.

Age: You must be at least 17 years old and not have passed your 23rd birthday on July 1 of the year you enter the Academy.

Citizenship: You must be a U.S. citizen to apply.

Medical: You must be medically qualified by the Academy Medical Officer.

Physical: You must meet the minimum physical standards for cadets, including the following:

-You must be able to pass the Cadet Initial Fitness Assessment (CIFA).

-You must not have any medical conditions that would limit your ability to participate in the strenuous activities required of all cadets, including but not limited to: running, swimming, and playing sports.

Academic: You must meet the Academy's academic requirements, which include the following:

-You must have a high school diploma or equivalent.

-You must have a minimum cumulative GPA of 3.0 on a 4.0 scale.

-You must have a minimum ACT composite score of 22 or a minimum SAT total score of 1070 (critical reading and math only).

-You must have completed a minimum of 17 academic units.

-You must have completed the following courses: English (4 units), math (4 units), social studies (3 units), natural science (3 units), and foreign language (2 units).
