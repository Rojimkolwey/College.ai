Purdue University-Main Campus is a public university in West Lafayette, Indiana and is the flagship campus of the Purdue University system. The university was founded in 1869 after Lafayette businessman John Purdue donated land and money to establish a college of science, technology, and agriculture in his name. Purdue is a member of the Big Ten Conference and is the largest university in Indiana, with an enrollment of over 43,000 students. The university is also home to the Purdue Boilermakers, a Division I athletic program.

Purdue University-Main Campus is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. The university offers over 200 undergraduate majors and over 70 graduate programs. Purdue is divided into 11 colleges and schools, including the College of Agriculture, the College of Engineering, the College of Liberal Arts, and the Krannert School of Management. Purdue is also home to a number of research centers and institutes, such as the Bindley Bioscience Center, the Birck Nanotechnology Center, and the Discovery Park.

Purdue University-Main Campus was ranked as the 21st best public university in the United States by U.S. News & World Report in 2020. The university is also ranked as the 75th best university in the world by Times Higher Education.

Purdue University-Main Campus is a public research university in West Lafayette, Indiana, and the flagship campus of the Purdue University system. The university was founded in 1869 after Lafayette businessman John Purdue donated land and money to establish a college of science, technology, and agriculture in his namesake city. The first classes were held in 1874, with six instructors and 39 students.

Today, Purdue enrolls over 43,000 students from all 50 states and over 130 countries. The university offers more than 200 undergraduate majors and over 70 graduate programs through its 13 colleges and schools. Purdue is a member of the Association of American Universities and is classified as an R1 Doctoral University by the Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education.

Purdue's admissions process is selective; the university received over 42,000 applications for the fall 2019 semester and had an acceptance rate of 57%. Admitted students had an average high school GPA of 3.75 and an average ACT score of 29. Purdue does not require the SAT for admission, but does consider SAT scores if submitted.

To be considered for admission, students must submit an application, high school transcripts, and ACT or SAT scores (if available). Purdue uses a holistic review process and considers each applicant's academic record, extracurricular involvement, and personal experiences when making admissions decisions.
