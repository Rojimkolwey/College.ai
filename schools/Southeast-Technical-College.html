Southeast Technical College is a two-year public college in Sioux Falls, South Dakota. It is part of the Minnesota State Colleges and Universities system. The college offers associate's degrees, diplomas, and certificates in over 50 technical and occupational fields.

Southeast Technical College was founded in 1968 as Southeast Area Vocational-Technical School. It became a community college in 1984 and was renamed Southeast Technical Institute. In 2003, the college was renamed Southeast Technical College.

The college has four campuses: the Main Campus in Sioux Falls, the Northwest Campus in Watertown, the Northeast Campus in Huron, and the Southwest Campus in Yankton.

The Main Campus in Sioux Falls is the largest campus, and it is home to the college's administrative offices. The campus has over 30 buildings, including academic buildings, student services buildings, and athletic facilities.

The Northwest Campus in Watertown is home to the college's School of Agriculture, Natural Resources, and Applied Technologies. The campus has over 20 buildings, including academic buildings, student services buildings, and agricultural facilities.

The Northeast Campus in Huron is home to the college's School of Health Sciences. The campus has over 10 buildings, including academic buildings, student services buildings, and health care facilities.

The Southwest Campus in Yankton is home to the college's School of Business and Professional Studies. The campus has over 10 buildings, including academic buildings, student services buildings, and business facilities.
