Aaniiih Nakoda College (ANC) is a small, private, two-year college located on the Fort Belknap Indian Reservation in north-central Montana. The College was founded in 1984 by the Fort Belknap Indian Community and is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities. The College offers two-year degrees in business, computer science, Early Childhood Education, and Liberal Studies, as well as one-year certificates in a variety of fields.

ANC’s mission is to provide quality higher education to American Indian students in a supportive and culturally-sensitive environment. The College is committed to helping students develop the skills and knowledge they need to succeed in their chosen field, and to become contributing members of their communities.

Since its founding, ANC has been serving the educational needs of the Fort Belknap Indian Community and the surrounding area. The College has a strong commitment to American Indian students and communities, and offers a variety of programs and services to support its students. ANC is an affordable option for higher education, with tuition and fees significantly lower than other colleges and universities.

The College has a beautiful campus, located on the banks of the Milk River. The campus includes a variety of modern facilities, including a library, computer lab, and fitness center. The College also has a strong commitment to sustainability, and is a leader in green initiatives among small colleges.

ANC is a great choice for American Indian students seeking a quality, affordable education in a supportive and culturally-sensitive environment.

Admission Requirements

Aaniiih Nakoda College (ANC) is a small, private, non-profit institution located on the Fort Belknap Indian Reservation in north-central Montana. The college is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities. Admission to ANC is open to all students who meet the general admission requirements, regardless of race, creed, color, national origin, sex, or disability.

To be eligible for admission to ANC, students must have a high school diploma or equivalent. GED scores must be at least 145 on each subject test and an overall score of 580. Students must also submit official transcripts from all colleges and universities previously attended. Admission requirements also include a minimum cumulative GPA of 2.0 on a 4.0 scale for all college-level coursework attempted.

All new students are required to complete an orientation program prior to enrolling in classes. Orientation provides an opportunity for students to learn about the college’s policies and procedures, as well as its programs and services. Additionally, students will have the opportunity to meet with an academic advisor to discuss their educational goals and develop a plan of study.
