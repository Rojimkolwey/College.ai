Michigan Technological University

Houghton, MI
1400 Townsend Drive, Houghton, MI 49931-1295
906-487-1885

Founded in 1885, Michigan Technological University is a leading public research university offering more than 120 undergraduate and graduate degree programs in science and technology, engineering, forestry, business and economics, health professions, humanities, mathematics, and social sciences. Our beautiful campus in Michigan’s Upper Peninsula overlooks the Keweenaw Waterway and is just a few miles from Lake Superior.

Michigan Tech is a comprehensive university with a student-centered focus on research. We are ranked among the nation’s top 100 public universities by U.S. News & World Report and among the top 50 public research universities by the National Science Foundation. Our students come from all 50 states and more than 60 countries.

Michigan Tech graduates are highly sought after by employers. They are well prepared to take on the challenges of an ever-changing global economy. Our alumni include astronauts, CEOs, senators, and Nobel laureates.

Michigan Tech is committed to providing an inclusive, equitable, and diverse environment for all members of our community. We offer a variety of programs and resources to support students, faculty, and staff from underrepresented groups.

Michigan Tech is a great place to learn, to work, and to live. We hope you will visit us soon.

Admission Requirements

Michigan Technological University is a public research university in Houghton, Michigan. Founded in 1885, the university offers more than 120 undergraduate and graduate degree programs in science and technology, engineering, forestry, business and economics, health professions, humanities, mathematics, and social sciences. With an enrollment of more than 7,000 students, Michigan Tech also has 400 faculty members and more than 130 staff members.

The university is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and is a member of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools. Michigan Tech is also a member of the Association of American Universities, the Association of Public and Land-grant Universities, the Council of Graduate Schools, and the Universities Research Association.

The university's main campus is located on 925 acres (3.74 km2) of land overlooking Portage Lake. The campus is home to more than 50 academic, administrative, and research buildings; the Michigan Tech Research Institute; the Rozsa Center for the Performing Arts; and the SDC. The university also owns and operates the Keweenaw Peninsula Field Station, the Houghton County Memorial Airport, and the Portage Lake Golf Course.

In order to be admitted into Michigan Tech, students must have a high school diploma or equivalent. They must also submit an application, transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a non-refundable application fee. International students must also submit a TOEFL or IELTS score.
