Shenandoah University

Winchester, VA
1460 University Dr, Winchester, VA 22601-5195
540-665-4500

Shenandoah University is a comprehensive university located in Winchester, Virginia in the United States. The university offers a broad range of undergraduate and graduate programs in the liberal arts, sciences, business, education, music, theatre, and health professions. Founded in 1875 by Dr. George F. Shultz, Shenandoah was the first private, coeducational, liberal arts college in Virginia. The university has an enrollment of approximately 4,000 students across its six schools, which include the School of Arts & Sciences, School of Business, School of Education, School of Health Professions, School of Music, and School of Theatre & Dance.

Shenandoah University is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges to award bachelor's, master's, and doctorate degrees. The university is also a member of the Association of American Colleges and Universities, the Council of Independent Colleges, and the National Association of Schools of Music.

Shenandoah's School of Arts & Sciences offers programs in the liberal arts and sciences, including majors in English, history, mathematics, and psychology. The School of Business offers programs in accounting, business administration, and economics. The School of Education offers programs in elementary education, special education, and educational leadership. The School of Health Professions offers programs in nursing, occupational therapy, physical therapy, and physician assistant studies. The School of Music offers programs in music education, music performance, and music therapy. The School of Theatre & Dance offers programs in theatre and dance.

Shenandoah University's campus is located in Winchester, Virginia, a city with a population of approximately 27,000 people. Winchester is located in the Shenandoah Valley, approximately 75 miles northwest of Washington, D.C. The university's campus is situated on 118 acres of land.

Shenandoah University's athletics teams are known as the Hornets. The university is a member of the National Collegiate Athletic Association (NCAA) Division III and the Old Dominion Athletic Conference (ODAC). The university offers a variety of varsity and intramural sports, including baseball, basketball, cross country, field hockey, football, golf, lacrosse, soccer, softball, tennis, track and field, and volleyball.

Admission Requirements

Shenandoah University is a private institution that was founded in 1875. The school has an urban campus in Winchester, Virginia. Shenandoah University offers undergraduate, graduate, and professional programs.

Undergraduate students must have a high school diploma or equivalent. They must also submit SAT or ACT scores, and a $50 application fee. Graduate students must have a bachelor's degree and submit GRE or GMAT scores. They must also submit a $50 application fee. Professional programs have different admission requirements.

Shenandoah University has a rolling admissions policy, meaning that applications are reviewed as they are received. The university does not have an application deadline, but students are encouraged to apply early.
