Connecticut Community Colleges


Connecticut is home to a variety of community colleges with diverse offerings. These colleges are part of the Connecticut State Colleges and Universities (CSCU) system, and offer associate and bachelor's degrees, certificates, diplomas, and workforce training in a variety of disciplines.

The 12 community colleges in Connecticut are: Asnuntuck Community College, Capital Community College, Gateway Community College, Housatonic Community College, Manchester Community College, Middlesex Community College, Naugatuck Valley Community College, Northwestern Connecticut Community College, Quinebaug Valley Community College, Three Rivers Community College, Tunxis Community College, and the University of Connecticut at Avery Point.

Each community college offers an array of courses and programs, including those in the areas of arts and sciences, business, health sciences, technology, and more. Many of the colleges also have special programs and services that are designed to meet the needs of the local community. These can include career services, transfer services, and tutoring.

The community colleges in Connecticut also serve as a pathway to four-year universities. Through articulation agreements, students can transfer credits from one college to another, allowing them to complete their degree with less time and expense.

The community colleges in Connecticut are committed to providing quality education at an affordable cost. Tuition at the community college level is significantly lower than at four-year universities, and students can take advantage of financial aid and scholarships to help pay for their education.

Connecticut's community colleges are an excellent choice for students who are looking for a quality education at an affordable price. With a variety of programs, services, and transfer options, the community colleges in Connecticut provide an excellent pathway to achieving your educational goals.
