Cecil College

North East, MD
One Seahawk Drive, North East, MD 21901-1900

Cecil College is a public two-year college in Cecil County, Maryland. The college offers associate's degrees, certificates, and continuing education opportunities. Founded in 1968, Cecil College is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education. The college's main campus is located in North East, Maryland, with satellite campuses in Elkton and Port Deposit.

Cecil College offers a variety of academic programs to its students. The college's most popular programs include business, health sciences, and liberal arts. Cecil College also offers a number of unique programs, such as its aviation program and its culinary arts program.

Cecil College is committed to providing its students with a quality education at an affordable price. The college offers a variety of financial aid options to its students, including scholarships, grants, and loans. Cecil College also offers a payment plan option for its students.

Cecil College is a great choice for students who are looking for a quality education at an affordable price. The college offers a variety of academic programs, financial aid options, and unique opportunities.

Admission Requirements

Cecil College strives to make the admissions process clear and straightforward for all students. The College has an open admissions policy, meaning that any student who meets the minimum requirements for admission will be accepted into the College.

The minimum requirements for admission to Cecil College are as follows:

-A completed application for admission

-A non-refundable $35 application fee

-Official high school transcripts or GED scores

-Official college transcripts (if applicable)

-SAT or ACT scores (if applicable)

In addition to the minimum requirements for admission, Cecil College also has a few special admissions programs in place. These programs include:

-The Early College Admission Program, which allows high school students to take college courses while still in high school

-The Adult Degree Completion Program, which is designed for adults who have some college credits but did not complete their degree

-The International Student Admission Program, which is for students who are not citizens or permanent residents of the United States

If you have any questions about the admissions process at Cecil College, please contact the Office of Admissions.
