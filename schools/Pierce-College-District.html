Pierce College District

Lakewood, WA
9401 Farwest Dr SW, Lakewood, WA 98498-1999
253-964-6500

Pierce College District is a public community college district located in Los Angeles County, California. The district serves the communities of Woodland Hills, West Hills, Canoga Park, Chatsworth, Granada Hills, Northridge, Porter Ranch, and Reseda. The district has an enrollment of over 18,000 students.

Pierce College District was established in 1947. The district's first college, Pierce College, was founded in 1947 as an extension of Los Angeles City College. The college was originally located in the San Fernando Valley community of Woodland Hills. The college moved to its current location in the West Hills community of Los Angeles in 1954.

Pierce College District offers associate's degrees and certificate programs in a variety of academic and vocational disciplines. The district's colleges also offer a number of community education and adult education programs.

Pierce College District is governed by a seven-member Board of Trustees. The Board of Trustees is responsible for setting district policy and overseeing the district's budget. The district is headquartered in the Woodland Hills community of Los Angeles.

Admission Requirements

Pierce College District has an open admissions policy, meaning that any student who meets the minimum requirements for admission will be accepted into the college. The minimum requirements for admission are as follows:

1. The applicant must be a high school graduate or have a GED.

2. The applicant must have completed the required college preparatory courses.

3. The applicant must have a minimum cumulative GPA of 2.0.

4. The applicant must submit official transcripts from all colleges and universities previously attended.

5. The applicant must submit a completed application for admission.

6. The applicant must submit a non-refundable application fee.

7. The applicant must complete an assessment test, if required.

8. The applicant must meet any additional requirements specified by the program to which they are applying.

Upon meeting the minimum requirements for admission, students will be granted access to registration for classes. It is important to note that meeting the minimum requirements for admission does not guarantee admission into a specific program or course of study. Some programs may have additional requirements or may be competitive in nature. In these cases, meeting the minimum requirements for admission does not guarantee admission into the program.
