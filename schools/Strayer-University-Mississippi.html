Strayer University-Mississippi

Jackson, MS
100 East Capitol Street Suite 100, Jackson, MS 39201
877-445-7180

Strayer University-Mississippi is a four-year, private, non-profit university located in Jackson, Mississippi. The university is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges (SACSCOC) to award bachelor's and master's degrees. The university is a member of the National Association of Independent Colleges and Universities (NAICU) and the Association of American Colleges and Universities (AAC&U).

Strayer University-Mississippi offers a variety of undergraduate and graduate programs through its five colleges: the College of Business, the College of Education, the College of Health Sciences, the College of Information Technology, and the College of Liberal Arts. The university also offers a Doctor of Education (Ed.D.) program.

The university's campus is located in the historic Belhaven neighborhood of Jackson. The campus includes several buildings, including the E.E. Cooley Building, which houses the university's administrative offices, classrooms, and laboratories; the Robert E. Smith Library; and the George E. Ohrstrom, Jr. Administration Building.

Admission Requirements

Strayer University-Mississippi is a regionally accredited institution. The admissions requirements are as follows:

A high school diploma or equivalent is required for admission.

Official transcripts from all colleges and universities previously attended must be submitted.

Applicants must have a minimum cumulative GPA of 2.0 on a 4.0 scale for all coursework attempted.

All applicants must complete an online application and submit a non-refundable $50 application fee.

International applicants must submit additional documentation, including proof of English proficiency.

Strayer University-Mississippi offers rolling admissions, meaning that applications are reviewed and decisions are made on a rolling basis throughout the year.
