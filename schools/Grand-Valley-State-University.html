Grand Valley State University

Allendale, MI
1 Campus Dr, Allendale, MI 49401-9403
616-331-2020

Grand Valley State University, located in Allendale, Michigan, is a public university founded in 1960. The university has an enrollment of over 25,000 students and offers over 200 programs of study. The university is organized into eight colleges: the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences, the College of Community and Public Service, the College of Education, the College of Business, the College of Engineering and Computing, the College of Health Professions, the College of Music, and the College of Nursing. The university is known for its strong academic programs, its commitment to sustainability, and its beautiful campus. The university has a strong reputation for its business, engineering, and education programs, and its nursing program is ranked among the best in the country. The university is also home to a thriving Greek life community, with over 50 fraternities and sororities. Grand Valley State University is a great choice for students looking for a well-rounded college experience.

Admission Requirements

Grand Valley State University is a public university located in Allendale, Michigan. The university was established in 1960 and its main campus is situated on 1,322 acres of land.
