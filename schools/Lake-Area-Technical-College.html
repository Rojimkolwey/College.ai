Lake Area Technical College

Watertown, SD
1201 Arrow Avenue, Watertown, SD 57201-0730
605-882-5284

Lake Area Technical College is a two-year technical college located in Watertown, South Dakota, United States. It is the oldest and largest technical college in the state of South Dakota. The college offers over 50 programs in a variety of fields, including agriculture, automotive technology, business, construction, cosmetology, culinary arts, health sciences, manufacturing, and more.

The college was founded in 1965 as Lake Area Vocational-Technical Institute. It became Lake Area Technical Institute in 1974 and Lake Area Technical College in 1987. The college has grown significantly since its founding, adding new programs and facilities over the years.

Today, the college serves over 4,000 students from across the state of South Dakota and beyond. The college is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and is a member of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools.

The college has a strong commitment to student success, offering a variety of services and resources to help students succeed in their programs and in their careers. The college also offers a variety of extracurricular activities and clubs, allowing students to get involved on campus and meet new friends.

Whether you're looking for a two-year degree, a certificate program, or just a few courses to upgrade your skills, Lake Area Technical College is a great choice. With over 50 programs to choose from, there's sure to be something that's right for you.

Admission Requirements

Lake Area Technical College (LATC) is a two-year technical college located in Watertown, South Dakota. The college offers over 50 programs in a variety of fields, including agriculture, business, health care, and manufacturing.

To be eligible for admission to LATC, students must have a high school diploma or equivalent. They must also take a placement test to determine their level of readiness for college-level coursework.

LATC has an open admissions policy, meaning that any student who meets the above requirements will be accepted into the college. However, preference for admission is given to students who live in the college's service area.

Once admitted, students must complete a New Student Orientation before they can register for classes. Orientation includes information about campus resources, academic policies, and student life.

After completing Orientation, students meet with an advisor to plan their course of study. Advisors help students choose courses that fit their interests and career goals.

Once students have registered for classes, they are responsible for paying their tuition and fees. Financial aid is available for students who qualify.

LATC is committed to helping its students succeed. The college offers a variety of services, including tutoring, academic advising, and career counseling. It also has an active student life, with over 50 clubs and organizations.
