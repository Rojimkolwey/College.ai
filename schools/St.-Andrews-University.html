St. Andrews University is a private research university in Scotland. The university is Scotland's third oldest university and the fifth oldest in the English-speaking world. St Andrews is made up of four main academic schools - Arts, Divinity, Medicine and Science - and is organized into a number of research institutes and centers. The university has a student body of around 9,000 students, from over 100 different countries. The university is consistently ranked as one of the top universities in the UK and in the world.

Founded in 1413 on the east coast of Scotland, St Andrews is the home of golf and the oldest university in Scotland. The university has a long and rich history, and has produced many notable alumni, including six Nobel Laureates and the current Prime Minister of the UK, Boris Johnson.

St Andrews is a world-renowned center of excellence for teaching and research. The university has a strong reputation in the sciences, and is particularly well-known for its work in medicine, biology and environmental science. The university is also home to a number of leading research institutes, including the Scottish Centre for Ecology & the Natural Environment and the Sea Mammal Research Unit.

St Andrews is a beautiful and historic city, and the university campus is located in the heart of the city. The campus is made up of a number of historic buildings, including the iconic St Salvator's Chapel, and the university also has a number of green spaces, including the North Haugh, home to a colony of red squirrels.

The university offers a wide range of undergraduate and postgraduate degrees, across a broad range of subject areas. St Andrews is also home to a number of world-leading research centers and institutes, making it an ideal place to study for a research degree.

Whether you're looking to study for an undergraduate or postgraduate degree, or you're interested in undertaking research in a world-leading institute, St Andrews University is an excellent choice.
