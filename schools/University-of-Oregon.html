University of Oregon

Eugene, OR
110 Johnson Hall, Eugene, OR 97403
541-346-1000

The University of Oregon, founded in 1876, is a public research university in Eugene, Oregon. The university is the state's flagship institution, and is one of only two land-grant universities in the Pacific Northwest. The university is classified as a research university with "highest research activity" by the Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education. The university's athletic teams, the Ducks, compete in the NCAA Division I Pac-12 Conference. With over 24,000 students and 2,500 faculty members, the university is the state's largest. The University of Oregon is one of 72 members of the Association of American Universities.

The university is organized into seven colleges and seven professional schools. The colleges of Arts and Sciences, Business, Education, Engineering, and Honors are the five undergraduate colleges. The Graduate School, School of Architecture and Allied Arts, School of Law, and School of Music and Dance are the professional schools. The university also operates several research centers and institutes, including the Oregon Social Learning Center, the Oregon Nanoscience and Microtechnologies Institute, and the Oregon Health & Science University Knight Cancer Institute.

Admission Requirements

Admission to the University of Oregon is competitive. In order to be considered for admission, students must submit an application, transcripts, test scores, and a non-refundable application fee. The University of Oregon does not have an minimum GPA or test score requirement for admission. However, the average GPA of admitted students is 3.7 and the average SAT score is 1290. The University of Oregon also requires two letters of recommendation and a personal essay. For more information about admission requirements, please visit the University of Oregon website.
