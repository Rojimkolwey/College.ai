City Colleges of Chicago-Harold Washington College is a community college in Chicago, Illinois. It is a part of the City Colleges of Chicago system. The college was established in 1983, and is named after the city's first African-American mayor, Harold Washington. The college serves approximately 10,000 students annually, and offers over 60 associate degree and certificate programs.

The college is located in the Loop neighborhood of Chicago, near many of the city's major tourist attractions, businesses, and cultural institutions. The college is accessible by public transportation, and has on-campus parking for students and faculty.

The college offers a variety of student services, including academic advising, tutoring, and career counseling. The college also has an active student government, which organizes campus events and advocates for student interests.

City Colleges of Chicago-Harold Washington College is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. The college is also a member of the American Association of Community Colleges.
