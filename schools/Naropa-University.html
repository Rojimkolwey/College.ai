Naropa University

Boulder, CO
2130 Arapahoe Ave, Boulder, CO 80302-6697
303-444-0202

Founded in 1974, Naropa University is a private, accredited institution located in Boulder, Colorado. The university offers a unique blend of contemplative education and academic excellence, with a curriculum that integrates the arts and sciences. The university is home to a diverse community of students, faculty, and staff who are committed to personal and spiritual growth, social justice, and environmental stewardship.

Naropa University's academic programs are grounded in the liberal arts and sciences, and emphasize critical thinking, creative expression, and personal transformation. The university offers bachelor's degrees in the arts and sciences, as well as master's degrees in counseling, education, and environmental studies. In addition, Naropa University is home to the Jack Kerouac School of Disembodied Poetics, the nation's only accredited Buddhist-inspired university.

The university's commitment to contemplative education is reflected in its unique pedagogy, which integrates Eastern wisdom traditions with Western academic traditions. This approach to learning cultivates the whole person—mind, body, and spirit—and prepares students to lead lives of engaged compassion.

Naropa University is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and is a member of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools.

Admission Requirements

In order to be considered for admission to Naropa University, applicants must submit an application form along with a $50 non-refundable application fee. Official transcripts from all colleges and universities previously attended must also be submitted, along with a personal essay and two letters of recommendation.

Applicants must have a minimum 2.75 GPA in order to be considered for admission. However, students with a GPA below 2.75 may still be considered for admission on a case-by-case basis.

Once an application has been submitted, it will be reviewed by the Admissions Committee. If an applicant is deemed to be a good fit for the university, they will be invited to participate in an on-campus interview.

After an interview has been conducted, the Admissions Committee will make a decision on whether or not to offer admission to the applicant. If an offer of admission is made, the applicant will be required to submit a $200 deposit in order to secure their place at the university.
