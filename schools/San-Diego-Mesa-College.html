San Diego Mesa College is a public community college in San Diego, California. The college offers associate's degrees, certificates, and transfer programs. The college is accredited by the Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges of the Western Association of Schools and Colleges.

San Diego Mesa College was established in 1964 as San Diego Junior College. The college was renamed San Diego Mesa College in 1974. The college is located on a 100-acre campus in the city of San Diego. The college enrolls over 18,000 students.

The college offers over 140 associate's degree and certificate programs. The college also offers a variety of transfer programs. The college has a strong commitment to student success and has a variety of student support services available.

The college is home to a number of athletic teams. The college's teams compete in the Pacific Coast Athletic Conference. The college has a number of student clubs and organizations. The college also offers a variety of cultural and enrichment activities.

Admission Requirements

San Diego Mesa College is a public community college in San Diego, California. It is the largest of the three community colleges in the San Diego Community College District. Mesa College offers associate degrees and certificate programs in over 100 areas of study.

Admission to San Diego Mesa College is open to all students who meet the following requirements:

1. Complete an online application for admission.

2. Pay the non-refundable $46 application fee.

3. Submit official transcripts from all colleges and universities previously attended.

4. Take and pass the Mesa College Placement Test, or submit qualifying SAT or ACT scores.

5. Complete any required prerequisite courses for the desired program of study.

6. Meet with a counselor to develop an educational plan.

7. Register for classes.

8. Pay tuition and fees.

9. Attend classes and complete coursework.
