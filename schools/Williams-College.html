Williams College

Williamstown, MA
880 Main St, Williamstown, MA 01267
413-597-3131

Founded in 1793, Williams College is a private liberal arts college located in Williamstown, Massachusetts. The college has an enrollment of approximately 2,000 students and is one of the eight schools that make up the Ivy League.

Williams College is consistently ranked as one of the top colleges in the United States. In 2019, Williams was ranked as the #1 liberal arts college in the country by U.S. News & World Report. The college is also ranked highly by other organizations, including Forbes (#4 in the nation), Kiplinger’s Personal Finance (#5), and The Princeton Review (#6).

Williams College is known for its rigorous academic program as well as its commitment to providing a well-rounded education. The college offers a variety of majors and minors, and students are encouraged to take courses in a variety of disciplines in order to develop a broad understanding of the world. In addition to coursework, Williams students can also get involved in research, internships, and study abroad programs.

Williams College is located in the Berkshire Mountains of western Massachusetts. The college’s campus is spread out over 1,000 acres of land, and includes a variety of buildings and facilities. The college’s library system is one of the largest in the country, and the campus also features an art museum, an outdoor amphitheater, and a variety of athletic facilities.

Williams College is a highly selective school, and admission is competitive. In 2019, the college received over 20,000 applications and admitted just over 1,000 students. Williams is need-blind in its admissions process, and provides financial aid to all students who qualify.

Williams College graduates go on to successful careers in a variety of fields. Many Williams alumni have gone on to become leaders in business, government, and academia.

Admission Requirements

Williams College is a private liberal arts college in Williamstown, Massachusetts. It was founded in 1793 with funds from the estate of Ephraim Williams. The college has an endowment of $2.3 billion as of June 2019. The admission process at Williams College is need-blind for domestic students and meets 100% of demonstrated financial need for all admitted students. For international students, the college is need-aware. The admissions process is highly competitive, with an acceptance rate of 9.4% for the class of 2023. The median SAT score for admitted students was 1470. The Williams College admissions website provides detailed information about the requirements for admission, including standardized test scores, essays, and letters of recommendation.
