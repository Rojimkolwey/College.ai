South Dakota State University

Brookings, SD
1004 Campanile Ave, Brookings, SD 57007-1898
800-952-3541

South Dakota State University is a public research university in Brookings, South Dakota. The university is the largest in the state and is a member of the South Dakota Board of Regents system. The university offers associate, bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees in more than 200 areas of study, and is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. Founded in 1881 as the state's land-grant university, SDSU became the largest institution of higher education in South Dakota in 1941 when it absorbed the former University of South Dakota School of Mines and Technology. The university has a strong research program, with over $200 million in annual research expenditures. SDSU is home to the South Dakota Art Museum, the Brookings Summer Arts Festival, and the annual Brookings JazzFest. The university also operates the Brookings Agricultural Experiment Station and the International Water Institute.

Admission Requirements

South Dakota State University is a public university located in Brookings, South Dakota. The school was founded in 1881 and enrolls over 12,000 students from all 50 states and more than 60 countries. The university offers more than 200 programs of study and is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission.

To be admitted to South Dakota State University, students must submit an application, transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a non-refundable application fee. International students must also submit proof of English proficiency. Admission is competitive, and students are encouraged to apply early.
