California College San Diego-CollegeAmerica-Phoenix (CCSD-CA-P) is a for-profit college located in Phoenix, Arizona. The college is accredited by the Accrediting Commission of Career Schools and Colleges (ACCSC). CCSD-CA-P offers associate's and bachelor's degrees in business, healthcare, and information technology.

CCSD-CA-P was founded in 2001 by Dr. Richard Rubenstein. The college is a subsidiary of Career Education Corporation (CEC), a for-profit education company. CCSD-CA-P is one of several campuses operated by CEC in the United States.

CCSD-CA-P offers small class sizes and hands-on training. The college has an externship program that allows students to gain real-world experience in their field of study. CCSD-CA-P also offers online programs for students who cannot attend classes on campus.

CCSD-CA-P has experienced financial difficulties in recent years. In 2016, CEC announced that it was exploring the sale of the college. However, no buyers were found and CEC continued to operate CCSD-CA-P.

CCSD-CA-P has been the subject of several lawsuits. In 2014, the U.S. Department of Education fined the college $30 million for allegedly misrepresenting job placement data. In 2015, a former student filed a class-action lawsuit against the college, alleging that she was misled about the cost of her education and the job prospects after graduation.

Despite its financial difficulties, CCSD-CA-P continues to operate and offer quality education and training to its students.

Admission Requirements

California College San Diego-CollegeAmerica-Phoenix is a regionally accredited, private college specializing in career-focused higher education. The college has campuses in San Diego and Phoenix, and offers online and evening programs.

Admission to California College San Diego-CollegeAmerica-Phoenix is open to all students who have a high school diploma or equivalent. The college does not have any specific GPA or test score requirements. However, all applicants must complete an online application and submit transcripts from all previous colleges or universities attended.

Once the admissions office has received all required materials, applicants will be contacted for an interview. During the interview, applicants will be asked about their educational and career goals. The interview is an opportunity for applicants to learn more about the college and to ask any questions they may have.

After the interview, applicants will be notified of the admissions decision. If accepted, students will need to complete a financial aid application and submit a deposit to secure their spot in the incoming class.
