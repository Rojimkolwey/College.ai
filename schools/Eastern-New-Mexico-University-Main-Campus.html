Eastern New Mexico University-Main Campus

Portales, NM
ENMU Station 2, 1500 S Ave K, Portales, NM 88130
575-562-1011

Eastern New Mexico University-Main Campus is a public university located in Portales, New Mexico. It is the largest and oldest member of the Eastern New Mexico University System. The university offers associate's, bachelor's, and master's degrees in a variety of disciplines. ENMU-Main Campus is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission.

The university was founded in 1934 as Eastern New Mexico Junior College. It became a four-year institution in 1940 and was renamed Eastern New Mexico University. The ENMU System was established in 1967, and ENMU-Main Campus has remained the flagship campus of the system ever since.

ENMU-Main Campus has an enrollment of approximately 4,000 students. The student body is diverse, with students coming from all over the United States and several foreign countries. The university offers a wide range of academic programs, including liberal arts, business, education, sciences, and health professions.

The university is committed to providing a quality education at an affordable price. ENMU-Main Campus is a great choice for students who are looking for a challenging academic experience and a supportive community.

Admission Requirements

Eastern New Mexico University-Main Campus is a public university located in Portales, New Mexico. The university is a member of the New Mexico State University System. ENMU-Main Campus offers associate's, bachelor's, and master's degrees in a variety of disciplines.

The university has an open admissions policy for undergraduate students. However, admission to the university's graduate programs is competitive. ENMU-Main Campus requires all undergraduate applicants to submit an application, transcripts, and a non-refundable application fee. The university also requires all graduate applicants to submit an application, transcripts, letters of recommendation, and a non-refundable application fee.

ENMU-Main Campus is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. The university's nursing program is accredited by the Commission on Collegiate Nursing Education.
