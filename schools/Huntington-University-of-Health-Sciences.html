Huntington University of Health Sciences is a private, non-profit university located in downtown Huntington, West Virginia. The university was founded in 1858 as the Medical Department of Marshall College, and became an independent institution in 1991. It is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and offers degrees in the health sciences, including osteopathic medicine, pharmacy, physician assistant studies, physical therapy, occupational therapy, and public health.

The university's osteopathic medical program is its largest and most popular, with over 300 students enrolled. The program is four years long and leads to the Doctor of Osteopathic Medicine (DO) degree. The curriculum includes coursework in the basic sciences, clinical sciences, and osteopathic principles and practices. Students also complete rotations in various medical specialties, including family medicine, surgery, pediatrics, and obstetrics/gynecology.

The university's pharmacy program is also quite popular, with over 200 students enrolled. The program is four years long and leads to the Doctor of Pharmacy (PharmD) degree. The curriculum includes coursework in the basic sciences, pharmacology, and clinical pharmacy. Students also complete rotations in various pharmacy settings, such as hospitals, community pharmacies, and long-term care facilities.

The physician assistant program is a relatively new addition to the university, with the first class graduating in May of 2019. The program is two years long and leads to the Master of Science in Physician Assistant Studies (MSPAS) degree. The curriculum includes coursework in the basic sciences, clinical sciences, and physician assistant studies. Students also complete rotations in various medical specialties, such as family medicine, surgery, pediatrics, and obstetrics/gynecology.

The physical therapy program is also a relatively new addition to the university, with the first class graduating in May of 2020. The occupational therapy program is the newest addition to the university, with the first class set to graduate in May of 2021. Students also complete rotations in various occupational therapy settings, such as hospitals, outpatient clinics, and long-term care facilities.

Huntington University of Health Sciences is a great choice for students interested in a career in the health sciences. The university offers a wide variety of programs, and the faculty are dedicated to teaching and preparing students for a successful career in the health care field.

Admission Requirements

Huntington University of Health Sciences is a private, non-profit university located in Huntington, West Virginia. The university offers a Doctor of Osteopathic Medicine (DO) degree, as well as graduate degrees in medical sciences.

The Doctor of Osteopathic Medicine program at Huntington University of Health Sciences is a four-year program that leads to the DO degree. The program is accredited by the American Osteopathic Association's Commission on Osteopathic College Accreditation (COCA).

Admission to the DO program at Huntington University of Health Sciences is competitive. Applicants must have a bachelor's degree from an accredited institution and must have completed the following prerequisite coursework:

-One year of biology with lab

-One year of chemistry with lab

-One year of physics with lab

-One year of English

-Six months of calculus

In addition to the above coursework requirements, applicants must also take the Medical College Admission Test (MCAT) and submit their scores as part of the application process.

Once all application materials have been received, qualified applicants will be invited to participate in an interview with the Admissions Committee. The interview is an opportunity for the applicant to demonstrate their motivation for pursuing a career in medicine, as well as their ability to communicate and interact with others.

After the interview, the Admissions Committee will make a decision on whether or not to offer the applicant admission to the DO program. Applicants who are offered admission will be required to submit a deposit to secure their spot in the class.
