Boston Architectural College

Boston, MA
320 Newbury Street, Boston, MA 02115-2703
617-262-5000

Founded in 1874, the Boston Architectural College (BAC) is a private, professional college located in the heart of Boston's historic Back Bay. The BAC offers undergraduate and graduate degrees in architecture, interior design, landscape architecture, and design studies. With an enrollment of over 1,000 students, the BAC is the largest accredited independent college of architectural design in New England.

The BAC's nine-acre campus is located in Boston's historic Back Bay neighborhood, just steps from the city's world-renowned museums, theaters, and restaurants. The BAC's main campus building, designed by noted American architect Charles McKim, is a National Historic Landmark.

The BAC's academic programs are organized into four schools: the School of Architecture, the School of Interior Architecture, the School of Landscape Architecture, and the School of Design Studies.

The BAC's School of Architecture offers undergraduate and graduate degrees in architecture. The four-year undergraduate program leads to a Bachelor of Architecture (B.Arch.) degree. The two-year graduate program offers a Master of Architecture (M.Arch.) degree.

The BAC's School of Interior Architecture offers undergraduate and graduate degrees in interior architecture. The four-year undergraduate program leads to a Bachelor of Interior Architecture (B.I.A.) degree. The two-year graduate program offers a Master of Interior Architecture (M.I.A.) degree.

The BAC's School of Landscape Architecture offers undergraduate and graduate degrees in landscape architecture. The four-year undergraduate program leads to a Bachelor of Landscape Architecture (B.L.A.) degree. The two-year graduate program offers a Master of Landscape Architecture (M.L.A.) degree.

The BAC's School of Design Studies offers undergraduate and graduate degrees in design studies. The four-year undergraduate program leads to a Bachelor of Design Studies (B.D.S.) degree. The two-year graduate program offers a Master of Design Studies (M.D.S.) degree.

The BAC is accredited by the National Architectural Accrediting Board (NAAB). The BAC's undergraduate and graduate programs in architecture are accredited by the NAAB. The BAC's undergraduate and graduate programs in interior architecture are accredited by the Council for Interior Design Accreditation (CIDA). The BAC's landscape architecture program is accredited by the Landscape Architectural Accreditation Board (LAAB).

The BAC is a member of the Association of Independent Colleges of Art and Design (AICAD), a consortium of the leading independent colleges of art and design in the United States.

Admission Requirements

The Boston Architectural College (BAC) is an independent, accredited college specializing in interior design, landscape architecture, and architecture. Founded in 1887, the BAC offers professional and post-professional programs in a range of design fields.

The BAC is accredited by the National Architectural Accrediting Board (NAAB) and the Landscape Architectural Accreditation Board (LAAB). Admission to the BAC is selective and based on a portfolio review and interview.

The BAC offers a four-year Bachelor of Architecture (B.Arch) degree as well as two- and three-year Master of Architecture (M.Arch) degrees. The BAC also offers a range of post-professional programs, including a Master of Landscape Architecture (MLA), Master of Interior Architecture (MIA), and Master of Design Studies (MDS).

The BAC’s undergraduate and graduate programs are both designed to prepare students for professional practice. The BAC’s curriculum emphasizes design studio courses, which are complemented by courses in history, theory, technology, and practice.

The BAC’s post-professional programs are designed for working professionals who wish to deepen their knowledge and skills in a particular area of design. The MLA, MIA, and MDS programs each offer a range of courses and studios that allow students to customize their studies to their specific interests and goals.
