Downey Adult School, located in Downey, California, is a public adult school that offers a variety of educational opportunities for adults in the community. The school offers adult basic education, ESL, and citizenship classes, as well as job training and GED prep. The school also has a strong community involvement, with programs such as the Senior Outreach Program and the Parent Involvement Program.

Admission Requirements

Downey Adult School is a public adult education institution located in Downey, California. It offers a variety of educational programs and services for adults 18 years and older.

The school’s mission is to provide quality education and training that empower adults to reach their personal, academic, and career goals.

Adults can enroll in classes for credit or non-credit. There are also a variety of non-credit courses offered, such as English as a Second Language (ESL), citizenship, and GED preparation.

To enroll in classes for credit, students must meet the following requirements:

• Be 18 years of age or older

• Have a government-issued photo ID

• Take a placement test (if required)

• Meet with a counselor to develop an educational plan

To enroll in non-credit classes, students must:

• Be 18 years of age or older

• Have a government-issued photo ID

Adults who want to enroll in the school’s GED program must:

• Be 18 years of age or older

• Have a government-issued photo ID

• Take a placement test

• Meet with a counselor to develop an educational plan

Downey Adult School also offers a variety of community services, such as adult literacy tutoring, job search assistance, and computer classes.
