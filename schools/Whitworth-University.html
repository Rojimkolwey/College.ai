Whitworth University is a private, liberal arts university in Spokane, Washington, United States. Founded in 1890, Whitworth is affiliated with the Presbyterian Church (USA) and offers bachelor's and master's degrees in a variety of academic disciplines. The university is nationally recognized for its commitment to academic excellence, diversity, and service.

Admission Requirements

Whitworth University is a private Christian university located in Spokane, Washington. The school was founded in 1890 and is affiliated with the Presbyterian Church (USA). The university has an undergraduate enrollment of 2,000 students and a graduate enrollment of 400 students. The student-to-faculty ratio is 12:1.

The university offers over 50 undergraduate majors and 10 graduate programs. The most popular majors include Business, Education, Psychology, and Nursing. Admission to Whitworth is competitive, with an acceptance rate of 73%. The average SAT score for incoming freshmen is 1130 and the average ACT score is 24. The middle 50% of admitted students have SAT scores between 1040 and 1240 and ACT scores between 21 and 27.

Whitworth University is committed to providing a Christ-centered education that equips students for lives of service and leadership. The university offers a supportive community, rigorous academics, and opportunities for spiritual growth.
