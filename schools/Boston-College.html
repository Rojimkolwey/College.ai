Boston College

Chestnut Hill, MA
140 Commonwealth Avenue, Chestnut Hill, MA 02467
617-552-8000

Boston College is a private research university in Chestnut Hill, Massachusetts, founded in 1863. It has 9,000 full-time undergraduates and almost 5,000 graduate students. The main campus is situated atop 500 acres (2.0 km2) on the southwest face of the Chestnut Hill plateau, overlooking the 830-acre (3.4 km2) Newton Cemetery. The university has two satellite campuses: in Downtown Boston and in the Boston suburb of Brighton.

The Boston College Eagles compete in the NCAA Division I as members of the Atlantic Coast Conference (ACC). They are one of the oldest and most successful programs in the history of college athletics, with 25 national championships.

Boston College's Chestnut Hill campus features some of the earliest examples of Collegiate Gothic architecture in North America. The main campus is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.

Boston College offers bachelor's degrees, master's degrees, and doctoral degrees through its nine schools and colleges: the Morrissey College of Arts and Sciences, the Carroll School of Management, the Lynch School of Education and Human Development, the Connell School of Nursing, the School of Social Work, the School of Theology and Ministry, the Boston College Graduate School of Arts and Sciences, the Boston College Law School, and the Woods College of Advancing Studies.

Admission Requirements

Boston College is a private research university with an acceptance rate of 32%. Located in Chestnut Hill, Massachusetts, Boston College has strong academics and is ranked as the 31st best college in the nation by US News and World Report.

To be admitted to Boston College, students must submit an application, high school transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a personal essay. The admissions committee looks for students with strong grades and test scores, as well as evidence of leadership and involvement in extracurricular activities.

While there is no minimum GPA or SAT/ACT score required for admission, the average GPA of admitted students is 3.8 and the average SAT score is 1430.

If you have any questions about the admissions process, Boston College offers admissions counseling and tours of the campus. You can also visit the school's website for more information.
