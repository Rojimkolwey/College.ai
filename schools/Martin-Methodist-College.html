Martin Methodist College is a small, private, liberal arts college located in Pulaski, Tennessee. The college was founded in 1870 by the Methodist Episcopal Church, South and is affiliated with the Tennessee Conference of the United Methodist Church. The college offers bachelor's degrees in over 30 majors and concentrations, as well as master's degrees in business administration and education. The college is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools and is a member of the Tennessee Independent Colleges and Universities Association. The student body is approximately 1,000 students, and the faculty-to-student ratio is 14-to-1. The average class size is 18 students. The college is committed to providing a quality education at an affordable price and offers a variety of scholarships and financial aid programs to help make college affordable for everyone.

Admission Requirements

Martin Methodist College is a private, coeducational, liberal arts college affiliated with the United Methodist Church. The college is located on a 68-acre (280,000 m2) campus in Pulaski, Tennessee, United States, approximately 45 miles (72 km) south of Nashville. It was founded in 1870 and is the oldest continuously operating institution of higher education in Giles County.

In order to be admitted to Martin Methodist College, students must have a high school diploma or equivalent. They must also submit an application, along with transcripts and test scores. The college uses a holistic admissions process, and students are encouraged to submit letters of recommendation and a personal essay.
