County College of Morris

Randolph, NJ
214 Center Grove Rd, Randolph, NJ 07869-2086
973-328-5000

County College of Morris (CCM) is a two-year public community college in Randolph, Morris County, New Jersey. The college is accredited by the Middle States Association of Colleges and Schools. CCM offers over 50 degree and certificate programs, as well as non-credit courses, in a variety of fields.

CCM was founded in 1968, and has since grown to serve over 8,000 students. The college is a member of the National Junior College Athletic Association (NJCAA) and offers a variety of sports teams. CCM also has a number of student clubs and organizations, as well as a vibrant campus life.

CCM is committed to providing an affordable, high-quality education to its students. The college offers a variety of financial aid options, and has a number of scholarships available. CCM also has a number of articulation agreements with four-year colleges and universities, which guarantee seamless transfer of credits.

CCM is an important part of the Morris County community. The college offers a variety of cultural and educational events open to the public, and is a major employer in the county. CCM is also home to a number of unique facilities, including an planetarium, an art gallery, and a performing arts center.

Admission Requirements

In order to be admitted to County College of Morris, students must submit an application, transcripts, and a non-refundable $50 fee. Applicants must also take either the SAT or ACT, and submit scores from either test. County College of Morris does not require students to submit letters of recommendation or an essay.

Students who have a cumulative high school GPA of 3.0 or higher will be automatically accepted into the college. Students with a GPA between 2.5 and 2.99 may be conditionally accepted, and will be required to take a placement test to determine which courses they will be eligible to take. Students with a GPA below 2.5 will not be accepted into the college.

In addition to the academic requirements, students must also demonstrate that they have completed a minimum of 40 hours of community service. Students may satisfy this requirement by completing volunteer work, internships, or other service-learning opportunities.
