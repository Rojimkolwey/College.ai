Lurleen B Wallace Community College is a two-year public institution of higher education located in Andalusia, Alabama. It is one of the twenty-eight member institutions of the Alabama Community College System. The college is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges to award the Associate in Arts, Associate in Science, and Associate in Applied Science degrees. The college's service area includes Covington, Crenshaw, and parts of Dale, Geneva, and Houston counties.

The college was established in 1966 and opened its doors to students in 1967. The school was originally named Andalusia Junior College and was later renamed in honor of former Alabama governor Lurleen B. Wallace. The college offers a variety of academic and career-oriented programs of study, as well as adult education and continuing education courses.

The Lurleen B Wallace Community College campus is situated on sixty-six acres of land in the city of Andalusia. The campus includes a variety of buildings and facilities, such as the Wallace Administration Building, the Wallace Learning Resources Center, the Wallace Physical Education Complex, and the Wallace Fine Arts Center. The college also operates a number of off-campus facilities, including the Lurleen B Wallace Community College Crenshaw Center in Luverne, Alabama, and the Wallace-Dothan Campus in Dothan, Alabama.

The college offers a wide range of student services and support programs, such as academic advising, career counseling, tutoring, and disability services. The college also operates a number of student clubs and organizations, including the Wallace Ambassadors, the Wallace Players, and the Wallace Singers.

Lurleen B Wallace Community College is committed to providing quality education and training opportunities that lead to successful careers and lives. Admission Requirements

Lurleen B Wallace Community College (LBWCC) is a two-year college located in Andalusia, Alabama. The college is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges to award the Associate in Arts, Associate in Science, and Associate in Applied Science degrees.

Admission to LBWCC is open to any student who has graduated from an accredited high school or has earned a GED. Students must submit an application for admission, official high school transcripts or GED scores, and ACT or SAT scores. Students who have not taken the ACT or SAT may be eligible for admission if they have a minimum high school GPA of 2.5 and meet one of the following criteria:

-Rank in the top 50% of their high school graduating class

-Earn a minimum score of 18 on the American College Test (ACT)

-Earn a minimum score of 810 on the SAT Reasoning Test

Students who do not meet the above criteria may still be eligible for admission to the college through the Alabama Community College System's Open Door Policy.
