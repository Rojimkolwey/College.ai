The University of Alabama at Birmingham (UAB) is a public research university in Birmingham, Alabama. The university is the state of Alabama's largest employer, with more than 21,000 employees. As of 2019, its total enrollment was 20,199. The university's athletic teams, the Blazers, compete in NCAA Division I as members of Conference USA.

Founded in 1969 as a two-year college, UAB is now one of three doctoral-level research universities in Alabama. The university's main campus is located on a 1,200-acre (490-hectare) site in the southern part of Birmingham. The campus is home to eight schools and colleges: the College of Arts and Sciences, the School of Business, the School of Education, the School of Engineering, the School of Health Professions, the School of Medicine, the School of Nursing, and the School of Public Health.

UAB is a comprehensive research university with a wide range of programs and facilities. The university's research enterprise is conducted through the UAB Comprehensive Cancer Center, the UAB School of Medicine, and the UAB Health System. The UAB Comprehensive Cancer Center is the only National Cancer Institute-designated comprehensive cancer center in Alabama. The UAB School of Medicine is the state's largest medical school and the only medical school in Alabama with a full-time faculty of more than 600. The UAB Health System is a not-for-profit, integrated healthcare delivery system that includes five hospitals, three clinics, and more than 2,000 licensed beds.

UAB is a member of the prestigious Association of American Universities, a select group of 62 research universities in the United States and Canada. The university is also a member of the Southeastern Universities Research Association and the Oak Ridge Associated Universities.

The university's origins can be traced to the founding of the Birmingham Medical School in 1945. The school was established by the Birmingham Chamber of Commerce with the goal of meeting the region's need for trained medical personnel. In 1957, the Birmingham Medical School merged with the University of Alabama's School of Medicine to form the University of Alabama School of Medicine at Birmingham. In 1969, the school became a four-year institution and was renamed the University of Alabama at Birmingham.

The university has been recognized for its contributions to the advancement of science and medicine. In 1984, UAB scientist Dr. Joseph E. Murray performed the world's first successful allogeneic bone marrow transplant, a procedure that has saved the lives of thousands of cancer patients. In 1990, UAB researchers Dr. Peter C. Doherty and Dr. Rolf Zinkernagel were awarded the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for their discovery of how the immune system recognizes viruses.

UAB is also home to a number of world-renowned centers and institutes, including the Comprehensive Cancer Center, the School of Medicine, the Health System, and the Center for AIDS Research. The university's economic impact on the state of Alabama is estimated to be $5.6 billion annually.

The University of Alabama at Birmingham is a public research university located in Birmingham, Alabama. The university is one of three doctoral-granting universities in the state of Alabama and is classified as a "Doctoral/Research University" by the Carnegie Foundation for the Advancement of Teaching.

The university was established in 1969 by an act of the Alabama Legislature. The University of Alabama at Birmingham is the largest research university in Alabama and the state's only Level I trauma center.

The university offers over 140 degree programs in 12 schools and colleges. The University of Alabama at Birmingham is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges to award bachelor's, master's, doctoral, and professional degrees.

The University of Alabama at Birmingham is a member of the Conference USA athletic conference and sponsors 19 NCAA Division I varsity teams. The university's athletic teams are known as the UAB Blazers.
