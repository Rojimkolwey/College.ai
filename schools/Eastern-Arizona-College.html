Eastern Arizona College

Thatcher, AZ
615 N. Stadium Ave., Thatcher, AZ 85552-0769
928-428-8272

Eastern Arizona College is a public community college located in Graham County, Arizona. The college serves approximately 7,400 students annually and offers associate degrees and certificate programs in a variety of fields.

The college was founded in 1888 as a normal school, which trained teachers for the public schools in Arizona Territory. In the early 20th century, the school began offering college-level coursework and was renamed Eastern Arizona Teachers College. The college was renamed again in 1966 to Eastern Arizona College.

The college is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and offers associate degrees in arts, science, and applied science, as well as certificate programs. The college has transfer agreements with several four-year colleges and universities in Arizona and elsewhere.

Eastern Arizona College has an active student life, with over 50 student clubs and organizations. The college also has a strong athletics program, with teams competing in the National Junior College Athletic Association.

Eastern Arizona College is a great choice for students seeking an affordable, quality education. The college offers a variety of programs to suit the needs of any student, and the campus is located in a beautiful, rural setting.

Admission Requirements

Eastern Arizona College is a public community college in Graham County, Arizona. The main campus is located in Thatcher, with satellite campuses in Safford and Greenlee County. Eastern Arizona College offers associate's degrees and certificate programs in a variety of fields, as well as a bachelor's degree in Nursing.

To be eligible for admission to Eastern Arizona College, applicants must have a high school diploma or equivalent. GED holders must also submit official high school transcripts. Applicants must submit official transcripts from all colleges and universities previously attended.

Eastern Arizona College has an open admissions policy and does not require applicants to take entrance exams such as the SAT or ACT. However, some programs may have additional admission requirements. For example, the Nursing program requires applicants to take the Test of Essential Academic Skills (TEAS) and have a minimum GPA of 2.5.

Once all required materials have been submitted, applicants will be notified of their admission status. If admitted, students will need to complete registration and pay tuition and fees before the start of classes.
