Rhodes College is a private liberal arts college in Memphis, Tennessee. Founded in 1848, it is the oldest college in the state of Tennessee and is considered one of the most prestigious colleges in the southern United States. Rhodes is a member of the selective Association of American Colleges and Universities and is ranked consistently as one of the top 50 liberal arts colleges in the country by various publications. The college enrolls approximately 2,000 students from across the United States and the world.

Rhodes College was founded in 1848 as the Masonic University of Tennessee. The university was established by the Grand Lodge of Tennessee of the Free and Accepted Masons to provide education to young men who were members of the Masonic Lodge. The university was renamed Rhodes College in 1855 in honor of its first benefactor, British philanthropist and South African diamond magnate Cecil Rhodes. Rhodes donated $100,000 to the college, which was used to construct a new campus on the outskirts of Memphis.

Rhodes College is a liberal arts college, meaning that it offers a broad curriculum in the liberal arts and sciences. The college has an emphasis on rigorous academic study and intellectual growth. Rhodes students are known for their high level of achievement and for their involvement in a variety of extracurricular activities. The college offers a wide range of student organizations, including academic, social, and service organizations. Rhodes students are also active in the Memphis community, volunteering with a variety of local organizations.

Rhodes College is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools. The college is also a member of the Association of American Colleges and Universities, the National Association of Independent Colleges and Universities, and the Council of Independent Colleges.

Rhodes College is a private liberal arts college in Memphis, Tennessee. Founded in 1848, it is the 10th oldest college in the Southern United States and one of the oldest west of the Appalachian Mountains. The college is named after British philanthropist and slave trader Cecil Rhodes.

Rhodes enrolls approximately 2,000 students from across the United States and the world. The student-faculty ratio is 10:1, and the average class size is 15. Rhodes offers more than 50 majors and concentrations.

Rhodes is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools. The college is a member of the National Association of Independent Colleges and Universities and the Association of American Colleges and Universities.

Rhodes College is ranked as the #1 Best Value School in the South and #2 Best Value School in the nation by The Princeton Review. The college is also ranked #5 in the nation for Best Undergraduate Teaching by U.S. News & World Report.

Rhodes College is committed to a diverse and inclusive community. The college does not discriminate on the basis of race, color, national origin, religion, sex, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, age, disability, veteran status, or genetic information in its educational programs and activities.

To be eligible for admission to Rhodes College, students must have completed a rigorous college-preparatory curriculum. The college prefers applicants who have taken at least four years of English, four years of mathematics, three years of science, three years of social studies, and two years of a foreign language.

Rhodes also requires all applicants to submit either the SAT or ACT. The college does not have a minimum SAT or ACT score for admission, but the middle 50% of admitted students scored between 1160 and 1340 on the SAT and between 26 and 31 on the ACT.

Rhodes accepts applications on a rolling basis, and the deadline for Early Decision applications is November 1. The deadline for regular decision applications is February 1.
