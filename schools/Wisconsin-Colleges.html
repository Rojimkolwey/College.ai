Wisconsin Universities and Colleges


Wisconsin is home to many excellent colleges and universities, offering a wide range of educational opportunities to students. The University of Wisconsin System is the largest public university system in the state, with 26 campuses across the state. The University of Wisconsin-Madison is the flagship campus of the system, and is one of the top research universities in the nation. It offers over 200 undergraduate majors, as well as graduate and professional degrees. Other campuses in the system include the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee, University of Wisconsin-Green Bay, University of Wisconsin-La Crosse, and University of Wisconsin-Stevens Point.

Wisconsin also has a number of private universities, including Marquette University, Lawrence University, and Beloit College. Each of these schools offers a variety of academic programs and opportunities for students.

In addition to the traditional four-year colleges, Wisconsin also has a number of two-year community and technical colleges. These schools offer associate's degrees and certificates in a variety of fields, and are a great option for students looking for a more affordable education.

For those looking to pursue a career in the trades, Wisconsin also has a number of technical colleges that offer certificate and associate's degree programs in a variety of fields, such as automotive technology, welding, and HVAC.

Overall, Wisconsin offers an excellent range of educational opportunities for students. Whether you're looking for a four-year university, two-year community college, or a technical college, there's something for everyone in the Badger State.
