CUNY Bernard M Baruch College

New York, NY
One Bernard Baruch Way (55 Lexington Ave at 24th St), New York, NY 10010
646-312-1000

CUNY Bernard M Baruch College is a public research university located in New York City. It is a member of the City University of New York (CUNY) system and is the only college within the system designated as a research university. The college is named after financier and statesman Bernard M. Baruch.

The college was established in 1919 as the School of Business and Public Administration. In 1955, it was renamed in honor of Bernard M. Baruch, who served as an advisor to Presidents Woodrow Wilson and Franklin D. Roosevelt and was a major figure in the development of the modern American financial system. The college offers degrees in business, public administration, economics, and liberal arts.

The college is located on Lexington Avenue in Manhattan's Gramercy Park neighborhood. It is adjacent to several other CUNY colleges, including the City College of New York, Hunter College, and the CUNY Graduate School and University Center. The college is accessible by the New York City Subway's 23rd Street station on the Lexington Avenue line.

The college enrolls approximately 18,000 students, of whom about one-third are undergraduates and two-thirds are graduate students. The college's faculty includes several notable scholars and researchers, including Nobel laureate economist Joseph Stiglitz and Pulitzer Prize-winning historian Eric Foner.

The college is organized into five schools: the School of Business, the School of Public and International Affairs, the Marxe School of Public and International Affairs, the Zicklin School of Business, and the Weissman School of Arts and Sciences. The college offers a wide range of programs and courses of study, including bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees.

The college is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education. It is ranked among the top 100 colleges and universities in the United States by several publications, including U.S. News & World Report, Forbes, and The Princeton Review.

Admission Requirements

CUNY Bernard M Baruch College is a public college with an open admissions policy. This means that any student who meets the minimum requirements for admission will be accepted into the college. The minimum requirements for admission are a high school diploma or equivalent, and a passing score on the CUNY Assessment Test.

Once you have been accepted into the college, you will need to complete an online application and submit your transcripts. You will also need to take a placement test to determine which classes you will need to take.
