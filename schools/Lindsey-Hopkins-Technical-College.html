Lindsey Hopkins Technical College

Miami, FL
750 NW 20 Street, Miami, FL 33127
305-324-6070

Lindsey Hopkins Technical College is a public, two-year technical college in Miami, Florida. It is part of the Miami-Dade County Public Schools district. The college offers programs in a variety of fields, including business, computer science, engineering, health care, and welding.

Lindsey Hopkins was founded in 1949 as a trade school for Miami-Dade County students. The school was named after Lindsey Hopkins, a local businessman and philanthropist. The school initially offered programs in auto mechanics, carpentry, and welding. In the 1950s, the school began to expand its programs and facilities. In the 1960s, the school became a technical college and began offering programs in a variety of fields. Today, the college offers over 50 programs and serves over 10,000 students.

The college is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools. It is also a member of the Florida Association of Community Colleges.

Admission Requirements

Admission to Lindsey Hopkins Technical College is based on a student's high school GPA, high school coursework, and standardized test scores. The college does not consider personal essays, letters of recommendation, or extracurricular activities in the admissions process.

Lindsey Hopkins Technical College is a two-year public college located in Miami, Florida. The college offers Associate in Science and Associate in Applied Science degrees in a variety of technical and trade fields.

To be eligible for admission to Lindsey Hopkins Technical College, students must have a high school diploma or equivalent. They must also have a minimum 2.0 GPA and minimum scores of 18 on the ACT or 860 on the SAT.

Students who do not meet the minimum GPA or test score requirements may still be eligible for admission if they have completed certain college-level coursework or have significant work experience in their field of interest.
