Glenville State College is a public college in Glenville, West Virginia. It is part of the West Virginia Community and Technical College System. The college offers associate's and bachelor's degrees. The school was founded in 1872 as the Glenville Normal School to train teachers. It became a state college in 1974. The school's sports teams are known as the Pioneers.

Admission Requirements

Glenville State College is a public institution of higher education located in Glenville, West Virginia, United States. The college was founded in 1872 as a normal school to train teachers and is now a four-year institution offering bachelor's degrees in a variety of fields. The college is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission.

Admission to Glenville State College is open to all students who have graduated from high school or obtained a GED. The college does not have an application fee. Students are encouraged to submit their transcripts, test scores, and any other relevant information to the admissions office for review.

Once all materials have been received, the admissions office will review the student's file and make a decision on admission. Admission to Glenville State College is based on a holistic review of the student's file and is not solely based on grades or test scores. The admissions office takes into account the student's high school GPA, class rank, and test scores when making a decision on admission.

In addition to the above requirements, students must also submit a completed FAFSA form in order to be eligible for financial aid. Glenville State College offers a variety of financial aid options, including grants, loans, and scholarships.
