Tulsa Community College

Tulsa, OK
6111 E Skelly Dr, Tulsa, OK 74135-6198
918-595-7000

Tulsa Community College (TCC) is a public community college in Tulsa, Oklahoma. The college is the largest two-year college in Oklahoma and one of the largest community colleges in the United States. TCC offers associate's degrees, certificates, and continuing education courses in a variety of fields.

TCC was founded in 1970 as a part of the Oklahoma State System of Higher Education. The college has grown rapidly since its founding, and now has four campuses in Tulsa: the Metro Campus, Northeast Campus, Southeast Campus, and West Campus. TCC also has a number of satellite campuses and centers throughout the Tulsa area.

TCC is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and offers over 100 degree and certificate programs. The college has an open admissions policy, meaning that any student who meets the minimum requirements for admission is eligible to enroll.

TCC is a diverse and inclusive community, with students from a variety of backgrounds and experiences. The college is committed to providing an affordable, quality education to all of its students.

Admission Requirements

Tulsa Community College (TCC) is a two-year institution of higher education located in Tulsa, Oklahoma, United States. The college offers associate's degrees and certificate programs in a variety of subject areas. TCC is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission.

To be eligible for admission to Tulsa Community College, applicants must:

1. Be a high school graduate or have a GED.

2. Submit an official transcript from each college or university previously attended.

3. Take the placement test, unless exempt.

4. Meet with an advisor.

5. Register for classes.

6. Pay tuition and fees.

7. Complete new student orientation.

8. Follow the college's immunization policy.

9. Provide official documentation of any disability that requires accommodations.

10. Complete any additional requirements for specific programs.
