Syracuse University

Syracuse, NY
900 South Crouse Ave., Syracuse, NY 13244
315-443-1870

Syracuse University is a private research university in Syracuse, New York. The university is a member of the Association of American Universities. Syracuse University is organized into 13 schools and colleges, with nationally recognized programs in information studies and library science, architecture, communications, business administration, sport management, public administration, engineering and the sciences. Syracuse University has a long and rich history dating back to its founding in 1870 as a land grant college. The university has since grown into a leading research institution, with over 21,000 students enrolled in over 200 programs. The university is also home to a number of world-renowned academic centers and institutes, including the Syracuse University College of Law, the S.I. Newhouse School of Public Communications, the Whitman School of Management, and the Maxwell School of Citizenship and Public Affairs.

Admission Requirements

Syracuse University is a private research university in Syracuse, New York. The university is organized into 13 schools and colleges, including the College of Arts and Sciences, the School of Architecture, the S.I. Newhouse School of Public Communications, the Martin J. Whitman School of Management, and the School of Education.

Syracuse University is ranked among the top 50 universities in the United States and is a member of the prestigious Association of American Universities. The university's admissions process is highly competitive, with an acceptance rate of just over 30%.

To be considered for admission to Syracuse University, students must submit an application form, high school transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a personal essay. The university also requires two letters of recommendation, one from a guidance counselor and one from a teacher. International students must also submit proof of English proficiency.
