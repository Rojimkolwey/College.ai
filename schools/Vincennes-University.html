Vincennes University is a public university in Vincennes, Indiana, in the United States. Founded in 1801 as Jefferson Academy, it is the oldest public university in Indiana and the first west of the Appalachian Mountains. It is also the second-oldest public university in the United States, after the University of Georgia. The university is a member of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools and the Higher Learning Commission. Vincennes University offers associate's and bachelor's degrees in more than 50 areas of study. The university has an enrollment of about 4,200 students. The university's athletic teams are known as the Trailblazers. The university is a member of the National Association of Intercollegiate Athletics (NAIA) and competes in the Mid-South Conference.

Admission Requirements

Vincennes University is a public university in Vincennes, Indiana, in the United States. Founded in 1801 as Jefferson Academy, it is the oldest public university in Indiana and the first west of the Allegheny Mountains. It is also a member of the Indiana Consortium for International Programs. The university is named for Francis Vincennes, a French-Canadian explorer. The campus is located on the city's northwest side, on a bluff overlooking the Wabash River.

Vincennes University is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. It has numerous associate and baccalaureate programs, as well as a master's degree in education.

To be eligible for admission to Vincennes University, applicants must have a high school diploma or equivalent. They must also submit official transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a $30 application fee. International students must additionally submit proof of English proficiency.
