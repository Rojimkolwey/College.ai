Tyler Junior College

Tyler, TX
1400 East Fifth Street, Tyler, TX 75701
903-510-2200

Founded in 1926, Tyler Junior College is one of the largest community colleges in Texas. It is a two-year institution that offers over 100 degree and certificate programs to more than 11,000 students each year. The college is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges and is a member of the American Association of Community Colleges.

Tyler Junior College is located in Tyler, Texas, about 100 miles east of Dallas. The college has two main campuses–the main campus in Tyler and the satellite campus in Lindale, Texas. The college also has several off-campus locations throughout Tyler and the surrounding area.

Tyler Junior College offers a wide variety of degree and certificate programs. Some of the most popular programs include business, nursing, health sciences, and liberal arts. The college also offers a number of unique programs, such as a two-year culinary arts program and a two-year automotive technology program.

In addition to its academic programs, Tyler Junior College also offers a number of student services and activities. The college has a strong athletics program, with teams competing in baseball, basketball, cross country, golf, soccer, softball, tennis, and track and field. The college also has a number of clubs and organizations, including the Student Government Association, the Tyler Junior College Democrats, and the Tyler Junior College Republicans.

Tyler Junior College is an affordable, accessible, and comprehensive community college that offers students a wide range of academic and co-curricular opportunities.

Admission Requirements

Tyler Junior College is a two-year community college in Tyler, Texas, United States. Tyler Junior College is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges to award Associate of Arts, Associate of Science and Associate of Applied Science degrees.

In order to be eligible for admission to Tyler Junior College, applicants must:

- Be a high school graduate or equivalent

- Submit an official high school transcript or equivalent

- Submit official ACT or SAT scores

- Complete the TSI Assessment

- Complete the application for admission

Once all required materials have been submitted, applicants will be notified of their admission status.
