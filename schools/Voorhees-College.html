Voorhees College

Denmark, SC
491 Porter Drive, Denmark, SC 29042
803-780-1234

Voorhees College is a small, private, historically black college located in Denmark, South Carolina. The college is affiliated with the Episcopal Church. It was founded in 1897 by the Right Reverend Francis B. Voorhees, the first African American Episcopal Bishop of the Diocese of South Carolina. The college's mission is to provide a quality liberal arts education to its students, with a focus on preparing them for leadership roles in their communities. The college offers a variety of degree programs, including business, education, and liberal arts. Voorhees College is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools. The college has an enrollment of approximately 700 students.

Admission Requirements

Voorhees College is a private, historically black college located in Denmark, South Carolina. The college is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges. The college is a member of the National Association of Intercollegiate Athletics and competes in the Division II of the NAIA. The college offers associate and baccalaureate degrees in business, education, liberal arts, and science.

Admission to Voorhees College is selective and competitive. The college seeks to enroll students who have the potential to succeed academically and contribute to the college community. The college evaluates each applicant on their academic record, test scores, extracurricular activities, and personal essay. The college also considers an applicant's character, motivation, and ability to contribute to the college community.

Voorhees College has a rolling admissions policy and accepts applications on a rolling basis. The college recommends that applicants submit their applications as early as possible to ensure that they are considered for admission.
