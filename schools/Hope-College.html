Hope College is a private, four-year liberal arts college located in Holland, Michigan, United States. The college was founded in 1866 by the Rev. A.C. Van Raalte, a Dutch Reformed minister. It is the oldest college in the state of Michigan and has been affiliated with the Reformed Church in America since 1866. The college's campus is located in the city of Holland on the west side of the state. The college offers a variety of undergraduate programs, including arts and sciences, business, education, engineering, music, and nursing. In addition to its undergraduate programs, Hope College also offers a number of graduate programs.

Hope College is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and is a member of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools. The college is also a member of the Great Lakes Colleges Association and the Michigan Colleges Alliance. Hope College is ranked as one of the top liberal arts colleges in the country by U.S. News & World Report.

Admission Requirements

Hope College is a private, coeducational liberal arts college located in Holland, Michigan, United States. The college was founded in 1866 by the Reformed Church in America and has been affiliated with the Reformed Church since 1884. Hope College is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools. The college has been ranked as the fourth best college in the Midwest by The Princeton Review.

Hope College is a member of the Great Lakes Colleges Association and the Michigan Intercollegiate Athletic Association. The college offers more than 80 majors, minors and pre-professional programs. Hope College's athletic teams compete in Division III of the NCAA and are known as the Flying Dutchmen and the Flying Dutch.

The college offers two types of admission: Early Decision and Regular Decision. Early Decision is a binding agreement for students who have decided that Hope is their first choice college. Regular Decision is a non-binding agreement for students who are still considering their college options.

To be considered for admission, students must submit an application, high school transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a personal essay. Hope College does not consider race, religion, or national origin in the admissions process.
