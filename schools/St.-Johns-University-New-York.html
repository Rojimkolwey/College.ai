St. John's University is a private Catholic university in New York City. Founded by the Congregation of the Mission in 1870, the school was originally located in the Brooklyn borough of New York City. In 1957, the school was relocated to its current location in the Queens borough. St. John's is organized into six colleges and schools: the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences, the School of Education, the Peter J. Tobin College of Business, the College of Pharmacy and Health Sciences, the School of Law, and the St. John's College of Nursing and Allied Health Professions. The university also operates a number of research centers and institutes, including the Center for Catholic Studies, the Institute for Core Studies, and the Institute for Asian Studies.

St. John's University offers a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs, as well as professional degrees in law, pharmacy, and health sciences. The university is also home to a number of athletic teams, which compete at the NCAA Division I level. The St. John's Red Storm men's basketball team has won a number of championships, including the NCAA Division I championship in 1985.

St. John's University is accredited by a number of accrediting bodies, including the Middle States Commission on Higher Education, the National Association of Schools of Music, and the American Bar Association.

Admission Requirements

St. John’s University-New York is a Catholic and Vincentian university located in the Queens borough of New York City. The university was founded in 1870 by the Congregation of the Mission (C.M., the Vincentian Fathers) with the approval of the New York State Board of Regents. St. John’s has six campuses in the New York metropolitan area, as well as study abroad locations in Rome, Paris, Spain, and Ireland.

The university is organized into five schools and six graduate centers. The five schools are: the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences; the Peter J. Tobin College of Business; the School of Education; the College of Pharmacy and Health Sciences; and the College of Professional Studies. The six graduate centers are: the Graduate School of Arts and Sciences; the School of Law; the School of Medicine and Health Sciences; the School of Professional Studies; the School of Theology and Religious Studies; and the Graduate Center for Worker Education.

Undergraduate admission to St. John’s is selective. For fall 2019, the university received over 26,000 applications and accepted approximately 53% of them. The middle 50% of admitted students had SAT scores between 1160 and 1340, and ACT scores between 25 and 30. The average high school GPA of admitted students was 3.4.

To be considered for admission, applicants must submit an application, high school transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a non-refundable application fee. International students must also submit proof of English proficiency. Additional materials may be required for certain programs. For example, the School of Education requires a writing sample and the School of Pharmacy and Health Sciences requires a supplemental application.

Admitted students are notified of their admission status on a rolling basis beginning in December for Early Action applicants and February for Regular Decision applicants. Accepted students have until May 1 to submit their enrollment deposit.
