Founded in 1820, Indiana University Bloomington is the flagship campus of Indiana University’s eight campuses, known for innovation, creativity, and academic freedom.

Bloomington is a medium-sized city with a big-city feel. The campus is situated adjacent to downtown, making it easy to get around and enjoy all the city has to offer.

The university is home to more than 40,000 students from all 50 states and more than 100 countries. The campus is also home to more than 1,200 faculty members, who are experts in their fields and committed to teaching and research.

The university offers more than 550 academic programs, including more than 200 undergraduate majors and more than 130 graduate and professional programs.

The university is also home to Indiana University Bloomington Athletics, which competes in the NCAA Division I. The athletics program is made up of 22 teams, including basketball, football, and track and field.

The university is committed to diversity and inclusion, and has been recognized as a leader in these efforts. The campus has a variety of resources and programs in place to support students, faculty, and staff from all backgrounds.

Indiana University Bloomington is a dynamic and vibrant community that is dedicated to excellence in teaching, research, and service. The university is a great place to learn, grow, and make a difference in the world.

Admission Requirements

Indiana University Bloomington is a public research university in Bloomington, Indiana. Founded in 1820, IU Bloomington is the flagship institution of the Indiana University system and its largest university. Indiana University is a "comprehensive doctoral university", classified by the Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education as having "high research activity". It has a diverse student body, with an enrollment of 42,855 undergraduate and 12,337 graduate students from all 50 U.S. states and 129 countries.

The university is organized into 17 schools and colleges at its flagship campus in Bloomington, and offers more than 550 undergraduate, graduate, and professional degree programs. The Indiana Hoosiers compete in NCAA Division I in 24 varsity sports and are members of the Big Ten Conference. Indiana University Bloomington has a strong Greek system and is home to the majority of fraternities and sororities in Indiana.

Undergraduate students at Indiana University Bloomington must meet the following requirements for admission:

1. A completed application for admission, including an essay

2. Official high school transcripts

3. Official SAT or ACT scores

4. A non-refundable application fee of $60

5. Optional: Additional materials such as letters of recommendation, a resume, or a portfolio

Graduate students must meet the following requirements for admission:

1. A completed application for admission, including an essay

2. Official transcripts from all colleges and universities attended

3. Official GRE, GMAT, or MAT scores

4. A non-refundable application fee of $75

5. Optional: Additional materials such as letters of recommendation, a resume, or a portfolio

6. For some programs, an interview may also be required

International students must meet the following additional requirements for admission:

1. A completed application for admission, including an essay

2. Official transcripts from all colleges and universities attended

3. Official TOEFL, IELTS, or PTE scores

4. A non-refundable application fee of $75

5. Optional: Additional materials such as letters of recommendation, a resume, or a portfolio

6. For some programs, an interview may also be required

7. Proof of financial support

8. A copy of your passport
