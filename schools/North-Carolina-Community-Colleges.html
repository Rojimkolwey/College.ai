North Carolina Community Colleges


North Carolina's community colleges are an integral part of the state's higher education system. With 58 campuses across the state, they provide educational opportunities to over 700,000 students each year. Community colleges provide a variety of educational programs, including certificates, diplomas, and associate degrees in a wide range of subjects. From traditional academic subjects to specialized fields such as cosmetology, culinary arts, and automotive technology, the community college system offers something for everyone. Community colleges provide an affordable alternative to traditional four-year colleges and universities, and many offer flexible scheduling options to accommodate working adults.

In addition to offering educational programs, community colleges also provide a variety of services to students and the community. These include career counseling, job training, and small business development. Many community colleges also offer community outreach programs, such as adult literacy classes, GED preparation, and ESL courses.

Community colleges are committed to serving the needs of their local communities. They partner with local businesses and organizations to provide job training and workforce development. They also work with local governments to provide educational opportunities for the citizens of their communities.

North Carolina's community colleges are an invaluable resource for individuals seeking to further their education and for communities looking to provide educational and employment opportunities. With a wide range of educational programs and services, the community college system is an essential part of the state's higher education system.
