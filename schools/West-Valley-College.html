Admission Requirements

West Valley College (WVC) is a two-year community college located in Saratoga, California. The college is part of the California Community Colleges system and is accredited by the Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges. WVC offers Associate of Arts and Associate of Science degrees, as well as certificates of achievement in over 50 fields of study.

Admission to WVC is open to all students who have a high school diploma or equivalent. Students must submit an application, transcripts, and a non-refundable application fee. International students must also submit proof of English proficiency. Once all required materials have been received, the college will determine if the student meets the minimum requirements for admission.

Students who do not meet the minimum requirements may be eligible for admission through the college’s Alternative Admission Process. This process allows students to demonstrate their ability to succeed in college-level coursework through a combination of factors, including high school grades, standardized test scores, and/or completion of college-level coursework.

Once admitted, students are required to complete a mandatory New Student Orientation prior to enrolling in classes. This orientation provides information about the college’s policies and procedures, as well as resources and services available to students.
