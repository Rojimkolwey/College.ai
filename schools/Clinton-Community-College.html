Clinton Community College (CCC) is a public community college in Clinton, Iowa. It is part of the Iowa Community College System. The college offers two-year associate's degrees and one-year certificates in over 50 areas of study.

Clinton Community College was founded in 1966 as a result of a community effort to establish a local college. The college opened its doors in 1968 with an initial enrollment of just over 500 students.

Since then, the college has grown steadily, serving over 4,000 students annually. CCC is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and is a member of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools.

The college has an active and supportive student government, which provides leadership and service opportunities for students. The Student Senate is the governing body for student organizations, and works to promote student involvement on campus.

The college offers a wide variety of student services, including academic advising, tutoring, career services, and personal counseling. CCC also has an active campus life, with over 30 student clubs and organizations.

Clinton Community College is committed to providing an affordable, quality education to its students. The college offers a variety of financial aid options, including scholarships, grants, and loans. CCC also offers a payment plan option for students who need assistance with tuition costs.

Clinton Community College is an excellent choice for students seeking a quality, affordable education. The college offers a wide range of programs and services to its students, and has a strong commitment to academic excellence.

Clinton Community College (CCC) is a public, two-year community college in Plattsburgh, New York. It is part of the State University of New York (SUNY) system. CCC offers associate degrees and certificate programs in a variety of academic and career fields.

To be eligible for admission to CCC, applicants must have a high school diploma or equivalent. They must also submit official transcripts and SAT or ACT scores. CCC uses a holistic admissions process, so applicants' essays and letters of recommendation are also considered.
