Notre Dame of Maryland University is a private Catholic university in Baltimore, Maryland. Founded in 1873 as a women's college, it is now co-educational. It is the oldest Catholic university in the United States and the only Catholic university in Maryland. The university has an enrollment of about 2,000 students, most of whom are women.

Notre Dame of Maryland University was founded in 1873 by the School Sisters of Notre Dame as the first Catholic women's college in the United States. The school was originally located on North Calvert Street in Baltimore, but moved to its current location on North Charles Street in 1876. In 1895, the school became a four-year college and began awarding bachelor's degrees. In 1896, the name was changed to Notre Dame College of Maryland. In 1911, the school began offering graduate degrees and the name was again changed, this time to Notre Dame of Maryland University.

Notre Dame of Maryland University is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education. The university offers bachelor's degrees in over 30 majors, as well as master's degrees in business, education, nursing, and counseling. The university also offers a doctorate in educational leadership.

Notre Dame of Maryland University has a strong commitment to service and social justice. The university's Center for Social Justice is dedicated to preparing students for lives of leadership and service. The center offers service-learning opportunities, internships, and fellowships. The university also has a robust study abroad program.

Notre Dame of Maryland University is a vibrant and welcoming community. The university's campus is located in the historic Mount Vernon neighborhood of Baltimore. The campus is home to a variety of academic, administrative, and residential buildings. The university also has a campus in Rome, Italy.

Notre Dame of Maryland University is a Catholic university committed to academic excellence and the development of the whole person. The university offers a supportive and nurturing environment where students can grow intellectually, spiritually, and personally. Notre Dame of Maryland University is a place where students can prepare for a lifetime of leadership and service.

Admission Requirements

Notre Dame of Maryland University is a private, Catholic university located in Baltimore, Maryland. The university was founded in 1873 by the School Sisters of Notre Dame and was the first women's college in the state of Maryland. The university offers a variety of undergraduate and graduate programs.

Undergraduate admission to Notre Dame of Maryland University is selective. The university looks for students who have demonstrated academic excellence, leadership potential, and a commitment to service. The university also takes into account an applicant's personal essay, recommendations, and extracurricular activities.

Graduate admission to Notre Dame of Maryland University is also selective. The university looks for students who have a bachelor's degree from an accredited institution, a minimum GPA of 3.0, and letters of recommendation. The university also requires a personal essay and a resume.
