The University of South Dakota (USD) is a public university in the U.S. state of South Dakota. The university is located in Vermillion, in the southeastern part of the state. USD was established in 1862 and is the oldest university in the state. The university is a member of the South Dakota Board of Regents, which governs the state's six public universities. USD offers more than 200 undergraduate and 70 graduate programs. The university has an enrollment of over 10,000 students. USD is home to the National Music Museum, the W.O. Farber Center for the Study of the Sioux, and the Center for the Study of the Native American. The university's athletic teams compete in the NCAA Division I Summit League.

Admission Requirements

The University of South Dakota is a public research university in Vermillion, South Dakota. The university is governed by the South Dakota Board of Regents. The university offers bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees in more than 200 areas of study.

Admission to the University of South Dakota is selective. The university uses a holistic review process when considering applications for admission. The university considers a student's academic record, test scores, essays, letters of recommendation, and extracurricular activities when making admissions decisions.

The University of South Dakota has a strong commitment to academic excellence. The university offers a wide range of academic programs that prepare students for success in their chosen field of study. The university is home to a nationally recognized faculty, and the university's research programs are among the best in the nation.

The University of South Dakota is located in a small city in the Midwest. The university is a short drive from Sioux Falls, and the university's campus is situated on a bluff overlooking the Missouri River. The university's location provides students with a unique opportunity to experience the culture and history of the Midwest.
