Shoreline Community College is a public community college in Shoreline, Washington. The college offers associate's degrees, bachelor's degrees, and certificate programs in a variety of fields.

Shoreline Community College was founded in 1964 as Shoreline Community College District 1. The college was originally located in the city of Shoreline, Washington. In its early years, the college served the local community with a variety of programs and courses.

In the 1970s, the college began to expand its programs and services. The college now offers a wide variety of associate's degrees, bachelor's degrees, and certificate programs. The college also offers a variety of continuing education and professional development courses.

Shoreline Community College is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities. The college is a member of the Washington State Board for Community and Technical Colleges.

Shoreline Community College serves the community by providing quality education and training. The college is committed to student success and to the economic development of the community.

Admission Requirements

Shoreline Community College (SCC) is a public community college in Shoreline, Washington. Founded in 1964, the college offers associate's degrees and professional certificates in over 100 programs. SCC is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities.

To be eligible for admission to SCC, applicants must have a high school diploma or equivalent. Applicants must also submit transcripts and take a placement test. International students must also submit proof of English proficiency.

Once admitted, students must complete orientation and meet with a counselor to develop a student education plan. Students must then register for classes and pay tuition.
