Coker University

Hartsville, SC
300 E College Ave, Hartsville, SC 29550
843-383-8000

Coker University is a private, coeducational college in Hartsville, South Carolina. The university is affiliated with the United Methodist Church. Coker University was founded in 1908 as a women's college. It became a coeducational institution in 1969. The university offers bachelor's degrees in arts, sciences, and business administration. Coker University is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges. The university's athletic teams compete in the NCAA Division II Conference Carolinas.

Admission Requirements

Coker University is a private institution that was founded in 1908. It has a total undergraduate enrollment of 1,077, its setting is rural, and the campus size is 100 acres. It utilizes a semester-based academic calendar. Coker University's ranking in the 2020 edition of Best Colleges is National Liberal Arts Colleges, #183. Its tuition and fees are $30,280 (2019-20).

Coker University is a small, private, liberal arts institution located in Hartsville, South Carolina. The university offers a wide variety of undergraduate and graduate programs, as well as a number of professional programs. Coker is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges to award bachelor's and master's degrees.

Coker University has an open admissions policy, meaning that any student who has completed the necessary high school coursework and has a high school diploma or equivalent is eligible to apply for admission. However, preference is given to students who have a strong academic record and who have demonstrated leadership qualities. In order to be considered for admission, students must submit an application, transcripts, and SAT or ACT scores.
