Minnesota State University-Mankato

Mankato, MN
South Rd and Ellis Ave, Mankato, MN 56001
507-389-1866

Minnesota State University-Mankato is a public university in Mankato, Minnesota. The school was founded in 1868 as the Mankato Normal School, and it is the second oldest member of the Minnesota State Colleges and Universities system. The school has an enrollment of over 15,000 students, and it offers bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees in a variety of fields. The school is divided into eight colleges, and its athletic teams compete in the NCAA Division II. Minnesota State University-Mankato is a member of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools.

Admission Requirements

Minnesota State University-Mankato's undergraduate admissions requirements are designed to ensure that students have the academic skills and preparation necessary to be successful in college-level coursework. All undergraduate students must have a high school diploma or equivalent, and must submit official transcripts from all previous institutions attended. Additionally, students must meet the minimum GPA requirements for their desired program of study. For most programs, this is a 2.0 GPA, but some programs may require a higher GPA.

In addition to academic requirements, all undergraduate students must also submit a completed application form, along with a non-refundable application fee. Once all required materials have been received, the Admissions Committee will review the file and make a decision on whether or not to offer admission. Admission decisions are based on a number of factors, including but not limited to: high school GPA, class rank, standardized test scores, and the strength of the student's application materials.
