Jackson College is a small, private college located in Jackson, Michigan. The college was founded in 1866 and is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. Jackson College offers associate's degrees in a variety of fields, as well as bachelor's degrees in business and nursing. The college also has a strong community college program, which offers associate's degrees and certificate programs. Jackson College is a member of the Michigan Association of Colleges and Universities.

Admission Requirements

Jackson College is a two-year college located in Jackson, Michigan. The college offers associate degrees and certificates in a variety of fields.

Admission to Jackson College is open to all students who have a high school diploma or equivalent. The college does not have any specific admission requirements, but does encourage students to submit an application and take placement tests.

Students who wish to enroll in Jackson College can do so by submitting an application online or in person. The college has an open enrollment policy, which means that students are not required to submit any additional materials, such as transcripts or essays.

Once an application has been submitted, students will be asked to take placement tests. These tests are used to determine which courses are best suited for each student. The tests are not required for admission, but are strongly encouraged.

After the placement tests have been taken, students will meet with an advisor to discuss their course options. Advisors will help students select courses that fit their interests and career goals.

Once students have registered for classes, they will be able to access their course materials online. Jackson College uses an online learning platform called Blackboard. Blackboard provides students with access to their course syllabi, assignments, and grades.

Jackson College is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. The college is also a member of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools.
