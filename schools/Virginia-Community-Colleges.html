Virginia Community Colleges


Virginia's community colleges are a great resource for students looking to pursue a post-secondary education. The state's 23 community colleges offer a wide range of associate degrees, certificates, and workforce training programs. Virginia's community colleges provide affordable tuition and fees, flexible scheduling options, and a variety of support services.

Virginia's community colleges are open to all students, regardless of prior academic performance. Students can choose from over 1,000 degree and certificate programs, including many that are designed to meet the needs of today's workforce. Many of Virginia's community colleges also offer transfer programs, allowing students to transfer credits earned at one college to another and complete their degree or certificate program.

Virginia's community college system is known for its exceptional student services. All campuses provide academic counseling, career planning and job placement services, and financial aid resources. Additionally, many campuses offer student clubs, clubs and organizations, and recreational activities. Many of Virginia's community colleges also have state-of-the-art facilities, such as libraries, computer labs, and gyms.

For those looking to continue their education, Virginia's community colleges also offer a variety of baccalaureate degrees and graduate programs. These programs are offered in partnership with four-year institutions, allowing students to complete their bachelor's or master's degree without ever leaving the comfort of their community college campus.

Virginia's community colleges are an excellent option for those looking to get a quality education without spending a fortune. With flexible scheduling options and a variety of support services, Virginia's community colleges are a great choice for students looking to further their education.
