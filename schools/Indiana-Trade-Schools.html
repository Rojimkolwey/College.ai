About Indiana

The state of Indiana is located in the Midwestern United States. It is bordered by the states of Illinois, Kentucky, Michigan and Ohio. Indiana is the 38th largest state in the United States with an area of 36,418 square miles. The capital of Indiana is Indianapolis and the largest city is Indianapolis. The population of Indiana is 6,691,878 as of 2019.

Indiana is a diverse state with a variety of industries. The state is home to the Indianapolis 500, the Indianapolis Motor Speedway, and the NCAA Men's Basketball Final Four. Indiana is also home to a number of large corporations, including Eli Lilly and Company, the Simon Property Group, and the steel company, Nucor.

The state of Indiana has a long history. The first people to live in the area were the Native Americans. The French explorer, Rene-Robert Cavelier, Sieur de La Salle, was the first European to visit the area in 1679. The British gained control of the area in 1763 and it became part of the United States in 1783. Indiana became a state in 1816.

The state of Indiana has a lot to offer its residents and visitors. There are a number of different attractions, including museums, parks, and historical sites. Indiana is also home to a number of community colleges, including Indiana University, Purdue University, and Notre Dame University.
