Long Beach City College

Long Beach, CA
4901 E. Carson St., Long Beach, CA 90808-1706
562-938-4111

Long Beach City College (LBCC) is a public community college in Long Beach, California. It is the third largest college in the state of California and the largest community college in the United States. LBCC serves more than 70,000 students each semester and offers over 1,700 courses in over 100 academic and career programs.

LBCC was founded in 1927 as a junior college. It was the first community college in California and the second community college in the United States. LBCC has grown significantly since its founding and now offers a wide variety of academic and career programs.

LBCC is accredited by the Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges of the Western Association of Schools and Colleges. It is also a member of the California Community Colleges System and the National Junior College Athletic Association.

LBCC offers a variety of student services, including counseling, tutoring, financial aid, and career services. The college also has a robust athletics program, with teams competing in a variety of sports.

Long Beach City College is an excellent choice for students seeking an affordable, high-quality education. The college offers a wide variety of academic and career programs, and its student services are second to none.

Admission Requirements

In order to be eligible for admission to Long Beach City College (LBCC), applicants must possess a high school diploma or equivalent. If you have not completed high school, you may still be eligible for admission if you pass the California High School Proficiency Examination (CHSPE) or the General Educational Development (GED) test. LBCC also offers an Adult Basic Education program for students who need to improve their basic academic skills before enrolling in college-level courses.

Once you have met the basic eligibility requirements, you will need to submit an application for admission. The application can be completed online or in person at the Admissions and Records Office. You will also need to submit official transcripts from all previous colleges and universities you have attended. If you have not attended college before, you will need to submit official high school transcripts or equivalent.

Once your application has been processed, you will be contacted by the Admissions and Records Office to schedule an appointment for an orientation session. Orientation is mandatory for all new students and will provide you with important information about registering for classes, using campus facilities, and other services available to you as a student at LBCC.
