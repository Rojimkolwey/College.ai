University of Toledo

Toledo, OH
2801 W Bancroft, Toledo, OH 43606-3390
800-586-5336

The University of Toledo, located in Toledo, Ohio, is a public research university with more than 20,000 students. Founded in 1872, the University of Toledo is a member of the Ohio State University System and offers more than 300 undergraduate and graduate programs in a wide range of disciplines. The University of Toledo is home to the nation's largest solar research and development center, the UT Solar Center, and is a leading institution in the fields of alternative energy and sustainability. The University of Toledo is also home to the world-renowned Toledo Museum of Art, one of the nation's top art museums, and the Toledo Zoo, one of the nation's top zoos.

Admission Requirements

The University of Toledo requires a few different things for admission into their institution. For high school students, they need either a 2.5 GPA or a 21 on the ACT/1070 on the SAT. They also need to have completed high school within the last five years and have earned a high school diploma or GED. For transfer students, they need a 2.0 GPA from all previous college coursework. Lastly, they need to have completed any developmental coursework and have official transcripts sent to the university.
