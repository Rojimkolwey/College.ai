About New Jersey

New Jersey is a state located in the Northeastern and Middle Atlantic regions of the United States. It is bordered by New York to the north and east, the Atlantic Ocean to the east and south, and Pennsylvania to the west. The state is named after the island of Jersey in the English Channel. The capital of New Jersey is Trenton, and the largest city is Newark.

New Jersey is the fourth smallest state by area, but it is the 11th most populous state. As of 2019, the population was 8,882,190. The state has a diverse population, with large communities of Italian, Irish, Polish, and Hispanic descent.

New Jersey is known for its beaches, especially along the Jersey Shore, which is a popular summer tourist destination. The state is also home to the Liberty Science Center, the Newark Museum, and the New Jersey Performing Arts Center.

The first European settlers in New Jersey were the Dutch, who established the colony of New Netherland in the early 17th century. In 1664, the English conquered the Dutch colony and renamed it New Jersey. The English colony was divided into two parts, East Jersey and West Jersey. In 1776, New Jersey was one of the thirteen colonies that revolted against British rule in the American Revolution.

After the war, New Jersey was the third state to ratify the United States Constitution, in 1787. The state became a major center of industry and commerce in the 19th century. In the 20th century, New Jersey's economy shifted from manufacturing to service industries.

Today, New Jersey is a leading state in the pharmaceutical and biotechnology industries. The state is also home to many major financial and telecommunications companies. New Jersey has a diverse economy, with significant contributions from the agricultural, manufacturing, service, and tourism sectors.
