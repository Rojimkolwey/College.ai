New Mexico State University-Dona Ana is a two-year community college located in the city of Las Cruces, New Mexico, United States. It is part of the New Mexico State University System. The school offers associate's degrees and certificate programs in a variety of fields, including business, education, health care, and liberal arts.

The college was founded in 1973 as a branch campus of New Mexico State University. It became a separate institution in 2009. The school has an enrollment of about 4,000 students.

New Mexico State University-Dona Ana is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. The school's nursing program is accredited by the Accreditation Commission for Education in Nursing.

The college offers a variety of student services, including academic advising, career counseling, and tutoring. The school also has a bookstore, a library, and a computer lab.

New Mexico State University-Dona Ana is a member of the National Junior College Athletic Association (NJCAA) and competes in Region V of the NJCAA. The school's sports teams include men's and women's basketball, cross country, and track and field.
