Bon Secours Memorial College of Nursing is a private, Catholic college located in Richmond, Virginia. The college is affiliated with the Bon Secours Health System and offers a four-year Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BSN) degree. The college also offers an RN-to-BSN program for registered nurses who wish to earn their BSN.

The college was founded in 1874 by the Sisters of Bon Secours as a school of nursing. The college is accredited by the Commission on Collegiate Nursing Education (CCNE) and is a member of the Virginia Nurses Association.

The college's four-year BSN program is designed to prepare students for a career in nursing. The program includes coursework in liberal arts, sciences, and nursing. Students also complete clinical rotations in various medical settings.

The college's RN-to-BSN program is designed for registered nurses who wish to earn their BSN. The program includes coursework in liberal arts, sciences, and nursing. Students also complete clinical rotations in various medical settings.

The college's faculty includes experienced nurses and educators who are committed to providing a quality education to their students. The college's small class sizes allow for personal attention and interaction between students and faculty.

The college's campus is located in Richmond, Virginia, a city with a rich history and a variety of cultural and recreational activities. The college is situated near several hospitals and medical facilities, providing students with opportunities for clinical experience.

The college's alumni are active in the nursing profession and are committed to serving the community. The college's alumni association provides support and networking opportunities for alumni.

The college's mission is to prepare competent, caring, and ethical nurses who are committed to serving the health needs of the community. The college's vision is to be a leader in nursing education and to prepare nurses who are prepared to meet the challenges of a changing healthcare environment.

Admission to the MSN program is based on a student's undergraduate GPA, GRE scores, and letters of recommendation.
