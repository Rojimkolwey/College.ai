The University of Georgia, also known as UGA or simply Georgia, is a public flagship research university with its main campus in Athens, Georgia. Founded in 1785, UGA is one of the oldest public universities in the United States. The university is classified as a Research I university by the Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education. It is also a member of the University System of Georgia and is one of four public comprehensive research universities in the state. The university offers more than 140 undergraduate and over 200 graduate degree programs in a wide range of disciplines.

As the state's flagship university, UGA is considered one of the Public Ivies, meaning that it is a public university of the same caliber as the Ivy League. The university is also a land-grant and sea-grant institution and a member of the Association of American Universities, the Universities Research Association, and the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools. The university's historic North Campus is listed on the U.S. National Register of Historic Places as a designated historic district. The contiguous campus areas include rolling hills, gardens, and extensive green space including nature walks, fields, shrubbery, and large and small bodies of water.

The University of Georgia's athletic teams, collectively known as the Georgia Bulldogs, compete in National Collegiate Athletic Association (NCAA) Division I and the Southeastern Conference (SEC). UGA served as a founding member of the SEC in 1932. In their more than 120-year history, the Bulldogs have won 41 national championships, including 27 NCAA team championships, and have claimed 118 individual national championships. The Georgia Redcoat Marching Band, the official marching band of the university, performs at athletic and other events.

Admission Requirements

The University of Georgia (UGA) is a public research university with its main campus in Athens, Georgia. Founded in 1785, UGA is one of the oldest public universities in the United States. UGA is the flagship university for the University System of Georgia and is classified as a Research I university by the Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education. The university enrolls approximately 37,000 students from all 50 states and over 140 countries.

UGA offers over 140 undergraduate and over 200 graduate and professional degree programs. The university is organized into seventeen schools and colleges: the Franklin College of Arts and Sciences, the Georgia School of Business, the College of Education, the College of Engineering, the College of Environment and Design, the College of Family and Consumer Sciences, the School of Forestry and Natural Resources, the College of Journalism and Mass Communication, the School of Law, the College of Pharmacy, the College of Public and International Affairs, the School of Social Work, the Graduate School, the Honors Program, the Odum School of Ecology, and the Terry College of Business.

UGA admissions are need-blind for U.S. citizens and permanent residents. The university has an acceptance rate of 53%. For the class of 2023, UGA received a record-breaking 41,084 applications; 8,608 were admitted, for an acceptance rate of 21%. The middle 50% range of SAT scores for admitted students was 1240-1410, and the middle 50% range of ACT scores was 27-32. The average high school GPA for admitted students was 3.93.

UGA requires all first-year applicants to submit an application, high school transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a non-refundable $50 application fee. International students must also submit proof of English proficiency. UGA does not require the submission of letters of recommendation or an application essay, but strongly recommends that applicants submit at least one letter of recommendation and an essay. The admissions committee reviews applications holistically, taking into consideration the student's academic record, test scores, extracurricular activities, letters of recommendation, and application essay.
