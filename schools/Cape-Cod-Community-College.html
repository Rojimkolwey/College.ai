Cape Cod Community College

West Barnstable, MA
2240 Iyannough Rd, West Barnstable, MA 02668-1599
508-362-2131

Cape Cod Community College (CCCC) is a public community college in Barnstable, Massachusetts. The college offers associate degrees and certificate programs in a variety of academic and vocational fields.

CCCC was founded in 1961 as Cape Cod Vocational Technical Institute. The school was renamed Cape Cod Community College in 1963. The college moved to its current location in West Barnstable in 1967.

CCCC is accredited by the New England Association of Schools and Colleges. The college is a member of the Massachusetts Community College System and the American Association of Community Colleges.

CCCC offers over 50 academic and vocational programs. The college also offers a variety of continuing education and workforce development programs.

CCCC has an enrollment of over 4,000 students. The college employs over 400 faculty and staff members.

The college is governed by a nine-member Board of Trustees. The trustees are appointed by the Governor of Massachusetts.

The college president is Dr. John Cox.

Admission Requirements

Cape Cod Community College (CCCC) is a public community college in West Barnstable, Massachusetts. CCCC enrolls approximately 6,000 students annually, and offers over 50 academic programs.

Admission to CCCC is open to all students who have a high school diploma or equivalent. CCCC does not have an application fee.

Students interested in attending CCCC should submit the following materials:

-A completed application form

-Official high school transcripts or equivalent

-SAT or ACT scores (if applicable)

Once all materials have been received, the CCCC Admissions Office will review the student's file and determine whether or not the student meets the minimum admission requirements. If the student meets the requirements, they will be issued an acceptance letter.

Students who are not accepted to CCCC may appeal the decision by submitting additional materials, such as a letter of recommendation or an essay. Appeals are reviewed on a case-by-case basis.
