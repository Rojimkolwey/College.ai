Admission Requirements

Mayland Community College (MCC) is a public community college in North Carolina. The college is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges. MCC offers associate degrees, diplomas, and certificates in over 50 programs of study.

Admission to MCC is open to all high school graduates and GED holders. The college does not require any entrance exams for admission. Students must complete an application and submit all required transcripts and documents. International students must also submit proof of English proficiency.

MCC has an open-admission policy for most programs. However, some programs have selective admission requirements. These programs include Allied Health, Dental Assisting, Emergency Medical Science, Nursing, and Radiography. Students interested in these programs must meet additional requirements, such as minimum GPA, prerequisite coursework, and/or an entrance exam.

MCC also offers a dual enrollment program for high school students. This program allows students to take college courses and earn college credit while still in high school. Students must meet certain eligibility requirements to participate in the program.
