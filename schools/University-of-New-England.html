University of New England

Biddeford, ME
11 Hills Beach Rd, Biddeford, ME 04005-9599
207-283-0171

The University of New England is a private research university in Biddeford, Maine. The school was founded in 1831 as a medical college and became a university in 1934. The university is made up of five colleges: the College of Arts and Sciences, the College of Business and Economics, the College of Education and Human Development, the College of Pharmacy, and the College of Osteopathic Medicine. The university has an enrollment of about 6,000 students, with a campus size of nearly 500 acres. The university is known for its strong programs in the liberal arts and sciences, as well as its health sciences programs. The University of New England is accredited by the New England Association of Schools and Colleges.

Admission Requirements

The University of New England is a private research university in New England with an acceptance rate of 72%. The university has an undergraduate student body of around 4,000 students and offers over 80 majors and programs. The most popular majors at University of New England include Business, Management, Marketing, and Related Support Services; Health Professions and Related Programs; Biological and Biomedical Sciences; and Psychology. The university also offers a number of online and distance learning programs.

To be admitted to the University of New England, students must submit an application, high school transcripts, and SAT or ACT scores. The university does not require letters of recommendation. The university has a rolling admissions policy and typically makes decisions within two weeks of receiving all required materials.
