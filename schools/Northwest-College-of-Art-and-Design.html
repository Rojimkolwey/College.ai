Northwest College of Art & Design

Tacoma, WA
1126 Pacific Avenue, Suite 101, Tacoma, WA 98402
253-272-1126

Northwest College of Art & Design (NCAD) is a small, private, accredited college located in the heart of downtown Tacoma, Washington. The college specializes in the visual arts, offering Bachelor of Fine Arts degrees in Animation, Fine Arts, Graphic Design, and Illustration, as well as Associate of Fine Arts degrees in Animation, Fine Arts, and Graphic Design. NCAD is also home to the Northwest School of Wooden Boatbuilding, which offers a two-year professional certificate in boatbuilding.

NCAD was founded in 1934 as the Tacoma School of Arts, a community art school offering classes to both children and adults. In the 1950s, the school began offering college-level courses, and in the 1960s, the school was renamed the Tacoma School of the Arts and began offering two-year Associate of Fine Arts degrees. In the 1970s, the school was renamed again, this time to the Northwest College of Art, and began offering four-year Bachelor of Fine Arts degrees. In the 1980s, the college added a graphic design program and in the 1990s, an animation program. In 2006, the college was renamed to its current name, the Northwest College of Art & Design.

NCAD is accredited by the National Association of Schools of Art and Design (NASAD) and is a member of the Northwest Association of Schools and Colleges (NWACC). The college is also home to the NCAD Gallery, which features rotating exhibitions of contemporary art by regional, national, and international artists.

Admission Requirements

Northwest College of Art & Design (NCAD) is a small, private, accredited college located in the heart of Portland’s Pearl District. We offer Bachelor of Fine Arts (BFA) and Master of Fine Arts (MFA) degrees in Illustration, Graphic Design, Animation, and Game Art & Design, as well as a minor in Creative Writing. Our curriculum is designed to prepare students for a successful career in the creative industries.

NCAD is accredited by the National Association of Schools of Art and Design (NASAD). We are also a member of the Association of Independent Colleges of Art and Design (AICAD).

Admission to NCAD is based on a portfolio review and an interview with the Admissions Committee. We are looking for students who have a strong interest in and commitment to the study of art and design. We also consider academic achievement, artistic potential, and personal motivation when making our admissions decisions.

To be considered for admission, applicants must submit the following:

-A completed application form

-A non-refundable application fee of $50

-Official transcripts from all colleges and universities attended

-A portfolio of artwork

Applicants are encouraged to submit a portfolio that demonstrates a range of skills and interests. The portfolio should include 10-20 examples of your best work, and can include drawings, paintings, photographs, digital art, or other types of visual art.

Interviews are conducted on a rolling basis, and we encourage you to apply early. Once your application and portfolio have been reviewed, you will be contacted to schedule an interview.

We look forward to reviewing your application and getting to know you better!
