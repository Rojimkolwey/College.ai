Anderson University is a private university in Anderson, South Carolina. The university is affiliated with the South Carolina Baptist Convention and offers undergraduate and graduate degrees in liberal arts, business, education, music, nursing, and theology. Anderson University has approximately 3,000 students.

Academics

The university offers 56 undergraduate majors and 40 undergraduate minors, as well as several graduate degrees.

Athletics

Anderson University is a NCAA Division II school and competes in the South Atlantic Conference. The university's teams are nicknamed the Trojans. The university's official colors are black and gold. The university fields teams in men's and women's basketball, men's and women's cross country, men's and women's golf, men's and women's soccer, softball, men's and women's tennis, men's and women's track and field, and volleyball.

Student life

The university has approximately 3,000 students. The university's campus is located in Anderson, South Carolina. The university offers a variety of on-campus housing options, including residence halls, apartments, and townhouses. The university also has a variety of student organizations and clubs, including fraternities and sororities.

Admission Requirements

Anderson University is a private university in Anderson, South Carolina. It is affiliated with the South Carolina Baptist Convention and offers bachelor's and master's degrees in a variety of disciplines. Admission to Anderson University is selective, and the university seeks students who are academically prepared and who will contribute to the university community. The university evaluates each applicant on their individual merits, and there are no minimum test score or GPA requirements for admission. The university does require that all applicants submit an application, transcripts, and a non-refundable application fee. International students are also required to submit proof of English proficiency. Anderson University has a rolling admissions policy, and applications are reviewed as they are received. Decisions are typically made within two weeks of receipt of a complete application.
