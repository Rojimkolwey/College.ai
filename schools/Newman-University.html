Newman University

Wichita, KS
3100 McCormick Ave, Wichita, KS 67213-2097
316-942-4291

Newman University is a private Catholic university in Wichita, Kansas. It was founded by the Adorers of the Blood of Christ in 1933. The university is named for John Henry Newman, an English Catholic priest and theologian.

Newman University offers bachelor's and master's degrees in a variety of disciplines. The university is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission.

Newman University's campus is located on 85 acres (34 ha) of land in northeast Wichita. The university's main academic building, St. John's Hall, was built in 1887 and is on the National Register of Historic Places.

Newman University has an enrollment of about 2,000 students. The university's student-to-faculty ratio is 14-to-1.

Newman University's athletics teams are known as the Jets and compete in the National Association of Intercollegiate Athletics (NAIA). The university's men's and women's basketball teams both won the NAIA national championship in 2019.

Admission Requirements

Newman University is a Catholic university located in Wichita, Kansas. The school was founded in 1933 and is named after John Henry Newman, a 19th-century English Cardinal. The university is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission.

Newman University offers over 50 undergraduate and graduate programs. The most popular majors are Nursing, Education, and Business. Admission to Newman University is selective, with an acceptance rate of 65%. The average SAT score for incoming freshman is 1070.

To be considered for admission, students must submit an application, transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a $30 application fee. International students must also submit proof of English proficiency. Newman University does not offer on-campus housing, but there are several off-campus housing options available.
