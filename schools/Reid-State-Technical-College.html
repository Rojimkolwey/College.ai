Reid State Technical College

Evergreen, AL
100 and Hwy 83, Evergreen, AL 36401
251-578-1313

Reid State Technical College is a public, two-year technical college located in Evergreen, Alabama, United States. The college is part of the Alabama Community College System. Reid State offers programs in Automotive Technology, Collision Repair Technology, Diesel Technology, HVAC Technology, Industrial Maintenance Technology, and Welding Technology. The college also offers a General Studies program for students who wish to complete their associate degree or transfer to a four-year institution.

Reid State was founded in 1963 as a trade school for the unemployed and underemployed citizens of Conecuh County. The school was named after John Reid, a local businessman and philanthropist who donated the land for the school. The first class of students enrolled in the fall of 1964. In its early years, the college offered programs in Automotive Mechanics, Diesel Mechanics, and Welding.

In the late 1960s, the college began to offer programs in other trades, such as Carpentry, Electrical Wiring, and Masonry. In 1971, the college was accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools. In 1972, the college began offering its first associate degree program, in General Studies. In the 1980s, the college added programs in Computer Science, Nursing, and Radiologic Technology.

In the 1990s, the college experienced significant growth, adding several new buildings and expanding its programs and services. In 1998, the college was renamed Reid State Technical College to reflect its status as a technical college. In 1999, the college added a campus in Atmore, Alabama.

Today, Reid State Technical College is a comprehensive two-year technical college serving the citizens of Conecuh and Escambia Counties. The college offers programs in a variety of trades and technical fields, as well as a General Studies program for students who wish to complete their associate degree or transfer to a four-year institution. The college is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools and is a member of the Alabama Community College System.

Admission Requirements

Reid State Technical College is committed to providing quality technical education and training that meets the needs of business, industry, and the community. The College offers programs in a variety of technical and career fields.

Admission to the College is open to any person who is a high school graduate or who has a GED certificate. The College does not discriminate on the basis of race, color, creed, religion, sex, age, national origin, or disability.

All applicants must complete an application for admission and submit all required transcripts and test scores. Applicants who have not yet completed high school or who have not taken the GED must complete the College Placement Test before being admitted to the College.

Applicants who have been out of school for more than five years may be required to take the College Placement Test or submit other evidence of their ability to benefit from the education and training offered at the College.

Once all required materials have been received, the Admissions Committee will review the application and make a decision on admission. The College will notify the applicant of the decision in writing.

Applicants who are not admitted to the College may reapply for admission after one semester.
