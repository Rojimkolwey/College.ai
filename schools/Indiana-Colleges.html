Indiana Universities and Colleges


Indiana is home to a wide variety of colleges and universities, ranging from small community colleges to large research universities. Indiana is home to two major public universities, Indiana University and Purdue University. Both of these universities offer a wide range of degree programs and are highly respected research institutions.

In addition to these two major universities, Indiana is home to several smaller public universities, including Ball State University, Indiana State University, and the University of Evansville. These universities offer a variety of degree programs, including engineering, business, and the arts.

Indiana also has a number of private colleges and universities, such as Butler University, DePauw University, and Wabash College. These institutions are known for their strong liberal arts programs and rigorous academic standards.

In addition to its four-year institutions, Indiana is home to a number of two-year community colleges, such as Ivy Tech Community College, Vincennes University, and Indiana University–Purdue University Fort Wayne. These schools offer associate’s degrees and certificates, and are a great option for those looking to save money on their college education.

Finally, Indiana is home to a number of specialty schools, such as the Indianapolis Art Institute and the University of Notre Dame. These schools offer unique programs that cater to the specific interests of their students.

Overall, Indiana has a wide variety of colleges and universities to choose from, and each school offers something unique. Whether you’re looking for a large research university or a small liberal arts school, Indiana has something for everyone.
