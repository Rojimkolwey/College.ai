The University of Alabama

Tuscaloosa, AL
739 University Blvd, Tuscaloosa, AL 35487-0100
205-348-6010

Founded in 1820, The University of Alabama is a senior comprehensive doctoral-level institution located in Tuscaloosa, Alabama, United States. The University of Alabama offers programs of study in 13 academic divisions leading to bachelor's, master's, Education Specialist, and doctoral degrees. The only publicly supported law school in Alabama is at The University of Alabama. The University of Alabama's intercollegiate sports teams, collectively known as the Alabama Crimson Tide, compete in National Collegiate Athletic Association (NCAA) Division I as a member of the Southeastern Conference (SEC).

Admission Requirements

In order to be admitted to The University of Alabama, students must meet the following requirements: have a high school diploma or equivalent, submit an application and application fee, and provide official transcripts from all previous institutions attended. Additionally, students must meet the minimum ACT or SAT score requirements and have a minimum GPA of 2.0. International students must also submit proof of English proficiency.
