South University-Savannah

Savannah, GA
709 Mall Blvd, Savannah, GA 31406
912-201-8000

Founded in 1899, South University is a private, nonprofit institution with a long history of driving student success. Through a commitment to excellence in education, South University prepares students to pursue rewarding careers and to become contributing members of society.

Located in Savannah, Georgia, South University-Savannah offers a unique and supportive learning environment where students can pursue their educational goals. The campus is situated in the historic district of the city, providing students with easy access to a variety of cultural and recreational opportunities.

South University-Savannah offers a variety of degree programs at the undergraduate and graduate level. Programs are designed to provide students with the knowledge and skills necessary to succeed in their chosen field. South University-Savannah is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges to award degrees at the bachelor's, master's, and doctor's level.

South University-Savannah is committed to providing students with an exceptional education. The faculty and staff are dedicated to helping students achieve their academic and professional goals. The university provides a variety of resources and support services to help students succeed. South University-Savannah is committed to providing a quality education that is affordable and accessible.

Admission Requirements

South University-Savannah is a private, for-profit university located in Savannah, Georgia. The university is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges to award bachelor's and master's degrees. The university is a member of the Georgia Independent College Association.

The university offers a variety of undergraduate and graduate programs. Undergraduate programs are offered in business administration, criminal justice, healthcare management, and psychology. Graduate programs are offered in business administration, counseling, nursing, and psychology.

Admission to the university is selective. The university requires applicants to submit an application, transcripts, and SAT or ACT scores. The university also considers letters of recommendation, essays, and extracurricular activities in the admission process.
