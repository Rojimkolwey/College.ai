Minnesota West Community and Technical College (Minnesota West) is a two-year technical and community college with five locations in southwestern Minnesota. The main campus is located in Granite Falls, Minnesota. The college offers over 50 programs in a variety of areas, including agriculture, business, health, and manufacturing.

Minnesota West was founded in 1965 as a two-year technical college. The college has grown significantly since then, and now has over 5,000 students enrolled. Minnesota West offers a variety of programs and services to its students, including career and academic counseling, tutoring, and job placement assistance.

Minnesota West is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and is a member of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools. The college is also accredited by the Minnesota State Board of Education.

Admission Requirements

Minnesota West Community and Technical College is a two-year public college located in the southwestern Minnesota town of Granite Falls. The college offers associate's degrees and certificates in over 50 areas of study.

To be eligible for admission to Minnesota West, students must have a high school diploma or equivalent. Students must also take a placement test to determine which courses they will need to take. The college has an open admissions policy, meaning that all students who meet the minimum requirements are accepted.

Once students are admitted to the college, they must complete a new student orientation and register for classes. Classes at Minnesota West are offered on a semester basis, and students must complete a minimum of 12 credits per semester to be considered full-time.

Students at Minnesota West can choose to live in on-campus housing or off-campus housing. On-campus housing is available at the college's Granite Falls and Pipestone campuses. Off-campus housing is available in the surrounding communities.

Minnesota West is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. The college is a member of the Minnesota State Colleges and Universities system.
