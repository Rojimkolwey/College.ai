Founded in 1789, the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill is the oldest public university in the United States. It is also one of the 17 campuses of the University of North Carolina system. The university is located in Chapel Hill, North Carolina, and is known for its strong research programs. The university is home to the Kenan-Flagler Business School, the School of Law, the School of Medicine, the School of Education, the Gillings School of Global Public Health, the School of Social Work, and the School of Information and Library Science. The university also has a strong liberal arts program, and is home to the Morehead Planetarium and the Carolina Performing Arts Center. The university's sports teams, the Tar Heels, compete in the NCAA Division I.

Admission Requirements

The University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill is a public university located in Chapel Hill, North Carolina. It is one of the 17 campuses of the University of North Carolina system. The university is also home to the Kenan-Flagler Business School and the School of Law. The university was founded in 1789, making it one of the oldest public universities in the United States. The university is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools.

The university has an undergraduate enrollment of 18,600 students and a graduate enrollment of 6,400 students. The student-to-faculty ratio is 16:1. The average SAT score for incoming freshmen is 1310 and the average ACT score is 29. The middle 50% of incoming freshmen have SAT scores between 1240 and 1410. The middle 50% of incoming freshmen have ACT scores between 27 and 32.

The university has a rolling admissions policy. The deadline for applications is January 1. The university does not require the SAT or ACT for admission. However, students who submit SAT or ACT scores will receive priority consideration for scholarships and honors programs. The university does not have an application fee.

The university offers over 200 undergraduate majors and minors. The most popular majors include business, psychology, biology, and political science. The university also offers a number of graduate programs, including business, law, medicine, dentistry, nursing, and education.

The University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill is committed to providing a world-class education to all students, regardless of their financial circumstances. The university offers a number of need-based and merit-based scholarships. In addition, the university offers a number of payment plans to help make college more affordable.
