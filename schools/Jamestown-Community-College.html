Jamestown Community College is a public community college in Jamestown, New York. It is part of the State University of New York (SUNY) system. The college was founded in 1950 and offers associate's degrees and certificate programs. The college has an enrollment of approximately 3,000 students. The college's campus is located on a hill overlooking downtown Jamestown. The college offers a variety of academic programs, as well as a variety of student clubs and organizations. The college is accredited by the Middle States Association of Colleges and Schools.

Admission Requirements

Jamestown Community College (JCC) is a public community college in New York state. JCC offers more than 50 academic programs and awards associate degrees and certificates. JCC is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education.

Admission to JCC is open to all students who have a high school diploma or equivalent. JCC does not have an application fee. Students must submit an official high school transcript or equivalent and SAT or ACT scores (if available). JCC also accepts GED scores and home-schooled students may be eligible for admission.

JCC has a rolling admissions policy, which means that students can apply at any time and will receive a decision within a few weeks. JCC does not have a specific deadline, but students are encouraged to apply early. JCC operates on a semester system, with Fall and Spring semesters.

JCC has a number of financial aid options available to students, including grants, loans, and scholarships. Students must complete the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) in order to be considered for financial aid. JCC also offers a payment plan option for students who are unable to pay their tuition in full.
