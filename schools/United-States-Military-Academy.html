United States Military Academy

West Point, NY

The United States Military Academy (USMA), also known as West Point, Army, Army West Point, The Academy, or simply The Point, is a four-year coeducational federal service academy located in West Point, New York, in Orange County. It was originally established as a fort that sits on strategic high ground overlooking the Hudson River with a scenic view, 50mi north of New York City. It is one of the five U.S. service academies.

The Academy was founded in 1802 and is the oldest of the five service academies. The Academy's stated mission is "to educate, train, and inspire the Corps of Cadets so that each graduate is a commissioned leader of character committed to the values of Duty, Honor, Country and prepared for a career of professional excellence and service to the Nation as an officer in the United States Army." It is the second-oldest of the United States' five service academies in continuous operation, after the United States Naval Academy and is one of the nation's oldest institutions of higher education.

The Academy is also one of the most selective colleges in the United States, with an acceptance rate of 11.3%, accepting 1,200 cadets from a pool of over 14,000 applicants. Cadets are required to adhere to the Cadet Honor Code, which states that "a cadet will not lie, cheat, or steal, or tolerate those who do." The academy trains cadets for commissioning into the United States Army. The academy's four-year curriculum is focused on the liberal arts for all cadets with a final year focused on service to the country as an Army officer.

Upon graduation, cadets receive a Bachelor of Science degree and are commissioned as second lieutenants in the United States Army. Cadets who graduate in the top 5% of their class are awarded the prestigious Thayer Award. The academy has produced a total of 36 Rhodes Scholars, 31 Marshall Scholars, and 16 Truman Scholars. Cadets compete in one intercollegiate sport every fall, winter, and spring season at the NCAA Division I level. A variety of intramural and club sports are available. Most graduates go on to serve as Army officers in the United States Army.

Admission Requirements

The United States Military Academy at West Point is one of the most prestigious institutions in the country. Founded in 1802, West Point is a four-year coeducational federal service academy. Located 50 miles north of New York City, West Point is situated on 16,000 acres along the Hudson River. The mission of the United States Military Academy is to "educate, train, and inspire the Corps of Cadets so that each graduate is a commissioned leader of character committed to the values of Duty, Honor, Country and prepared for a career of professional excellence and service to the Nation as an officer in the United States Army."

To be eligible for admission to West Point, candidates must be at least 17 years of age and not older than 23 on July 1 of the year of admission. Candidates must also be unmarried, have no dependents, and be citizens of the United States. In addition to these general requirements, candidates must meet the physical, mental, and medical standards for admission.

The physical standards for admission are that candidates must be able to complete the following: a timed two-mile run, sit-ups, and push-ups. The mental standards for admission are that candidates must be able to demonstrate proficiency in English, mathematics, and science. The medical standards for admission are that candidates must be able to pass a medical examination and have no medical conditions that would limit their ability to serve in the military.

To be considered for admission to West Point, candidates must first complete the online application. The application includes biographical information, essays, and short answer questions. Once the application is complete, candidates will be evaluated on their academic record, standardized test scores, extracurricular activities, and leadership potential. The most competitive candidates will be selected for an interview.

After the interview, candidates will be evaluated on their overall qualifications and then placed into one of three categories: qualified, qualified with reservations, or not qualified. The qualified candidates will be offered admission to West Point. The qualified with reservations candidates will be placed on a waiting list. The not qualified candidates will not be offered admission to West Point.
