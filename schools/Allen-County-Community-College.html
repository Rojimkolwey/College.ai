Allen County Community College (ACCC) is a public, two-year institution of higher education located in Iola, Kansas, United States. ACCC was established in 1923 as Allen County Junior College and is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. The college offers associate degrees and certificate programs in a variety of academic and career fields.

ACCC serves a rural area of east-central Kansas with a population of approximately 28,000. The college's primary service area includes Allen, Neosho, and Woodson counties. ACCC also serves Anderson, Bourbon, Coffey, Franklin, Linn, and Miami counties. The college has an enrollment of approximately 1,500 students.

ACCC offers a variety of academic programs leading to an Associate in Arts (AA), an Associate in Science (AS), or an Associate in Applied Science (AAS) degree. The college also offers certificate programs in a variety of career and technical fields. ACCC is committed to providing quality education and training that prepare students for success in their chosen field.

The college provides a variety of student services to help students succeed in their academic and career goals. ACCC offers academic advising, career counseling, tutoring, and other support services. The college also has a variety of student organizations and clubs that provide leadership and service opportunities.

ACCC is located in Iola, Kansas, a community of approximately 5,500 people located in east-central Kansas. Iola is the county seat of Allen County and is home to a variety of businesses and industries. The community is served by a variety of local, state, and federal government agencies. ACCC is located on a 100-acre campus that includes a variety of academic and support buildings.

Admission Requirements

Allen County Community College (ACCC) is a public community college in Iola, Kansas. It is part of the Kansas Community College System. ACCC offers associate degrees and certificate programs. The college also provides adult education and continuing education courses.

Admission to ACCC is open to all students who meet the following requirements:

1. Complete an application for admission.

2. Submit official high school transcripts or GED scores.

3. Take the ACCUPLACER placement test or submit ACT/SAT scores.

4. Meet with an advisor to discuss program of study and complete registration.

5. Pay tuition and fees.

ACCC also offers a dual enrollment program for high school students. This program allows students to take college courses while still in high school. Students must meet the following requirements to be eligible for dual enrollment:

1. Be enrolled in a participating high school.

2. Have a cumulative high school GPA of 2.5 or higher.

3. Score at least a 19 on the ACT or 950 on the SAT.

4. Obtain permission from a parent or guardian.

5. Meet with an ACCC advisor to discuss program of study and complete registration.

6. Pay tuition and fees.
