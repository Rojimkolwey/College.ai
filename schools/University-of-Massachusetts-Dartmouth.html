University of Massachusetts-Dartmouth

North Dartmouth, MA
285 Old Westport Rd, North Dartmouth, MA 02747-2300
508-999-8000

Founded in 1895, the University of Massachusetts Dartmouth (UMass Dartmouth or UMassD) is an public research university in Dartmouth, Massachusetts. It is one of five campuses of the University of Massachusetts system.

The university offers bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees in business, engineering, liberal arts, nursing, and marine sciences. The main campus is situated on 710 acres (2.9 km2) of land in North Dartmouth, with a satellite campus - the StarStore - in downtown New Bedford.

UMass Dartmouth is ranked #193 in National Universities by U.S. News & World Report in their 2021 rankings. The online graduate engineering program is ranked #33 and the part-time MBA program is ranked #60. The university is also ranked #75 in Top Public Schools.

The university enrolls over 8,000 students, with a student-to-faculty ratio of 16:1. The average class size is 21 students. The campus is home to a variety of research centers, including the Center for Innovation and Entrepreneurship, the Center for Marketing Research, and the Marine Science and Technology Center.

The athletic teams at UMass Dartmouth are known as the Corsairs and compete in the NCAA Division III. The university is a member of the Little East Conference. The Corsairs have won numerous conference championships and have made several appearances in NCAA Division III tournaments.

Admission Requirements

The University of Massachusetts-Dartmouth is a public research university located in North Dartmouth, Massachusetts. It is part of the University of Massachusetts system, and offers bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees in a variety of disciplines.

Undergraduate students at UMass-Dartmouth must complete a minimum of 120 credit hours to earn a bachelor's degree. Of those, at least 45 must be in upper-level coursework. Students must also maintain a minimum GPA of 2.0 and earn a "C" or better in all major and minor courses.

In addition to coursework, UMass-Dartmouth also requires students to complete a First-Year Experience Seminar, a Senior Capstone, and an internship or field experience. The First-Year Experience Seminar is a one-credit, pass/fail course that helps students transition to college life. The Senior Capstone is a two-credit, pass/fail course that requires students to complete a major research project. Internships and field experiences are not required, but are strongly encouraged.

To be eligible for admission, students must submit an application, transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a $60 application fee. International students must also submit proof of English proficiency. UMass-Dartmouth uses a rolling admissions process, meaning that applications are reviewed as they are received. Decisions are typically made within two weeks of receipt.
