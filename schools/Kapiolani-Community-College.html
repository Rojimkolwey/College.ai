Kapiolani Community College (KCC) is a public, co-educational community college in Honolulu, Hawaii situated on the slopes of Diamond Head in Waikiki. It is one of ten campuses of the Hawaii Community College System and is accredited by the Western Association of Schools and Colleges. The college offers two-year degrees and certificates in various liberal arts and vocational-technical programs.

Kapiolani Community College was established in 1955 as Honolulu Community College (HCC). It was the first community college in the state of Hawaii and was founded to meet the needs of the growing population of post-World War II veterans attending college on the G.I. Bill. The college was originally located in downtown Honolulu near Fort Street Mall. In 1960, the college moved to its current location in Waikiki. In 1970, the college was renamed Kapiolani Community College after Queen Kapiolani, the queen consort of King Kalakaua.

Kapiolani Community College is one of the largest community colleges in the state of Hawaii, with an enrollment of over 10,000 students. The college offers over 50 different degree and certificate programs. The most popular programs at the college are in the areas of business, health sciences, and hospitality.

Kapiolani Community College is known for its strong commitment to student success. The college has a variety of student support services available, including academic advising, tutoring, and career counseling. The college also offers a robust distance learning program, with over 700 courses available online.

Kapiolani Community College is an important part of the Waikiki community. The college is located just minutes from the world-famous Waikiki Beach and is within walking distance of many of the area's hotels, restaurants, and shops. The college is also home to the Waikiki Aquarium, one of the most popular tourist attractions in Hawaii.

Kapiolani Community College (KCC) is a public, co-educational college located in Honolulu, Hawaii, United States. It is the largest community college in the state of Hawaii and is accredited by the Western Association of Schools and Colleges. KCC is a member of the Hawaii Council of Community Colleges.

KCC offers over 100 certificate and degree programs in a wide variety of fields, including business, liberal arts, nursing, and health sciences. The college also offers a number of distance learning and online courses.

Admission to KCC is open to all students who have a high school diploma or equivalent. However, preference is given to residents of Hawaii. To apply, students must submit an application form, transcripts, and a non-refundable application fee. International students must also submit proof of English proficiency.

Once admitted, students are required to complete a placement test to determine their level of English and math skills. Based on the results of the placement test, students may be required to take one or more remedial courses before they can begin taking courses for credit.
