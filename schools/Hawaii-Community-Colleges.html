Hawaii Community Colleges


Hawaii’s community colleges offer a wide variety of educational opportunities to students of all ages. From two-year Associate Degrees to certificates and other short-term programs, Hawaii’s seven community colleges provide students with the chance to explore their educational interests, develop career skills, and gain a higher education.

Hawaii’s community colleges are located on all four major islands, making them conveniently accessible to most residents. They offer a wide range of degrees and programs, from Associate Degrees in the Arts and Sciences to specialized certificates in areas such as healthcare and hospitality. In addition, many of the colleges offer programs in distance learning, which give students the flexibility to complete their studies from home or while traveling.

Hawaii’s community colleges are also known for their commitment to providing a quality education and student experience. Their small class sizes, dedicated faculty, and commitment to student success ensure that each student can receive the individual attention they need to succeed.

In addition to their courses, Hawaii’s community colleges are involved in various initiatives to serve their local communities. These include providing educational programs for high school students, offering career training for adults, and providing job placement services.

Hawaii’s community colleges are an invaluable resource for students of all ages who are looking to further their education and gain valuable career skills. With their wide range of degree and certificate programs, commitment to student success, and involvement in the local community, Hawaii’s community colleges provide the perfect opportunity for students to pursue their educational goals.
