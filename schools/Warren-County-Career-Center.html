Admission Requirements

In order to be admitted to the Warren County Career Center, students must be enrolled in one of the six public high schools in Warren County, Ohio. The Career Center offers programs in a variety of career and technical fields, and students must complete an application and interview process in order to be admitted to their desired program.

The Career Center strives to provide a quality education that prepares students for success in the workforce. The admissions process is designed to ensure that students are placed in programs that best fit their interests and abilities.

Students interested in attending the Career Center must complete an application and submit it to their high school guidance counselor. The guidance counselor will review the application and determine if the student is eligible for admission.

If the student is eligible, they will be scheduled for an interview with a Career Center staff member. During the interview, the student will be asked about their career goals and interests. The staff member will also review the student's academic record to ensure that they are prepared for the rigors of the Career Center's programs.

Upon completion of the interview, the student will be notified of their admission status. If admitted, the student will be required to complete a registration process and pay a fee prior to beginning their program.
