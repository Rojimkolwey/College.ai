Missouri Universities and Colleges


Missouri is home to a wide variety of colleges and universities, ranging from large public institutions to small private colleges. The University of Missouri, located in Columbia, is the state's flagship university and the largest public school in the state. It offers more than 300 undergraduate and graduate degree programs, and is known for its strong academic programs in areas such as engineering, business, and medicine. Other large public universities in the state include Missouri State University in Springfield and Southeast Missouri State University in Cape Girardeau.

The University of Missouri-Kansas City and the University of Missouri-St. Louis are two large public universities located in the metropolitan areas of Kansas City and St. Louis, respectively. These schools offer a wide range of undergraduate and graduate degrees, and are known for their research and academic excellence.

Missouri also has a number of smaller private colleges and universities, including Washington University in St. Louis, Saint Louis University, and Rockhurst University. These schools offer a variety of undergraduate and graduate degree programs, and are known for their strong academic programs, research opportunities, and student life.

Missouri also has a number of community colleges and technical schools, including St. Charles Community College, Metropolitan Community College, and Ozarks Technical Community College. These schools offer associate degrees and certificates in a variety of fields, and are a great option for students who are looking to enter the workforce quickly.

No matter what type of college or university you are looking for, Missouri has something to offer. With its diverse range of institutions and academic programs, Missouri is a great place to pursue a higher education.
