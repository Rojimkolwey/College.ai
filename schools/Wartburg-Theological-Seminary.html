Wartburg Theological Seminary is a seminary of the Evangelical Lutheran Church in America, located in Dubuque, Iowa. The seminary was founded in 1854 by German immigrant pastors and is one of the oldest Lutheran seminaries in the United States. It is named after Wartburg Castle in Germany, where Martin Luther spent time in hiding, translating the Bible into German.

The seminary offers a variety of degree programs, including a Master of Divinity, Master of Arts in Religion, and Doctor of Ministry. It also offers several non-degree programs, such as a Certificate in Lutheran Studies and a Diploma in Anglican Studies.

Wartburg Theological Seminary is accredited by the Association of Theological Schools in the United States and Canada. It is also a member of the Lutheran Educational Conference of North America.

The seminary's campus is located on a bluff overlooking the Mississippi River. The campus includes several historic buildings, including Old Main, which was built in 1854 and is on the National Register of Historic Places.

Admission Requirements

Wartburg Theological Seminary is a seminary of the Evangelical Lutheran Church in America, located in Dubuque, Iowa. The seminary prepares men and women to serve as pastors and other professional church leaders.

To be eligible for admission to Wartburg Theological Seminary, applicants must hold a bachelor's degree from an accredited institution. In addition, applicants must submit official transcripts, a personal essay, a resume, and three letters of recommendation. International applicants must also submit proof of English proficiency.

Upon review of all application materials, qualified applicants will be invited to interview with a member of the admissions committee. The interview is an opportunity for the applicant to discuss their call to ministry, their academic and professional goals, and their personal faith journey.

After the interview, the admissions committee will make a decision on whether or not to offer admission to the applicant. Admission to Wartburg Theological Seminary is competitive and not all applicants who meet the minimum requirements will be offered admission.
