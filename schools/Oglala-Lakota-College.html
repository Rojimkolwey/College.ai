Oglala Lakota College

Kyle, SD
490 Piya Wiconi Road, Kyle, SD 57752-0490
605-455-6000

Oglala Lakota College (OLC) is a tribal college located on the Pine Ridge Indian Reservation in South Dakota. It is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. The college was founded in 1971 and offers associate and baccalaureate degrees. The school is located in the town of Kyle, South Dakota. The campus is situated on 180 acres of land.

OLC is one of the 32 tribal colleges and universities in the United States. The school's mission is to provide quality education to American Indian students in a culturally relevant way. OLC offers a variety of programs and services that promote academic success and personal development. The college also provides opportunities for students to engage in Lakota culture and traditions.

OLC has an enrollment of about 1,200 students. The student body is about evenly split between men and women. The majority of students are enrolled in associate degree programs. The college employs about 100 faculty and staff members.

OLC is governed by a nine-member Board of Trustees. The college is funded by the federal government, the state of South Dakota, and private donations. OLC is a member of the American Indian Higher Education Consortium.

Admission Requirements

Oglala Lakota College (OLC) is a accredited institution of higher learning that offers Associates and Bachelors degrees in a variety of areas. The College is committed to providing quality education to American Indian students in the Lakota tradition of excellence. OLC is located on the Pine Ridge Indian Reservation in South Dakota, the second largest reservation in the United States. The Oglala Lakota people are the largest tribe of the Lakota Nation.

To be eligible for admission to OLC, students must meet the following requirements:

1. Be a citizen or legal resident of the United States

2. Be at least 18 years of age

3. Have a high school diploma or equivalent

4. Submit a completed application for admission

5. Pay the non-refundable application fee

Students who do not meet the above requirements may still be eligible for admission if they have completed at least 24 semester hours of college coursework with a cumulative GPA of 2.0 or higher.

OLC also offers a GED program for students who have not completed high school. The GED program is open to anyone 18 years of age or older. To enroll in the GED program, students must take and pass a placement test.
