Northwestern Connecticut Community College (NCCC) is a public community college in Winchester, Connecticut. It is part of the Connecticut State Colleges and Universities (CSCU) system. The college was founded in 1965 as part of the state's community college system. NCCC offers over 40 degree and certificate programs, as well as a variety of continuing education and workforce development courses. The college is accredited by the New England Association of Schools and Colleges (NEASC).

NCCC serves the northwestern Connecticut region, which includes the towns of Barkhamsted, Colebrook, Goshen, Hartland, Norfolk, North Canaan, Plymouth, Torrington, and Winchester. The college has an enrollment of over 2,000 students, and its campus covers over 100 acres.

NCCC offers a variety of academic programs, including Associate in Arts, Associate in Science, and Associate in Applied Science degrees, as well as certificate programs. The college also offers a number of continuing education and workforce development courses.

NCCC is committed to providing a quality education at an affordable price. The college offers a variety of financial aid options, including grants, loans, and scholarships. NCCC also offers a variety of services to help students succeed, including tutoring, advising, and career services.

Admission Requirements

In order to be admitted to Northwestern Connecticut Community College, applicants must submit a completed application, high school transcripts, and a non-refundable application fee. Applicants must also demonstrate English proficiency by submitting one of the following: SAT/ACT scores, AP/IB scores, TOEFL/IELTS scores, or a passing score on the NCCC English Placement Test. International students must also submit proof of financial support.
