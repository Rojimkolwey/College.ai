Rappahannock Community College (RCC) is a public, two-year community college serving the residents of the Northern Neck and Middle Peninsula regions of Virginia. The college offers a wide variety of academic programs and services to meet the needs of its students. RCC is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges (SACSCOC) to award associate degrees.

RCC was established in 1971 and has since grown to serve over 4,000 students annually. The college has two campuses, one in Warsaw and one in Glenns, as well as a number of off-campus locations. RCC offers a variety of academic programs leading to an associate degree, including arts and sciences, business, health sciences, and public service. In addition to its credit programs, the college also offers non-credit courses and programs, workforce development services, and community education opportunities.

RCC is committed to providing a quality education that is affordable and accessible to all. The college offers a variety of financial aid options to help students meet the cost of tuition and other expenses. RCC also offers a number of services to support students, including academic advising, tutoring, and career services.
