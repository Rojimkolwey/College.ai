About South Carolina

South Carolina is a state located in the southeastern United States. The state is bordered by North Carolina to the north, Georgia to the southwest, and the Atlantic Ocean to the east. South Carolina is the 40th most extensive and 23rd most populous U.S. state. The state is divided into 46 counties. The capital and largest city is Columbia with a population of 133,451.

South Carolina's coastline consists of many salt marshes and estuaries, as well as natural ports such as Georgetown and Charleston. The state's inland terrain is mostly flat and consists of coastal plains and the low country. The state is known for its subtropical climate and its abundance of rainfall.

The state's economy is primarily based on agriculture, tourism, and manufacturing. The state is also home to many military bases and facilities.

South Carolina was one of the thirteen colonies that revolted against British rule in the American Revolution. The state was the first to declare its independence from the British Crown on May 23, 1776. After the war, the state was divided into two parts, the Upcountry and the Lowcountry.

The American Civil War began in 1861 when South Carolina seceded from the United States. The state was the first to do so. The state was also the site of many important battles such as the First and Second Battles of Bull Run, the Battle of Antietam, and the Battle of Gettysburg. After the war, the state was Reconstruction.

In the 20th century, the state's economy began to diversify. The state became a leader in the textile industry and also developed a strong tourism industry. The state also began to attract retirees from other parts of the country.

Today, South Carolina is a growing state with a strong economy. The state's population is estimated at 4.96 million. The state is also home to many historical sites and landmarks.
