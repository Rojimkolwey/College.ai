Yeshiva Gedolah Tiferes Boruch is a yeshiva located in the borough of Brooklyn, New York. The yeshiva was founded in 2006 by Rabbi Dovid Feinstein, a world-renowned Talmudic scholar and the current Rosh Yeshiva, and is named after Rabbi Feinstein's father, Rabbi Moshe Feinstein. The yeshiva is housed in a state-of-the-art facility and offers a full range of academic and Judaic studies, as well as extracurricular activities, to its students.

The yeshiva's curriculum is based on the traditional Talmudic studies of the yeshiva, which include the study of the Mishnah, Talmud, and halakha (Jewish law). In addition, the yeshiva offers courses in Jewish history, philosophy, and literature. The yeshiva also offers a wide range of extracurricular activities, including sports, clubs, and cultural events.

Yeshiva Gedolah Tiferes Boruch is committed to providing a well-rounded education to its students and preparing them for leadership roles in the Jewish community. The yeshiva's graduates have gone on to assume positions of leadership in the Orthodox Jewish community, in Jewish education, and in Jewish organizations.

Yeshiva Gedolah Tiferes Boruch is a post-high school yeshiva located in the Flatbush neighborhood of Brooklyn, New York. The yeshiva was founded in 2001 by Rabbi Dovid Lebovitz, a noted Talmudic scholar and author of numerous sefarim. The yeshiva is accredited by the New York State Board of Regents and offers a four-year program leading to a Bachelor of Talmudic Law (BTalm) degree. The yeshiva is also a member of the Association of Modern Orthodox Day Schools (AMODS).

Yeshiva Gedolah Tiferes Boruch admits students of all Jewish backgrounds and levels of observance. The yeshiva does not require any specific Jewish educational background for admission, though all students are expected to be proficient in Hebrew. The yeshiva does not have a minimum GPA requirement, but all applicants must submit a high school transcript and SAT or ACT scores. The yeshiva also requires two letters of recommendation, one from a rabbi or rebbetzin and one from a teacher, guidance counselor, or employer.

The yeshiva offers a rigorous academic program that includes daily classes in Talmud, halakha, Jewish philosophy, and Jewish history. The yeshiva also places a strong emphasis on developing students' skills in writing, critical thinking, and public speaking. In addition to their academic coursework, students are required to participate in a weekly chavrusa (study partner) program, as well as a monthly mesechta (Talmudic tractate) club. Yeshiva Gedolah Tiferes Boruch also offers a number of extracurricular activities, including a student-run newspaper, an annual musical production, and a variety of sports teams.
