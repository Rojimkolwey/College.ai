Parker University

Dallas, TX
2540 Walnut Hill Lane, Dallas, TX 75229-5668
972-438-6932

Parker University is a private, non-profit university located in Dallas, Texas. The university is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges. Parker University offers associate, bachelor, and master's degrees in chiropractic, acupuncture, Oriental medicine, and other health-related programs. The university also offers online programs.

Parker University was founded in 1982 by Dr. James Parker, a chiropractor. The university was originally named Parker Chiropractic College. In 1998, the college was renamed Parker College of Chiropractic. In 2005, the college began offering acupuncture and Oriental medicine programs and was renamed Parker University.

Parker University's mission is to educate and train students to become health care professionals who are committed to excellence, service, and leadership. The university's vision is to be a premier institution for health care education and research.

Parker University has a student-centered approach to education. The university offers small class sizes, individualized attention, and hands-on learning experiences. Parker University also has a strong commitment to research. The university's research programs are designed to advance the science of chiropractic, acupuncture, and Oriental medicine.

Parker University is located on a beautiful, wooded campus in North Dallas. The university is just minutes from downtown Dallas and the Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport.

Admission Requirements

Parker University is a private, for-profit university in Dallas, Texas. It was founded in 1982 as Parker Chiropractic College by James William Parker. The university offers bachelor's, master's, and doctorate degrees in chiropractic, as well as associate's and bachelor's degrees in other health-related fields.

Admission to Parker University is competitive, and the university does not guarantee admission to all applicants. The university considers several factors when making admission decisions, including an applicant's academic record, standardized test scores, letters of recommendation, and personal essay.

Parker University has a rolling admissions policy, meaning that applicants can submit their materials at any time and will receive a decision within 4-6 weeks. The university offers both early decision and regular decision options. Early decision applicants must submit their materials by November 1 and will receive a decision by December 15. Regular decision applicants must submit their materials by February 1 and will receive a decision by April 1.

Parker University is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges.
