Chaffey College is a community college located in Rancho Cucamonga, California, United States. The college offers Associate's degrees and certificates in over 100 areas of study. Chaffey College is one of the oldest community colleges in California, having been founded in 1883. The college is named after George Chaffey, who helped bring irrigation to the area.

Chaffey College serves the cities of Rancho Cucamonga, Fontana, Ontario, and Upland. The college has an enrollment of over 14,000 students. The college is accredited by the Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges of the Western Association of Schools and Colleges.

Chaffey College offers a variety of student services, including counseling, tutoring, and career services. The college also has a bookstore, library, and fitness center. Chaffey College is a member of the California Community Colleges System and the Associated Students of Chaffey College.

Chaffey College was founded in 1883 by George Chaffey and Ontario City Councilman Hiram Clay Kellogg. The college was originally located in Ontario, California. In 1955, the college moved to its current location in Rancho Cucamonga.

Chaffey College offers over 100 Associate's degrees and certificates. The college has five academic divisions: Business and Technology, Education and Social Sciences, Health and Public Services, Language Arts and Sciences, and Math and Science.
