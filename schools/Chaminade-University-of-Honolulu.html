Chaminade University of Honolulu is a private university in Honolulu, Hawaii. The university is affiliated with the Catholic Church and is named after Father William Joseph Chaminade, a French Catholic priest who founded the Society of Mary, of which the university is a part. The university was founded in 1955 and offers bachelor's and master's degrees in a variety of disciplines. The university has an enrollment of approximately 2,000 students.

Admission Requirements

Chaminade University of Honolulu is a Catholic university in the United States. The school's mission is to provide quality education in a caring environment. The university is accredited by the Western Association of Schools and Colleges.

Chaminade University of Honolulu offers a variety of undergraduate and graduate programs. The university has an open admissions policy, meaning that any student who meets the minimum requirements for admission will be accepted.

The minimum requirements for admission to Chaminade University of Honolulu are as follows:

- A completed application form

- A non-refundable application fee of $50

- Official transcripts from all previous colleges or universities attended

- A personal essay

- Two letters of recommendation

Students who meet the minimum requirements for admission will be reviewed on a rolling basis. Admission decisions will be based on the strength of the student's academic record, personal essay, and letters of recommendation.
