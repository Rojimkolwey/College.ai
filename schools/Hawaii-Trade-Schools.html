About Hawaii

The island of Hawaii is the largest and most populous of the Hawaiian Islands, a chain of volcanic islands in the Pacific Ocean. Hawaii is the 50th state of the United States of America. It is the only state located in the Pacific Ocean and the only state composed entirely of islands. Hawaii is the most recent state to join the Union, having been admitted on August 21, 1959.

The island of Hawaii is the third largest island in the United States and is the largest island in the Hawaiian Islands chain. The island is often referred to as the "Big Island" to avoid confusion with the state as a whole. The island is home to Hawaii Volcanoes National Park, as well as nine other national parks and monuments. Hawaii's highest point is Mauna Kea, a dormant volcano that last erupted in 1984.

The climate of Hawaii is tropical, but it can vary depending on the elevation. The island of Hawaii is home to a diverse range of plant and animal life. The island is also home to a number of endangered species, including the Hawaiian monk seal and the Hawaiian green sea turtle.

The economy of Hawaii is largely based on tourism and the military. The state is home to a number of military bases, as well as the headquarters of the United States Pacific Command. Hawaii is also a major tourist destination, with millions of visitors each year.
