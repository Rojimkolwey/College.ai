New Jersey Institute of Technology is a public research university in Newark, New Jersey. Founded in 1881 as Newark Technical School, NJIT opened its doors in 1885 with 88 students enrolled in six courses. NJIT is a member of the Association of Public and Land-grant Universities (APLU) and the Association of American Universities (AAU). It is categorized as a Research University with Very High Research Activity (RU/VH) by the Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education.

As of fall 2019, NJIT enrolls over 11,500 students, 2,000 of whom live on campus. The university offers 128 undergraduate and graduate degree programs in six schools and colleges: Newark College of Engineering, College of Architecture and Design, College of Science and Liberal Arts, School of Management, Albert Dorman Honors College, and Ying Wu College of Computing Sciences. NJIT is a polytechnic university that emphasizes experiential learning, research, and entrepreneurship within the context of a comprehensive liberal arts education.

The university is noted for its programs in engineering, computing, business, and architecture, as well as its cooperative education (co-op) program, which integrates classroom study with paid work experience. NJIT is also home to the New Jersey Innovation Institute, a technology incubator and economic development engine that supports the commercialization of NJIT research and technology. The university has an annual economic impact of over $1 billion and is a major engine of economic growth in New Jersey.

NJIT is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education. It also has specialized accreditations for its engineering, business, computing, and architecture programs. The university is ranked among the top tier of national research universities by several measures, including U.S. News & World Report, Forbes, and Washington Monthly. It is also ranked among the nation's top producers of Fulbright Scholars and is a member of the selective Association of American Universities.

NJIT is a large, urban, public research university with a diverse student body. The university is committed to providing a high-quality, affordable education to students from all backgrounds. NJIT is a leader in STEM education and research and is one of the nation's top producers of Fulbright Scholars. The university is also a member of the selective Association of American Universities.

Admission Requirements

New Jersey Institute of Technology (NJIT) is a public research university in Newark, New Jersey. Founded in 1881 as Newark Technical School, it became the Newark College of Engineering in 1930 and the New Jersey Institute of Technology in 1975. NJIT is a member of the Association of Public and Land-grant Universities (APLU).

As of fall 2019, NJIT offers 124 undergraduate and graduate degree programs across five schools and colleges: Newark College of Engineering, Albert Dorman Honors College, College of Architecture and Design, College of Science and Liberal Arts, and Ying Wu College of Computing Sciences.

The university's admissions process is selective. For fall 2019, NJIT received over 18,000 applications and admitted approximately 4,700 students. The middle 50% of admitted students had SAT scores between 1140 and 1340, and ACT scores between 25 and 30. The average high school GPA of admitted students was 3.5.

NJIT offers a variety of financial aid programs, including merit-based scholarships, need-based grants, and loans. In 2018-19, over 80% of NJIT students received some form of financial aid.

To be eligible for admission to NJIT, students must complete a rigorous high school curriculum that includes courses in math, science, English, and social studies. Students must also submit standardized test scores, a personal essay, and a letter of recommendation.
