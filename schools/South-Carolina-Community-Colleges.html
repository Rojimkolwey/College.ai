South Carolina Community Colleges


South Carolina's community colleges provide students with access to quality, affordable higher education. The state is home to 16 community colleges located in all 46 counties, and each college offers a variety of courses and certificates for students to choose from. Community colleges provide a great opportunity for students to gain a high-quality education without having to travel far away or pay expensive tuition fees. Some of the most popular programs offered at South Carolina's community colleges include Associate's degrees in Business, Science, and Technology, as well as certificate programs in Nursing, Healthcare, and Automotive Technology.

The South Carolina Commission on Higher Education (CHE) is responsible for overseeing the state's community colleges. The CHE works with each college to ensure they are meeting the educational needs of the state's citizens. The commission also works to ensure that each college is providing an affordable, quality education to its students.

The South Carolina Technical College System (SCTCS) is another important organization in the state's higher education system. The SCTCS is responsible for providing technical and vocational training to students in the state. It is comprised of 16 technical colleges located throughout the state, and each college offers a variety of courses and certificates in the areas of Business, Healthcare, and Science.

South Carolina's community colleges are a great option for students looking to pursue a quality, affordable education. With a variety of courses and certificates, students can find a program that fits their needs and goals. Whether a student is looking to pursue an Associate's degree or a certificate, South Carolina's community colleges offer a great opportunity for students to receive a quality education.
