Universidad Ana G. Mendez-Gurabo Campus is a public university in Gurabo, Puerto Rico. It is part of the Ana G. Mendez University System. It was founded in 1972 as a two-year institution and became a four-year institution in 2009. The university offers bachelor's and associate's degrees in a variety of fields.

The university is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education. It is also a member of the Association of Puerto Rican Colleges and Universities.

The campus is located on a hill in Gurabo, about 20 miles east of San Juan. It has four main buildings: the Administration Building, the Academic Building, the Student Center, and the Library. The campus also has a gymnasium, a track, and a soccer field.

The university has about 2,500 students, most of whom are from Gurabo or the surrounding area. The student body is about evenly split between men and women. The average age of students is 22.

The university offers a variety of extracurricular activities, including clubs, sports, and cultural events. It also has a student government and a newspaper.

The campus is served by the Gurabo Municipal Bus Company and the Puerto Rico Trolley Company.
