Founded in 1988, DigiPen Institute of Technology is a private, for-profit institution of higher education located in Redmond, Washington, USA. The school offers bachelor's and master's degrees in computer science and digital art and animation. The school's primary focus is on the education and development of video game programmers and designers.

DigiPen Institute of Technology was founded by Claude Comair and Gary Digiulio. Comair, a native of Lebanon, had immigrated to the United States in 1972. Digiulio, a native of the United States, had been working as a software engineer for Microsoft. Admission Requirements

Admission to DigiPen Institute of Technology is highly competitive. Each year, we receive thousands of applications from prospective students all over the world.

To be considered for admission, applicants must meet the following minimum requirements:

1. A high school diploma or equivalent

2. A minimum cumulative GPA of 2.5 on a 4.0 scale

3. A minimum score of 500 on the SAT Math section, or a minimum score of 22 on the ACT Math section

4. A minimum score of 550 on the SAT Reading section, or a minimum score of 23 on the ACT Reading section

5. A minimum score of 21 on the ACT English section, or a minimum score of 480 on the SAT Writing and Language section

6. A minimum score of 2 on the AP Calculus AB or BC exam, or a minimum score of 3 on the AP Statistics exam

In addition to meeting the minimum requirements, applicants must also submit a portfolio of their work. The portfolio is reviewed by a committee of faculty and staff, and is used to assess an applicant’s creativity, technical skills, and potential for success in the program.

Applicants who meet the minimum requirements and submit a strong portfolio will be invited to interview with a member of the admissions committee. The interview is an opportunity for the admissions committee to get to know the applicant on a personal level, and to assess their motivation for pursuing a career in the game industry.

After the interview, the admissions committee will make a decision on whether to offer the applicant admission to the program. Admission decisions are based on a holistic review of the applicant’s file, and are made with the goal of admitting a class of students that is academically strong and creatively diverse.
