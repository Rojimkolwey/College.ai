J. F. Ingram State Technical College is a public two-year technical college located in Deatsville, Alabama. It is one of the twenty-two two-year colleges that comprise the Alabama Community College System. The college was established in 1965 and is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools. The college offers associate degrees and technical certificates in a variety of programs.

The college opened its doors in September 1965 with an initial enrollment of 125 students. The first graduation class, consisting of thirteen students, received their degrees in May 1967.

Today, the college serves over 1,700 students annually. The college offers a variety of programs leading to an Associate in Applied Science degree, a technical certificate, or both. The programs are divided into five academic divisions: Business and Computer Technology, Health Sciences, Industrial and Construction Technology, Public Service, and Transportation.

The Industrial and Construction Technology Division offers programs in automotive technology, building construction technology, industrial maintenance technology, and welding technology. The Public Service Division offers programs in fire science technology and law enforcement. The Transportation Division offers programs in aviation maintenance technology and truck driving.
