Midwestern University-Glendale

Glendale, AZ
19555 N 59th Ave, Glendale, AZ 85308
623-572-3200

Midwestern University-Glendale is a private, non-profit university located in Glendale, Arizona. The university is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. Midwestern University offers more than 50 degree and certificate programs in the health sciences.

The University was founded in 1955 as the Glendale Memorial Hospital School of Nursing. In response to the growing need for health care professionals, the school began offering additional health science programs and changed its name to Midwestern University in 1989.

Today, Midwestern University is a comprehensive health science university that offers programs at the undergraduate, graduate, and professional level. The University’s five colleges – the Chicago College of Osteopathic Medicine, the College of Health Sciences, the College of Dental Medicine-Illinois, the College of Pharmacy-Glendale, and the College of Veterinary Medicine – offer programs that prepare students for careers in a variety of health care fields.

Midwestern University is committed to providing an excellent education at an affordable price. The University offers a variety of scholarships and financial aid programs to help students cover the cost of tuition and other expenses. In addition, the University’s flexible scheduling options allow students to complete their degrees on a part-time or full-time basis.

Midwestern University is dedicated to preparing its students for successful careers in the health care field. The University’s faculty members are experienced professionals who are committed to helping students reach their full potential. In addition to providing a well-rounded education, the University offers a variety of extracurricular opportunities that allow students to gain real-world experience in the health care field.
