Brewster Technical College

Tampa, FL
2222 N Tampa St, Tampa, FL 33602-2196
813-276-5448

Brewster Technical College is a public two-year technical college located in Tampa, Florida. The college offers programs in a variety of technical and career fields, including automotive technology, business technology, cosmetology, culinary arts, digital media, electrical technology, HVAC technology, nursing, and welding technology.

Brewster Technical College was founded in 1958 as a trade school for adults. The college was named after Dr. William H. Brewster, who was the Superintendent of Hillsborough County Schools from 1943 to 1957. The college initially offered programs in automotive mechanics, carpentry, and plumbing. In the 1960s, the college began to expand its program offerings to include other technical and trade programs.

Today, Brewster Technical College is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges. The college enrolls over 4,000 students annually. The college offers both credit and non-credit programs, and students can earn associate degrees, diplomas, and certificates.

Brewster Technical College is committed to providing quality technical and career education to its students. The college offers a variety of services to help students succeed, including academic advising, career counseling, tutoring, and job placement assistance. The college also has an active alumni association that provides support and networking opportunities for graduates.

Admission Requirements

Brewster Technical College is a public, two-year institution of higher education located in Tampa, Florida. The college offers programs leading to the Associate in Arts, Associate in Science, and Associate in Applied Science degrees, as well as technical certificates. Brewster also offers a variety of continuing education and adult education courses.

Admission to Brewster Technical College is open to any student who has a high school diploma or its equivalent. All students must complete a college placement test prior to enrolling in any credit coursework. Students who do not meet the minimum placement test scores may be required to complete developmental coursework prior to enrolling in credit coursework.
