Dine College is a public community college in Tsaile, Arizona. It is one of the largest community colleges in the United States and is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. The college offers associate's degrees and certificate programs in a variety of fields, including business, education, health, and liberal arts. Dine College is a member of the American Indian Higher Education Consortium and is one of the few community colleges in the country with a tribal college designation. The college is also home to the Dine Policy Institute, which is a think tank that focuses on issues affecting the Navajo Nation.

Dine College is a public two-year college located in Tsaile, Arizona, on the Navajo Nation. The college is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. It is one of the largest tribal colleges in the United States.

Dine College offers over 50 associate degree and certificate programs in a variety of fields, including business, education, health care, and the arts and sciences. Admission to the college is open to all students, regardless of tribal affiliation.

To be eligible for admission, students must submit an application, transcripts, and a $20 application fee. Admission is based on a holistic review of the applicant's academic record, test scores, and personal statement.

Dine College is committed to providing access to higher education for all students, regardless of financial need. The college offers a variety of financial aid options, including grants, loans, and scholarships.
