James Sprunt Community College

Kenansville, NC
133 James Sprunt Drive, Kenansville, NC 28349
910-296-2400

James Sprunt Community College is a public community college in Kenansville, North Carolina. The college is named after James Sprunt, a local planter, merchant, and philanthropist. The college was founded in 1965 as a two-year institution and became a four-year college in 1968. The college offers associate's and bachelor's degrees in a variety of disciplines, as well as certificate programs.

James Sprunt Community College is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges. The college is a member of the North Carolina Community College System.

The college's Kenansville campus is located on a 200-acre (81 ha) site in Duplin County. The campus includes several academic and administrative buildings, a library, a student center, a gymnasium, and athletic fields. The college also operates a small campus in Jacksonville, North Carolina, which offers classes and services to military personnel and veterans.

James Sprunt Community College enrolls approximately 2,500 students. The college's student body is approximately evenly split between men and women. The majority of students are from Duplin and Sampson counties.

The college offers a variety of academic programs, including arts and sciences, business and industry, health and public service, and military and veterans' services. The college also offers a number of distance learning and online courses.

James Sprunt Community College is committed to providing a quality education at an affordable price. The college offers a variety of financial aid options, including grants, loans, and scholarships. The college also offers a number of payment plans to make college more affordable.

James Sprunt Community College is dedicated to serving the community. The college offers a variety of community services, including a food bank, a thrift store, and a child care center. The college also offers a number of continuing education and workforce development programs.

Admission Requirements

Prospective students must submit a completed application, along with official high school transcripts or GED scores. Students must also demonstrate basic proficiency in English and math by taking a placement test. The college offers placement testing on-campus and online.
