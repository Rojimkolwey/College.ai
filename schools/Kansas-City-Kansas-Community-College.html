913-334-1100

Kansas City Kansas Community College (KCKCC) is a public two-year institution of higher education located in Wyandotte County, Kansas, United States. It is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. KCKCC serves the cities of Bonner Springs, Edwardsville, Kansas City, Lake Quivira, Lansing, Leavenworth, and Piper. The college offers more than 50 programs of study leading to an Associate in Arts, Associate in Science, and Associate in Applied Science degrees, as well as technical certificates. KCKCC is known for its culinary arts, nursing, and automotive programs.

KCKCC was founded in 1923 as the Municipal University of Kansas City. In 1965, the institution merged with the Junior College of Wyandotte County to form the Metropolitan Community College of Kansas City. The name was changed to Kansas City Kansas Community College in 1972. The college is governed by a seven-member Board of Trustees, which is appointed by the mayor of Kansas City, Kansas. The president of the college is Dr. Greg Gunderson.

KCKCC is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and offers more than 50 programs of study leading to an Associate in Arts, Associate in Science, and Associate in Applied Science degrees, as well as technical certificates. The college is known for its culinary arts, nursing, and automotive programs.

KCKCC offers a variety of student services, including academic advising, career counseling, tutoring, and disability services. The college also has a bookstore, library, and computer lab on campus. KCKCC is a member of the Kansas Association of Community College Trustees and the American Association of Community Colleges.

In 2017, KCKCC was awarded the Bellwether Award for its innovative and effective practices in workforce development. The college was also named one of the Top 150 Community Colleges in the nation by the Aspen Institute.

Admission Requirements

In order to be admitted to Kansas City Kansas Community College, applicants must submit an application form along with a non-refundable application fee. Official high school transcripts or GED scores are also required. Applicants must have a cumulative high school GPA of 2.0 or higher, or a score of at least 45 on the GED. Students who have not yet completed high school may also be admitted if they have a cumulative GPA of 2.5 or higher in college-level coursework.
