Quinsigamond Community College

Worcester, MA
670 West Boylston Street, Worcester, MA 01606-2092
508-853-2300

Founded in 1963, Quinsigamond Community College is a two-year, public institution of higher education located in Worcester, Massachusetts. The college enrolls approximately 10,000 students annually and offers over 60 academic programs leading to associate degrees and certificates.

Quinsigamond Community College is accredited by the New England Association of Schools and Colleges and is a member of the Massachusetts Community College system. The college is situated on a 97-acre campus in the heart of Worcester and is within walking distance of many restaurants, shops, and businesses.

The college offers a variety of student services and support programs, including tutoring, advising, and career counseling. Quinsigamond Community College also has an active Student Government Association that organizes social and cultural events for the student body.

The college's athletics teams, known as the Wombats, compete in the National Junior College Athletic Association. The Wombats offer a variety of sports including basketball, baseball, softball, and soccer.

Quinsigamond Community College is an affordable and accessible option for students seeking to earn an associate degree or certificate. The college's small class sizes and supportive environment provide students with the opportunity to succeed academically and prepare for transfer to a four-year institution or for entry into the workforce.

Admission Requirements

Quinsigamond Community College (QCC) is a public, two-year institution of higher learning located in Worcester, Massachusetts. The College offers associate degree and certificate programs in a variety of academic and career fields. QCC is committed to providing accessible, high-quality education and training opportunities to a diverse population of students.

To be eligible for admission to QCC, applicants must have a high school diploma or equivalent. Adult learners who do not have a high school diploma or equivalent may be eligible for admission if they meet certain criteria.

QCC has an open admissions policy, meaning that all applicants who meet the basic eligibility requirements will be accepted into the College. However, some programs may have additional requirements or preferences.

Once an applicant has been accepted to QCC, they will need to complete a placement test to determine which courses they will need to take. The placement test is used to assess a student’s skills in math, reading, and writing. Based on the results of the placement test, students may be required to take one or more developmental courses before they can begin taking credit-bearing courses.

QCC offers a variety of services and resources to help students succeed. These include academic advising, tutoring, career services, and disability services. For more information about admission requirements and the services and resources available at QCC, please visit the College’s website.
