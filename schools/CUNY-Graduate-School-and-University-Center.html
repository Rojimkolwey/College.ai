The CUNY Graduate School and University Center is one of the oldest and most prestigious public graduate schools in the United States. It is a part of the City University of New York system, and is located in Manhattan. The school offers a wide variety of graduate programs, and is especially well-known for its programs in the humanities and social sciences.

The CUNY Graduate School was founded in 1961, and has since become one of the leading public graduate schools in the country. It is highly selective, and only admits students who are able to maintain a high GPA. The school offers a wide variety of programs, including in the humanities, social sciences, and natural sciences.

The school is located in Manhattan, which is one of the most vibrant and diverse cities in the world. This provides students with a unique opportunity to study in a truly global environment. The school has a strong commitment to social justice, and many of its programs are focused on addressing issues of inequality and injustice.

The CUNY Graduate School is an excellent choice for students who are looking for a challenging and enriching academic experience. The school provides a supportive and stimulating environment, and is dedicated to preparing its students for successful careers in a variety of fields.

Admission Requirements

CUNY Graduate School and University Center is one of the nation’s leading urban public graduate schools. Located in New York City, we offer more than 40 doctoral, master’s and professional degree programs in the arts, humanities, sciences and social sciences.

We welcome applications from all qualified individuals. Our admissions process is designed to identify and attract the most promising students from a wide range of backgrounds.

To be considered for admission, you must submit the following:

• A completed online application

• Official transcripts from all colleges and universities attended

• A non-refundable application fee of $75

• GRE or GMAT scores (if required by your program)

• Two letters of recommendation

• A personal statement

• A resume or CV

• Additional materials required by your program

International applicants must also submit:

• Official TOEFL or IELTS scores (if required by your program)

• A copy of your passport

• A financial statement demonstrating that you have the resources to cover the cost of your education

Once we have received all of the required materials, your application will be reviewed by the admissions committee of your chosen program. If you are offered admission, you will be required to submit a non-refundable deposit to secure your place in the program.
