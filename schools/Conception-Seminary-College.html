Conception Seminary College is a Catholic seminary in Conception, Missouri. It is one of five seminaries in the United States that is owned and operated by the Benedictine monks of Conception Abbey. The seminary is a four-year institution that offers a Bachelor of Arts in Philosophy and a Bachelor of Sacred Theology. The seminary's mission is to prepare men for the Catholic priesthood. The seminary has an enrollment of about 100 men, most of whom are in their twenties. The average age of the seminarians is 26. The seminary is accredited by the Association of Theological Schools in the United States and Canada. The seminary's faculty consists of 22 Benedictine monks, all of whom are priests, and 11 lay faculty members. The seminary offers a wide range of extracurricular activities, including intramural sports, clubs, and service opportunities. The seminary has a strong commitment to the Catholic Church's social justice teachings, and its students are actively involved in a variety of social justice initiatives.
