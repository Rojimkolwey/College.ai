West Virginia Community Colleges


West Virginia’s community colleges provide an affordable, accessible education to students of all ages and backgrounds. With 13 campuses located throughout the state, students have a variety of options to choose from. Each college offers a wide range of degree and certificate programs, as well as workforce development and continuing education courses. Community colleges in West Virginia are committed to providing quality education that meets the needs of their local communities.

The colleges are accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and offer associate degrees, certificates, and diplomas in a variety of fields. Students can pursue a two-year degree in fields such as nursing, business, education, and more. In addition, many of the colleges offer specialized programs in areas like health sciences, technology, and hospitality. This allows students to gain a comprehensive education that can lead to successful careers in their chosen field.

West Virginia’s community colleges also provide a wide range of student services to ensure that all students have the resources they need to succeed. These services include academic advising, career counseling, financial aid, and more. Additionally, many of the colleges offer a variety of student activities, clubs, and organizations to help students get involved in their communities.

For those looking to continue their education, many of the community colleges have partnerships with four-year institutions that allow students to transfer credits and complete their bachelor’s degree. This makes it easier for students to pursue their academic and career goals without having to start from scratch.

West Virginia’s community colleges are committed to providing an affordable, accessible education that prepares students for success in their chosen fields. With a variety of degree and certificate programs, as well as student services and activities, the colleges offer something for everyone. Whether you’re looking to start a new career, transfer to a four-year institution, or just take a few classes, West Virginia’s community colleges have something to offer.
