Montclair State University is a large, public university located in New Jersey. It offers a wide variety of undergraduate and graduate programs, as well as a strong research program. The university has a diverse student body, with students from all over the world. The campus is located just outside of New York City, making it a great place to study and live. The university is known for its strong academic programs, its beautiful campus, and its friendly and welcoming community.

Admission Requirements

Montclair State University is a public research university in Montclair, New Jersey. It is the second largest university in New Jersey. As of Fall 2014, there were 21,039 total enrolled students: 18,566 undergraduates and 2,473 graduate students, with 1,614 faculty members. The campus covers approximately 500 acres (2.0 km2).

Admission to Montclair State University is selective. For Fall 2014, the university received 26,256 applications; 3,485 were admitted (13.3%) and 2,377 enrolled (9.1%). The average SAT score for incoming freshmen was 1060 and the average ACT score was 21. In terms of high school GPA, 51% of incoming freshmen had a GPA of 3.50 or higher, 24% had a GPA between 3.25 and 3.49, and 25% had a GPA below 3.25.

Admission to the university's graduate programs is also selective. For Fall 2014, the university received 5,812 applications; 1,067 were admitted (18.3%) and 752 enrolled (13.0%). The average GRE score for incoming graduate students was 297 (verbal), 303 (quantitative), and 4.0 (analytical writing).

Montclair State University has a rolling admissions policy, meaning that there is no specific deadline to apply for admission. However, it is recommended that students submit their applications as early as possible in order to increase their chances of being admitted.
