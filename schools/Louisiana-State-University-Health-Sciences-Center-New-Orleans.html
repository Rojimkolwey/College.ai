Louisiana State University Health Sciences Center-New Orleans

New Orleans, LA
433 Bolivar St, New Orleans, LA 70112-2223
504-568-4808

Founded in 1931, Louisiana State University Health Sciences Center-New Orleans is a public university dedicated to the health sciences. The university comprises six schools: School of Medicine, School of Dentistry, School of Public Health, School of Allied Health Professions, School of Graduate Studies, and School of Nursing.

The university offers a wide range of degree programs at the undergraduate, graduate, and professional levels. Undergraduate programs include Bachelor of Science in Nursing, Bachelor of Science in Dental Hygiene, and Bachelor of Science in Allied Health Professions. Graduate programs include Master of Science in Nursing, Master of Science in Dentistry, and Master of Science in Public Health. The professional programs offered at the university are Doctor of Medicine, Doctor of Dental Surgery, and Doctor of Veterinary Medicine.

The university is committed to providing a world-class education to its students and to preparing them for successful careers in the health sciences. The faculty is comprised of highly qualified and experienced professionals who are dedicated to teaching and research. The university has a state-of-the-art facilities and equipment, and its campuses are located in the heart of New Orleans, Louisiana.

The Louisiana State University Health Sciences Center-New Orleans is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges.

Admission Requirements

In order to be considered for admission to Louisiana State University Health Sciences Center-New Orleans, applicants must meet the minimum requirements set forth by the Louisiana Board of Regents. These requirements include a minimum high school GPA of 2.5 and a minimum composite ACT score of 20 or equivalent SAT score. In addition, applicants must complete the following college preparatory courses: English, mathematics, natural science, social science, and foreign language.

Once these requirements have been met, applicants must then submit an application, along with all required supporting documents, to the Office of Admissions. Applications will be reviewed by the Admissions Committee, and those who are deemed to be the most qualified will be offered admission.

Louisiana State University Health Sciences Center-New Orleans is committed to providing a world-class education to its students, and as such, only accepts those who are able to meet the high standards set forth by the faculty and staff. Those who are admitted can expect to receive a top-notch education that will prepare them for a successful career in the healthcare field.
