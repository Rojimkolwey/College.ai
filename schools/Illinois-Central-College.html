Illinois Central College

East Peoria, IL
1 College Drive, East Peoria, IL 61635-0001
309-694-5422

Illinois Central College is a community college in East Peoria, Illinois. It is part of the Illinois Community College System. It was established in 1967 and serves Peoria, Tazewell, and Woodford counties. It has an enrollment of over 11,000 students. The college offers over 150 programs of study. The college has an open admissions policy and accepts all students who have a high school diploma or GED. The college is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. The college has a campus in East Peoria and a branch campus in Peoria Heights. The college also has a center in Pekin and a center in Washington. The college offers online and distance learning courses. The college is governed by a board of trustees. The college president is Dr. Sheila Quirk-Bailey.

Admission Requirements

Illinois Central College (ICC) is a public community college in East Peoria, Illinois. Founded in 1967, ICC serves Peoria, Tazewell, and Woodford counties in Illinois. The college offers associate degrees and certificate programs in a variety of fields, as well as online and dual-credit courses.

To be eligible for admission to ICC, students must have a high school diploma or equivalent. GED holders must also submit official high school transcripts. Students who have completed college-level coursework at another institution must submit official transcripts from that institution.

ICC uses a placement test to determine course placement for students who have not yet completed college-level coursework. The placement test is not required for students who have completed college-level English and math courses with a C or better.

Students who have not yet taken the placement test must do so before registering for classes. The placement test is offered on a walk-in basis, Monday through Thursday, 8:00 a.m. to 4:00 p.m., and Friday, 8:00 a.m. to 3:00 p.m.

Once students have been admitted to ICC, they must complete a new student orientation before registering for classes. Orientation is offered online and in-person. During orientation, students will learn about ICC policies and procedures, and will have the opportunity to meet with an academic advisor to discuss their educational goals.
