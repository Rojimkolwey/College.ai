Irvine Valley College (IVC) is a two-year community college in Irvine, California, United States. It is part of the California Community Colleges System and one of the 112 colleges administered by the Irvine Valley College District. The college offers Associate's degrees and certificate programs in a variety of disciplines.

IVC was founded in 1985 and is accredited by the Western Association of Schools and Colleges. The college is located on a 105-acre (42 ha) campus in Irvine, California. The campus is adjacent to the Irvine Spectrum, a major shopping center in Orange County.

IVC offers a variety of Associate's degree and certificate programs in a variety of disciplines, including business, computer science, engineering, fine arts, health science, liberal arts, and social and behavioral sciences. The college also offers a variety of distance learning and online courses.

IVC has a student population of approximately 11,000 students. The college offers a variety of student services, including academic advising, counseling, tutoring, and career services. The college also has a variety of student clubs and organizations, including the Associated Students of Irvine Valley College, which is the college's student government organization.

The college's athletics teams are known as the Lasers. The college offers a variety of intercollegiate sports teams, including men's and women's basketball, cross country, soccer, and tennis. The college is a member of the Orange Empire Conference.

IVC has an active alumni association, which offers a variety of benefits and services to alumni, including career services, scholarships, and social events.

2. Be a resident of California

3. Be 18 years of age or older (or have a high school diploma or equivalent)

4. Have completed the required high school coursework
