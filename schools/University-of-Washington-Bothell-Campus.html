The University of Washington-Bothell Campus is a four-year public university in Bothell, Washington. The campus is one of three University of Washington campuses, and is part of the University of Washington system. The campus is located on a 55-acre (22 ha) site in north Bothell, near the junction of Interstate 405 and State Route 522. The campus opened in 1990, and offers bachelor's and master's degrees in a variety of disciplines.

The University of Washington-Bothell Campus is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities. The campus offers bachelor's degrees in business, education, engineering, information technology, and interdisciplinary studies. The campus also offers a master's degree in business administration, and a number of certificate programs.

The University of Washington-Bothell Campus is home to a number of research centers and institutes, including the Center for Education Policy Research, the Institute for Systems Biology, and the Center for the Study of the Universe. The campus is also home to a number of student organizations, including the Student Government Association, the Interfraternity Council, and the Panhellenic Council.

Minimum Requirements

To be eligible for admission to UW Bothell, you must meet the minimum requirements for your student type. These requirements vary by student type and are outlined below.

First-Year Applicants

To be eligible for admission as a first-year student, you must:

Be a graduate of a high school or have completed the GED

Have a cumulative high school GPA of 2.5 or higher

Have completed the following high school courses: English: 4 years Math: 3 years, including Algebra I, Geometry, and Algebra II/Integrated Math III or higher Social Studies: 2 years Science: 2 years, including 1 year of lab science



Transfer Applicants

To be eligible for admission as a transfer student, you must:

Have completed at least 45 transferable quarter credits (30 semester credits) of college-level coursework

Have a cumulative GPA of 2.5 or higher in all college-level coursework

Have completed the following college-level courses: English Composition: 1 course Math: 1 course



International Applicants

To be eligible for admission as an international student, you must:

Have completed the equivalent of a U.S. high school education

Have a cumulative GPA of 2.5 or higher in all college-level coursework

Have completed the following college-level courses: English Composition: 1 course Math: 1 course
