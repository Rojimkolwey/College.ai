Admission Requirements

The University of Bridgeport is a private, non-profit university located in Bridgeport, Connecticut. The school was founded in 1927 and is accredited by the New England Commission of Higher Education. The University of Bridgeport offers over 60 undergraduate and graduate programs, as well as a Doctor of Chiropractic degree program.

Undergraduate admission to the University of Bridgeport is selective. The school requires all applicants to submit an application, high school transcripts, and SAT or ACT scores. The University of Bridgeport does not have a minimum GPA or test score requirement for admission. The school does, however, consider each applicant's academic record, personal essay, and letters of recommendation when making admission decisions.

Graduate admission to the University of Bridgeport is also selective. The school requires all applicants to submit an application, transcripts from all previous colleges and universities attended, and GRE or GMAT scores. The University of Bridgeport does not have a minimum GPA or test score requirement for admission. The school does, however, consider each applicant's academic record, personal essay, letters of recommendation, and professional experience when making admission decisions.

The University of Bridgeport offers a rolling admissions policy, which means that applications are reviewed and decisions are made on a rolling basis. This means that there is no set deadline for applications. The University of Bridgeport recommends that all applicants submit their materials as early as possible to ensure the best possible chance of admission.
