New Hampshire Community Colleges


New Hampshire is home to seven community colleges that have been providing quality education opportunities for more than 50 years. These colleges are located in various cities and towns throughout the state, including Berlin, Claremont, Conway, Laconia, Manchester, Nashua, and Portsmouth. Each college offers a wide range of programs and services, from academic and career-focused classes to student support services, financial aid, and more. New Hampshire's community colleges are open to all students, regardless of age, background, or income level.

The colleges offer associate degrees in arts, sciences, and applied sciences, as well as certificate and diploma programs. Students can choose from dozens of majors, ranging from health sciences and engineering to business, education, and the humanities. In addition, many of the colleges offer continuing education courses, such as ESL classes, computer skills training, and leadership development. The colleges also have transfer agreements with four-year institutions, so students can easily transfer credits to pursue a bachelor's degree.

New Hampshire's community colleges are committed to providing an affordable, quality education. Tuition and fees are significantly lower than those at four-year institutions, and many of the colleges offer financial aid and scholarships to help students cover the cost of their education. The colleges also provide a variety of resources and services to support student success, including career counseling, academic advising, tutoring, and more.

New Hampshire's community colleges are proud to serve their communities and strive to provide a valuable educational experience for all students. With a wide range of programs, services, and resources, the colleges are committed to helping students reach their goals and pursue their dreams.
