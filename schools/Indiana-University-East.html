Admission Requirements

Indiana University-East is a four-year, public university located in Richmond, Indiana. The university is part of the Indiana University system.

Admission to Indiana University-East is selective. The university uses a holistic approach to admissions, taking into consideration factors such as academic achievement, leadership, community service, and extracurricular involvement.

Academic achievement is the most important factor in the admissions process. Applicants must have a high school diploma or equivalent. They must also submit official transcripts from all colleges and universities attended.

Leadership is another important factor in the admissions process. Applicants should have demonstrated leadership ability through their involvement in extracurricular activities, community service, or work experience.

Community service is also considered in the admissions process. Applicants should have a record of volunteerism or community involvement.

Extracurricular involvement is another factor that is considered in the admissions process. Applicants should have participated in activities outside of the classroom that have helped them develop leadership skills or have otherwise contributed to their personal growth.

Indiana University-East is committed to enrolling a diverse student body. The university offers a variety of programs and services to support students from all backgrounds.

Indiana University-East has a rolling admissions policy. Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis, and decisions are made as soon as all required materials have been received.
