Saint Mary's College

Notre Dame, IN
133 Le Mans Hall, Notre Dame, IN 46556
574-284-4000

Founded in 1844 by the Sisters of the Holy Cross, Saint Mary's College is a Catholic, residential, women's college located in Notre Dame, Indiana, adjacent to the University of Notre Dame. Saint Mary's College offers five bachelor's degrees and more than 30 minor programs of study, as well as master's degrees in education and counseling.

Approximately 1,400 students from across the United States and 74 countries live and learn together on the College's beautiful 104-acre campus. The student body represents a wide range of ages, interests, religions, and cultural backgrounds.

Saint Mary's College is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and is a member of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools. The College is also a member of the Association of Catholic Colleges and Universities, the Council of Independent Colleges, and the College Consortium of the Greater Midwest.

The College's rigorous academic program is grounded in the liberal arts and sciences and prepares students for a wide range of careers and graduate study. The faculty is committed to teaching and mentoring students, and the average class size is just 17 students.

Saint Mary's College is committed to providing an inclusive and welcoming environment for all members of the College community. The College does not discriminate on the basis of race, color, creed, religion, national origin, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity or expression, age, disability, veteran or marital status.

Admission Requirements

Saint Mary's College is a private, Catholic, liberal arts college in Notre Dame, Indiana. The college was founded in 1844 by the Sisters of the Holy Cross and is affiliated with the United States Conference of Catholic Bishops. The college offers bachelor's degrees in over 30 majors and minors, as well as three master's degrees and a doctorate.

Admission to Saint Mary's College is selective and competitive. The college seeks students who are academically prepared and who will contribute to the intellectual and social life of the campus community. The college uses a holistic review process when considering applications, taking into account factors such as academic achievement, leadership, service, and extracurricular involvement.

Saint Mary's College is committed to making a Catholic education accessible to all qualified students. The college offers a variety of scholarships and financial aid programs to help make college affordable. For more information about admission requirements and the financial aid process, interested students are encouraged to contact the Office of Admission or the Office of Financial Aid.
