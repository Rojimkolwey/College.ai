Eastern Iowa Community College District

Davenport, IA
101 W Third Street, Davenport, IA 52801-1221
563-336-3300

Eastern Iowa Community College District (EICCD) is a community college district in eastern Iowa. It is headquartered in Davenport, Iowa. The district serves the cities of Davenport, Bettendorf, Clinton, Muscatine, and Scott County.

EICCD was established in 1963, and its first campus, Scott Community College, opened in 1965. The college district now includes four campuses: Scott Community College, Clinton Community College, Muscatine Community College, and Eastern Iowa Community College (formerly Black Hawk College).

EICCD offers over 150 programs of study, including associate's degrees, certificates, and diploma programs. The college district also offers distance learning opportunities and adult education programs.

EICCD is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission.

Admission Requirements

Eastern Iowa Community College District (EICCD) has an open-admission policy, meaning that any person who meets the minimum high school graduation requirements or completes a high school equivalency program is eligible to enroll in credit courses. There is no application fee.

Prospective students must complete an online application and submit official high school transcripts or GED scores. Once these materials are received, the student will be contacted to schedule an advising appointment. During this appointment, the student will meet with an advisor to discuss program options and develop an individualized education plan.

EICCD offers a variety of financial aid options to help students cover the cost of tuition and fees. Scholarships are also available for qualifying students. For more information about admission and financial aid, interested individuals are encouraged to contact the college's admissions office.
