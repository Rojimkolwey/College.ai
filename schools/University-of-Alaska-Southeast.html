The University of Alaska Southeast is a public research university located in Juneau, Alaska, USA. It is a member of the University of Alaska system. The university offers bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees in a wide variety of disciplines, including business, fisheries, forestry, liberal arts, and natural sciences. The university's main campus is situated on the shores of Auke Lake, just minutes from downtown Juneau. The university also operates two other campuses in Sitka and Ketchikan.

The University of Alaska Southeast was founded in 1987, when the Alaska Legislature merged two existing institutions, the University of Alaska Juneau and the Alaska Vocational Technical Center. The university has grown rapidly in recent years, and now enrolls over 4,000 students from all over the world. The university is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities.

The University of Alaska Southeast is committed to providing a high-quality education to its students. The university offers a wide variety of programs and degrees that prepare students for successful careers in a variety of fields. The university also offers a number of research opportunities for students interested in pursuing advanced degrees. The University of Alaska Southeast is an excellent choice for students looking for a challenging and rewarding educational experience.
