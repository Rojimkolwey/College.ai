Capital Community College (CCC) is a public, two-year community college in Hartford, Connecticut. The college offers associate's degrees and certificate programs in a variety of fields, as well as continuing education and workforce development programs.

CCC was founded in 1967 as a result of a community effort to provide higher education opportunities in Hartford. The college originally operated out of a converted department store downtown, but moved to its current location on Main Street in 1972.

Since its founding, CCC has grown to become one of the largest community colleges in Connecticut, serving over 10,000 students each year. The college is accredited by the New England Association of Schools and Colleges.

CCC offers a wide variety of academic programs, including over 50 associate's degree and certificate programs. The college is also home to a number of unique programs, such as the Early College Experience, which allows high school students to earn college credit, and the Collegiate Academy, which provides college-level coursework for adult learners.

In addition to its academic programs, CCC also offers a number of workforce development and continuing education programs. These programs are designed to meet the needs of businesses and individuals in the Hartford area, and include everything from computer classes to English as a Second Language (ESL) courses.

CCC is an important part of the Hartford community, and its programs and services touch the lives of thousands of people each year. The college is committed to providing quality education and training opportunities for all members of the community, and will continue to play a vital role in the city's future.
