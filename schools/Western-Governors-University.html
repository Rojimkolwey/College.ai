Western Governors University

Salt Lake City, UT
4001 South 700 East Suite 700, Salt Lake City, UT 84107
801-274-3280

Founded in 1997 by 19 U.S. governors, Western Governors University is an online, nonprofit, competency-based university. WGU offers more than 60 undergraduate and graduate degree programs in business, information technology, K-12 teacher education, and health professions, including nursing.

WGU's unique approach to education is based on the belief that students should be assessed on their competencies - what they know and can do - rather than on their time in class or credits earned. This approach has revolutionized the way adult learners are able to earn a degree.

WGU students are able to move through their programs at their own pace, taking as little or as much time as they need to complete their courses and demonstrate their competencies. Students are assigned a faculty member as a mentor who provides one-on-one guidance and support throughout their time at WGU.

WGU's programs are accredited by some of the most prestigious organizations in the country, including the National Council for Accreditation of Teacher Education (NCATE) and the Accreditation Commission for Education in Nursing (ACEN).

Since its inception, WGU has awarded more than 60,000 degrees to students in all 50 states and more than 80 countries. WGU has been recognized by a number of organizations for its innovative approach to higher education, including being named one of Fast Company's "Most Innovative Companies" in 2013 and one of Forbes' "America's Top Colleges" for four consecutive years.

Admission Requirements

Western Governors University (WGU) is a private, nonprofit, online university based in Salt Lake City, Utah. The university was founded in 1997 by 19 U.S. governors with a mission to provide access to high-quality, affordable, online degree programs for working adults. WGU is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities, and its nursing programs are accredited by the Commission on Collegiate Nursing Education.

WGU's competency-based learning model allows students to progress through their programs at their own pace, based on their demonstrated mastery of the material. As a result, students can save time and money by not having to repeat coursework they have already mastered.

To be eligible for admission to WGU, applicants must have a high school diploma or equivalent. They must also complete an application and an interview with a WGU admissions counselor. In addition, applicants must demonstrate competency in math and English by passing a placement exam.
