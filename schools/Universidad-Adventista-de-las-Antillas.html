Universidad Adventista de las Antillas is a private, Seventh-day Adventist university in Mayagüez, Puerto Rico. Founded in 1962, it is part of the Seventh-day Adventist education system, the world's second largest Christian school system.

The university offers associate's, bachelor's, and master's degrees in a variety of disciplines. Associate's degrees are offered in business administration, computer science, and nursing. Bachelor's degrees are offered in biology, business administration, communication, computer science, elementary education, English, history, nursing, psychology, religion, and social work. Master's degrees are offered in business administration, counseling, education, and nursing.

The university is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education and the Puerto Rico Department of Education. It is also a member of the Puerto Rico Association of Colleges and Universities.

The university's campus is located in the city of Mayagüez, on the west coast of Puerto Rico. The campus is situated on a hill overlooking the city and the surrounding mountains. The campus is home to the School of Business, the School of Education, the School of Nursing, and the School of Religion. The university also has a campus in the city of San Juan, which is home to the School of Medicine.

The university has a student body of over 2,000 students, representing over 40 countries. The university offers a variety of student organizations and clubs, as well as a variety of intramural and varsity sports teams.

The university is committed to providing a quality education that is affordable and accessible to all. The university offers a variety of financial aid options, including scholarships, grants, and loans. The university also offers a payment plan option for students who are unable to pay their tuition in full.

Admission Requirements

Universidad Adventista de las Antillas is a private, coeducational university located in Mayagüez, Puerto Rico. The university is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education. It is affiliated with the Seventh-day Adventist Church. The university offers associate, bachelor's, and master's degrees in a variety of disciplines.

Admission to Universidad Adventista de las Antillas is open to all qualified students. The university does not discriminate on the basis of race, color, national or ethnic origin, gender, disability, or age. Admission requirements vary by program.

All applicants must submit official transcripts from all colleges and universities attended. Applicants to bachelor's and master's programs must also submit SAT or ACT scores. Bachelor's applicants must have a minimum GPA of 2.5, while master's applicants must have a minimum GPA of 3.0.

International students must submit proof of English proficiency. The university offers a variety of English as a Second Language (ESL) programs to help students meet this requirement.

Once all required materials have been received, the admissions committee will review the application and make a decision. The committee may request an interview with the applicant. Applicants will be notified of the admissions decision by mail.
