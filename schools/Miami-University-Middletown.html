Miami University-Middletown is a public university in Middletown, Ohio. It is one of the four campuses of Miami University. The university offers bachelor's degrees, associate degrees, and certificate programs in a variety of disciplines.

The campus is located on a beautiful, wooded, 500-acre site in Middletown, Ohio. The university is committed to providing a quality education at an affordable price. The campus is home to a variety of student organizations, including fraternities and sororities, and offers a variety of extracurricular activities.

The university is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and is a member of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools.

Admission Requirements

Miami University-Middletown is a public university located in Middletown, Ohio. The university is part of the Miami University system. The university offers associate's and bachelor's degrees.

Admission to Miami University-Middletown is open to all students who have a high school diploma or equivalent. The university does not have an application fee. Students are encouraged to submit their transcripts, test scores, and other materials as soon as possible. The university has rolling admissions, so students will be notified of their admission status on a rolling basis.

Miami University-Middletown does not require students to submit SAT or ACT scores for admission. However, students who do submit scores may be eligible for scholarships and other benefits. The university has a holistic admissions process and will consider all aspects of an applicant's file when making an admission decision.
