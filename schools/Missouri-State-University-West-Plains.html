Missouri State University-West Plains

128 Garfield Avenue, West Plains, MO 65775-2715
417-255-7255

Missouri State University-West Plains (MSUWP) is a two-year public university located in West Plains, Missouri, United States. MSUWP is part of the Missouri State University System and offers associate's degrees and certificate programs. The campus is located on the west side of town on a 200-acre (0.81 km2) site.

MSUWP was established in 1963 as the West Plains campus of the Springfield-based Southwest Missouri State University. In 1965, the campus became a separately accredited institution known as the West Plains campus of SMSU. In 1968, the campus was renamed Missouri State University-West Plains. The campus has undergone several name changes and realignments over the years, most recently becoming part of the MSU System in 2005.

MSUWP offers a variety of academic programs, including arts and sciences, business and technology, and health sciences. The campus also houses the Missouri State University-West Plains Grizzly Athletics program, which competes in the National Junior College Athletic Association (NJCAA) Division I.

MSUWP is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and is a member of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools. The campus is also accredited by the Missouri Department of Higher Education.
