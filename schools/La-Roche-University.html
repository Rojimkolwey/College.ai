La Roche University

Pittsburgh, PA
9000 Babcock Blvd, Pittsburgh, PA 15237-5898
412-367-9300

Founded in 1963, La Roche University is a private institution located in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. The university is comprised of four schools: the School of Business, the School of Education, the School of Humanities and Social Sciences, and the School of Nursing and Health Sciences. La Roche University offers a variety of undergraduate and graduate programs, as well as a Doctor of Nursing Practice program.

The university is committed to providing a well-rounded education to its students and offers a variety of extracurricular activities, including intramural sports, clubs and organizations, and service opportunities. La Roche University also has a strong commitment to community engagement and offers a number of programs and resources to support local businesses and organizations.

With a student-to-faculty ratio of 13:1, La Roche University is able to provide its students with individualized attention and support. The university’s faculty members are experts in their fields and are dedicated to helping students succeed. La Roche University’s small class sizes allow for more interaction between students and faculty members and create a more intimate learning environment.

Located in a safe and welcoming neighborhood, La Roche University is situated on a beautiful campus that includes a variety of amenities, such as a fitness center, a library, and a dining hall. The university also has a number of on-campus housing options, including apartments and townhouses, to meet the needs of its students.

La Roche University is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education and is a member of the Association of Catholic Colleges and Universities.

Admission Requirements

La Roche University is a private institution that was founded in 1963. The school has an urban campus located in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. La Roche University is a 4-year institution that offers bachelor's and master's degrees.

The admission requirements for La Roche University vary depending on the program of study. For undergraduate programs, applicants must submit an application, transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a non-refundable application fee. For graduate programs, applicants must submit an application, transcripts, GRE or GMAT scores, and a non-refundable application fee.

La Roche University has a rolling admissions policy, which means that there is no set deadline for submitting an application. However, it is recommended that applicants submit their materials as early as possible to ensure that they are considered for admission.
