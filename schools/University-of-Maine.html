The University of Maine (UMaine) is a public research university in Orono, Maine. The university is the flagship campus of the University of Maine System. It was established in 1865 as a land-grant university and is the state's land-grant university and the flagship of the University of Maine System. The University of Maine is one of the nation's leading research universities, with an extensive research enterprise that encompasses more than 60 centers, institutes, and laboratories. The university is also a member of the prestigious Association of American Universities (AAU). The University of Maine is home to over 11,000 students from all 50 states and more than 70 countries. The university offers more than 100 undergraduate and 70 graduate programs in a wide range of disciplines, and is one of the few universities in the nation with a comprehensive ocean sciences program. The University of Maine is also a leader in sustainability, with a commitment to reducing its environmental impact and to promoting social and economic justice. The university has been recognized as a national leader in sustainable practices, and was the first university in the nation to sign the American College & University Presidents' Climate Commitment.

Admission Requirements

The University of Maine is a public research university in Orono, Maine. The university offers bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees in 188 fields of study and is the flagship university of the University of Maine System. The university was established in 1865 as a land-grant university and is the state's only public research university. The University of Maine is classified as a Research University with very high research activity by the Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education. The university is a member of the New England Association of Schools and Colleges and the Oak Ridge Associated Universities.

The University of Maine's main campus is located on 3,500 acres (1,400 ha) of land in Orono, Maine, about 80 miles (130 km) north of Portland. The university offers bachelor's degrees in over 188 fields of study, master's degrees in over 120 fields, and doctoral degrees in 61 fields of study through its eight colleges and schools. The University of Maine offers programs of study in the liberal arts, the sciences, education, business, engineering, forestry, and law. The university also offers cooperative programs with other universities, such as the University of New Hampshire, and Maine Maritime Academy.

The University of Maine's admissions process is selective. The university accepts applications from first-time freshmen, transfer students, and international students. The university has an acceptance rate of 68%. First-time freshmen must have a high school diploma or equivalent and submit SAT or ACT scores. The university does not require SAT or ACT scores from transfer students. International students must submit proof of English proficiency. The University of Maine uses a holistic review process when considering applications for admission. The university considers factors such as academic performance, leadership experience, community service, and extracurricular activities when making admissions decisions.
