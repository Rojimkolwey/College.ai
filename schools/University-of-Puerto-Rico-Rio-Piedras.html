The University of Puerto Rico, Rio Piedras Campus was founded on November 22, 1903, as the Puerto Rico Normal School, with the purpose of training teachers for the public school system of Puerto Rico. It was the first higher education institution in Puerto Rico and the Caribbean. The school was located in the old San Juan Arsenal building, which is now the site of the Puerto Rico Museum of Contemporary Art. In 1919, the school was renamed the Puerto Rico Normal and Industrial School. In 1924, the school was again renamed, this time to the Puerto Rico Normal and Teachers College. In 1931, the institution was renamed the University of Puerto Rico, Rio Piedras Campus.

Admission Requirements

The University of Puerto Rico-Rio Piedras is a public research university located in San Juan, Puerto Rico. Founded in 1903, it is the oldest and largest campus of the University of Puerto Rico system. The university offers bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees in over 100 academic programs.

To be eligible for admission to the University of Puerto Rico-Rio Piedras, applicants must meet the following requirements:

1. Be a citizen or permanent resident of Puerto Rico.

2. Be a high school graduate or have completed a GED.

3. Submit official transcripts from all colleges and universities attended.

4. Submit SAT or ACT scores.

5. Pay the non-refundable application fee.

6. Submit the completed application form.

7. Applicants who are not citizens or permanent residents of Puerto Rico may be required to submit additional documentation.

8. International applicants whose first language is not English must submit TOEFL or IELTS scores.

Once the above requirements have been met, applicants will be evaluated on their academic record, test scores, and personal statements. The most competitive applicants will be offered admission to the university.
