Boston University

Boston, MA
One Silber Way, Boston, MA 02215
617-353-2000

Boston University is a leading private research university with an enrollment of over 33,000 students. The University is comprised of 17 schools and colleges, offering more than 250 programs of study. BU is an internationally recognized institution, with students and faculty coming from all over the world. The University is located in Boston, Massachusetts, one of the most vibrant and historic cities in the United States.

Founded in 1839, Boston University is a non-profit, private research university. The University is accredited by the New England Association of Schools and Colleges. BU is ranked 42nd in the 2019 edition of Best Colleges in National Universities. The University offers a wide range of undergraduate, graduate, and professional programs.

Undergraduate programs are offered through the College of Arts and Sciences, the School of Communication, the School of Hospitality Administration, the School of Management, the Metropolitan College, and the School of Social Work. Graduate programs are offered through the Graduate School of Arts and Sciences, the School of Law, the School of Medicine, the School of Public Health, the School of Theology, and the School of Dental Medicine.

The University has a strong commitment to research, with over $400 million in research expenditures annually. Boston University is a member of the Association of American Universities, an organization of the nation’s leading research universities.

The University is located on a 135-acre campus in Boston’s historic Back Bay neighborhood. The campus is adjacent to the Charles River and features a variety of buildings, including historic landmarks and state-of-the-art facilities.

Boston University is a diverse and vibrant community of scholars and learners. The University is committed to providing an inclusive and welcoming environment for all members of the community.

Admission Requirements

Boston University is a private research university with an acceptance rate of 18%. Located in Boston, Massachusetts, BU consists of 17 schools and colleges, offering over 250 programs of study. The university has a strong commitment to research, with over $400 million in research expenditures annually.

BU's admissions process is highly selective. The university looks for students with strong academic records, high standardized test scores, and involvement in extracurricular activities. The admissions essay and letters of recommendation are also important factors in the decision-making process.

For students interested in applying to Boston University, the first step is to submit an application through the Common Application or the Coalition Application. BU also has its own supplemental application, which includes an essay. Once the application is complete, students will need to submit official high school transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and two letters of recommendation.
