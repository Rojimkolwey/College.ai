The University of Cincinnati-Blue Ash College (UCBA) is a public community college in Blue Ash, Ohio. It is one of four campuses of the University of Cincinnati. UCBA offers more than 50 associate's degree and certificate programs in business, health, information technology, and the liberal arts. The college is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission.

UCBA was founded in 1967 as a two-year branch of the University of Cincinnati. The college moved to its current location in Blue Ash in 1974. In 2006, the college began offering four-year bachelor's degrees in business and information technology. In 2012, the college began offering a four-year degree in health information management.

UCBA is located on a 140-acre campus in Blue Ash, a suburb of Cincinnati. The campus includes five academic buildings, a library, a student center, and athletic facilities. The college has a student-to-faculty ratio of 18:1 and an average class size of 24.

Approximately 4,000 students are enrolled at UCBA. The college offers a variety of student services, including academic advising, tutoring, and career services. The college also has an active student government and more than 30 student clubs and organizations.

UCBA is part of the University of Cincinnati, which is a public research university located in Cincinnati, Ohio. The University of Cincinnati is the largest university in Ohio and one of the largest universities in the United States. The university is made up of 14 colleges and schools, and offers more than 300 undergraduate and graduate programs. The University of Cincinnati is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission.

Admission Requirements

The University of Cincinnati-Blue Ash College is a public institution of higher education located in Blue Ash, Ohio, United States, a suburb of Cincinnati. It is part of the University of Cincinnati system. The college offers two-year associate's degrees and one-year certificates in over 50 areas of study.

Admission to the University of Cincinnati-Blue Ash College is open to all qualified applicants. The college uses a holistic approach to admissions, meaning that all aspects of an applicant's file are considered in the admissions decision. There are no minimum test score or GPA requirements for admission.

The college does require that all applicants submit official high school transcripts and SAT or ACT scores. Applicants are also encouraged to submit letters of recommendation and a personal statement. Admission decisions are made on a rolling basis, so applicants are encouraged to apply early.
