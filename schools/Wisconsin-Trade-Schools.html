About Wisconsin

Wisconsin is a state located in the Midwestern United States. As of 2018, the population of Wisconsin was 5,813,568. The state capital is Madison, and the largest city is Milwaukee. Wisconsin is bordered by the states of Michigan, Illinois, and Iowa.

Wisconsin is known for its dairy industry, as well as its production of corn, soybeans, and wheat. The state is also home to many large companies, such as Johnson Controls, Kimberly-Clark, and Harley-Davidson. Tourism is also a major industry in Wisconsin, with the state's lakes and parks attracting millions of visitors each year.
