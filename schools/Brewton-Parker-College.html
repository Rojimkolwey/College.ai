Brewton-Parker College is a small, private, Christian liberal arts college located in Mount Vernon, Georgia. The school was founded in 1904 by the Baptist Convention of Georgia as Mount Vernon Baptist Normal and Industrial School. In 1937, the name was changed to Brewton-Parker College in honor of two former presidents of the Baptist Convention of Georgia, Robert E. Park and J. B. Brewton. The college is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools and offers bachelor's degrees in over 30 areas of study. The school's athletics teams compete in the National Association of Intercollegiate Athletics (NAIA) and are known as the Barons.

Admission Requirements

Brewton-Parker College is a private, Christian liberal arts college located in Mount Vernon, Georgia. The college was founded in 1904 and is affiliated with the Georgia Baptist Convention. The college offers bachelor's degrees in over 30 majors and pre-professional programs. The college has an enrollment of over 1,000 students and a student-to-faculty ratio of 13-to-1.

The admissions process at Brewton-Parker College is designed to be personal and informative. The admissions staff is committed to providing each student with the individual attention needed to make the college selection process as smooth as possible.

Brewton-Parker College admits students of any race, color, national and ethnic origin to all the rights, privileges, programs, and activities generally accorded or made available to students at the college. It does not discriminate on the basis of race, color, national and ethnic origin in administration of its educational policies, admissions policies, scholarship and loan programs, and athletic and other school-administered programs.

In order to be considered for admission to Brewton-Parker College, applicants must submit an application for admission, official high school transcripts, and SAT or ACT scores. Homeschooled applicants must submit an official transcript or portfolio evaluation. The admissions staff will review all materials and make a decision based on the student's academic record, test scores, and personal essay.

Brewton-Parker College has a rolling admissions policy, meaning that there is no deadline for applications. However, students are encouraged to apply early in order to ensure that they are able to take advantage of all the college has to offer.
