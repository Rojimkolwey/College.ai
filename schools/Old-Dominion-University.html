Old Dominion University

Norfolk, VA
5115 Hampton Blvd, Norfolk, VA 23529
757-683-3000

Old Dominion University is a public research university in Norfolk, Virginia. The university was established in 1930 as the Norfolk Division of the College of William & Mary and became an independent institution in 1962. It is categorized as a doctoral university with "high research activity" by the Carnegie Foundation for the Advancement of Teaching.

Old Dominion offers 168 degree programs, including bachelor's, master's, doctoral, and professional degrees. The university has four undergraduate and three graduate schools, and its main campus covers over 251 acres (1.02 km2) in the city of Norfolk. It also operates a regional campus in the Hampton Roads region, and its distance-learning program offers education to students worldwide.

The university has an extensive research enterprise, with annual research expenditures of over $110 million. Old Dominion is home to the Jefferson Laboratory, a U.S. Department of Energy facility, and is also a leading partner in the Large Synoptic Survey Telescope project. The university has produced two Nobel laureates, two Pulitzer Prize winners, and one Fields Medalist.

Old Dominion University was founded in 1930 as the Norfolk Division of the College of William & Mary. It became an independent institution in 1962, taking the name Old Dominion College. The name was changed to Old Dominion University in 1969.
