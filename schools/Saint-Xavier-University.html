Saint Xavier University

3700 W 103rd St, Chicago, IL 60655
773-298-3000

Saint Xavier University is a Catholic institution of higher learning located in Chicago, Illinois. The school was founded in 1846 by a group of seven Jesuits, and today the university is still run by the Jesuits. Saint Xavier is the oldest Catholic university in Illinois, and the oldest university in Chicago. The school enrolls approximately 4,000 students from over 60 different countries. The university offers undergraduate and graduate programs, and is divided into four colleges: the College of Arts and Sciences, the Graham School of Management, the College of Nursing and Health Sciences, and the School of Education.

Saint Xavier University is committed to providing a quality education to its students. The school has a strong focus on the liberal arts, and offers a wide range of programs in the humanities, sciences, and business. The university also has a number of unique programs, such as a Master of Arts in Catholic Studies, and a Doctor of Philosophy in Pastoral Studies. Saint Xavier is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission, and its nursing program is accredited by the Commission on Collegiate Nursing Education.

The university is located on a beautiful campus in the southwest side of Chicago. The campus includes a number of historic buildings, as well as modern facilities. Saint Xavier has a number of athletic teams, and competes in the National Association of Intercollegiate Athletics. The university also has a number of clubs and organizations, and a vibrant campus life.

Admission Requirements

Saint Xavier University is a private, Catholic university located in Chicago, Illinois. Founded in 1846 by the Sisters of Mercy, Saint Xavier University is the oldest Catholic university in Illinois and the oldest women's college in the state. The university is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and is a member of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools. Saint Xavier University offers more than 60 undergraduate and graduate programs, including online programs, through its five schools: the School of Arts and Sciences, the School of Education, the School of Nursing and Health Sciences, the Graham School of Management, and the School of Nursing.

Undergraduate students must complete an application for admission and submit all required transcripts and test scores. The university requires a minimum 2.5 GPA for admission, although some programs may require a higher GPA. Graduate students must submit an application for admission, transcripts, and test scores. The university requires a minimum 3.0 GPA for admission, although some programs may require a higher GPA.
