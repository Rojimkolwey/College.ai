Ross Medical Education Center in Muncie, Indiana is a private for-profit medical school with campuses throughout the United States. It is owned and operated by Ross Education, LLC, a subsidiary of DeVry Education Group. The school offers Associate's and Bachelor's degrees in medical assisting and medical office administration, as well as a diploma in medical billing and coding. Ross Muncie also offers an externship program that allows students to gain hands-on experience in a medical setting.

The school was founded in 1976 by Barbara Ross-Lee, who was the first African-American woman to be licensed as a doctor in the state of New York. Ross-Lee believed that everyone should have access to quality medical care, regardless of their background or economic status. Ross Education has since grown to include 22 campuses in 11 states, serving over 4,500 students each year.

Ross Medical Education Center in Muncie is accredited by the Commission on Accreditation of Allied Health Education Programs (CAAHEP). The school's medical assisting and medical office administration programs are both accredited by the Accrediting Bureau of Health Education Schools (ABHES). Ross Muncie is also a member of the Indiana Association of Private Colleges and Universities (IAPCU).

The school offers a variety of financial aid options to help students pay for their education, including grants, loans, and scholarships. Ross Muncie also participates in the Indiana Statewide Transfer General Education Core (GECC) program, which allows students to transfer credits from other colleges and universities.

Ross Medical Education Center in Muncie is located in the city of Muncie, Indiana, which is about 60 miles northeast of Indianapolis. The city is home to Ball State University, and the school is situated near a variety of medical facilities, including IU Health Ball Memorial Hospital and Community Health Network.
