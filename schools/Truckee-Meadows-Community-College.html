Truckee Meadows Community College (TMCC) is a two-year institution of higher education in Reno, Nevada. Founded in 1969, TMCC is part of the Nevada System of Higher Education (NSHE). The college offers Associate of Arts, Associate of Science and Associate of Applied Science degrees, as well as certificate programs.

TMCC serves the greater Reno area with campuses in Reno and Sparks. The college also offers classes at the University of Nevada, Reno and at area high schools.

TMCC is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities. The college is also a member of the Association of Community College Trustees and the American Association of Community Colleges.

TMCC is committed to student success and provides a variety of services to support students, including academic advising, tutoring, career services, and disability services. The college also offers a variety of student clubs and organizations, as well as athletics programs.

TMCC is an affordable option for higher education, with tuition and fees that are lower than the national average. The college also offers a variety of financial aid options to help students pay for college.

TMCC is a great choice for students who want to start their college career close to home. The college offers a supportive environment, small class sizes, and a variety of programs and services to help students succeed.

To be eligible for admission to TMCC, students must:

• Be a high school graduate or have earned a GED

• Be 18 years of age or older

• Take the College Placement Test (CPT) or submit acceptable scores on the SAT or ACT

• Complete the application for admission

• Pay the non-refundable application fee
