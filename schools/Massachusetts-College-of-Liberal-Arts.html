Massachusetts College of Liberal Arts

Founded in 1894, Massachusetts College of Liberal Arts (MCLA) is a public liberal arts college located in North Adams, Massachusetts. The college offers more than 50 undergraduate degree programs and 20 graduate degree programs, as well as a variety of continuing education and professional development opportunities. MCLA is accredited by the New England Commission of Higher Education (NECHE).

MCLA is committed to providing a high-quality, affordable education to all students. The college offers a variety of financial aid options, including grants, loans, and scholarships. MCLA also participates in the MassTransfer program, which allows students to transfer credits from other colleges and universities in Massachusetts.

MCLA is a diverse and inclusive community. The college offers a variety of support services for students, including academic advising, career counseling, and tutoring. MCLA also offers a variety of student organizations and clubs, as well as Greek life.

MCLA is located in the Berkshires, a region of western Massachusetts known for its natural beauty and cultural attractions. The college is situated on a 220-acre campus, and its facilities include a variety of academic, athletic, and residential buildings. MCLA is home to a variety of sports teams, and its students have the opportunity to participate in a variety of intramural and club sports.

MCLA is an active member of the North Adams community. The college offers a variety of community-based learning opportunities, and its students volunteer for a variety of local organizations. MCLA also hosts a number of events and programs open to the public, including art exhibitions, musical performances, and lectures.
