Northeast Texas Community College (NTCC) is a two-year public college located in Mount Pleasant, Texas. The college offers associate degrees and certificate programs in a variety of academic and technical fields. NTCC is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges.

NTCC was founded in 1971 as Northeast Texas Junior College. The college originally operated out of a single building in downtown Mount Pleasant. In its first year, the college enrolled just over 400 students. Today, NTCC serves over 5,000 students each year.

The college offers a variety of academic programs, including arts and sciences, business, health sciences, and industrial technologies. NTCC also offers a number of certificate programs in fields such as welding, cosmetology, and automotive repair.

In addition to its academic programs, NTCC also offers a number of student services, including counseling, tutoring, and job placement assistance. The college also has an active campus life, with a variety of clubs and organizations for students to get involved in.

NTCC is an affordable option for students looking to get a quality education. The college offers a variety of financial aid options, including scholarships, grants, and loans. NTCC also offers a number of payment plans to make college more affordable for students.

Admission Requirements

Northeast Texas Community College (NTCC) is a two-year public college located in Mount Pleasant, Texas. NTCC offers Associate of Arts, Associate of Science, and Associate of Applied Science degrees, as well as certificates in a variety of fields.

To be eligible for admission to NTCC, applicants must:

- Be a high school graduate or have a GED

- Submit an application for admission

- Submit official high school and/or college transcripts

- Take the Texas Success Initiative Assessment (TSIA) or an equivalent placement test

Applicants who do not meet the above requirements may still be eligible for admission through the Texas Success Initiative (TSI) program. For more information on admission requirements and the TSI program, please visit the NTCC website.
