Arkansas Community Colleges


Arkansas's community colleges are an invaluable part of the state's higher education system, providing access to quality education at an affordable cost. The Arkansas Department of Higher Education oversees the state's 22 public two-year colleges, which serve over 70,000 students each year. The colleges offer a wide range of certificate and associate degree programs, as well as workforce training and continuing education courses.

The Arkansas community college system is committed to providing affordable and accessible educational opportunities to all Arkansans. The colleges provide financial aid, scholarships, and other forms of assistance to help students cover the cost of tuition, fees, and books. The colleges also offer a variety of services to help students succeed, such as tutoring, advising, and career counseling.

The Arkansas community college system is focused on preparing students for successful careers. The colleges have strong partnerships with local businesses and industries, and offer a variety of career-focused programs and courses that are designed to equip students with the skills and knowledge they need to succeed in the workforce. The colleges also offer transfer programs that allow students to easily transfer credits to four-year universities.

The Arkansas community college system is dedicated to providing a quality education to all students, regardless of background or financial need. The colleges strive to create an inclusive and supportive environment, and to ensure that all students have access to the resources they need to reach their educational and career goals.
