St Olaf College is a small, private, liberal arts college located in Northfield, Minnesota. The college was founded in 1874 by a group of Norwegian-American immigrant pastors and farmers, and is named after the King of Norway. St Olaf College is affiliated with the Evangelical Lutheran Church in America, and its campus is situated on the historic Hill of Three Crosses. The college enrolls around 3,000 students each year, and is known for its rigorous academic program, strong Scandinavian culture, and beautiful campus.
