Founded in 1787, York College of Pennsylvania is a private, coeducational college of the liberal arts and sciences. The college offers more than 50 undergraduate majors and graduate programs in business, education and nursing. York College students come from more than 30 states and more than 20 countries. The student-faculty ratio is 14-to-1, and the average class size is 20. The college is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education.

Admission Requirements

In order to be admitted to York College, students must submit an application along with all required supporting documentation. Applications can be submitted online or via mail.

York College requires all applicants to submit official high school transcripts or equivalent documentation. GED scores, home-schooled transcripts, and international transcripts must be evaluated by an outside credential evaluation service before being submitted to the college.

In addition to transcripts, applicants must also submit SAT or ACT scores, as well as two letters of recommendation. One letter must be from a guidance counselor or teacher, and the other must be from someone who can speak to the applicant's character.

Once all required materials have been received, the college will review the application and make a decision. Accepted students will be notified of their admission status and will be given further instructions on how to complete the enrollment process.
