Oregon Community Colleges


Oregon’s community colleges are a great asset to the state, offering a wide variety of educational opportunities to students of all ages. Oregon’s 17 community colleges offer certificate and degree programs in a wide range of fields, including business, health care, engineering, and computer science. The colleges also provide a variety of services, such as job placement assistance, career counseling, and financial aid.

Oregon’s community colleges are also known for their affordability. Tuition rates at Oregon’s community colleges are among the lowest in the nation. Additionally, many of the colleges offer scholarship and grant programs to help make college more accessible to Oregonians.

The community colleges also provide a variety of support services to students. Many of the colleges offer tutoring, academic advising, and other resources to help students succeed. Additionally, some of the colleges offer specialized programs and services for veterans, single parents, and other students with unique needs.

Oregon’s community colleges are also a great way to get involved in the community. Many of the colleges offer internships, volunteer opportunities, and other ways to get involved in the local community. Additionally, many of the colleges have active student organizations and clubs that help students get connected and engaged with their college and the surrounding community.

Overall, Oregon’s community colleges are a great asset to the state, offering a wide variety of educational opportunities, support services, and community engagement opportunities. With their affordability, quality programs, and commitment to student success, Oregon’s community colleges are a great choice for anyone looking for an affordable and accessible college education.
