Athens Technical College

Athens, GA
800 U.S. Hwy 29 North, Athens, GA 30601
706-355-5000

Founded in 1958, Athens Technical College is a public, two-year institution of higher education located in Athens, Georgia, United States. Athens Tech is part of the University System of Georgia and offers more than 50 associate's degrees, diplomas, and technical certificates of credit. The college also has adult education, continuing education, and economic development programs.

Athens Technical College serves Clarke, Madison, Oconee, and Oglethorpe counties. The college has its main campus in Athens, and additional locations in Elbert County, Greene County, and Morgan County.

The mission of Athens Technical College is to provide quality technical and adult education that contributes to the economic development of northeast Georgia.

Athens Technical College is accredited by the Commission on Colleges of the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools to award associate degrees, diplomas, and technical certificates of credit. The college is also accredited by the following agencies:

Accrediting Commission of Career Schools and Colleges of Technology

Georgia Board of Nursing

Georgia Department of Early Care and Learning

National Automotive Technicians Education Foundation

National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence

The college is a member of the American Association of Community Colleges, the Association of Georgia Colleges, and the Technical College System of Georgia.

Admission Requirements

Admission requirements at Athens Technical College vary depending on the program of study. For most programs, students must have a high school diploma or equivalent. Some programs may require additional admission requirements, such as SAT or ACT scores, letters of recommendation, or essays.
