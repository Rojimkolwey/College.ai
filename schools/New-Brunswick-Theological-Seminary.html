New Brunswick Theological Seminary (NBTS) is a private, graduate school of theology affiliated with the Reformed Church in America (RCA). It is located in New Brunswick, New Jersey, United States, and offers Master of Divinity (M.Div.), Master of Arts in Religion (M.A.R.), and Doctor of Ministry (D.Min.) degrees.

NBTS was founded in 1784 as the Theological Seminary of the Reformed Protestant Dutch Church. It is the oldest seminary in the United States that is affiliated with the Reformed tradition, and one of the oldest seminaries in the country period. It was chartered by the state of New Jersey in 1787. The seminary moved to its current location in 1810.

The seminary has been accredited by the Association of Theological Schools in the United States and Canada since 1938. It is also a member of the ecumenical Graduate Theological Union.

NBTS has produced a number of notable alumni, including several presidents of colleges and universities, and a number of prominent clergy in the RCA and other denominations.

Admission Requirements

New Brunswick Theological Seminary (NBTS) is a private, ecumenical Christian seminary located in New Brunswick, New Jersey. It is the oldest seminary of the Reformed Church in America (RCA) and one of the nine seminaries of the RCA.

NBTS offers both Master of Divinity (M.Div.) and Master of Arts in Religion (M.A.R.) degrees. The M.Div. degree is a three-year program designed for those who wish to enter ordained ministry in the RCA. The M.A.R. degree is a two-year program designed for those who wish to pursue careers in religious education, chaplaincy, or other non-ordained ministry positions.

Admission to NBTS is open to all students who have earned a bachelor's degree from an accredited college or university. There are no specific admission requirements beyond a bachelor's degree, though all applicants are required to submit an application, transcripts, and a personal essay.

NBTS is accredited by the Association of Theological Schools in the United States and Canada (ATS).
