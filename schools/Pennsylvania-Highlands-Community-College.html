Pennsylvania Highlands Community College

Johnstown, PA
101 Community College Way, Johnstown, PA 15904-2949
814-262-6400

Pennsylvania Highlands Community College is a two-year institution of higher education located in the scenic Laurel Highlands of southwestern Pennsylvania. The college serves a rural population in a five-county area and offers a broad range of academic programs and services that are responsive to the needs of the community. The college is committed to providing quality education and training that lead to successful careers and lifelong learning.

Pennsylvania Highlands Community College was founded in 1965 as a result of a feasibility study conducted by the Pennsylvania State Board of Education. The college began offering classes in the fall of 1967 with an initial enrollment of just over 200 students. The college has grown steadily over the years and now serves a five-county area with an enrollment of over 4,000 students.

The college offers a variety of academic programs that lead to an Associate in Arts, Associate in Science, or Associate in Applied Science degree. The college also offers a number of certificate programs that provide students with the skills and training needed for specific careers. In addition to academic programs, the college provides a variety of student services that support the academic and personal success of students.

Pennsylvania Highlands Community College is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education. The college is also a member of the American Association of Community Colleges and the Pennsylvania Association of Community Colleges.

Admission Requirements

Pennsylvania Highlands Community College is an accredited, two-year institution that offers a wide variety of academic and career-oriented programs. The College is committed to providing accessible, high-quality educational opportunities to a diverse student population.

Admission to Pennsylvania Highlands Community College is open to all individuals who have a high school diploma or equivalent. The College does not discriminate on the basis of race, color, national origin, sex, disability, or age in its admissions policies.

Individuals interested in enrolling at Pennsylvania Highlands Community College must complete an application for admission and submit all required transcripts and test scores. The College has an open admissions policy, meaning that all applicants who meet the minimum requirements will be accepted.

Once an individual has been accepted to the College, they will be required to complete a placement test to determine which courses they will need to take. Pennsylvania Highlands Community College offers a variety of courses, both online and on-campus, to meet the needs of all students.
