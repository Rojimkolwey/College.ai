Community College of Baltimore County

Baltimore, MD
7201 Rossville Boulevard, Baltimore, MD 21237
443-840-2222

The Community College of Baltimore County (CCBC) is a public community college in Baltimore County, Maryland. CCBC offers associate's degrees, certificates, and adult education courses in a variety of subjects.

CCBC was founded in 1957 as the Baltimore County Junior College. The college was renamed in 1975 to better reflect its mission of serving the community. CCBC is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education.

CCBC has four campuses located in Catonsville, Essex, Dundalk, and Owings Mills. The college also has a number of satellite locations and an online learning program.

CCBC offers over 100 degree and certificate programs, as well as adult education courses. The college's most popular programs include business, health care, and liberal arts. CCBC also has a strong reputation for its STEM programs.

CCBC is committed to serving the community and providing affordable, high-quality education. The college offers a variety of financial aid options and scholarships to help students pay for college. CCBC also has a number of programs and services designed to support students, including academic advising, tutoring, and career services.

Admission Requirements

CCBC has an open admissions policy, meaning that any student who has a high school diploma or equivalent is eligible to enroll. However, some programs may have additional requirements, such as a minimum GPA or placement test scores.

Once students are admitted, they must complete a new student orientation and meet with an advisor to plan their course of study. CCBC offers a variety of academic and career-oriented programs, so students should take time to explore their options and choose a path that is right for them.
