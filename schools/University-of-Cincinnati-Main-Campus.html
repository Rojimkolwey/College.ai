Founded in 1819, the University of Cincinnati (UC) is one of the first American institutions of higher education. The public research university is located in Cincinnati, Ohio, and is the largest university in the state. The university has an enrollment of over 44,000 students, making it one of the largest universities in the United States. The University of Cincinnati is classified as a Doctoral University with the highest research activity by the Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education. The university offers more than 300 majors and 100 minors, as well as pre-professional programs in law, medicine, and dentistry. The University of Cincinnati is home to a number of research institutes and centers, including the Cincinnati Children's Hospital Medical Center, the Lindner Center of HOPE, and the UC Cancer Institute. The University of Cincinnati is also a member of the Association of American Universities (AAU).
