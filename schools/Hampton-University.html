Hampton University is a private historically black university in Hampton, Virginia. It was founded in 1868 by black and white leaders of the American Missionary Association after the American Civil War to provide education to freedmen. These leaders believed that all people, regardless of race, gender, or socioeconomic status, deserved access to education.

Hampton University has a long and rich history. It has been ranked as one of the best historically black colleges and universities (HBCUs) in the country. The university has produced many notable alumni, including civil rights leader Booker T. Washington, journalist and television personality Oprah Winfrey, and astronaut Mae Jemison.

Hampton University is committed to providing a quality education to all of its students. The university offers a variety of undergraduate and graduate programs, as well as professional programs. Hampton University is also home to a number of research centers and institutes, which contribute to the university's academic excellence.

Hampton University is a beautiful campus located on the Hampton River. The university's grounds are landscaped with trees, flowers, and shrubs. The campus also includes a number of historic buildings, such as the Emancipation Oak, which is a symbol of the university's commitment to education and equality.

Admission Requirements

Hampton University is a private, historically black university in Hampton, Virginia. It was founded in 1868 by black and white leaders of the American Missionary Association after the American Civil War to provide education to freedmen. The university is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools.

The university offers bachelor's degrees in 50 academic majors, master's degrees in 31 areas of study, and doctorates in education and physical therapy. Admission to Hampton University is selective; the university had an acceptance rate of 43% for the fall semester of 2019.

To be considered for admission, applicants must submit an application, official high school transcripts, SAT or ACT scores, and a non-refundable application fee. Hampton University does not require letters of recommendation or an essay.

First-year students at Hampton University must complete a first-year experience program, which includes a first-year seminar, a service-learning experience, and a research experience. The first-year experience program is designed to help students transition to college and to develop the skills necessary for success in college and beyond.
