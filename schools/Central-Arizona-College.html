Central Arizona College is a community college located in Arizona. The college offers associate degrees and certificate programs in a variety of fields, including business, healthcare, and liberal arts. Central Arizona College is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission.

Central Arizona College was founded in 1965 as a two-year college. The college originally served the residents of Pinal County, but has since expanded to serve students from all over Arizona. Central Arizona College offers a variety of associate degrees and certificate programs, as well as continuing education and workforce development courses.

The college has four campuses located in Coolidge, Florence, Maricopa, and Apache Junction. Central Arizona College also has a distance learning program that offers online and hybrid courses.

Central Arizona College is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. The college is committed to providing quality education to its students and preparing them for successful careers.

Admission Requirements

Central Arizona College (CAC) is a public community college located in Arizona. CAC offers associate degrees and certificate programs. The college has an open admissions policy, meaning that any student who meets the minimum requirements for admission will be accepted into the college.

The minimum requirements for admission to CAC are as follows:

1. The applicant must be a high school graduate or have a GED.

2. The applicant must submit official transcripts from all previous colleges attended.

3. The applicant must take and pass the Arizona Placement Test (APT).

4. The applicant must complete a CAC application form.

5. The applicant must pay the application fee.

Once these requirements have been met, the applicant will be considered for admission to the college. Admission to CAC does not guarantee admission into a particular program or course of study. Some programs may have additional requirements that must be met in order to be considered for admission.
