Iowa Western Community College

Council Bluffs, IA
2700 College Rd, Council Bluffs, IA 51503-1057
712-325-3200

Iowa Western Community College (IWCC) is a two-year public institution of higher education in Council Bluffs, Iowa. It is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. The college offers both credit and non-credit programs.

IWCC was established in 1967 as the Iowa Western Development Corporation. The first classes were held in 1968 with an enrollment of 537 students. In 1971, the name was changed to Iowa Western Community College. The college has grown steadily since then and now serves over 5,000 students.

IWCC offers over 50 different programs of study leading to an Associate of Arts, Associate of Science, or Associate of Applied Science degree. The college also offers a number of certificate programs.

IWCC is committed to providing affordable, quality education and training. The college has a variety of financial aid programs available to help students pay for their education.

IWCC is located in Council Bluffs, Iowa, just across the Missouri River from Omaha, Nebraska. The college is situated on a beautiful 200-acre campus. The college is within easy driving distance of several major metropolitan areas, including Des Moines, Kansas City, and Lincoln.

Admission Requirements

Iowa Western Community College has an open-door admissions policy, meaning that any student who has a high school diploma or equivalent is welcome to enroll. There are no minimum GPA or standardized test score requirements. However, students are encouraged to complete the college's placement tests prior to enrolling in order to ensure they are placed in the appropriate level classes.

Students interested in attending Iowa Western Community College can begin the admissions process by completing an online application. Once the application is submitted, students will need to submit official high school transcripts or GED scores. If the student has completed any college coursework, they will also need to submit official transcripts from those institutions. Once all required materials have been received, the student's file will be reviewed by the admissions committee and a decision will be made.

If you have any questions about the admissions process or requirements at Iowa Western Community College, you can contact the admissions office for assistance.
