Saddleback College

Mission Viejo, CA
28000 Marguerite Pky, Mission Viejo, CA 92692-3635
949-582-4500

Saddleback College is a community college in Mission Viejo, California, United States. Saddleback College is part of the Saddleback Valley Community College District and is accredited by the Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges. The college offers more than 300 academic and career technical education programs. The college also offers distance education, continuing education, and adult education programs.

Saddleback College was founded in 1968. The college was originally located in a converted elementary school in Santa Ana, California. The college moved to its current location in Mission Viejo in 1972.

Saddleback College serves more than 34,000 students each semester. The college has a diverse student body, with students from more than 100 countries enrolled at the college.

The college offers a variety of student services, including academic advising, tutoring, career counseling, and disability services. The college also has a vibrant student life, with more than 60 student clubs and organizations.

Saddleback College is located in the heart of Orange County, California. The college is situated on a beautiful 128-acre campus. The college is within easy driving distance of Los Angeles, San Diego, and the Inland Empire.

Admission Requirements

Saddleback College is a public community college in Mission Viejo, California. It is one of the 112 colleges in the California Community Colleges System. The college offers Associate's degrees and certificate programs in over 100 areas of study.

To be eligible for admission to Saddleback College, students must meet the following requirements:

1. Be a high school graduate or have a GED certificate.

2. Submit official transcripts from all previous colleges and universities attended.

3. Complete the Saddleback College application form.

4. Pay the non-refundable application fee.

5. Take the Saddleback College placement tests, if required.

6. Meet with a counselor to develop an educational plan.

7. Register for classes.

8. Pay tuition and fees.

9. Attend classes.
