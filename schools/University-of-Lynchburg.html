University of Lynchburg

Lynchburg, VA
1501 Lakeside Dr, Lynchburg, VA 24501-3113
434-544-8100

Founded in 1871, the University of Lynchburg is a private, comprehensive institution located in Lynchburg, Virginia, just 90 minutes from both Richmond and Charlottesville. The University is committed to excellence in teaching, personal development, and service to the community. The University of Lynchburg offers more than 60 majors and programs, and is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges to award bachelor's and master's degrees. Lynchburg is home to more than 2,000 students from across the United States and more than 40 countries. The University of Lynchburg is located on 134 acres in historic Lynchburg, Virginia. The campus is a short walk from downtown Lynchburg, and is adjacent to the James River and Percival's Island Natural Area. The University of Lynchburg is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges to award bachelor's and master's degrees.

Admission Requirements

The University of Lynchburg is a private university located in Lynchburg, Virginia. The university was founded in 1903 as Lynchburg Baptist College. The university offers over 60 undergraduate majors and 20 graduate programs. The university has an enrollment of approximately 2,000 students.

The admission requirements for undergraduate students at the University of Lynchburg are as follows:

-A completed application form

-A non-refundable application fee of $40

-Official high school transcript

-Official SAT or ACT scores

-Two letters of recommendation

-A personal essay

-An interview (optional)

Graduate admission requirements vary by program. For more information, please visit the university's website.
