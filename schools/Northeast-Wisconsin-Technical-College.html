Northeast Wisconsin Technical College, or NWTC, is a public, 2-year college located in Green Bay, Wisconsin. It is part of the Wisconsin Technical College System. NWTC offers over 200 associate degree, technical diploma, and certificate programs in a variety of fields, such as business, health, manufacturing, and more. The college also offers distance learning opportunities and adult basic education classes.

NWTC was founded in 1912 as a trade school for the Green Bay area. Since then, the college has grown and expanded its programs and services. Today, NWTC serves over 40,000 students each year. The college is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and is a member of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools.

Admission Requirements

Northeast Wisconsin Technical College (NWTC) is a public, two-year college located in Green Bay, Wisconsin. The college offers associate degrees, technical diplomas, and certificates in a variety of programs.

To be eligible for admission to NWTC, students must have a high school diploma or equivalent. Students must also take the ACT or SAT and submit their scores to the college. In addition, students must complete a college placement test and meet with a college advisor to discuss their program of study.

NWTC has an open admissions policy, meaning that all students who meet the above requirements will be admitted to the college. However, some programs may have additional admission requirements. For example, the nursing program requires students to complete a pre-admission exam.

Once admitted, students must complete a new student orientation and register for classes. Classes at NWTC are offered on a semester basis, and students must complete a minimum of 12 credits per semester to be considered full-time.

NWTC offers a variety of financial aid options to help students pay for their education, including grants, loans, and scholarships. Students must complete the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) to be considered for financial aid.
