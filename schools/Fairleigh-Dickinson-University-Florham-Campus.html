Fairleigh Dickinson University-Florham Campus is a private university in Madison, New Jersey. The university is part of the Fairleigh Dickinson University system, which includes two other campuses in New Jersey and one in Vancouver, Canada. The university was founded in 1942 as a two-year college and became a four-year institution in 1957. The university is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education. The university offers bachelor's and master's degrees in a variety of disciplines.

The Fairleigh Dickinson University-Florham Campus is located on a 500-acre estate in Madison, New Jersey. The university's academic programs are divided into four schools: the School of Arts and Sciences, the School of Business, the School of Education, and the School of Nursing. The university also operates a Center for Graduate Studies and a School of Professional and Continuing Studies.

The university's athletic teams compete in the NCAA Division I and are known as the Devils. The university's men's and women's basketball teams both compete in the Northeast Conference. The university's men's soccer team has won the NCAA Division I national championship four times.
