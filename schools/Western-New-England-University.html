Western New England University

Springfield, MA
1215 Wilbraham Rd, Springfield, MA 01119-2684
413-782-3111

Founded in 1919, Western New England University is a private, coeducational institution located in Springfield, Massachusetts. The university has an enrollment of more than 4,000 students, representing 37 states and more than 30 countries. The university offers more than 80 undergraduate programs and more than 20 graduate programs, including law and business.

The university is accredited by the New England Association of Schools and Colleges. The business programs are accredited by the AACSB International. The engineering programs are accredited by the ABET.

The university is divided into four schools: the School of Business, the School of Engineering, the College of Arts and Sciences, and the School of Law.

The School of Business offers undergraduate and graduate programs in accounting, finance, management, and marketing. The School of Engineering offers undergraduate and graduate programs in biomedical, chemical, civil, electrical, and mechanical engineering.

The College of Arts and Sciences offers undergraduate programs in the liberal arts and sciences. The School of Law offers Juris Doctor and Master of Laws programs.

Western New England University is committed to providing a world-class education. The university has a faculty of scholars who are experts in their fields. The university also has state-of-the-art facilities and a supportive community.

Admission Requirements

In order to be admitted to Western New England University, applicants must submit a completed application, high school transcripts, and SAT or ACT scores. The admissions committee will also consider the applicant's personal essay, letters of recommendation, and extracurricular activities. Western New England University is committed to enrolling a diverse student body and does not discriminate on the basis of race, color, national origin, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, age, or disability.
