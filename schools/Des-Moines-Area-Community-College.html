Des Moines Area Community College (DMACC) is a public community college in the state of Iowa. It is the largest community college in the state, with an enrollment of over 11,000 students. The college has campuses in Ankeny, Boone, Carroll, Newton, and West Des Moines.

DMACC offers over 100 programs of study, including associate degree and certificate programs. The college is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. DMACC is a member of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools.

DMACC is governed by a nine-member Board of Trustees, who are appointed by the Governor of Iowa. The college is administered by a president, who is appointed by the Board of Trustees.

The college was established in 1966, and opened its doors to students in 1968. DMACC was created to provide accessible, affordable, and quality education to the residents of central Iowa.

Since its inception, DMACC has grown tremendously. The college has added new campuses, programs, and services to meet the ever-changing needs of its students and the community.

DMACC is committed to its mission of providing accessible, affordable, and quality education. The college strives to meet the needs of its students and the community, and to make a positive impact on the lives of those it serves.

Admission Requirements

Des Moines Area Community College (DMACC) is a public community college in central Iowa. It is part of the Iowa Community College System. DMACC serves the Greater Des Moines area and surrounding communities.

The college has five locations: the Ankeny Campus, the Boone Campus, the Carroll Campus, the Newton Campus and the Urban Campus in downtown Des Moines.

DMACC offers more than 190 programs of study leading to an Associate in Arts (AA), an Associate in Science (AS) or an Associate in Applied Science (AAS) degree. The college also offers a number of certificate programs.

Admission to DMACC is open to anyone who has a high school diploma or equivalent. The college does not require placement tests for admission.

Students can apply for admission online or in person at any DMACC location. Once an application is complete, the student will be contacted by a DMACC admissions counselor to discuss next steps.

Once admitted, students will need to complete a new student orientation and register for classes. DMACC offers a variety of class formats, including online, hybrid and in-person.

Classes at DMACC are affordable and flexible, and the college offers a number of financial aid options to help students pay for college.
