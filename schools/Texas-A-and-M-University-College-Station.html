Texas A&M University is a public research university in College Station, Texas. It was founded in 1876 as the Agricultural and Mechanical College of Texas, the first public institution of higher education in the state of Texas. The school's mission is to "provide the best possible education for its students, preparing them for leadership roles in a global society." The university is a member of the SEC and ranks in the top 20 of public universities in the United States. The university has a student body of over 60,000 and offers over 350 degree programs. The school's flagship campus covers 5,200 acres and houses over 1,000 buildings. The university also has a branch campus in Galveston, Texas and a teaching hospital in Temple, Texas. Texas A&M is home to a number of research institutes, including the Texas A&M Health Science Center, the Texas A&M Engineering Experiment Station, and the Texas Transportation Institute. The school has a strong tradition of military service and is one of the largest producers of commissioned officers in the United States. Texas A&M is also a member of the "land-grant university" system and is designated as a "sea-grant" and "space-grant" university.

Texas A&M University-College Station is a public research university in College Station, Texas, United States. It is a land-grant, sea-grant, and space-grant institution and is the flagship institution of the Texas A&M University System. The university has the sixth-largest single-campus enrollment in the nation as of fall 2019, with over 69,000 undergraduate and over 28,000 graduate students. As of fall 2020, Texas A&M's student body is 52.8% White, 18.6% Hispanic or Latino, 14.5% African American, 7.0% Asian American, 3.2% International, 0.6% Native American, and 0.3% Pacific Islander.

Texas A&M was established in 1876 as the first public university in the state of Texas. The school's origins can be traced back to 1871, when the legislature of the Texas Republic authorized the establishment of a branch of the Agricultural and Mechanical College of Texas in Bryan. The school officially opened in 1876 with the enrollment of six students. In 1963, the school was renamed to Texas A&M University in recognition of its past and present students who served in World War II. The "A" and "M" in the school's name no longer stand for "Agricultural and Mechanical", but instead "Agricultural and Life Sciences", in keeping with the school's land-grant, sea-grant, and space-grant designations.

Texas A&M is classified as a "Doctoral University: Higher Research Activity" by the Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education. It offers bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees in over 150 courses of study through ten colleges and schools. The school's flagship campus is located in College Station, about 90 miles (140 kilometers) northwest of Houston. The university has two additional campuses in Qatar and Galveston, as well as a presence in Doha, Qatar.

Texas A&M is a member of the SEC Academic Consortium and is home to the George Bush Presidential Library. The school has produced many notable alumni, including two U.S. presidents, numerous astronauts, and members of the U.S. Congress.
