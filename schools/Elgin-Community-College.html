Elgin Community College (ECC) is a comprehensive community college located in Elgin, Illinois, a northwest suburb of Chicago. It is the second largest community college in Illinois. The college offers over 100 degree and certificate programs, as well as non-credit and continuing education courses.

ECC was founded in 1949 as Elgin Academy, a two-year college. In 1966, the college became Elgin Community College and began offering four-year degrees. In addition to its main campus, the college has satellite campuses in Bartlett, South Elgin, andStreamwood.

ECC is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and is a member of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools. The college is also accredited by the National League for Nursing and the Illinois Board of Higher Education.

ECC has an enrollment of over 18,000 students, of which over 60% are part-time. The student body is racially and ethnically diverse, with students from over 80 countries represented. The college also has a large number of international students.

The college offers a wide variety of degree and certificate programs, as well as non-credit and continuing education courses. ECC is known for its strong nursing program, which is one of the largest in the state of Illinois. The college also offers programs in health sciences, business, and technology.

ECC is committed to providing affordable, accessible, and quality education to its students. The college offers a variety of financial aid options, including grants, loans, and scholarships. ECC also offers a tuition payment plan and a military tuition waiver.

ECC is an active member of the community, with a variety of clubs and organizations available to students. The college also offers a number of athletics programs, including basketball, cross country, golf, and soccer.
