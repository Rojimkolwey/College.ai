Pasadena City College

Pasadena, CA
1570 E Colorado Blvd., Pasadena, CA 91106-2003
626-585-7123

Pasadena City College (PCC) is a public community college in Pasadena, California. Founded in 1924, PCC is the oldest community college in the Los Angeles area and one of the largest in the state of California. PCC serves the cities of Pasadena, South Pasadena, and Altadena, as well as the unincorporated areas of the San Gabriel Valley. The college offers over 130 associate's degree and certificate programs.

Pasadena City College is accredited by the Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges of the Western Association of Schools and Colleges. The college is also approved by the California State Board of Education and is a member of the American Association of Community Colleges and the Association of California Community Colleges.

Pasadena City College is located on a 38-acre (15 ha) campus in Pasadena, California. The campus is adjacent to the Rose Bowl Stadium and the Brookside Golf Club. The college is accessible via the Metro Gold Line light rail system.

Pasadena City College offers a variety of student services including counseling, tutoring, career services, and an on-campus child care center. The college also operates a campus police department and a student-run newspaper.

Pasadena City College is a diverse institution with students from over 100 countries enrolled in its programs. The college offers a variety of academic and career-oriented programs and courses of study.

Pasadena City College is committed to providing an affordable and accessible education to all students. The college offers a variety of financial aid options including grants, loans, and scholarships.
