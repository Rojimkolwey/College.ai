United Tribes Technical College

Bismarck, ND
3315 University Drive, Bismarck, ND 58504
701-255-3285

United Tribes Technical College (UTTC) is a federally chartered community college located on the Standing Rock Sioux Tribe reservation in Bismarck, North Dakota, United States. It is a two-year institution offering Associate of Arts, Associate of Science, and Associate of Applied Science degrees, as well as one-year certificates in various trades. The college also operates a Tribal Employment Rights Office, which provides career counseling and job placement services for Native Americans.

UTTC was founded in 1969 as United Tribes Vocational Technical Institute. It was established in response to a request from the Standing Rock Sioux Tribe for a vocational school to serve the needs of the Tribe and other Native American tribes in the region. The school began with just six students and has grown to serve over 1,200 students from more than 100 tribes across the United States.

The college offers a wide range of programs and services, including academic and career counseling, tutoring, financial aid, and job placement assistance. UTTC also operates a number of on-campus facilities, including a library, a computer lab, and a child care center. In addition, the college offers a variety of extracurricular activities, such as clubs, sports, and cultural events.

UTTC is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and is a member of the American Indian Higher Education Consortium. The college is also approved by the Bureau of Indian Affairs to offer programs for eligible Native American students.

Admission Requirements

Admission to United Tribes Technical College (UTTC) is open to any individual who meets one or more of the following criteria:

1. Is a high school graduate or has a high school equivalency diploma;

2. Has been a student in good standing at another college or university;

3. Has completed at least one year of satisfactory work or military service since high school;

4. Is at least 21 years of age;

5. Has been determined by the college to have the potential to benefit from its programs and services.

In addition to the above criteria, all applicants must also submit the following:

1. A completed application for admission;

2. Official high school and/or college transcripts;

3. A non-refundable application fee;

4. Any additional required documentation.

Once all required materials have been received, the admissions office will review the application and determine if the applicant meets the minimum requirements for admission. If so, the applicant will be notified of their acceptance and be given further instructions on how to complete the enrollment process. If the applicant does not meet the minimum requirements, they will be notified of the specific requirements they must meet in order to be considered for admission.
