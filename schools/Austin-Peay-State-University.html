Austin Peay State University (APSU) is a public university in Clarksville, Tennessee. The university is the state's fastest-growing university and is part of the Tennessee Board of Regents system. APSU Clarksville campus is located on the site of the former Fort Campbell, which was a U.S. Army post from 1942 to 2011. The university also has a campus in Fort Campbell, Kentucky.

APSU was founded in 1927 as Austin Peay Normal School, a two-year teachers' training school. The school was named for then-Governor Austin Peay, who is credited with expanding access to education in Tennessee. The school became a four-year institution in 1930 and was renamed Austin Peay State College. In 1943, the school was designated a state teachers college and granted the authority to award bachelor's degrees. In 1963, the college was renamed Austin Peay State University, and in 1979, it became part of the Tennessee Board of Regents system.

Today, APSU is a comprehensive university offering bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees. The university has an enrollment of over 10,000 students, making it the state's fifth-largest university. APSU is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges. The university's athletics teams compete in the NCAA Division I Ohio Valley Conference.
