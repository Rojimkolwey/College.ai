About Florida

Florida is a state located in the southeastern region of the United States. Florida is the 22nd most populous state in the US. Florida is the 3rd most populous state in the US after California and Texas. The most populous county in Florida is Miami-Dade County. The state capital of Florida is Tallahassee. The largest city in Florida is Jacksonville.

Florida was admitted as a state to the US on March 3, 1845. Florida is nicknamed the "Sunshine State" due to its warm climate. Florida is a popular tourist destination due to its beaches, theme parks, and other attractions.

Florida has a humid subtropical climate. The state has a long summer from May to October and a short winter from November to April. The average temperature in Florida is 21 degrees Celsius. The highest temperature ever recorded in Florida is 42 degrees Celsius.

Florida is home to many different animal species. The state is home to the American alligator, the Florida panther, the manatee, and the Key deer.

Florida is also home to many different plant species. Some of the more common plants found in Florida are the palmetto, the cypress, and the royal poinciana.

Florida has a diverse economy. The state's major industries include agriculture, tourism, fishing, and forestry. Florida is also a major producer of citrus fruits, sugarcane, and tobacco.
