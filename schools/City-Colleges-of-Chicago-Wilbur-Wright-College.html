City Colleges of Chicago-Wilbur Wright College (CCC-Wright) is a public community college in Chicago, Illinois. It is a part of the City Colleges of Chicago system. CCC-Wright was established in 1934 as Wright Junior College. It was renamed Wilbur Wright College in honor of the Wright brothers in 1948. The college is located on the northwest side of Chicago in the Portage Park neighborhood. It serves approximately 8,000 students.

CCC-Wright offers associate's degrees and certificate programs in a variety of fields, including business, health care, and liberal arts. The college also offers a number of adult education and ESL courses. CCC-Wright is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission.

The college has a strong commitment to diversity and inclusion. It has an active International Student Association and a robust international education program. CCC-Wright also has a number of student support services, including tutoring, career counseling, and financial aid.

CCC-Wright is an affordable option for higher education in Chicago. Tuition and fees are significantly lower than at four-year colleges and universities. The college also offers a number of scholarships and financial aid opportunities.

CCC-Wright is a great choice for students who are looking for an affordable, accessible, and supportive college experience. The college offers a variety of programs and services to help students succeed.

City Colleges of Chicago-Wilbur Wright College (CCC-WWC) is a two-year community college located on the Northwest Side of Chicago, Illinois. CCC-WWC is part of the City Colleges of Chicago system.

CCC-WWC offers programs leading to an Associate in Arts (AA) degree, an Associate in Science (AS) degree, and various certificates. The college also offers a number of non-credit courses and programs.

Admission to CCC-WWC is open to all students who meet the following requirements:

1. Must be a high school graduate or have a GED.

2. Must take and pass the Accuplacer test, or have completed college-level coursework in English and math.

3. Must complete an application for admission and submit all required documents.

4. Must pay the non-refundable application fee.

5. Must complete any required orientation and assessment programs.

6. Must meet any additional requirements for specific programs or courses.

Once admitted, students must complete a registration form and pay the tuition and fees for the semester they wish to attend.
