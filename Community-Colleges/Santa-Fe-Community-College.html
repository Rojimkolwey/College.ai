Santa Fe Community College

Santa Fe, NM
6401 Richards Ave, Santa Fe, NM 87508-4887
505-428-1000

Santa Fe Community College (SFCC) is a public two-year college in Santa Fe, New Mexico. SFCC is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and offers associate degrees and certificate programs in a variety of fields.

SFCC was founded in 1983 as a branch of the New Mexico Junior College system. The college originally operated out of a single building in downtown Santa Fe. In its first year, SFCC enrolled just over 400 students.

Today, SFCC has grown to serve over 6,000 students each year. The college has expanded its campus to include multiple buildings, including a library, a performing arts center, and a childcare center. SFCC offers over 60 degree and certificate programs, as well as a variety of continuing education and workforce development courses.

SFCC is committed to providing an affordable, high-quality education to its students. The college offers a variety of financial aid options and scholarships to help make college accessible to all. SFCC also provides support services to help students succeed, including tutoring, counseling, and career services.

Whether you're just starting your college journey or looking to further your career, Santa Fe Community College can help you reach your goals.

Admission Requirements

In order to be admitted to Santa Fe Community College, students must submit an application and official transcripts from all previous colleges or universities attended. Santa Fe Community College accepts applications on a rolling basis, so there is no set deadline to apply. However, students are encouraged to submit their applications as early as possible to ensure they are considered for the semester they wish to attend.

Once an application is received, the Admissions Office will review the studentâ€™s transcripts to determine if they meet the minimum admission requirements. These requirements include having a cumulative GPA of 2.0 or higher from all previous college coursework and successfully completing any required developmental coursework. If the student meets these requirements, they will be granted admission to the college.

Once admitted, students will need to complete a New Student Orientation before they can register for classes. This orientation will provide students with important information about the college, its policies and procedures, and how to be successful in their academic pursuits. After completing orientation, students will be able to register for classes and begin working towards their educational goals.
