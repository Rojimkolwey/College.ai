Lansing Community College

Lansing, MI

Lansing Community College (LCC) is a public community college in Lansing, Michigan. Founded in 1957, it is the fifth-largest community college in Michigan and the third largest in the state's Capital Region. LCC offers more than 200 programs of study, including associate degrees and certificates, and is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. The college's main campus is in downtown Lansing, with additional campuses in East Lansing and Delta Township.

LCC's mission is to provide accessible, high-quality education and training that prepares a diverse student population for success in their chosen field of study, in further education, and in life. The college strives to be a catalyst for economic development and a leader in workforce development, and to contribute to the enrichment of the community.

LCC is committed to student success and provides a variety of services to support students in their academic and career goals. These include academic advising, tutoring, career services, and disability services. The college also offers a number of programs and initiatives to promote student engagement and success, such as the First-Year Experience, the Student Success Center, and the Passport to Success program.
