City Colleges of Chicago-Harry S Truman College is a public community college in Chicago, Illinois. It is a part of the City Colleges of Chicago system. The college was established in 1974 and is named after the 33rd President of the United States, Harry S. Truman. The college is located on the Northwest Side of Chicago in the Portage Park neighborhood. The college serves over 10,000 students each year.

The college offers Associate's degrees and certificate programs in a variety of fields, including business, liberal arts, and sciences. The college also offers a number of career and technical programs, such as automotive technology, cosmetology, and culinary arts. The college has a strong commitment to providing affordable and accessible education to its students. In addition to its academic programs, the college also offers a number of student services, such as tutoring, counseling, and job placement assistance.

The college is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. The college has an active alumni association and a number of student organizations. The college is a member of the American Association of Community Colleges.

Admission Requirements

City Colleges of Chicago-Harry S Truman College is a public community college in Chicago, Illinois. Truman College is one of the seven City Colleges of Chicago. The college is named after U.S. President Harry S. Truman.

Truman College offers programs leading to an Associate in Arts (AA) or an Associate in Science (AS) degree, as well as certificate programs. The college also offers adult education and continuing education courses.

Admission to Truman College is open to all students who meet the following requirements:

• Must be a resident of the state of Illinois

• Must be at least 18 years of age

• Must have a high school diploma or equivalent

• Must take and pass the City Colleges of Chicago Placement Test (CCCPT)

• Must meet any additional requirements for the specific program of study

Once students have met the above requirements, they can then complete an online application or submit a paper application. After the application has been processed, students will be contacted to schedule an appointment for the CCCPT.

Students who do not meet the above requirements may still be eligible for admission to Truman College through the GED or Adult Basic Education (ABE) programs. For more information about these programs, please contact the college directly.
