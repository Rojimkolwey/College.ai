San Jose City College was founded in 1921 as San Jose Junior College. It was the first community college in Santa Clara County and the third community college in California. The college was originally located in downtown San Jose. In 1940, the college moved to its current location on Bascom Avenue.

The college has undergone several name changes over the years. In 1947, it was renamed San Jose City College. In 1974, it was renamed San Jose Metropolitan Community College. In 1981, it reverted back to its original name, San Jose City College.

San Jose City College (SJCC) is a public community college in San Jose, California. It is part of the San Jose-Evergreen Community College District. SJCC is accredited by the Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges of the Western Association of Schools and Colleges.

SJCC offers over 60 associate's degree and certificate programs in a variety of subject areas, including business, computer science, engineering, nursing, and liberal arts. Admission to SJCC is open to all students who meet the California residency requirements. All students are required to submit an application, transcripts, and a non-refundable application fee.

SJCC has a rolling admissions policy, which means that applications are accepted on a first-come, first-served basis. The college does not have a specific deadline for applications, but it is strongly recommended that students apply early to ensure that they are able to enroll in their desired courses.
