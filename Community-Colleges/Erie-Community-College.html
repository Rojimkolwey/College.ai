Erie Community College, located in Buffalo, New York, is a two-year college that offers associate degrees and certificate programs. The college was founded in 1961 and is part of the State University of New York (SUNY) system. Erie Community College has three campuses in the Buffalo area: the North Campus in Amherst, the South Campus in Orchard Park, and the City Campus in downtown Buffalo. The college also has a satellite campus in Williamsville.

Erie Community College offers over 100 degree and certificate programs in a variety of fields, including business, education, health care, and the liberal arts. The college has a strong commitment to academic excellence and student success, and its faculty members are experts in their fields. Erie Community College is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education.

Erie Community College is an affordable option for higher education, and its tuition is significantly lower than that of four-year colleges and universities. The college also offers a variety of financial aid options to help students pay for their education.

Erie Community College is a diverse and inclusive community, and its students come from all backgrounds. The college offers a variety of student services and support programs to help students succeed. Erie Community College is committed to making a difference in the lives of its students and in the community.

Admission Requirements

Erie Community College (ECC) is a two-year institution of higher education located in Buffalo, New York. ECC was founded in 1961 and is part of the State University of New York (SUNY) system. ECC offers over 60 degree and certificate programs and serves over 20,000 students annually.

Admission to ECC is open to all students who have a high school diploma or equivalent. Students must submit an application, transcripts, and a non-refundable application fee. International students must also submit proof of English proficiency. Once all required materials have been received, the Admissions Office will review the application and make a decision.

ECC offers a variety of financial aid options to help students cover the cost of tuition and other expenses. Students who complete the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) may be eligible for grants, loans, and work-study programs. ECC also offers a number of scholarships for students who meet certain criteria.

For more information about admission to ECC, please visit the Admissions Office website or contact the office at 716-851-1120.
