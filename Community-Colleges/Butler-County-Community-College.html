Butler County Community College (BC3) is a two-year public college located in Butler County, Pennsylvania. BC3 offers over 50 degree and certificate programs. BC3 is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education.

BC3 was founded in 1964 as Butler County Community College. The college was originally located in the city of Butler and consisted of a single building. In its early years, the college offered associate's degrees in business, education, and the liberal arts.

In the 1970s, the college began to expand its programs and facilities. A new campus was built in the city of Pittsburgh and the college began to offer a wider range of programs, including health care and engineering.

Today, BC3 is a comprehensive community college that serves over 10,000 students each year. The college offers a wide range of programs and services to its students, including academic advising, career counseling, and tutoring.

BC3 is committed to providing its students with a quality education at an affordable price. The college offers a variety of financial aid options to help students pay for their education.

If you are looking for a quality education at an affordable price, Butler County Community College is the right choice for you.

Butler County Community College (BCCC) is a public community college in Butler County, Pennsylvania. It is part of the Pennsylvania State System of Higher Education (PASSHE). BCCC offers associate's degrees, certificate programs, and continuing education opportunities.

BCCC is an open-admission college, meaning that any student who has a high school diploma or equivalent is eligible to enroll. However, some programs may have additional requirements. For example, the nursing program requires students to take a pre-admission exam.

Once students are admitted to BCCC, they must complete a placement test to determine which courses they need to take. Based on their test scores, students may be placed in remedial courses, which do not count towards a degree.

Students must also complete a student orientation program before they can register for classes. Orientation helps students learn about the college, its resources, and how to be successful in college.

After completing orientation and placement testing, students can register for classes. BCCC offers classes during the day, evening, and online. Students can also choose to take classes part-time or full-time.

Most classes at BCCC are taught by professors, but some classes may be taught by teaching assistants. Classes typically meet once a week for three hours.

BCCC has a variety of student services and support programs available to help students succeed. These include tutoring, advising, career services, and disability services.
