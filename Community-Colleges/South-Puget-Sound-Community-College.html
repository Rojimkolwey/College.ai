South Puget Sound Community College

Olympia, WA
2011 Mottman Rd SW, Olympia, WA 98512-6292
360-596-5200

South Puget Sound Community College (SPSCC) is a two-year public community college in Olympia, Washington. It is part of the Washington Community and Technical College System. SPSCC serves approximately 11,000 students annually. The college offers associate degrees, professional certificates, and continuing education opportunities.

SPSCC was founded in 1961 as Olympia Vocational Technical Institute. The college changed its name to Olympia Community College in 1964 and began offering transferable academic courses. In 1967, the college moved to its current location on Lakeridge Drive. In 1990, the college changed its name to South Puget Sound Community College to better reflect its service area.

SPSCC offers over 60 academic programs leading to associate degrees and professional certificates. The college has strong programs in health care, business, and information technology. SPSCC also offers a variety of continuing education opportunities, including non-credit courses, distance learning, and professional development.

The college is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities. SPSCC is a member of the American Association of Community Colleges and the Washington State Board for Community and Technical Colleges.

South Puget Sound Community College (SPSCC) is a public community college in Olympia, Washington. It is part of the Washington State Community and Technical College System. SPSCC serves approximately 8,000 students annually.

The college offers over 80 programs of study, including Associate in Arts and Associate in Science degrees, professional and technical certificates, and GED and high school completion programs.

Admission to SPSCC is open to all students who have a high school diploma or equivalent. Students must submit an application, transcripts, and a non-refundable application fee. Placement tests are required for students who wish to enroll in English and/or math courses. International students must also submit proof of English proficiency.
