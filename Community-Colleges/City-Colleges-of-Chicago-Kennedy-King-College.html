Chicago, IL
6301 S Halsted St, Chicago, IL 60621-2709
773-602-5000

City Colleges of Chicago-Kennedy-King College is a community college in Chicago, Illinois. It is a part of the City Colleges of Chicago system. The college was established in 1974 and is located on the city's South Side. It is named after two former United States Senators, Robert F. Kennedy and Edward M. Kennedy. The college offers associate's degrees and certificate programs. The college is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission.

Admission Requirements

City Colleges of Chicago-Kennedy-King College is a public community college in Chicago, Illinois. Kennedy-King College is a part of the City Colleges of Chicago system.

The college is located on the city's South Side in the Englewood community area. The college is accessible by the CTA Red Line and numerous bus routes.

The college offers Associate in Arts, Associate in Science, and Associate in Applied Science degrees, as well as certificate programs.

To be eligible for admission to Kennedy-King College, applicants must:

1. Be a resident of the State of Illinois

2. Be at least 18 years of age or have a high school diploma or GED

3. Have completed the required high school coursework or equivalent

4. Submit an application and $30 application fee

Applicants who do not meet the above requirements may still be eligible for admission if they have:

1. Completed at least 12 college credit hours with a minimum GPA of 2.0

2. Completed an Associate's degree or higher

3. Completed the required high school coursework through an accredited home school program

4. Completed the General Educational Development (GED) test with a score of at least 145 on each subject area test

Applicants who have met the above requirements will be asked to complete a placement test to determine their course level.
