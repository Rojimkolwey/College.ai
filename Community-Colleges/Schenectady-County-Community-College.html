Schenectady County Community College (SCCC) is a public community college in Schenectady, New York. It is part of the State University of New York (SUNY) system. The college offers associate's degrees and certificate programs in a variety of fields, including business, liberal arts, health care, and technology.

SCCC was founded in 1967 as the Schenectady County Technical Institute. The institute was created in response to the need for skilled workers in the local area. The college initially offered programs in business and technology. In the 1970s, the college began to expand its offerings to include liberal arts and health care programs. In the 1980s, the college changed its name to Schenectady County Community College to reflect its expanded mission.

Today, SCCC enrolls over 7,000 students. The college has a strong commitment to diversity and inclusion. Students come from a variety of backgrounds, and the college offers a variety of support services to help them succeed. SCCC is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education.

SCCC offers a high-quality, affordable education that prepares students for success in their careers and in life. The college is committed to student success and offers a variety of programs and services to help students reach their goals.
