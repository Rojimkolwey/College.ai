North Shore Community College

Danvers, MA
1 Ferncroft Rd, Danvers, MA 01923-0840
978-762-4000

North Shore Community College (NSCC) is a public community college in Massachusetts. It is part of the Massachusetts Community College system. NSCC has campuses in Lynn, Danvers and Beverly, Massachusetts. The college offers associate degrees and certificate programs. NSCC is accredited by the New England Association of Schools and Colleges.

NSCC was founded in 1965. The college opened its first campus in Lynn, Massachusetts. The college expanded its programs and campuses over the years. NSCC now offers over 100 associate degree and certificate programs. The college has over 10,000 students.

NSCC offers programs in a variety of fields, including business, health care, liberal arts, and technology. The college has a strong focus on career readiness. NSCC offers many services to help students succeed, including academic advising, tutoring, and job placement assistance.

NSCC is committed to diversity and inclusion. The college has a diverse student body, with students from all backgrounds and walks of life. NSCC is an affordable option for higher education, with many financial aid options available.

Whether you’re just starting your college journey or looking to advance your career, NSCC can help you reach your goals. With a wide range of programs and services, NSCC is a great choice for your education and career needs.

Admission Requirements

North Shore Community College (NSCC) is a public community college in Massachusetts. It is part of the Massachusetts Community College System. NSCC offers associate degrees and certificate programs. Admission to NSCC is open to all students who have a high school diploma or equivalent. NSCC does not have an application fee.

To be eligible for admission, students must submit the following:

-A completed NSCC application

-Official high school transcript or equivalent

-Placement test scores (if applicable)

-Proof of immunization

-Proof of residency (if applicable)

Students who have not yet completed high school or who have not yet taken the required placement tests may be admitted to NSCC on a provisional basis. Provisional students are required to complete all admission requirements within their first semester of enrollment.

NSCC uses a holistic admissions process. This means that we consider all aspects of your application, not just your academic record. We also take into account your personal experiences, interests, and goals. Our goal is to admit students who will be successful in college and who will contribute to the NSCC community.
