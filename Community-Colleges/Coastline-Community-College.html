Coastline Community College

Fountain Valley, CA
11460 Warner Ave., Fountain Valley, CA 92708-2597
714-546-7600

Coastline Community College is a two-year public community college in Fountain Valley, California. The college offers academic and vocational programs and awards an Associate's degree. Coastline is accredited by the Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges of the Western Association of Schools and Colleges.

The college was established in 1976 and opened its doors to students in the fall of 1977. The college was founded to serve the residents of the growing communities of Westminster, Huntington Beach, and Fountain Valley. The college originally operated out of portable buildings on a rented high school campus. In 1980, the college moved to its current location on a 38-acre site in Fountain Valley.

Coastline offers over 100 academic and vocational programs leading to an Associate's degree. The college also offers a variety of non-credit courses and programs for personal enrichment and career development.

Coastline is committed to providing quality education and services to its students and the community. The college strives to create a learning environment that is supportive, challenging, and stimulating. Coastline is dedicated to helping its students achieve their educational and career goals.

Admission Requirements

Coastline Community College is a two-year public community college in Fountain Valley, California. It is part of the Coast Community College District. The college offers Associate in Arts and Associate in Science degrees, as well as certificate programs.

Admission to Coastline Community College is open to all students who meet the following requirements:

1. Complete an online application for admission.

2. Submit official high school transcripts or equivalent (GED, CHSPE, etc.).

3. Take the Placement Tests or submit qualifying SAT, ACT, or AP scores.

4. Meet with a counselor to develop an educational plan.

5. Register for classes.

6. Pay tuition and fees.

7. Complete new student orientation.

8. Follow the college's immunization policy.

9. Complete any required developmental courses.

10. Maintain satisfactory academic progress.
