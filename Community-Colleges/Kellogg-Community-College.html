Kellogg Community College (KCC) is a public, two-year institution of higher education located in Battle Creek, Michigan. KCC offers a wide variety of programs and courses, including associate degrees and certificate programs in a variety of fields such as business, health, and technology.

KCC is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and is a member of the North Central Association. The college is also a member of the Michigan Community College Association.

KCC was founded in 1956 as Battle Creek Community College. The college was renamed Kellogg Community College in 1967 after the W.K. Kellogg Foundation made a major financial contribution to the college.

KCC is governed by a seven-member Board of Trustees, all of whom are appointed by the Governor of Michigan. The college has an annual operating budget of approximately $50 million.

KCC employs approximately 400 full-time and 600 part-time faculty and staff. The college has an enrollment of approximately 8,000 students, of whom approximately 60% are full-time.

KCC is located on a 120-acre campus in Battle Creek, Michigan. The campus includes a number of buildings and facilities, including the Binda Performing Arts Center, the Kellogg Arena, and the W.K. Kellogg Foundation Conference Center.
