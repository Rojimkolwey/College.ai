The Lancaster County Career and Technology Center is a public school that offers career and technical education to high school students in grades 9-12. The school is located in Willow Street, Pennsylvania. The school offers programs in Automotive Technology, Business and Information Technology, Construction and Carpentry Technology, Culinary Arts, Health Occupations, and Welding Technology. The school also offers a cooperative education program, which allows students to earn credit while working at a job related to their field of study. The school has a 97% graduation rate and 86% of graduates are placed in jobs or further education within six months of graduation.

Admission Requirements

Lancaster County Career and Technology Center (LCCTC) is a public career and technical school that serves students in grades 10-12 from all of Lancaster County, Pennsylvania. The school is located in Willow Street, Pennsylvania.

LCCTC offers over 50 career and technical programs in a variety of fields, such as agriculture, automotive technology, business and finance, culinary arts, health sciences, information technology, and more.

To be eligible to attend LCCTC, students must be residents of Lancaster County and must be enrolled in a public, private, or home school within the county. Students must also be in good academic and disciplinary standing, and must have a minimum 2.0 GPA.

LCCTC does not have a traditional admissions process; instead, students are accepted on a first-come, first-served basis. However, students are encouraged to complete an application and submit it to the school as soon as possible to secure their spot.

Once students are accepted, they will be required to complete a career interest survey and meet with a counselor to develop a career plan. Students will then be registered for their chosen program by their school district.

LCCTC is committed to helping all students succeed in their chosen career paths. The school offers small class sizes, hands-on learning experiences, and state-of-the-art facilities and equipment. LCCTC also has a strong partnership with local businesses and organizations, which provides students with opportunities for internships, job shadowing, and more.
