Gateway Technical College

Kenosha, WI
3520 30th Ave, Kenosha, WI 53144-1690
262-564-2200

Gateway Technical College is a public two-year technical college in Kenosha, Wisconsin. The college offers associate degrees and technical diplomas in a variety of fields, as well as adult education and continuing education courses.

Gateway Technical College was founded in 1901 as the Kenosha County Normal School, a teacher training school. In 1913, the school became a two-year college and began offering courses in other fields such as business and agriculture. In 1964, the college was renamed Gateway Technical Institute. In 1972, the college became part of the Wisconsin Technical College System and was renamed Gateway Technical College.

Gateway Technical College has four campuses in Kenosha, Racine, and Elkhorn, Wisconsin. The college also has a number of off-campus locations. The college offers over 100 programs of study.

Gateway Technical College is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. The college is a member of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools.

Admission Requirements

Gateway Technical College has an open-door admissions policy, meaning that any student who meets the basic requirements for enrollment is welcome to attend. The college does not require applicants to take any entrance exams, but they must submit high school transcripts or equivalent documentation. Gateway also offers a number of programs that allow students to earn college credit while still in high school. These programs have different admissions requirements, so interested students should contact the college for more information.
