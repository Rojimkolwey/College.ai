Big Bend Community College (BBCC) is a public community college in Moses Lake, Washington. Established in 1967, the college serves students in Grant, Adams, and Franklin counties. BBCC offers associate degrees and professional certificates in over 50 academic and technical programs. The college also provides adult basic education, English as a second language, and GED preparation courses. BBCC is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities.

Admission Requirements

Big Bend Community College (BBCC) is a public community college in Moses Lake, Washington. Founded in 1962, the college serves an area of approximately 12,000 square miles in Central Washington, including the cities of Moses Lake, Ephrata, Quincy, Soap Lake, Warden, and Royal City. BBCC offers a variety of two-year degrees and one-year certificates in academic and professional/technical programs.

In order to be admitted to BBCC, students must submit an application, transcripts, and a non-refundable application fee. International students must also submit proof of English proficiency. Once all required materials have been received, students will be notified of their admission status.

BBCC is an open-admission college, meaning that any student who meets the minimum requirements for admission will be accepted. However, some programs may have additional requirements or be competitive in nature, so students are encouraged to consult with an advisor prior to applying.

BBCC is committed to providing affordable and accessible education to all students. The college offers a variety of financial aid options, including grants, scholarships, and loans. Students are encouraged to complete the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) in order to determine their eligibility for financial assistance.
