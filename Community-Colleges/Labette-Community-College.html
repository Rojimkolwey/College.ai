Admission Requirements

In order to be admitted to Labette Community College, students must submit an application, transcripts, and a non-refundable application fee. Official high school transcripts or GED scores are required for all first-time college students. Students who have completed college coursework at another institution must also submit official transcripts from each college attended. All transcripts must be submitted in a sealed envelope directly from the issuing institution. The application fee is $20 for domestic students and $40 for international students.

Labette Community College does not require students to submit SAT or ACT scores for admission. However, students who do not submit test scores will be required to complete a placement test upon arrival to campus. The placement test is used to determine which level of English and math courses students will be placed in. Students who have already taken college coursework in English or math may be exempt from the placement test.

Students who are not residents of the United States must also submit proof of financial responsibility and a copy of their passport. International students are also required to complete a TOEFL exam unless they have completed at least 30 semester hours of coursework at a college or university where English is the primary language of instruction.
