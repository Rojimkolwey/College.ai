Wytheville Community College (WCC) is a two-year public college located in Wytheville, Virginia. It is part of the Virginia Community College System. The college offers over 50 degree and certificate programs in a variety of fields, as well as a number of continuing education and workforce development courses.

WCC was founded in 1963 as a branch of Virginia Polytechnic Institute and State University (VPI&SU). It became an independent college in 1967 and was renamed Wytheville Community College. The college moved to its current location in 1974.

WCC is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges (SACSCOC). It is also a member of the Virginia Association of Community Colleges (VACC) and the American Association of Community Colleges (AACC).

The college has an open admissions policy, meaning that any student who meets the minimum requirements for admission is accepted. However, preference is given to students who live in the college's service area, which includes Bland, Buchanan, Carroll, Dickenson, Grayson, Lee, Montgomery, Russell, Scott, Smyth, Tazewell, Washington, Wise, and Wythe Counties in Virginia.

WCC offers a variety of student services, including academic advising, tutoring, career services, and disability services. The college also has a number of clubs and organizations, as well as intramural sports.

The college is located on a 100-acre campus in Wytheville, Virginia. It is situated near the Appalachian Mountains, which provide a variety of outdoor recreational opportunities.
