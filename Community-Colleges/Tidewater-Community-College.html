Tidewater Community College

Norfolk, VA
121 College Place, Norfolk, VA 23510
757-822-1122

Founded in 1968, Tidewater Community College is a comprehensive two-year institution of higher education that serves the residents of Virginia's Tidewater region. The college offers a wide variety of academic programs leading to the Associate of Arts, the Associate of Science, and the Associate of Applied Science degrees, as well as a number of certificate programs. In addition to its academic programs, the college provides a variety of services to the community, including a Child Development Center, a Small Business Development Center, and a Senior Citizens Center.

Tidewater Community College is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges to award the Associate of Arts, Associate of Science, and Associate of Applied Science degrees. The college is committed to providing a quality education at an affordable price, and its tuition and fees are among the lowest in the state.

The college has four campuses located in the cities of Chesapeake, Norfolk, Portsmouth, and Virginia Beach. The Chesapeake Campus is the largest of the four, and it is home to the college's administration and most of its academic programs. The Norfolk Campus is home to the college's School of Health Sciences, which offers a variety of programs in nursing and allied health. The Portsmouth Campus is home to the college's School of Maritime Studies, which offers programs in maritime transportation, marine engineering, and nautical science. The Virginia Beach Campus is home to the college's School of Aviation, which offers programs in aviation science and aviation maintenance.

Tidewater Community College is a diverse and inclusive community that values the unique contributions of its students, faculty, and staff. The college is committed to providing an environment that is supportive of all members of the community.

Admission Requirements

Tidewater Community College (TCC) is a two-year public institution of higher education located in Virginia. TCC is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges to award the Associate of Arts, Associate of Science, and Associate of Applied Science degrees.

The mission of TCC is to empower our students to achieve their academic and career goals. We provide accessible, high-quality and affordable education and training opportunities to meet the diverse needs of our students and the community.

TCC serves a diverse student population of over 46,000 credit and non-credit students annually. We offer over 200 programs of study leading to associate degrees, certificates and diplomas.

TCC is committed to student success. We offer a variety of services and resources to support our students, including academic advising, tutoring, career services, disability services, and more.

To be eligible for admission to TCC, you must:

1. Be a high school graduate or have a GED®. If you are not a high school graduate or do not have a GED®, you may still be eligible for admission if you meet one of the following criteria:

a. You are at least 18 years old and have not been enrolled in a high school for at least one year.

b. You are a transfer student from another college or university and have completed at least 12 college-level credits with a cumulative GPA of 2.0 or higher.

2. Submit an application for admission.

3. Submit official high school and/or college transcripts.

4. Take the Virginia Placement Test (VPT) and/or submit SAT or ACT scores. You may be exempt from taking the VPT if you have:

a. Completed the Virginia Standards of Learning (SOL) tests with a passing score in reading and writing, or

b. Completed an associate degree or higher from a regionally accredited college or university, or

c. Completed college-level English and math courses with a grade of C or higher.

5. Meet any additional requirements for your chosen program of study.

Once you have been admitted to TCC, you will need to complete a New Student Orientation and register for classes.
