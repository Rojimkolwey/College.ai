Founded in 1946, Montgomery College is a public, coeducational college located in Montgomery County, Maryland. With more than 60,000 students, it is the largest institution of higher education in Montgomery County and the third-largest community college in Maryland. The college offers more than 130 associate's degree and certificate programs in a wide variety of disciplines, as well as continuing education and workforce development opportunities. Montgomery College is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education.

Montgomery College has three campuses: the Rockville Campus in Rockville, the Germantown Campus in Germantown, and the Takoma Park/Silver Spring Campus in Takoma Park/Silver Spring. The Rockville Campus is the college's flagship campus and is home to the majority of the college's academic programs. The Germantown Campus offers a wide variety of health science programs, while the Takoma Park/Silver Spring Campus focuses on arts and humanities programs.

The college is committed to providing an affordable, high-quality education to all students. In addition to its low tuition rates, the college offers a variety of financial aid options, including scholarships, grants, and loans. Montgomery College also has a number of initiatives in place to support students from underrepresented groups, including first-generation college students and students from low-income families.

Montgomery College is an active member of the Montgomery County community. The college offers a variety of services and programs to support the community, including a small business incubator, a literacy program, and a job training program. The college also hosts a number of events and activities on its campuses, including concerts, lectures, and exhibitions.

Montgomery College is a public, co-educational community college in Montgomery County, Maryland, United States, and is part of the Maryland Community College System. The college has three campuses, the Germantown Campus in Germantown, the Rockville Campus in Rockville, and the Takoma Park/Silver Spring Campus in Takoma Park/Silver Spring. In addition, Montgomery College offers off-campus courses and degree programs at several locations throughout Montgomery County.

Admission to Montgomery College is open to any student who has a high school diploma or equivalent. Students must submit an application, transcripts, and a non-refundable application fee. International students must also submit proof of English proficiency.

Once an application is complete, it is reviewed by the Admissions Committee. The Committee considers factors such as high school GPA, college GPA (if applicable), strength of curriculum, and standardized test scores. An interview may also be required.

Montgomery College uses a holistic approach to reviewing applications, and the Admissions Committee looks at the whole student when making a decision. The college strives to create a diverse and inclusive community, and welcomes students from all backgrounds.
