North Central Michigan College is a public community college in Petoskey, Michigan. The college offers associate's degrees and certificate programs in a variety of academic and career-oriented fields. NCMC is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and is a member of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools.

The college was founded in 1951 as the Little Traverse Bay Branch of Ferris State College. The branch campus offered evening and weekend classes to residents of Emmet and Charlevoix counties. In 1962, the branch campus became a separate institution known as North Central Michigan College. The college moved to its current location in Petoskey in 1968.

NCMC offers over 50 academic programs of study. The most popular programs are in the fields of business, health sciences, and liberal arts. The college also offers a number of career and technical programs, including automotive technology, carpentry, and cosmetology.

NCMC is known for its small class sizes and personal attention to students. The average class size is just 15 students, and the student-to-faculty ratio is 16-to-1. The college also offers a number of services and support programs to help students succeed, including tutoring, academic advising, and career counseling.

NCMC is an affordable option for higher education, with tuition and fees that are lower than the national average. The college also offers a number of financial aid and scholarship programs to help students cover the cost of tuition and other expenses.

NCMC is located in the beautiful resort community of Petoskey, Michigan. The area is known for its scenic beaches, golf courses, and ski resorts. Petoskey is also home to a number of cultural attractions, including the Crooked Tree Arts Center and the Little Traverse History Museum.

The college is just a short drive from a number of major cities, including Chicago, Detroit, and Grand Rapids. NCMC is an accessible and convenient option for students who want to pursue higher education.
