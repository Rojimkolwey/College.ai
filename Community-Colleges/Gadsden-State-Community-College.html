Gadsden State Community College is a two-year public institution of higher education located in Gadsden, Alabama. The college is accredited by the Commission on Colleges of the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools to award the Associate in Arts, Associate in Science, and Associate in Applied Science degrees. The college is composed of five campuses: the Main Campus in Gadsden, the Ayers Campus in Anniston, the McClellan Campus in Anniston, the Valley Street Campus in Southside, and the Wallace Drive Campus in Rainbow City. The college also operates the Gadsden State - Cherokee Center in Centre and the Gadsden State - Lincoln Center in Lincoln. The college's service area includes Cherokee, Cleburne, Etowah, and Calhoun counties.

Gadsden State Community College was established in 1925 as the Alabama Polytechnic Institute Junior College, a two-year branch of the Alabama Polytechnic Institute (now Auburn University). The college became a part of the Alabama Community College System in 1971 and was renamed Gadsden State Community College. The college offers a wide variety of programs and courses of study, including over 50 associate degree and certificate programs. The college also provides a number of services to its students, including academic advising, tutoring, career counseling, and job placement assistance.

Gadsden State Community College is committed to providing its students with a quality education at an affordable price. The college offers a number of financial aid options to help students meet the cost of their education, including grants, loans, and scholarships. The college also offers a number of payment plans to make tuition more affordable.

Admission Requirements

Gadsden State Community College is a public, two-year institution of higher education located in Gadsden, Alabama. The College is accredited by the Commission on Colleges of the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools to award the Associate in Arts, Associate in Science, and Associate in Applied Science degrees.

The mission of Gadsden State Community College is to provide accessible, high-quality educational opportunities that are responsive to the needs of our students, communities, and businesses.

To be eligible for admission to Gadsden State Community College, an applicant must:

1. Be a high school graduate or the equivalent (GED);

2. Be at least 18 years of age;

3. Submit a completed application for admission;

4. Submit official high school and/or college transcripts; and

5. Meet placement testing requirements (if applicable).

Students who do not meet all of the above admission requirements may be admitted to the College on a provisional basis. Provisional admission is granted on a case-by-case basis and is not guaranteed.

Gadsden State Community College has an open-door admissions policy, meaning that any person who meets the above requirements and desires to pursue a course of study at the College will be admitted.
