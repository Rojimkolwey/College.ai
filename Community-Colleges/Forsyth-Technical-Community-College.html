Forsyth Technical Community College is a two-year public institution of higher education located in Winston-Salem, North Carolina. Forsyth Tech is a member of the North Carolina Community College System. The college offers associate degrees, diplomas, and certificates in over 50 areas of study. Forsyth Tech also offers continuing education and workforce development programs.

Forsyth Tech was founded in 1960 as Forsyth County Industrial Education Center. The name was changed to Forsyth Technical Institute in 1963 and to Forsyth Technical Community College in 1987. Forsyth Tech's main campus is located on a 110-acre (0.45 km2) site in Winston-Salem. The college also has campuses in Clemmons, King, and Yadkinville, and centers in Kernersville, Lewisville, and Walkertown.

Forsyth Tech is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges to award associate degrees. The college is also accredited by the Accreditation Commission for Education in Nursing and the National Automotive Technicians Education Foundation.

Forsyth Tech offers over 50 associate degree, diploma, and certificate programs in a variety of fields, including business, health sciences, information technology, engineering, and public safety. The college also offers continuing education and workforce development programs.

Forsyth Tech's athletics teams are known as the Titans. The college fields teams in men's and women's basketball, cross country, golf, and tennis. Forsyth Tech is a member of the National Junior College Athletic Association (NJCAA) and the Carolinas-Virginia Athletics Conference (CVAC).

Forsyth Tech is committed to providing an affordable, high-quality education that prepares students for success in a global economy. The college offers a variety of financial aid options to help students pay for their education, including grants, loans, and scholarships. Forsyth Tech also offers a variety of services to help students succeed, including tutoring, advising, and career services.
