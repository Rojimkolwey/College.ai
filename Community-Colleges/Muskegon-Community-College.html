Muskegon Community College (MCC) is a public, two-year institution of higher education located in Muskegon, Michigan. MCC offers a variety of programs and courses leading to an Associate's degree or certificate. The college also provides many opportunities for students to get involved in campus life through clubs, organizations, and athletics.

MCC was founded in 1926 as a junior college, and was later expanded to offer four-year degrees in partnership with Ferris State University. The college now serves over 5,000 students each year.

The academic programs at MCC are divided into five main divisions: Business and Information Technology, Health Sciences, Industrial and Engineering Technologies, Liberal Arts and Sciences, and Public Service. Within each division, there are a variety of programs and courses to choose from.

MCC also offers a number of services and resources to help students succeed in their studies, including tutoring, advising, and career services. The college has a strong commitment to diversity, and offers a number of programs and services to support students from underrepresented groups.

Muskegon Community College is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission.

Admission Requirements

Muskegon Community College (MCC) is a public, two-year institution of higher education located in Muskegon, Michigan. MCC offers associate degrees and professional certificates, as well as transfer opportunities to four-year colleges and universities.

MCC is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission. Admission to MCC is open to all students who have a high school diploma or equivalent. Students must complete an application and submit all required transcripts and test scores.

MCC has a rolling admissions policy, meaning that applications are reviewed and decisions are made on a rolling basis throughout the year. Applications are reviewed holistically, taking into account the student's academic record, test scores, and personal statements.

MCC offers a variety of scholarships and financial aid opportunities to help make college affordable for all students. For more information on admission requirements and the application process, please visit the MCC website.
