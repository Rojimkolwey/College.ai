Oakland Community College (OCC) is a public community college in Oakland County, Michigan. Founded in 1964, it is the largest community college in Michigan and one of the largest in the United States. OCC has seven campuses, with the largest in Farmington Hills. The college offers more than 130 academic programs and awards associate degrees and certificates.

OCC provides educational opportunities to more than 60,000 students annually. The college has a strong commitment to student success, offering small class sizes, affordable tuition, and a variety of support services. OCC also has a robust online learning program, with more than 1,000 courses available.

OCC is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and is a member of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools. The college is also accredited by the Commission on Collegiate Nursing Education and the National League for Nursing Accreditation Commission.

OCC is home to a number of nationally recognized programs, including the Michigan Advanced Technician Training program, the OCC Culinary Arts Institute, and the OCC Automotive Technology program. The college also offers a number of unique programs, such as the OCC Center for Peace and Conflict Studies and the OCC Center for Sustainable Living.

OCC is an active member of the Oakland County community, with campuses located in Auburn Hills, Highland Lakes, Orchard Ridge, and Southfield. The college is also home to the OCC Foundation, which provides scholarships and other support to OCC students.

Admission Requirements

Admission to OCC is open to all high school graduates and GED holders who are at least 18 years old. There is no application fee.

OCC does not require placement testing for admission. However, some programs may require placement testing or other assessments prior to enrollment.

OCC offers over 100 degree and certificate programs. Many programs have specific admission requirements, which can be found on the college website.

In general, students must have a 2.0 GPA or higher to be eligible for admission to most programs. Some programs may have higher GPA requirements.

Students who have not yet met the GPA requirements may be eligible for admission to OCC through the Pathway Promise program. Pathway Promise is a program for students who have the potential to succeed in college but need additional support to reach their goals.

To be eligible for Pathway Promise, students must:

-Be a high school graduate or GED holder

-Be a Michigan resident

-Be at least 18 years old

-Have a 2.0 GPA or higher

-Complete an application for admission

-Complete an assessment

Students who are admitted to OCC through Pathway Promise are required to complete a Success Plan with an advisor. The Success Plan outlines the student's goals and the steps they will take to achieve them.

-Dual Enrollment

-Running Start
