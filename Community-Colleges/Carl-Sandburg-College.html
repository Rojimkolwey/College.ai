Carl Sandburg College

Galesburg, IL
2400 Tom L. Wilson Boulevard, Galesburg, IL 61401
309-344-2518

Founded in 1966, Carl Sandburg College is a community college in Galesburg, Illinois. The college offers more than 50 academic programs and awards associate degrees and certificates in a variety of fields.

The college is named after Carl Sandburg, a Pulitzer Prize-winning poet and writer who was born in Galesburg. Sandburg was also a passionate advocate for the rights of workers and farmers, and his work helped to shape the American experience in the early 20th century.

Today, Carl Sandburg College is a vital part of the Galesburg community, and its students have access to a variety of resources and support services. The college is committed to providing an affordable, high-quality education to its students, and its faculty and staff are dedicated to helping students reach their academic and career goals.

Admission Requirements

Admission to Carl Sandburg College is open to any student who has graduated from an accredited high school or who has successfully completed the General Educational Development (GED) test. College-level coursework from another institution may be eligible for transfer credit.

In order to be eligible to enroll in credit courses, students must complete an application for admission and submit all required transcripts and test scores. A non-refundable application fee is also required.

Upon review of the application, transcripts, and test scores, students will be notified of their admission status. Those who are admitted will be required to complete a new student orientation prior to enrolling in classes.
