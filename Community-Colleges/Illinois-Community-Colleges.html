Illinois Community Colleges


Illinois is home to 48 public community colleges, providing educational opportunities to students seeking to pursue higher education. These community colleges offer a variety of courses, including associate degrees and certificates in a wide range of academic disciplines. These colleges can also provide students with the opportunity to transfer to four-year universities and colleges, allowing them to complete their degrees more quickly and at a lower cost.

Illinois community colleges are accredited by the Higher Learning Commission, ensuring that the education they provide meets the same standards as those of four-year universities. Community colleges in Illinois also offer a variety of student services and activities, such as counseling, career services, student clubs and organizations, and student government.

The cost of attending an Illinois community college is usually much lower than that of a four-year university. The average cost for tuition and fees for a full-time student is around $4,000 per year, compared to the average cost of four-year universities, which is typically around $15,000 per year. Additionally, many community colleges offer financial aid and scholarships to help students pay for their education.

Each community college in Illinois offers a unique range of academic programs, allowing students to pursue their educational goals in a variety of fields. Common programs include business, healthcare, engineering, computer science, and the liberal arts. Many colleges also offer specialized programs, such as culinary arts, automotive technology, and veterinary technology.

Illinois community colleges are an excellent option for students looking to pursue higher education without the expense of a four-year university. With a variety of programs, low tuition costs, and numerous student services, these colleges provide a great opportunity for students to achieve their educational goals.
