McLennan Community College is a two-year institution of higher education located in Waco, Texas. The school is a member of the Texas Association of Community Colleges and the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools. McLennan Community College is accredited by the Commission on Colleges of the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools to award associate degrees and certificates. The college is also accredited by the Texas Commission on Law Enforcement Officer Standards and Education, the National Automotive Technicians Education Foundation, and the Council on Occupational Education.

The mission of McLennan Community College is to provide accessible, high quality education and training opportunities that are responsive to the needs of the students and the community. The college strives to provide a learning-centered environment that fosters student success. McLennan Community College is committed to the following values: excellence, learning, diversity, service, and community.

Admission Requirements

McLennan Community College (MCC) is a two-year institution of higher education located in Waco, Texas. MCC is a member of the Texas Association of Community Colleges and is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges. MCC offers associate degrees and certificate programs in a variety of academic and technical areas.

MCC has an open admissions policy, meaning that any student who meets the minimum requirements for admission will be accepted into the college. However, students are encouraged to apply early as some programs may have limited enrollment.

The minimum requirements for admission to MCC are as follows:

• Must be a high school graduate or have a GED

• Must be 18 years of age or older

• Must submit a completed application for admission

• Must submit official high school and/or college transcripts

• Must submit proof of Texas residency

• Must submit proof of immunization

Students who do not meet the minimum requirements for admission may still be considered for admission on a case-by-case basis. For more information, please contact the Office of Admissions at MCC.
