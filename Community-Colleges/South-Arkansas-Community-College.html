South Arkansas Community College is a two-year public institution of higher education located in El Dorado, Union County, Arkansas. The college offers associate degrees, certificates, and continuing education opportunities to residents of South Arkansas. The college is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and is a member of the North Central Association.

The college was founded in 1991 as a result of a merger between two-year public institutions, Union County Junior College and South Arkansas Technical Institute. The college serves Union County and the surrounding areas of South Arkansas. The college has an enrollment of approximately 3,500 students.

The college offers a variety of academic programs leading to an Associate of Arts degree, an Associate of Science degree, an Associate of Applied Science degree, or a certificate. The college also offers a variety of continuing education opportunities for adults.

The college is governed by a nine-member Board of Trustees appointed by the Governor of Arkansas. The college is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and is a member of the North Central Association.

Admission Requirements

South Arkansas Community College (SACC) is a public community college in El Dorado, Arkansas. SACC offers associate degrees, technical certificates, and non-credit courses.

Admission to SACC is open to any student who has a high school diploma or GED. SACC does not require any entrance exams for admission. However, some programs may require placement tests.

Students must complete an application for admission and submit all required transcripts and documents. SACC has an open-admission policy, meaning that all students who meet the minimum requirements will be accepted into the college.

Once admitted, students will be placed into one of three categories: regular admission, conditional admission, or special admission. Regular admission students have met all admission requirements and will be able to enroll in classes without any restrictions. Conditional admission students may need to complete additional requirements before being able to enroll in classes. Special admission students will need to meet with an advisor to determine which courses they are eligible to take.
