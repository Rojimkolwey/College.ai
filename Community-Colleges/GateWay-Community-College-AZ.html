Gateway Community College (GCC) is a public community college in New Haven, Connecticut. It is part of the Connecticut State Colleges and Universities system. GCC was founded in 1992, and offers associate's degrees and certificate programs.

GCC offers a variety of academic programs that prepare students for transfer to four-year colleges and universities, or for entry into the workforce. GCC also offers a number of adult education and continuing education programs.

GCC is accredited by the New England Association of Schools and Colleges. It is also a member of the American Association of Community Colleges.

GCC is located on a 33-acre (13 ha) campus in the Long Wharf area of New Haven. The campus includes several academic buildings, a library, a student center, and a performing arts center. GCC also has a satellite campus in North Haven.

GCC serves the Greater New Haven area, which includes the cities of New Haven, West Haven, East Haven, and Hamden. GCC enrolls approximately 9,000 students, and has a faculty and staff of over 400.
