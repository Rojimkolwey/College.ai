Kansas Community Colleges


Kansas is home to 19 community colleges located throughout the state. These institutions offer a variety of educational opportunities for students, including associate degrees, certificates, diplomas, and workforce training programs. The community colleges in Kansas are committed to providing quality education and services to their students, and strive to meet the educational needs of the communities they serve.

The community colleges in Kansas offer a wide range of academic programs and services. Students can pursue a variety of Associate of Arts, Associate of Science, and Associate of Applied Science degrees. Additionally, many of the colleges offer certificates and diplomas in areas such as business, health care, and technology. The community colleges also offer a variety of workforce training and continuing education programs.

In addition to offering academic programs, the community colleges in Kansas are committed to providing a variety of services and resources to their students. These include student support services, such as academic advising, career counseling, and tutoring. The colleges also provide a variety of student activities and organizations, such as clubs and organizations, student government, and student media.

The community colleges in Kansas are dedicated to serving their local communities. Many of the colleges offer courses and services specifically designed to meet the needs of the local community. These include courses in business and entrepreneurship, adult education, and career and technical education. Additionally, many of the colleges partner with local businesses to provide workforce training and development opportunities.

The community colleges in Kansas are a valuable resource for students seeking to further their education and pursue their career goals. With a wide range of academic programs, services, and resources, the community colleges in Kansas provide students with the opportunity to reach their educational and career goals.
