Wayne Community College

Goldsboro, NC
3000 Wayne Memorial Dr, Goldsboro, NC 27534-8002
919-735-5151

Wayne Community College (WCC) is a public, two-year community college in Goldsboro, North Carolina. WCC is a part of the North Carolina Community College System. The college offers associate degrees, diplomas, and certificates in over 50 programs of study.

WCC was founded in 1957 as Wayne County Industrial Education Center. The name was changed to Wayne County Community College in 1963. In 1992, the name was again changed, this time to Wayne Community College. The college moved to its current campus on Corporate Drive in 1996.

WCC is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges. The college’s nursing program is accredited by the Accreditation Commission for Education in Nursing.

WCC is governed by a nine-member Board of Trustees. The college is led by President Thomas J. Flynn.

Wayne Community College offers a variety of academic and career programs. The college’s academic programs include arts and sciences, business and industry, health sciences, and public service. The college’s career programs include automotive systems technology, cosmetology, culinary arts, and welding technology.

Wayne Community College also offers a variety of continuing education and workforce development programs. These programs are designed to help adults improve their skills and knowledge.

Wayne Community College is committed to providing an affordable, high-quality education to the residents of Wayne County. The college offers a variety of financial aid options to help students pay for their education.

Wayne Community College is an active member of the community. The college offers a variety of programs and services to the community. The college also hosts a number of events throughout the year.

Admission Requirements

In order to be admitted to Wayne Community College, applicants must have a high school diploma or equivalent. They must also submit an application, transcripts, and any applicable test scores.

Wayne Community College uses a holistic admissions process, meaning that they consider all aspects of an applicant's file when making a decision for admission. In addition to academic credentials, they also look at an applicant's life experiences, motivation, and goals.

The admissions process is designed to be accessible and fair, and the college works to enroll students who will be successful in their programs and contribute to the Wayne Community College community.
