Honolulu Community College (HonCC) is a public, two-year college in Honolulu, Hawaii. Founded in 1955, HonCC offers associate degrees and certificates in over 70 programs of study. The college also provides adult education and continuing education courses, as well as workforce development and community outreach programs.

HonCC is accredited by the Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges of the Western Association of Schools and Colleges. The college is a member of the American Association of Community Colleges and the Hawaii Association of Community Colleges.

HonCC is located on 25 acres of land in the Kalihi district of Honolulu, near the intersection of Dillingham Boulevard and North King Street. The college is adjacent to the Honolulu International Airport and the Hawaii Convention Center.

HonCC is governed by a nine-member Board of Regents, appointed by the Governor of Hawaii. The college is administered by a president, who is appointed by the Board of Regents.

Admission Requirements

Honolulu Community College (HonCC) is a public, co-educational community college in Honolulu, Hawaii, United States. HonCC offers two-year degrees and certificate programs in various areas of study.

Admission to HonCC is open to all high school graduates and adults 18 years of age or older. Applicants must submit a completed application form, official high school transcripts or GED scores, and SAT or ACT scores (if applicable). International students must also submit proof of English proficiency.

Once all required materials have been received, the HonCC Admissions Office will review the application and determine whether the applicant meets the minimum admission requirements. If so, the applicant will be granted admission to the college and will be able to register for classes.
