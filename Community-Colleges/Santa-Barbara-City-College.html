Santa Barbara City College (SBCC) is a public community college in Santa Barbara, California. It is one of the 112 colleges in the California Community Colleges System. The college offers Associate's degrees and certificates in over 100 areas of study.

SBCC was founded in 1909 as the Santa Barbara Continuation School to provide educational opportunities for the growing number of high school students in the area. The school was renamed Santa Barbara Junior College in 1925 when it began offering two-year Associate's degrees. In 1966, the college was renamed Santa Barbara City College.

SBCC is accredited by the Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges of the Western Association of Schools and Colleges. The college's athletic teams compete in the Western State Conference of the California Community College Athletic Association.

SBCC is located on a 74-acre (30 ha) campus in the city of Santa Barbara. The college is adjacent to the Santa Barbara Botanic Garden and the Santa Barbara Museum of Natural History.

SBCC offers over 100 associate's degree and certificate programs in a wide variety of disciplines. The college also offers a number of transfer programs that allow students to transfer to four-year colleges and universities.

SBCC has a strong commitment to teaching and learning, and provides a number of support services to help students succeed in college. The college also offers a variety of extracurricular activities and programs to help students get the most out of their college experience.

Admission Requirements

Santa Barbara City College (SBCC) is a public community college in Santa Barbara, California. It is one of the 112 colleges in the California Community Colleges system. SBCC offers associate degrees, certificates, and transfer programs. The college also provides adult education, ESL, and distance learning programs.

SBCC is accredited by the Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges of the Western Association of Schools and Colleges.

Admission to SBCC is open to all students who meet the following requirements:

1. Complete an application for admission.

2. Submit official high school transcripts or equivalent (e.g. GED).

3. Take the College Placement Test (CPT) and complete any required remedial coursework.

4. Complete any additional requirements for specific programs of study.

5. Pay the non-refundable application fee.

International students must also submit proof of English proficiency and complete additional admission requirements.

SBCC uses a holistic approach to admissions, meaning that all factors are considered when making admission decisions. The college strives to enroll students who have the potential to succeed in college and who will contribute to the diversity of the student body.

SBCC offers a variety of financial aid programs to help students pay for college. Eligibility for financial aid is determined by the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA).

Santa Barbara City College is committed to providing an affordable, high-quality education to all students. The college offers a variety of programs and services to help students succeed.
