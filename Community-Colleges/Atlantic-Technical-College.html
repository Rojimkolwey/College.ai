Atlantic Technical College

Coconut Creek, FL
4700 Coconut Creek Parkway, Coconut Creek, FL 33063-3902
754-321-5100

Atlantic Technical College (ATC) is a public, two-year technical college located in South Florida. ATC is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges and is a member of the Florida Association of Community Colleges. The college offers over 50 technical programs leading to the Associate in Science (A.S.) degree, the Associate in Applied Science (A.A.S.) degree, and technical certificates. ATC also offers adult general education courses leading to the high school diploma and the Adult Basic Education (ABE) diploma.

ATC was established in 1971 and is located on a 34-acre campus in Coconut Creek, Florida. The college is centrally located in Broward County and is easily accessible from all major highways. ATC serves a diverse student population of over 8,000 students annually.

ATC offers a variety of student services and support programs to help students succeed in their academic and career goals. The college has an open-door admissions policy and provides academic advising, career counseling, tutoring, and other support services to help students succeed. ATC also offers a variety of student clubs and organizations, as well as athletics, to help students get involved on campus.

ATC is committed to providing quality education and training that prepares students for success in their chosen careers. The college offers small class sizes, state-of-the-art facilities, and experienced faculty who are experts in their field. ATC also offers flexible scheduling options to meet the needs of busy adults.

Whether you are just starting out in your career or looking to make a change, ATC can help you reach your goals. With over 50 technical programs to choose from, ATC has the right program to fit your needs. Start your journey today and discover the possibilities at Atlantic Technical College.

Admission Requirements

In order to be eligible to attend Atlantic Technical College, students must meet the following requirements:

-Be a high school graduate or have earned a GED

-Be at least 18 years of age

-Take and pass a college placement test

-Have a valid government-issued ID

Once these requirements have been met, students can then begin the admissions process which includes submitting an application and paying the associated fees. After the admissions office has reviewed and processed the application, students will be contacted to schedule an advising appointment. During this advising appointment, students will be given information about the programs offered at the college and will be advised on which courses to take in order to complete their desired program.
