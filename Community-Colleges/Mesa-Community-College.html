Mesa Community College (MCC) is a public community college in Mesa, Arizona. It is one of the largest community colleges in the state of Arizona. Mesa Community College is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission.

MCC offers associate degrees and certificate programs in a variety of fields, including business, engineering, health care, and liberal arts. The college also offers online and distance learning opportunities.

Mesa Community College has three campuses: the Southern and Dobson Campus in Mesa, and the Red Mountain Campus in Scottsdale. The college also has a number of satellite locations.

MCC is a member of the Maricopa County Community College District (MCCCD), one of the largest community college districts in the United States.

Mesa Community College was founded in 1963. The college was originally part of the Maricopa County Junior College District, but became a separate institution in 1965.

MCC has grown significantly since its inception, and now serves over 40,000 students each year. The college has an extensive array of programs and services, and is an important part of the Mesa community.
