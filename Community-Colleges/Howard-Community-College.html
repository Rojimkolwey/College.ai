Howard Community College (HCC) is a two-year, public institution of higher education located in Columbia, Maryland. The college offers a wide variety of credit and non-credit courses, as well as workforce development and continuing education programs. HCC is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education and is a member of the Maryland Association of Community Colleges and the American Association of Community Colleges.

Founded in 1957, HCC was the first community college in Maryland and one of the first in the nation. The college was established to provide affordable, accessible, and high-quality education and training opportunities to the residents of Howard County. Today, HCC serves nearly 30,000 students each year, making it the largest two-year college in Maryland.

HCC offers more than 100 degree and certificate programs, as well as a variety of non-credit courses and programs. The college’s academic offerings are divided into five divisions: Arts and Humanities, Business and Social Sciences, Education and Human Development, Health Sciences, and STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics).
