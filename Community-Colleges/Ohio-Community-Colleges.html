Ohio Community Colleges


Ohio is home to a large network of community colleges that offer students a variety of educational opportunities. These community colleges are designed to provide quality education at an affordable cost, and they offer a wide range of programs and services to meet the needs of Ohio's diverse population.

Ohio's community colleges are part of the Ohio Board of Regents, the governing body responsible for coordinating the activities of all public higher education institutions in the state. The Board of Regents consists of 15 members, including the Governor of Ohio, the Chancellor of the Ohio Board of Regents, and the Presidents of the Ohio State University and the University of Cincinnati.

Ohio's community colleges are accredited by the Higher Learning Commission (HLC) of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools. The HLC is responsible for ensuring that colleges meet certain standards for quality and excellence in education.

Each of Ohio's community colleges offers a variety of degree and certificate programs. These programs range from certificates in specific fields such as nursing or automotive technology, to associate's degrees in a variety of subjects such as business, education, and criminal justice.

Ohio's community colleges also offer a variety of support services to help students succeed. These include tutoring and counseling services, career services, and financial aid. Additionally, many of the colleges offer online classes and distance learning programs.

The Ohio Board of Regents also provides a variety of grants and scholarships to help students afford the cost of attending community college. These grants and scholarships are available to all students, regardless of their financial situation.

Ohio's community colleges are an important part of the state's higher education system. They provide students with quality education at an affordable cost, and offer a wide range of programs and services to meet the needs of Ohio's diverse population.
