Texas State Technical College

Waco, TX
3801 Campus Drive, Waco, TX 76705
800-792-8784

Texas State Technical College (TSTC) is a public technical college system in the state of Texas. With campuses in Abilene, Amarillo, Brenham, Brownsville, Fort Bend County, Marshall, North Texas, Waco, and Williamson County, TSTC is one of the largest technical college systems in the United States. TSTC offers over 50 technical programs leading to associate degrees, certificates, and apprenticeships, in a variety of high-demand fields such as aviation, business and industry, healthcare, information technology, manufacturing, and more.

TSTC was created in 1965 as the Texas State Technical Institute, with the mission of providing high-quality, affordable technical education to the citizens of Texas. Since then, TSTC has grown rapidly, adding campuses and programs to meet the ever-changing needs of the Texas workforce. Today, TSTC graduates thousands of students each year who are ready to enter the workforce and make an immediate impact in their chosen field.

TSTC is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges (SACSCOC) to award associate degrees and certificates. TSTC’s programs are also accredited by a variety of industry-specific organizations, ensuring that students receive the highest quality education possible.

TSTC is committed to providing an affordable education, and offers a variety of financial aid options to help students cover the cost of tuition and fees. TSTC also offers a number of scholarships to help students pay for their education.

If you’re looking for a high-quality, affordable technical education, TSTC is the right choice for you. With campuses across Texas, TSTC has the programs and services you need to succeed in your chosen field.

Admission Requirements

In order to be eligible to attend Texas State Technical College, students must meet the following requirements: they must be a resident of Texas, they must be a high school graduate or have a GED, and they must have a minimum 2.0 GPA. If a student does not meet one of these requirements, they may still be eligible to attend if they have completed at least six hours of college credit with a C or better grade in each class.
