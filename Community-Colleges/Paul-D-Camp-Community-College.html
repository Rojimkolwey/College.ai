Paul D. Camp Community College (PDCCC) is a public, two-year institution of higher education serving the residents of the City of Franklin and Southampton County, Virginia. The college is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges to award the Associate Degree. PDCCC is a comprehensive community college offering a wide range of programs and services to meet the needs of a diverse student population. The college provides quality academic, career and technical, and workforce development programs that are accessible and affordable. PDCCC is committed to student success and to the economic and workforce development of the communities it serves.

The college offers a variety of academic programs leading to the Associate Degree, including arts and sciences, business, health sciences, and public service. PDCCC also offers a wide range of career and technical programs leading to certificates and diplomas in a variety of fields. The college’s workforce development programs provide training and retraining for individuals and businesses in the region.

PDCCC is located on a beautiful, tree-lined campus in the city of Franklin, Virginia. The college serves a population of approximately 60,000 residents in Franklin and Southampton County. The college is committed to providing a quality education that is accessible and affordable. PDCCC offers a variety of financial aid programs to help students meet the cost of their education.

Admission Requirements

Paul D Camp Community College (PDCCC) is a public, two-year institution of higher education located in Franklin, Virginia. The College offers a variety of degree and certificate programs, as well as workforce development and continuing education opportunities.

Admission to PDCCC is open to all individuals who have a high school diploma or equivalent. The College does not have an application fee.

Individuals interested in enrolling at PDCCC should submit the following items:

• A completed application for admission

• Official high school transcripts or equivalent

• Official college transcripts (if applicable)

• Placement test scores (if applicable)

• A non-refundable $25 registration fee

Once all required materials have been received, the College will review the applicant’s file and determine whether or not they have met the admission requirements. If the applicant is eligible for admission, they will be notified and given further instructions on how to complete the enrollment process.
