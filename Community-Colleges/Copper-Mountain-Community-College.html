Copper Mountain Community College (CMCC) is located in the San Bernardino Mountains, east of Los Angeles, California. The college offers Associate in Arts (AA) and Associate in Science (AS) degrees, as well as certificates in a variety of career and technical fields. CMCC is accredited by the Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges of the Western Association of Schools and Colleges.

The college was founded in 1966 as a satellite campus of San Bernardino Valley College. In 1972, the campus became an independent community college and was renamed Copper Mountain College. The college moved to its current location in 1974. In 2016, the college was renamed Copper Mountain Community College.

CMCC serves the mountain communities of the San Bernardino Mountains, including the cities of Big Bear Lake, Crestline, Lake Arrowhead, and Running Springs. The college also serves the communities of Highland, Loma Linda, and Redlands.

The college offers a variety of academic and career programs. The academic programs include English, math, science, social science, and humanities. The career programs include business, computer science, engineering, health care, and public safety.

The college has a variety of student services and support programs. These include academic advising, career counseling, tutoring, and financial aid. The college also has a variety of student clubs and organizations.

The college is located on a beautiful, wooded campus. The campus includes classrooms, laboratories, a library, a gymnasium, and a theater. The college also has a child care center and a food court.
