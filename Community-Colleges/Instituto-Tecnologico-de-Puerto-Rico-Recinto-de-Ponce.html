Founded in 1966, Instituto Tecnologico de Puerto Rico-Recinto de Ponce is a public university located in Ponce, Puerto Rico. It is part of the Instituto Tecnologico de Puerto Rico system, the largest public university system in Puerto Rico. The university offers bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees in a variety of fields, including engineering, business, education, and the liberal arts.

The university is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education and is a member of the Association of Universities and Colleges of Puerto Rico. It is also home to a chapter of the Phi Beta Kappa honor society.

The university's campus is located on a hill overlooking the city of Ponce. It is spread out over 100 acres and includes a variety of buildings and facilities, including an auditorium, library, gymnasium, and student center.

The university's student body is made up of over 5,000 students, representing all regions of Puerto Rico. The university also has a large number of international students, representing over 50 countries.

The university's faculty is made up of over 400 full-time and part-time faculty members, who are experts in their respective fields. The university also has a number of visiting faculty members from other institutions.

The university offers a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs, as well as a number of research centers and institutes. The university is also home to a number of student organizations and clubs.

The university is committed to providing a quality education to its students and to preparing them for successful careers. It is also dedicated to conducting research that will contribute to the economic and social development of Puerto Rico.

Admission Requirements

In order to be admitted to Instituto Tecnologico de Puerto Rico-Recinto de Ponce, applicants must meet the following requirements:

1. Be a citizen of the United States or Puerto Rico, or have a valid visa to study in the United States.

2. Have a high school diploma or equivalent.

3. Submit official transcripts from all colleges and universities attended.

4. Submit a completed application form, including a non-refundable application fee.

5. Submit SAT or ACT scores (if applicable).

6. Submit two letters of recommendation.

7. Submit a personal essay.

8. Interview with a member of the admissions committee (if applicable).

9. Meet any additional requirements for the specific program to which you are applying.

International students must also submit the following:

1. Proof of English proficiency (TOEFL or IELTS scores).

2. Transcripts evaluated by World Education Services (WES) or Education Credential Evaluators (ECE).

3. Financial documentation demonstrating the ability to pay for one year of study.

4. A copy of the student’s passport.

5. A completed Affidavit of Support form.

6. A completed Declaration and Certification of Finances form.

7. A completed International Student Data Form.
