Southern Maine Community College (SMCC) is a public, two-year institution of higher education located in the city of South Portland, Maine. The college opened its doors in 1946 as the first community college in the state. Today, SMCC enrolls over 6,000 students each year from across Maine and from 37 other countries. The college offers over 50 degree and certificate programs, as well as a variety of non-credit courses and workshops.

SMCC is accredited by the New England Commission of Higher Education (NECHE). The college is also a member of the Maine Community College System (MCCS) and the Maine Association of Community Colleges (MACC).

The mission of SMCC is to provide high-quality, affordable education and training that meets the needs of individuals, businesses, and the community. The college strives to create a supportive and stimulating learning environment that encourages personal and professional growth.

SMCC offers a variety of services to its students, including academic advising, tutoring, career services, and disability services. The college also has an active Student Government Association (SGA) that represents the interests of students.

The college is located on a picturesque campus overlooking Casco Bay. The campus features several state-of-the-art facilities, including a library, a fitness center, and a performing arts center.

SMCC is committed to being an accessible, affordable, and inclusive institution of higher education. The college offers a variety of financial aid options to help students pay for their education. In addition, the college participates in the Maine State Tuition Waiver Program, which provides tuition waivers for eligible Maine residents.

SMCC is an exciting and vibrant community college that offers its students a high-quality, affordable education. The college is committed to being accessible, inclusive, and affordable, and offers a variety of services and activities to its students.
