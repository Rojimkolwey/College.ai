Maryland Community Colleges


Maryland’s community colleges offer a wide range of educational and career opportunities for students of all ages. The state’s 16 community colleges are part of the Maryland Higher Education Commission’s network of public institutions. Community colleges provide students with access to associate degrees, certificates, and other educational programs. These colleges offer transfer programs to four-year institutions, allowing students to complete their associate degrees and then transfer to a four-year college or university. Additionally, community colleges provide workforce development programs to help students gain the skills they need to obtain employment or enhance their existing career.

In addition to traditional academic programs, community colleges offer a variety of non-credit courses and programs, including English as a Second Language (ESL), adult basic education, and career and technical education. These courses help students gain the skills and knowledge they need to succeed in the workplace. Additionally, community colleges provide valuable resources to support student success, including academic and career counseling, financial aid, and tutoring services.

Community colleges in Maryland are committed to providing access to high-quality education at an affordable cost. Tuition and fees vary by institution, but all Maryland residents pay a flat rate for tuition. Additionally, many students are eligible for grants, scholarships, and other forms of financial aid to help cover the cost of tuition and fees. Additionally, community colleges offer flexible scheduling options to accommodate students with work or family obligations.

Maryland’s community colleges are an excellent option for students looking for an affordable, accessible education. With a wide range of educational opportunities, flexible scheduling options, and financial aid, community colleges provide students with the tools they need to succeed.
