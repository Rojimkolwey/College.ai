Camden County College

Blackwood, NJ
200 College Drive, Blackwood, NJ 08012
856-227-7200

Founded in 1967, Camden County College is a public, two-year community college that serves the residents of Camden County, New Jersey. The college has two campuses in Camden and Blackwood, as well as a satellite location in Cherry Hill. Camden County College offers over 100 degree and certificate programs, as well as continuing education and workforce development courses. The college is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education.

Camden County College enrolls over 12,000 students each year, the majority of whom are Camden County residents. The college has a strong commitment to serving the community, and provides a number of programs and services designed to meet the needs of its students and the community.

The Camden County College Foundation provides financial support to the college and its students through scholarships, grants, and other programs. The Foundation also provides funding for a number of college initiatives, including the Camden County College Promise program, which provides free tuition and fees for Camden County residents who meet certain criteria.

Camden County College is a vital part of the Camden County community, and is committed to providing quality education and training opportunities for its residents.

Admission Requirements

Camden County College (CCC) is a public community college in New Jersey. CCC is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education. The college offers over 60 degree and certificate programs.

Admission to Camden County College is open to all students who have a high school diploma or equivalent. CCC also offers a variety of non-credit courses and programs.

Students can apply to CCC online or in person. The college has an open admissions policy, meaning that all students who meet the minimum requirements will be accepted.

Once students are accepted, they will need to complete a placement test to determine their course level. CCC offers both developmental and college-level courses. Developmental courses are designed to help students improve their basic skills, while college-level courses prepare students for transfer to a four-year institution.

After placement testing, students will meet with an advisor to create a schedule. CCC offers day, evening, and weekend classes to accommodate all schedules.

Tuition at CCC is very affordable, and the college offers a variety of financial aid options to help students cover the cost of their education. Scholarships are also available for students who qualify.
