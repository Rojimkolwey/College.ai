Mississippi Gulf Coast Community College (MGCCC) is a public community college in Mississippi. It was established in 1911 and is the oldest and largest community college in the state. MGCCC offers associate's degrees, certificate programs, and workforce training.

MGCCC has campuses in Harrison, Jackson, George, and Perry counties. The college also has off-campus locations in Biloxi, Gautier, Long Beach, and Wiggins.

MGCCC enrolls over 15,000 students each year. The student body is diverse, with students from all backgrounds and walks of life.

MGCCC is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges. The college is also a member of the American Association of Community Colleges.

MGCCC is committed to providing an affordable, quality education to its students. The college offers a variety of financial aid options, including scholarships, grants, and loans.

MGCCC has a strong commitment to student success. The college offers a variety of support services, including tutoring, advising, and career counseling. MGCCC also has an active student life, with over 50 clubs and organizations.

MGCCC is an important part of the Mississippi Gulf Coast community. The college is involved in a variety of economic development initiatives, including workforce training and small business development. MGCCC also provides cultural enrichment through its arts and humanities programs.
