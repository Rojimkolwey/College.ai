Clackamas Community College (CCC) is a public community college in Oregon. It is the largest community college in the state, serving more than 17,000 students each year. The college offers over 100 degree and certificate programs, as well as workforce training and continuing education.

CCC was founded in 1966 and is accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities. The college has two campuses in Oregon City and Milwaukie. The college also operates a number of off-campus centers and sites, including the Harmony Campus in Portland.

Clackamas Community College (CCC) is a public community college in Oregon. Founded in 1966, the college serves Clackamas County and the southern portion of Portland metropolitan area. CCC offers associate degrees, professional certificates, and transfer pathways to four-year colleges and universities. The college has three campuses: the main campus in Oregon City, the Harmony Community Campus in Milwaukie, and the Wilsonville Training Center.

To be eligible for admission to CCC, students must have a high school diploma or equivalent. Students must also take a placement test to determine their level of English and math skills. CCC offers a variety of programs of study, including business, health care, human services, manufacturing and engineering technology, and more.
