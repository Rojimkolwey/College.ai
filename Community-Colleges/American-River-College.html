American River College

Sacramento, CA
4700 College Oak Dr, Sacramento, CA 95841-4286
916-484-8011

American River College (ARC) is a public community college in Sacramento, California. The main campus is located on a 153-acre (62 ha) site in the suburban Rancho Cordova area. It is one of the largest community colleges in California with an enrollment of over 34,000 students. The college offers Associate's degrees, certificate programs, and transfer courses to four-year universities.

ARC was established in 1955 and is part of the Los Rios Community College District. The college is accredited by the Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges of the Western Association of Schools and Colleges. It is also approved by the California State Board of Education and the Commission on Accreditation of Allied Health Education Programs.

The college offers over 100 Associate's degree and certificate programs. The most popular majors include business, liberal arts, psychology, and nursing. The college also offers a wide variety of transfer courses to four-year universities.

ARC is home to a number of student clubs and organizations. The college also offers a variety of athletics programs. The ARC Raiders compete in the California Community College Athletic Association (CCCAA). The school's colors are green and gold, and its mascot is the Raider.

American River College is a comprehensive community college that provides quality education to a diverse student population. The college offers a wide variety of programs and services that prepare students for successful transfer to four-year colleges and universities, and for rewarding careers.

Admission Requirements

American River College (ARC) is a public community college in Sacramento, California. The college is part of the Los Rios Community College District and offers associate's degrees, certificates, and transfer programs.

Admission to American River College is open to all students who meet the minimum requirements. To be eligible for admission, students must:

1. Be a high school graduate or have a GED certificate.

2. Be 18 years of age or older.

3. Take and pass the California High School Proficiency Exam (CHSPE) or the California Basic Educational Skills Test (CBEST).

4. Submit official transcripts from all previous colleges and universities attended.

5. Complete the ARC application for admission.

6. Pay the non-refundable application fee.

7. Take and pass the ARC placement tests, if required.

Once all of the above requirements have been met, students will be granted admission to American River College.
