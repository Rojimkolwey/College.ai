Chattahoochee Technical College

Marietta, GA
980 S Cobb Drive Dr SE, Marietta, GA 30060
770-528-4545

Chattahoochee Technical College is a two-year public college located in Georgia, United States. It is part of the Georgia Department of Technical and Adult Education. The college offers over 150 programs of study in Business, Health, Industrial, and Public Service technologies. Chattahoochee Technical College has eight campuses and two satellite locations throughout northwest Georgia.

Chattahoochee Technical College was founded in 1966 as part of the Georgia Department of Technical and Adult Education. The college was originally named North Georgia Technical Institute. In 1986, the college was renamed Chattahoochee Technical Institute. In 2000, the name was changed again to Chattahoochee Technical College.

Chattahoochee Technical College offers over 150 programs of study in Business, Health, Industrial, and Public Service technologies. The college has eight campuses and two satellite locations throughout northwest Georgia.

Chattahoochee Technical College is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges. The college is also accredited by the following agencies:

The college is a member of the American Association of Community Colleges, the Association of Georgia Colleges, and the Technical College System of Georgia.

Chattahoochee Technical College is committed to providing accessible, affordable, and high-quality education and training opportunities that promote student success and enhance the economic vitality of the community.

Admission Requirements

Chattahoochee Technical College has an open-door admissions policy, meaning that any student who meets the minimum requirements for admission will be accepted into the college. The minimum requirements for admission are as follows:

1. The applicant must be a high school graduate or have a GED.

2. The applicant must submit an official transcript from their high school or GED program.

3. The applicant must take and pass a placement test, unless they have already taken and passed a college-level English or math class.

4. The applicant must meet with an advisor to develop a degree plan.

5. The applicant must submit a completed application for admission.

6. The applicant must pay the applicable fees.

Once an applicant has met all of the minimum requirements for admission, they will be issued an acceptance letter and will be able to register for classes.
