Springfield Technical Community College

Springfield, MA
1 Armory Sq, Springfield, MA 01105-1296
413-781-7822

Springfield Technical Community College (STCC) is a public, coeducational college located in Springfield, Massachusetts. The college is the only technical community college in Massachusetts and is a member of the Massachusetts Community College System. STCC offers associate degrees and certificate programs in a variety of technical and occupational fields, as well as liberal arts and sciences. The college also offers a variety of continuing education and workforce development programs.

STCC was founded in 1967 as Springfield Technical Institute, a public, two-year technical institute. The institute was created in response to the need for skilled workers in the Springfield area. The institute became a community college in 1974 and was renamed Springfield Technical Community College. The college has since grown and now enrolls over 9,000 students.

STCC is accredited by the New England Association of Schools and Colleges (NEASC). The college is also accredited by a number of professional and occupational organizations, including the National League for Nursing and the National Automotive Technicians Education Foundation.

STCC is committed to providing quality education and training that meet the needs of the community and the workforce. The college offers a variety of programs that prepare students for careers in fields such as healthcare, business, information technology, manufacturing, and more. The college also offers a number of programs that provide students with the skills and knowledge needed to succeed in the workforce. In addition to its academic programs, STCC also offers a variety of student services, including career counseling, tutoring, and financial aid.

Admission Requirements

In order to be admitted to Springfield Technical Community College, applicants must have a high school diploma or equivalent. They must also submit an application, transcripts, and any applicable test scores. Once all materials have been received, the admissions office will review the file and make a decision. If an applicant is offered admission, they will need to pay a deposit in order to secure their spot in the incoming class.
