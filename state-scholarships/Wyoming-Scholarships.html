Carlton R Barkhurst Fellowship For Graduate Students
The Carlton R. Barkhurst Writing Fellowship is made possible by a generous gift from the late Irene Ahlbom Barkhurst.
The Carlton R. Barkhurst Writing Fellowship is intended to help PhD and master’s candidates finish their dissertation/thesis in a timely manner.
Up to eight Barkhurst Writing Fellowships will be awarded each academic year. Awards will be limited to a single semester of funding, either fall or spring. The single-semester awards will comprise of the following:
Fellowship of $8,919
1 Credit Hour of Tuition
Optional Student Fee Package
Student Health Insurance (if selected)
Eligibility requirements:
Ph.D. candidate (preliminary exam passed within four years prior to Anticipated Date of Graduation). Other graduate or professional students are not eligible.
Master’s student in their last semester, completing thesis. Other graduate or professional students are not eligible.
Must be an U.S. citizen or eligible non-citizen (see: studentaid.gov) Eligibility Requirements with unmet financial need as determined by the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA). Information on how to submit a FAFSA can be found here: uwyo.edu.
Anticipated Graduation Date form must be on file with the Office of the Registrar.
o For fall awards, student must be graduating in December of the current year. For spring awards, student must be graduating no later than August of the current year.
Must be eligible for the Optional Student Fee Package (an approved Optional Student Fee Package form must be on file with the Office of the Registrar before the Fellowship begins).
Must be in good academic standing.
Selection criteria:
Degree of unmet financial need.
Academic record and advisor’s recommendation.
Likelihood of completing degree by end of Barkhurst Fellowship semester.
Barkhurst Fellows may not hold any form of employment with the University of Wyoming during their fellowship semester. Furthermore, as fellowship recipients, Barkhurst Fellows are not employees and will not have any work expectations associated with their fellowship other than conducting their own research and writing their dissertation/thesis.
A nomination of an eligible student ideally should come from the student’s dissertation/thesis advisor, but could come from another faculty member. The nomination must include a letter of nomination and recommendation.
Fall semesters: Applications and a faculty submitted letter of nomination and recommendation must be completed by June 30 for Barkhurst Fellowship consideration.
Spring semesters: Application will be available later in the fall semesters. more...

Hathaway Scholarship
The Wyoming State Legislature approved legislation during their Spring 2006 session to establish a scholarship fund for Wyoming students to attend Western Wyoming Community college, any of the other six Wyoming community colleges, or the University of Wyoming. The legislation creates merit and need-based awards for eligible students to provide an incentive to pursue post-secondary education within the State of Wyoming. In addition, students initiating their Hathaway Scholarship at Western before July 31st who are Honors or Performance level will receive an enhancement to their Hathaway award. Hathaway award levels, requirements, and values are as follows.
Honors: $3,360 yearly award + $1,200 yearly enhancement (if requirement met)

Requires a minimum 3.5 high school GPA and an ACT composite of 25
Must complete State required class curriculum
Performance: $2,520 yearly award + $1,000 yearly enhancement (if requirement met)

Requires a minimum 3.0 high school GPA and an ACT composite of 21
Must complete State required class curriculum
Opportunity: $1,680 yearly award

Requires a minimum 2.5 high school GPA and an ACT composite of 19
Must complete State required class curriculum
Provisional: $1,680 yearly award

Requires a minimum 2.5 high school GPA and an ACT composite of 17
Must complete State required class curriculum
In order to receive awards, the Hathaway scholarship must be initiated and final transcripts must be received by July 31st. Hathaway Scholarships are awarded based on college-level class enrollment; if below 12 college-level credit hours, awards are prorated. If you earned a high school equivalency certificate (GED, HiSET) or were homeschooled please mark that on the application under the “Name of High School” field and send in any required documents including ACT scores or certificates. Students receiving Honors or Performance must maintain a cumulative GPA of 2.5 and Opportunity and Provisional must maintain a cumulative 2.25. more...

Wyoming Works Scholarship
Wyoming Works is a state grant program and resources are targeted toward adult students to advance their skills in areas that lead to secure employment. Space and grant funding is limited. Funding is only guaranteed for one year. Maximum award amounts for the Wyoming Works Standard Grant will be up to $840 per semester and the Wyoming Works Critical Grant will be up to $1,680 per semester.
Steps to apply for Wyoming Works:
Apply for Admission to Western
File your FAFSA

Apply for Wyoming Works (click the sign-in button, use your westernwyoming.edu email address and password, answer the eligibility questions and submit)
