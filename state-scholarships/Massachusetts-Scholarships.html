Bsu Study Abroad Scholarship
BSU Study Abroad Scholarships are provided to support BSU students participating in academic international education and study away programs including BSU Travel Courses, Semester Exchange, Bridgewater IN Semesters, Affiliate Programs, International Internships, and International Student Teaching Placements. Awards are based on financial need and are secondary funding opportunities to Financial Aid opportunities. The BSU Study Abroad Scholarship amounts range from $500 $750 for a Travel Course; $750$1,000 for summer programs, international internships, and international student teacher placements; and $1,000-$2,500 for semester programs. Individual award amounts will vary depending on demonstrated financial need and availability of funds.
ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA
1. Student must be enrolled in a BSU undergraduate or graduate degree program and applying for an approved BSU study abroad/away program. Students must be earning academic credit(s) for their program; students auditing a BSU Travel Course are ineligible.
2. Student must demonstrate financial need and have a current FAFSA record on file at BSU.
3. Student must have a minimum GPA of 2.5 for travel courses and 2.75 for semester programs.
4. Student must provide specific and realistic travel and/or educational plans. Any personal financial situations that you feel comfortable sharing should be included in detail.
Acceptance of a BSU Study Abroad Scholarship is contingent on the student completing a post-travel evaluation and thank you letter submitted to the BSU Study Abroad Office. Students withdrawing from their study abroad program will forfeit the award and funds already deposited in the student’s account will be withdrawn.
Submission Deadline: BSU Study Abroad Scholarship Applications are reviewed three (3) times a year and completed applications should be submitted by the deadline date to be considered for an award. Students will be contacted via BSU email within 2 weeks of each submission deadline advising their award status.

Student Parent Programs - Graduate Student Child Care Subsidy Program
Student Parent Programs awards funding to graduate student parents to help defray the cost of child care expenses and help students focus on their educational goals. Eligibility for funding is limited to student parents with documented financial need (as determined by Financial Aid Services and Student Parent Programs) with current or anticipated eligible child care expenses. As you complete the application, please consider all options that will allow you the best chance of success in your program this year. Your child care plan may include a mix of several types of child care options – please include all that you anticipate utilizing this year.
The child care subsidy is awarded to student parents as a reimbursement for documented expenses or directly to the child care provider. This subsidy is for graduate students only. If you are an undergraduate student, please apply using the undergraduate child care subsidy application.
To qualify for child care subsidies, student parents must be actively enrolled at UMass Amherst as an undergraduate or graduate student on a part-time or full-time basis and must meet the income eligibility criteria for program participants – similar to Pell grant income eligibility, determined through Financial Aid Services and federal tax documents.
We primarily fund child care through licensed providers however, some funding may be available for other types of child care. Please note that the majority of our funds are restricted by our funding source to licensed or accredited providers only.
Child care expenses can include daycare, pre-k, before and after school programs, and summer child care through EEC licensed or nationally accredited providers. Some babysitting, home-based non-EEC licensed care, and other informal child care may also qualify for limited funding.
Subsidies will be determined based on applicant demonstrated need, enrollment status and may be adjusted based on funds available within the program or a change to applicant’s status.
Please do not apply for this opportunity if you will not have a dependent child in your care at least part-time more...

Undergraduate Child Care Support - Spp And Ccampis
Student Parent Programs awards funding to undergraduate student parents to help defray the cost of child care expenses and help students focus on their educational goals. Eligibility for funding is limited to student parents with documented financial need (as determined by Financial Aid Services.) with current or anticipated eligible child care expenses. As you complete the application, please consider all options that will allow you the best chance of success in your program this year. Your child care plan may include a mix of several types of child care options – please include all that you anticipate utilizing this year.
The child care subsidy is awarded to student parents as a reimbursement for documented expenses or directly to the child care provider. This subsidy is for undergraduate students only.
To qualify for child care subsidies, student parents must be actively enrolled at UMass Amherst as an undergraduate or graduate student on a part-time or full-time basis and must meet the income eligibility criteria for program participants – similar to Pell grant income eligibility, determined through Financial Aid Services.
We primarily fund child care through licensed providers however, some funding may be available for other types of child care. Please note that the majority of our funds are restricted by our funding source to licensed or accredited providers only.
Child care expenses can include daycare, pre-k, before and after school programs, and summer child care through EEC licensed or nationally accredited providers. Some babysitting, home-based non-EEC licensed care, and other informal child care may also qualify for limited funding.
Subsidies will be determined based on applicant demonstrated need, enrollment status and may be adjusted based on funds available within the program or a change to applicant’s status.
