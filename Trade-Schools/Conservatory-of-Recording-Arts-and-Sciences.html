Conservatory of Recording Arts and Sciences

Tempe, AZ
2300 E Broadway Rd, Tempe, AZ 85282-1707
480-858-9400

The Conservatory of Recording Arts and Sciences (CRAS) in Tempe, Arizona is a world-renowned recording arts and sciences school. Founded in 1985, CRAS has been training the next generation of audio engineers, producers, and musicians for over three decades. With over 30,000 alumni, CRAS is one of the most respected and well-known recording arts and sciences schools in the world.

CRAS offers a variety of programs to fit the needs of any student, from those just starting out in the recording industry to those who are looking to take their career to the next level. The school offers Associate’s and Bachelor’s degrees in Recording Arts, Music Production, and Live Sound Engineering, as well as a Master’s degree in Music Business. CRAS also offers a variety of certificate programs for those who want to specialize in a specific area of the recording arts and sciences.

The Conservatory of Recording Arts and Sciences is accredited by the National Association of Schools of Music and is a member of the National Association of Recording Arts and Sciences. CRAS is also an active member of the Audio Engineering Society and the Producers & Engineers Wing of the Recording Academy.

The Conservatory of Recording Arts and Sciences has a long history of producing some of the most successful and well-known audio professionals in the world. alumni include Grammy and Emmy-winning producers, engineers, and musicians, as well as executives and entrepreneurs in the music industry. CRAS graduates are working at the top of the music industry, in all aspects of the business.

The Conservatory of Recording Arts and Sciences is committed to providing its students with the skills and knowledge they need to succeed in the ever-changing world of the music industry. With a faculty of experienced industry professionals, state-of-the-art facilities, and a curriculum that is constantly evolving, CRAS is the perfect place for anyone looking to start or further their career in the recording arts and sciences.
