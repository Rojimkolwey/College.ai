About Alabama

The state of Alabama is located in the southeastern region of the United States and is bordered by Tennessee to the north, Georgia to the east, and Mississippi to the west. Alabama is the 30th largest state in the country with an area of 52,423 square miles, and is the 24th most populous state with a population of 4,887,871. The state capital of Alabama is Montgomery, and the largest city is Birmingham.

Alabama's climate is generally humid subtropical, with long, hot summers and short, mild winters. The state is prone to hurricanes and tropical storms, as well as tornadoes. Alabama is also home to a variety of plant and animal life, including the American alligator, the bald eagle, and the red-cockaded woodpecker.

Alabama's economy is largely based on agriculture, forestry, and manufacturing. The state is also a leading producer of coal, iron ore, and natural gas. Alabama's history is marked by a number of significant events, including the American Civil War, the Civil Rights Movement, and the Space Race.
