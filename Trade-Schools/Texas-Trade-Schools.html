About Texas

Texas is the second most populous state in the United States with a population of over 28 million people. It is also the second largest state in terms of land area, covering 268,820 square miles. Texas is located in the south-central part of the country and borders the states of Louisiana, Arkansas, Oklahoma, New Mexico, and Mexico. The state's capital is Austin, and its largest city is Houston.

Texas has a diverse economy that is fueled by oil, agriculture, healthcare, technology, and manufacturing. The state is home to numerous Fortune 500 companies, including ExxonMobil, ConocoPhillips, AT&T, and Dell. Texas is also a leading producer of oil, cotton, cattle, and sheep.

The state's climate varies depending on location, but is generally hot and humid in the summer and mild to cool in the winter. Texas is prone to hurricanes and tornadoes, and is also affected by droughts and floods.

Texas is rich in history and culture. It was once part of Mexico, and later became an independent republic before joining the United States in 1845. The state is known for its cowboy culture, as well as its music, food, and architecture.
