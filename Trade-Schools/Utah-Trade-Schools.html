About Utah

The state of Utah is located in the western United States and is bordered by Idaho, Wyoming, Colorado, and Arizona. It is the 13th largest state in the US with an area of 84,899 square miles. The population of Utah is 3,205,958 as of 2019. The capital and largest city of Utah is Salt Lake City with a population of 193,864.

The climate in Utah is mostly arid with cold winters and hot summers. The landscape of Utah is very diverse with mountains, forests, deserts, and canyons. Some of the most popular tourist destinations in Utah include Zion National Park, Bryce Canyon National Park, Arches National Park, and Canyonlands National Park.

The economy of Utah is driven by tourism, agriculture, mining, and manufacturing. The state is home to several major corporations including Huntsman Corporation, Zions Bancorporation, and USANA Health Sciences.

The history of Utah is fascinating and includes early Native American tribes, the arrival of the first European explorers, the establishment of Mormon settlements, and the state's admission to the Union. Utah has a rich cultural heritage and is home to many different ethnic and religious groups.
