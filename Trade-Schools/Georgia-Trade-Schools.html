About Georgia

The state of Georgia is located in the southeastern United States. It is bordered by the states of Alabama, Tennessee, South Carolina, and Florida. Georgia has a population of over 10 million people, and its capital is Atlanta.

Georgia is known for its peaches, its peanuts, and its pecans. The state is also home to the world's largest aquarium, the Georgia Aquarium. Georgia is a popular tourist destination, with attractions such as the Georgia Dome, Stone Mountain, and the Savannah Historic District.

The economy of Georgia is largely fueled by agriculture, tourism, and manufacturing. The state is the leading producer of peaches, peanuts, and pecans in the United States. Georgia is also a leading producer of cotton, timber, and chicken.

Georgia is home to several major colleges and universities, including the University of Georgia, Georgia Tech, and Emory University. The state is also home to the Georgia Institute of Technology, one of the top engineering schools in the country.
