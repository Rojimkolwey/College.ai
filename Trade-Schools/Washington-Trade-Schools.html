About Washington

The state of Washington is located in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States. The state capital is Olympia, and the largest city is Seattle.

The climate of Washington varies depending on location. The western part of the state, which is west of the Cascade Mountains, has a temperate climate, with mild winters and cool summers. The eastern part of the state, which is east of the Cascade Mountains, has a more continental climate, with colder winters and hotter summers.

The economy of Washington is driven by a number of different industries. The state is home to a number of major corporations, including Microsoft, Amazon, and Boeing. Washington is also a major producer of agricultural products, including apples, potatoes, and wheat. The state is also a leading producer of timber.

Washington is a major tourist destination, due to its diverse landscape and array of attractions.
