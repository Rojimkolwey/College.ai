Elaine Sterling Institute is a private, for-profit cosmetology school in Atlanta, Georgia. The school is accredited by the National Accrediting Commission of Career Arts and Sciences.

The Institute offers a variety of cosmetology programs, including a diploma in cosmetology, an Associate of Applied Science in Cosmetology, and a Master of Cosmetology degree. The Institute also offers a variety of other programs, such as a diploma in esthetics, a diploma in nail technology, and a certificate in make-up artistry.

The Institute's cosmetology program includes courses in hair cutting, hair coloring, hair styling, skin care, and nail care. The program takes approximately 1,500 hours to complete, and students must complete a minimum of 1,000 hours of practical training.

The Institute's esthetics program includes courses in facial treatments, body treatments, make-up application, and salon management. The program takes approximately 600 hours to complete, and students must complete a minimum of 300 hours of practical training.

The Institute's nail technology program includes courses in nail care, nail art, and salon management. The program takes approximately 600 hours to complete, and students must complete a minimum of 300 hours of practical training.

The Institute's make-up artistry program includes courses in make-up application, color theory, and salon management. The program takes approximately 300 hours to complete, and students must complete a minimum of 150 hours of practical training.

Graduates of the Institute's cosmetology program are eligible to take the Georgia State Board of Cosmetology exam to become licensed cosmetologists. Graduates of the Institute's esthetics program are eligible to take the Georgia State Board of Cosmetology exam to become licensed estheticians.
