Dr. Carolyn Williams History Research Award Endowment
The Dr. Carolyn Williams Research Award was established in honor of Dr. Carolyn Williams, Associate Professor of History at UNF until her passing in 2012. In 1975, Dr. Williams graduated from UNF with a BA in History, subsequently earning a Ph.D. at UCLA. She returned to Jacksonville, serving for more than two decades as a beloved member of UNF history faculty, where she specialized in U.S., women’s, African American, and local history, a subject about which she became fascinated in her later years.
The Department of History is seeking applications for the Dr. Carolyn Williams Research Award. Grants will provide [up to] $1500 to support original research on any historical topic. Undergraduate and graduate History majors are encouraged to apply. Applicants must have a minimum UNF GPA of 3.0 to be eligible for consideration.
