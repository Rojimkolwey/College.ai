University of New Mexico-Main Campus Scholarships


Amigo Scholarship
The Amigo Scholars Program is open to non-resident incoming freshmen accepted into the University of New Mexico. You must either have a minimum 3.5 GPA and an ACT score of at least 23 (or SAT equivalent 1070), or a minimum 3.0 GPA and at least a 26 on the ACT (or SAT equivalent 1190) to be considered for this award. Additionally, you must be a U.S. citizen. more...

Amigo Scholarship For Transfer Students
The Amigo Transfer Scholarship is open to non-resident transfer students accepted into the University of New Mexico. You must have a minimum 3.5 GPA or a minimum 3.25 GPA with at least 30 transferable credit hours to be eligible for this renewable award. You must also be a U.S. citizen. more...

Cert-Council Of Energy Resource Tribes Program
CERT is a multi-Tribal organization (American Indian Tribes & Canadian Indian nations.) TRIBES is a pre-college program to enhance academic skills and prepare for transition to college life for Indian high school graduates pursuing careers in Energy Resource Development, Engineering, Business, Science and related fields. The 7-week college credit program is currently offered at the University of New Mexico in Albuquerque. Cost of instruction, activities, room & board are paid by TRIBES program. American Indian students must be graduating High School seniors, plan to attend college in the fall, pursue a career in business, engineering, science or related fields. Selections based on school grades, ACT/SAT scores, recommendations, & essay. TRIBES graduates are eligible for CERT scholarships/internships. more...

Presidential Scholars Program
The Presidential Scholars Program is open to incoming freshmen at the University of New Mexico. You must be a graduate of a New Mexico high school, have a minimum 3.75 GPA and an ACT score of at least 25 (or 1140 SAT), and demonstrate leadership experience with an outstanding extracurricular record to be considered for this award. You must provided a statement of philosophy and purpose to be considered for this award. more...

Regents' Scholar Award
The Regents' Scholar Award is open to incoming freshmen accepted into the University of New Mexico. You must be the valedictorian of your high school class, or have an ACT score of at least 31 or 1368 SAT, or have a cumulative sixth semester GPA of 3.9 or higher. National Scholarship recognition, advanced college preparatory work, and extracurricular and leadership activities will also be considered. You must provide a statement of your goals and philosophy and two letters of recommendation. more...
