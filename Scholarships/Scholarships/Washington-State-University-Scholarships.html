Washington State University Scholarships


April Seehafer Scholarship
The April Seehafer Scholarship is available to returning female undergraduates at Washington State University. You must be majoring in English/business, English/pre-law or English with a minimum GPA of 3.0. You must also demonstrate financial need to be considered for this award. more...

Basil And Ella Alexander Jerard Scholarship
The Basil and Ella Alexander Jerard Scholarship is open to full-time students at Washington State University. You must be majoring in English with a minimum 3.0 GPA to be considered for this award. This award is based on academic merit and financial need. more...

Doug Pride Memorial Scholarship
Scholarship for graduating high school seniors of Woodinville High School attending Washington State University, Seattle University, or Eastern, Central or Western Washington University pursuing a major in Architecture, Business Administration, Engineering, Veterinary Medicine, Science, or Teaching. Applicants must have a minimum 3.0 GPA and be a U.S. citizen. Renewable. Contact students high school guidance counselor or the Foundation for more information and/or application. more...

Edward F. Fields Memorial Fund
Scholarship for graduates of a Walla Walla County, Washington high school who were active in 4-H or FFA and will are full-time undergraduate students at Washington State University pursuing studies relating to Livestock, Veterinary Medicine or Extensions Services. Student must file the FAFSA. Due date, number of awards, and award amount may vary. Nonrenewable. more...

Eva Feryl Peterson Scholarship
The Eva Feryl Peterson Scholarship is available to female undergraduate and graduate students at Washington State University. You must be of Native American descent and be pursuing a graduate degree in English to be considered for this award. more...

Graduate Scholar Award - Wsu
The Graduate Scholar Award is available to incoming graduate students at Washington State University. This award supplements a Teaching Assistantship and is renewable. more...

Kache Scholarship
Scholarship for Washington residents who are graduating high school seniors planning to attend Washington State University within the Colleges of Agriculture, Home Economics, Education and/or Veterinary Medicine. Number of awards and award amount varies. Nonrenewable; may reapply. For application information, contact the Office of Financial Aid at Washington State University. more...

Master Gardeners Of Pierce County Scholarship
Annual award for Washington state resident that is a Pierce county high school graduate, undergraduate or graduate student planning to enroll, or currently attending, Washington State University to study Horticulture or the Environment at the Puyallup Research and Extension Center. Applicant must have demonstrated financial need. Preference will be given to currently enrolled graduate students. Due date, number of awards and award amount varies. more...

Maynard L. Daggy Scholarship
The Maynard L. Daggy Scholarship is available to speech and hearing sciences students at Washington State University. You must have a minimum GPA of 3.0 and demonstrate general service and financial need to be considered for this award. more...

Melvin P. Mel & Lyndell Crowder Ncw Memorial Horticulture Scholarship
Scholarship for Wasington residents who are undergraduate students enrolled at Wenatchee Valley College in a Tree Fruit Production program or Washington State University in Tree Fruit Management in the Horticulture program. Number of awards may vary. more...

Murray W. Bundy Scholarship
The Murray W. Bundy Scholarship is available to junior or senior English majors at Washington State University. You must be committed to a career in secondary teaching and have a minimum GPA of 3.0. Additionally, you must be enrolled in the English Teaching option to be eligible for this award. more...

Robert & Anna Neill Wsu Scholarship
Scholarship for students who are attending Washington State University within the Colleges of Agriculture, Home Economics, Education and/or Veterinary Medicine. Number of awards and award amount varies. For application information, contact the Office of Financial Aid at Washington State University. more...

Sarah Davies Kravik Scholarship
Annual award for female undergraduate students who are active Sigma Tau Chapter members of the Kappa Delta Sorority at Washington State University. Selection is based on campus, Greek, and Kappa Delta leadership and involvement, academic excellence, service to the chapter, alumnae association or national Kappa Delta, service to the campus and community, financial need, personal objectives and goals, potential and recommendations. A personal statement and a recent professional quality photograph must be included with application packet. more...

Umatilla County Cattlemens Association Scholarship
Scholarship for Oregon residents who are graduates of a Umatilla or Morrow County high school who will be enrolling as a sophomore or higher in a select four-year college/university majoring in Agricultural Science or Home Economics. Select schools are: Blue Mountain Community College, Eastern Oregon University, Oregon State University, Washington State University, or the University of Idaho. Applicants must be U.S. citizens/eligible noncitizens, file the FAFSA, and be full-time students. Recipients may reapply and compete annually. Award amount and number of awards vary. Contact financial aid office, the OCF, or OSAC for more information and/or application. more...

William W. Kilworth Scholarship
Annual award for Pierce County, Washington residents that are graduating high school seniors from Clover Park High School, Lincoln High School, Stadium High School, or Wilson High School that plans to pursue postsecondary education at the University of Puget Sound, University of Washington, Washington State University, University or Oregon or Oregon State University. Applicant must have demonstrated financial need. Due date, number of awards and award amount varies. Contact guidance office at applicable high school for additional information and/or application. more...

Woodinville Rotary Scholarship In Viticulture-Enology Studies
Scholarship for undergraduate students attending Washington State University, Walla Walla Community College, or Central Washington University pursuing a degree in Wine Grape Production, Enology, or Marketing of wine products. Consideration is given to students who have resided in or attended high school in the Northshore School District. Renewable. more...
