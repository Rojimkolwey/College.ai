filled out by the student and/or an advisor declaring a major);
6. Student must have a 3.00 or higher grade point average and must maintain a 3.00 or higher grade
point average while receiving the scholarship;
7. Student must submit a recent copy of his/her official college transcript;
8. Student must complete a 2014-15 Lou McGlothian Memorial Scholarship application;
9. Student has applied for federal financial aid and can attach a copy of his/her 2013-2014 Student
Aid Report (SAR) to the scholarship application. If it is not submitted at the time of the
application, it must be forwarded before the interviewing process begins on December 3, 2014;
10. Student must attach a copy of his/her Financial Aid Offer/Award Letter from the college they
attend. If it is not submitted at the time of the application, it must be forwarded before the
interviewing process begins on December 3, 2014.
11. Student must submit a self-written, typed essay, not exceeding 2 pages, answering the following:
1) Why he/she feels deserving of this scholarship?
2) Once you finish your education, how do you plan to impact your community?

12. Student has two written recommendations from 1) a professor in their declared major field; and
2) an academic advisor or another professor;
13. If selected for a panel interview, the student must be available on Wednesday, December 3,
2014, for this purpose.
15. If selected as the winner of the Lou McGlothian Memorial Scholarship, the student (or a
designee) must attend the Annual Strategic Partnership Luncheon on Thursday, February 19,
2015.
