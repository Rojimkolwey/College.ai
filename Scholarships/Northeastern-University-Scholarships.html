Northeastern University Scholarships


Leonora Keeney Pemberton Memorial Scholarship
The Leonora Keeney Pemberton Memorial Scholarship is available to students at Northeastern University School of Law. You must demonstrate a desire to help those in need and be interested in women's issues, poverty law and family law to be eligible for this award. more...

Marion K. Terry Scholarship
The Marion K. Terry Scholarship is available to first-year female students at Northeastern University School of Law. You must demonstrate financial need to be eligible for this award. more...

Martin Luther King, Jr. Graduate Fellowship - Northeastern University
The Martin Luther King, Jr. Graduate Fellowship is available to African - American graduate students at Northeastern University. You must demonstrate financial need and academic progress to be eligible for this award. more...

Melvin R. Wintman Scholarship
The Melvin R. Wintman Scholarship is available to second- and third-year students at Northeastern University School of Law. You must demonstrate financial need to be eligible for this award. more...

Nathan And Adele Hillman Scholarship
The Nathan and Adele Hillman Scholarship is available to students at Northeastern University School of Law. You must be either an orphan or from a broken home and demonstrate financial need to be eligible for this award. more...

Philip E. Tesorero Scholarship
The Philip E. Tesorero Scholarship is available to students at Northeastern University School of Law. You must demonstrate both superior academic achievement and financial need to be eligible for this award. more...

Richard G. And Clare E. Lesser Scholarship
The Richard G. and Clare E. Lesser Scholarship is available to female students at Northeastern University School of Law. You must demonstrate academic promise, financial need and a special interest in public service law to be eligible for this award. more...

Townsend Patent Law Scholarship
The Townsend Patent Law Scholarship is available to students at Northeastern University School of Law. You must complete all of the requirements to practice before the U.S. Patent Office and hold a graduate degree in science or engineering to be eligible for this award. more...
