University of Wisconsin-Madison Scholarships


Cals Continuing Student Scholarship - Uw - Madison
The CALS Continuing Student Scholarship is open to students attending the University of Wisconsin - Madison. You must be enrolled in the College of Agricultural and Life Sciences to be eligible for this award. You must also submit a CALS scholarship application and one letter of recommendation. This award is renewable. more...

Cals Freshman Scholarship - Uw - Madison
The CALS Freshman Scholarship is open to incoming freshmen accepted into the University of Wisconsin - Madison. You must be enrolled in the College of Agricultural and Life Sciences to be eligible for this award. You must also submit an application form and a letter of recommendation. All students who indicate CALS on their admission application will be sent a CALS scholarship application. more...

Cals Minority Scholarship - Uw - Madison
The CALS Minority Scholarship is open to minority students attending the University of Wisconsin - Madison. You must be enrolled in the College of Agricultural and Life Science to be eligible for this award. You must also submit an application form and one letter of recommendation. This award is renewable. more...

Eli Lilly And Company Academic Merit Award
The Eli Lilly and Company Academic Merit Award is available to students in the College of Agricultural and Life Sciences at the University of Wisconsin, Madison. You must be an undergraduate major in animal sciences to be eligible for this award. more...

Foth & Van Dyke & Associates Inc.
Scholarship for residents of Brown, Door, Kewaunee, Oconoto, or Outagamie counties, Wisconsin who are FFA members and will be incoming freshmen or current undergraduate students enrolling/enrolled at selected colleges pursuing studies in Civil Engineering, Electrical Engineering or Mechanical Engineering. Selected colleges are: University of Wisconsin-Madison, University of Wisconsin-Platteville, Milwaukee School of Engineering, Madison Area Technical College or Northwest Wisconsin Technical College. Student must be a U.S. citizen. Criteria used for selection includes students GPA (minimum 3.2), ACT or SAT scores, class rank, leadership in FFA activities, leadership in community activities, and involvement in the Supervised Agricultural Experience Program (SAE). Nonrenewable. more...

Fox River Valley Contractors Association Scholarship (Association)
Scholarship for full-time undergraduate students who are children of Fox River Valley Contractors Association member firms and are attending Marquette University, Milwaukee School of Engineering, University of Wisconsin-Madison, Unversity of Wisconsin-Platteville, or University of Wisconsin-Stout. Students must be pursuing a bachelors degree in Engineering, Architecture, or Construction Management. An essay must be included with application packet. Number of awards may vary. Renewable. more...

Fox River Valley Contractors Association Scholarship (High Schools)
Scholarship for graduating high school seniors of a high school in Brown, Calumet, Fond du Lac, Green Lake, Manitowoc, Oconto, Outagamie, Shawano, Sheboygan, Waupaca, Waushara, or Winnebago Counties, Wisconsin. Students must be attending Marquette University, Milwaukee School of Engineering, University of Wisconsin-Madison, Unversity of Wisconsin-Platteville, or University of Wisconsin-Stout pursuing a bachelors degree in Engineering, Architecture, or Construction Management. An essay must be included with application packet. Number of awards may vary. Renewable. more...

Graduate School Of Banking Association Membership Scholarship
Scholarship for graduate students who are employees of an Illinois Bankers Association Member Institution enrolled in the University of Wisconsin-Madison Graduate School of Banking. Applicants must be applying for the first year of the GSB program, have a minimum of five years banking experience, and have the endorsement of senior management. Due date may vary. more...

James T. Schremp Memorial Scholarship Fund
Scholarship for graduating high school seniors from Mosinee High School planning to attend a postsecondary institution pursuing studies in a Business related field. Students must be scholar/athletes. Preference given to students planning to attend the University of Wisconsin-Madison. Deadlines and award amounts may vary. Contact students guidance counselor or the Foundation for more information and/or application. more...

Project Assistantship - Uw Madison, Business School
The Project Assistantship is available to MBA students at the University of Wisconsin, Madison, Business School. You must work at least 13 hours per week to be eligible for this award. more...

Scholarship-University Of Wisconsin-Madison
Scholarship for juniors or seniors attending the University of Wisconson-Madison who are majoring in a field related to cranberry production. Selection based on academic achievement and financial need. Preference given to applicants with prior experience in cranberry production. Deadlines and award amounts may vary. Contact the College of Agriculture and Life Sciences for more information. more...

Summer Fellowship In Government & Community Service
Annual fellowship for full-time medical students to gain knowledge of state government and the private sector delivering health care service to the public. Fellowship is an 8-10 week course held at University of Wisconsin-Madison Medical School or the Medical College of Wisconsin. Applicants must be U.S. citizens and be members of the Wisconsin Medical Society. Number of awards may vary. Nonrenewable. more...

T.P. Olson Engineering Scholarship
Annual award for South Wood County, Wisconsin resident, who is a sophomore or higher college student at an accredited four-year college or university pursuing a degree in the field of Engineering. Preference will be given to students attending the University of Wisconsin-Madison. Award amount varies. more...

Teaching Assistantship - Uw Madison, Business School
The Teaching Assistantship is available to MBA students at the University of Wisconsin, Madison, Business School. You must be responsible for one or more lecture or discussion section of undergraduate students and work at least 13 hours per week to be eligible for this award. more...
