Opportunity Scholars Program
The Opportunity Scholars Program is a two-year, cohort experience that will support students in obtaining the education and skills needed to earn a career-focused, technical degree, and embark on a pathway to a successful career.
Program participants will be selected from applicants who are recent Mecklenburg County high school graduates with many being graduates from the following Charlotte Mecklenburg Schools high schools—Garinger, Harding, Vance, West Charlotte, and West Mecklenburg.
Program details include:
Scholarships will cover the complete cost of attendance—tuition, books, and fees—for the two years required to earn a technical, career-focused degree
Students will receive notebook computers to help meet their digital needs
Students will participate in Central Piedmont’s Summer Bridge academic enrichment program prior to their first fall semester to help prepare them for college-level work
Students will receive the support of an academic counselor/career coach to help with course and career path selection, identify internships and other workplace learning opportunities, and provide assistance with job placement
Student selection for the Opportunity Scholars Program will be based upon a student’s demonstrated potential for success, including:
School performance
A minimum GPA of 2.6
A letter of recommendation from a teacher or counselor

Presidential Scholars Program
The Central Piedmont Community College Presidential Scholars Program provides an opportunity to receive a scholarship award from one of the college’s most prestigious scholarship programs and offers a unique cohort experience for first-time freshmen attending Central Piedmont.
As a Presidential Scholar, you will benefit from leadership development, service-learning, and peer mentoring opportunities as a part of an intentional community of scholars. Over the course of your educational journey, you will participate in a customized seminar series and volunteer projects, and help to successfully orient new students into the Presidential Scholars Program.
This merit-based honor will be awarded to 30 students in 2022-2023. Eligible students must:
*be a 2022 graduate from high school
have an unweighted high school GPA of 3.0 or higher
be committed to finishing an associate degree at Central Piedmont
maintain a minimum 3.0 GPA while at Central Piedmont and stay continuously enrolled full time (summer excluded)
Presidential Scholarships cover tuition and fees for a maximum of two years (fall and spring semesters). If eligible, Presidential Scholars could receive other financial aid funds, such as Pell Grants.
Steps to Apply:
Apply to Central Piedmont Community College (https://www.cpcc.edu/admissions/apply)
Activate your student login and email after receiving your student id number

Apply to the Presidential Scholars program by clicking the “Apply To” button next to Presidential Scholars Program

Wings
Women Initiating and Nurturing Growth through Scholarships (WINGS) is a life-changing scholarship program for non-traditional female students at Charlotte area universities offered by Women Executives (WE). As the sole philanthropic initiative of WE, WINGS provides scholarship and mentoring support for female undergraduate students, 25 years or older, who are pursuing higher education as a way to improve their lives.
Since 1995, WE’s Women Executives for Community Service, WECS, (the 501c-3 non-profit affiliate of WE), has been awarding WINGS scholarships to women attending the University of North Carolina at Charlotte, Queens University of Charlotte, Central Piedmont Community College, and Johnson C. Smith University.  Each year, WINGS supports 20 scholars.  Unique to many scholarships, WINGS follows and funds each scholar throughout her undergraduate career as long as she is in compliance with the scholarship requirements.
To find out more about the WINGS Scholarship view this video. WECS and WINGS thanks Tonya Jameson of Darling Media Group for her support in filming and producing this video: http://www.vimeo.com/12056353
The women selected as WINGS scholars are dealing with life’s challenges, but are determined to persevere.  Though they may struggle financially and personally, they realize that they can improve their circumstances, and their families, through education.
Scholarship recipients must meet established financial need criteria and participate in a competitive application and interview process to be considered for a scholarship.
Commonalities among all WINGS scholars include:
Demonstrated financial need
Clear educational goals
A strong vision of their professional future
A history of overcoming challenges
A desire to form a strong mentor/mentee relationship
Scholarship funds are administered by the financial aid offices at each of the four partner schools, and students’ academic progress and achievement are monitored annually. more...
