Hathaway Scholarship
The Wyoming State Legislature approved legislation during their Spring 2006 session to establish a scholarship fund for Wyoming students to attend Western Wyoming Community college, any of the other six Wyoming community colleges, or the University of Wyoming. The legislation creates merit and need-based awards for eligible students to provide an incentive to pursue post-secondary education within the State of Wyoming. In addition, students initiating their Hathaway Scholarship at Western before July 31st who are Honors or Performance level will receive an enhancement to their Hathaway award. Hathaway award levels, requirements, and values are as follows.
Honors: $3,360 yearly award + $1,200 yearly enhancement (if requirement met)

Requires a minimum 3.5 high school GPA and an ACT composite of 25
Must complete State required class curriculum
Performance: $2,520 yearly award + $1,000 yearly enhancement (if requirement met)

Requires a minimum 3.0 high school GPA and an ACT composite of 21
Must complete State required class curriculum
Opportunity: $1,680 yearly award

Requires a minimum 2.5 high school GPA and an ACT composite of 19
Must complete State required class curriculum
Provisional: $1,680 yearly award

Requires a minimum 2.5 high school GPA and an ACT composite of 17
Must complete State required class curriculum
In order to receive awards, the Hathaway scholarship must be initiated and final transcripts must be received by July 31st. Hathaway Scholarships are awarded based on college-level class enrollment; if below 12 college-level credit hours, awards are prorated. If you earned a high school equivalency certificate (GED, HiSET) or were homeschooled please mark that on the application under the “Name of High School” field and send in any required documents including ACT scores or certificates. Students receiving Honors or Performance must maintain a cumulative GPA of 2.5 and Opportunity and Provisional must maintain a cumulative 2.25. more...

Wyoming Works Scholarship
Wyoming Works is a state grant program and resources are targeted toward adult students to advance their skills in areas that lead to secure employment. Space and grant funding is limited. Funding is only guaranteed for one year. Maximum award amounts for the Wyoming Works Standard Grant will be up to $840 per semester and the Wyoming Works Critical Grant will be up to $1,680 per semester.
Steps to apply for Wyoming Works:
Apply for Admission to Western
File your FAFSA

Apply for Wyoming Works (click the sign-in button, use your westernwyoming.edu email address and password, answer the eligibility questions and submit)
Who is Eligible for a Wyoming Works Grant?
Must be a new program participant starting Fall 2019 or later
Must complete the FAFSA form, showing financial need
Must be a U.S. citizen or a permanent resident alien who meets the definition of an eligible noncitizen under federal Title IV financial aid requirements
Must be a Wyoming resident
As required, must be registered with the selective service
Must maintain satisfactory academic progress
Must apply for this opportunity prior to the start of the term you are applying for
You must be enrolled in one of the eligible degree, certificate, or solo course programs:

Degrees:

AAS in Automotive Technology

AAS in Diesel and Heavy Equipment Technology
