Bobbi Biron Theater Scholarship
The Bobbi Biron Scholarship is awarded to an incoming freshman who has achieved a 3.0 high school cumulative GPA and shown outstanding dedication and promise in theatre. Recipients may qualify for scholarship renewal if achievements are met, including a 3.0 cumulative GPA and participation in theater productions. Additional information, requirements and tips to help you prepare for the performance audition or portfolio review can be found at this site: Bobbi Biron Scholarship
Requirements: What You Need To Do
• SU Admissions Application. Submit online application by
December 1 for Freshman Early Action or January 15 for Freshman Regular Decision.
• Please provide a link to download your resume, along with your audition video or online portfolio. Please see for details: CLICK HERE

How to Prepare
• Performance Audition: Introduce yourself and what you will do; perform one 60 to 90 second classical monologue and one 60 to 90
second contemporary monologue; end in a clear, strong, definitive manner.
Work with your drama teacher to select material that suits your age, type, and facility with language and ideas. Pay attention to
enunciation and pronunciation, vocal and expressive choices, character type, and storytelling to create a believable character. Be
prepared to discuss the theme or central idea, the emotional intent or mood of the selection, and what the character is attempting to
communicate. What artistic choices did you make and why?
• Portfolio Review: Seek help from your design or technical director to select and focus on one work. Consider scenery (perspective renderings,
storyboards, drafting, research materials, elevations, model photos, concept statement, and paperwork), costume (swatched costume
renderings, sketches, research material, photos, concept statement, and paperwork), or lighting (studies, storyboards, research, lights
plot, production photos, concept statement, and paperwork).

Cma - Certified Management Accountant
Are you planning to be a management, finance, or accounting professional working in industry? Would you like to highlight your current knowledge and skills to interested employers? You could do so by passing the CMA exam and becoming a CMA Candidate prior to graduation.
Through the Institute of Management Accountants (IMA®), graduate and undergraduate students in the Franklin P. Perdue School of Business at Salisbury University can apply for a nomination to receive one of ten CMA scholarships. To learn more about the CMA Click Here
Why take the CMA exam while still in school?
· Students like you have demonstrated a high ability to succeed on the exam because much of the exam content is covered in coursework.
· You’re already in study mode and exam material is top of mind
· You can take both exam parts and submit your work experience later
· The amazing ROI of holding both CMA & CPA certifications Click Here to Learn More
· CMAs between the ages of 20-29 can earn 16% more Click Here to Read More in total compensation than non certified peers globally. (IMA’s 2018 Global Salary Survey)
What is included?
· Entrance fee to the CMA program
· Exam support package with retired exam questions and a glossary of terms
· Registration fees covering both parts of the exam (additional fees will apply for retakes)
· Wiley CMA excel Online Test Bank access for both exam parts for up to two years (U.S. students only)
· Up to three years of IMA membership while pursuing the certification
Am I Eligible?
· Currently enrolled full time in a bachelor’s or master’s business degree program at SU
· Scholarships are awarded based on faculty nomination.
· Completion of ACCT 302 Cost Accounting I with a B or higher at Salisbury University
· Intent to take the CMA exam and become a CMA
How Do I Apply?
· The email address the students should use to submit their application is dals@salisbury.edu
· Please include a statement of purpose in the body of your email, your resume (pdf) attached along with your program of study, expected graduation date, and mailing address.
Who Will Be Chosen?
· Applicants will be ranked based on academic performance in accounting courses and overall GPA.
· Awards will be made to students in order of ranking until (1) all scholarships have been issued or (2) all applicants have received scholarships.
· 5 Awards will be made for each application cycle. Submission for the five spring awards must be received no later than March 1st at 5:00 pm. Submission for the five fall awards must be received no later than October 1st at 5:00 pm more...

David H. And Erma Pearl Scholarship
The David H. and Erma Pearl Scholarship will provide scholarship support for a student in good standing at Salisbury University in the Seidel School of Education, per the following criteria: a high school graduate of Worcester County, MD. If there are no qualified students from Worcester County, MD then a student from Somerset County, MD may be selected; a education major; junior or senior grade level, financial need from the current FAFSA and a 3.0 cumulative GPA or better. The scholarship may be renewed for a second year if awarded as a junior and the eligibility criteria continue to be maintained with a cumulative 3.0 GPA, eligible Seidel major and completion of 24 credits each year. more...

David Yuri Hooper Scholarship
The David Yuri Hooper Scholarship was established by David and Wilma Hooper to honor the excellent education received by their son who graduated from Salisbury University in 1994. It provides $1,000 scholarships for incoming, full-time freshman students from single-income families demonstrating financial need. The scholarship is renewable annually provided the recipient continues to meet the eligibility criteria. Preference will be given to students majoring in biology, but in the event that there are no qualified biology applicants, students majoring in any other department in the Henson School will be considered. Eligible candidates must submit a two-page essay explaining their need and demonstrating that they are of good moral character. Recipients are selected by the Deans of the Henson School and Enrollment Management. more...

Deirdre Adina Campbell Scholarship
The Deirdre Adina Campbell Scholarship will provide financial support to a student meeting the following criteria: 1) Student must be full time and in good standing at Salisbury University; 2) Have at least one semester remaining until degree completion at Salisbury University; 3) Have a cumulative GPA of 2.5 or better; 4) Pursue a major or minor in Biology; 5) Preference given to a student with financial need from the FAFSA; 6) Preference given to incoming students who identify themselves to be active in their local NAACP Chapter; 7) Preference given to current students who identify themselves to be members of an NAACP chapter and active in campus life through one or more of the following registers student organizations: Black Student Union; African Student Association; National Council of Negro Women. more...
